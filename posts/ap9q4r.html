    robbak A:

    One reason why they don't get the bends - or get it as badly - is that they do not hold their breath. Instead, they load up their muscles with oxygen, breathe out, and then submerge, surviving on that stored oxygen until they surface and breathe in.

    So there isn't much high-pressure air, with its toxic nitrogen, in their lungs to be absorbed into their blood.

    evil_burrito A:

    The bends are caused by inhaling pressurized/too much nitrogen at depth which escapes your blood and tissues as the ambient pressure decreases as you surface. This is a danger for SCUBA divers because the pressure regulator that's used to allow them to safely breathe at depth equalizes the pressure of the gas being inhaled with the current ambient pressure, which increases at depth. Whales don't inhale at depth, they suck in air on top and do not breathe again until they resurface. That means that all the nitrogen they inhale is at surface pressure.

    I am neither a lungologist nor a whaleologist, though, just a diver, so, I might be completely wrong.
