    Q:

    ELI5: Why is our Moon just called ‘The Moon’, while all the other Moons in our solar system have their own names?

    togoboldly A:

    Our moon was "discovered" long before all other moons in our solar system, and was named "the moon" by historic civilisations. Once we determined there were other moons, we used the name for our own moon to describe them.

    However, the correct name for them is actually "natural satellites".

    km89 A:

    The technical term for "moon" is "satellite".

    When we say that a planet has "moons", we mean it has large, orbiting satellites like the one orbiting ours, which we call "moon."

    "Moon" is the English name for our largest satellite. Other languages call it something different. "Luna," for one, or "Selene."
