    Q:

    ELI5: Why was New York City so dangerous in the 70s + 80s and how did it get that way?

    VieFirionaVie A:

    As others said, it was a national trend. Almost certainly it was many different reasons and not just one thing:

    • Higher alcohol consumption
    • Cheaper and more available crack cocaine, heroin, and other hard drugs
    • The drug wars accelerating organized crime
    • Demographics: Large boom of Gen X teenagers, low secondary education rates caused by high costs and short supply of college, poor inner-city primary education, white flight, etc.
    • PTSD rates from Vietnam and other wars
    • Riots and civil unrest caused by political and social factors
    • Maybe lead and other chemicals

    For more information, there is an excellent series of documentaries on urban crime in the 70s - 80s, Death Wish.

    Crime was higher everywhere in the US then. It wasn’t just New York City. Crime rates rose until the 90’s, and have since dropped. No one is quite sure why. There are a lot of theories.

    One of my favorites is low level lead poisoning. We know that kids had higher blood lead levels on average back when leaded gasoline was still being used and lead paint was more common. We also know that lead exposure in kids can cause behavior and learning problems.

    AgoraiosBum A:

    Lots of incomplete answers. Number one is the construction of freeways and urban decay combined with industrialization.

    Lots of money left NYC and went to the suburbs. Falling rents led to poorly maintained properties; dirty old factories closed and went elsewhere (sometimes overseas, sometimes down south or upstate). People employed at the many docks in Manhattan lost out to containerization and the Port of Bayonne; those with good longshoreman jobs moved to the suburbs. People too poor to shop at nice places meant those places closed. So you have the conditions for lots of people with no good jobs, and the City is straining under the loss of revenue and population and more expensive services.

    Then, you have lead poisoning. All the cars and expressways that get built pump a lot of exhaust into the air. With leaded gasoline, that means lead in the air, which means lead poisoning, which makes people dumber, more violent, and with impulse control issues (and if you have a population more inclined to those traits, it will statistically lead to more violent crime).

    So you have a lot more people who are impoverished, who can't get a good job, who are more inclined to be violent and impulsive, and you have a city that has less resources available to police them.
