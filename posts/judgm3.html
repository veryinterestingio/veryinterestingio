  • Earth Sciences How did the “Avatar Mountains” (Zhangjiajie, China) form, and why isn’t there any mountains similar to them anywhere else in the world?

    NEW
    Q:

    If my understanding of weathering and erosion is correct, there’s nothing special about the area to cause the odd-looking mountains to form

    Bm0ore A:

    Though this looks like karst terrain (which is a formation from the dissolution of soluble rocks such as limestone) this area is not limestone, it’s mostly quartz-sandstone, and is not thought to be the product of chemical erosion. It’s believed instead to be the product of many, many years of physical erosion mostly in the form of the expanding ice in the winter and from the plants that grow on the peaks. The material weathered by the ice and plants is then mostly carried away by streams.
