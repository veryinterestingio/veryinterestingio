    soccer_trekkie A:

    Your question implies intent that doesn't exist. A virus does not "mutate to evade our vaccines". It mutates usually because it's not great at copying itself. As it mutates a new strain may be more infectious and thus spread more, there are surely other mutations that spread less but they are more likely to die out or be less noticeable.

    It's also important to know that at least the Pfizer vaccine is still effective against the South African and British mutations. So the virus is still being slowed by them. There is now way to know though about any further mutations and if the current vaccines will work for those.
