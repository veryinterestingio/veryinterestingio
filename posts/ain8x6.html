  • Why are children allowed to star in TV and movies but are not allowed to work “normal” jobs?

    NEW
    Q:

    I assume either position would involve parental consent, so why one but not the other?

    blipsman A:

    Because laws were passed to allow for it, based on reasoning that there is no realistic alternative. But the laws also set strict rules for work hours, mandate on-set schooling, handling of minors' paychecks, etc. to protect the children who appear in TV and movies.

    freelanceforever A:

    The laws are very strict for child actors. Even on a small local commercial or indie film, production needs to hire a Studio Teacher if there will be any children on set. Leads or background, doesn’t matter. The teachers are also there to protect the children and to make sure production is treating them right.

    When they aren’t on set acting they are often in tutoring, even on weekend shoots.

    This is often why they cast 20 somethings to play high schoolers. It’s kind of a pain to hire children-it’s more money and a longer shoot schedule.
