    Q:

    Could you preserve a meal in the vacuum? Without all those pesky bacteria present (and low temperature), is space effectively a giant fridge?

    Lithuim A:

    The vacuum of space will quickly dry out food and make it unsuitable for any particularly tough bacteria/fungi that have survived being depressurized.

    So no, things will not rot in space.

    Whether you’d want to eat it is a very different question though. This isn’t just freeze drying, this is a hard vacuum with the frozen abyss on one end and the ultraviolet rage of a star on the other. The food is completely dehydrated down to a shriveled mummy and blasted with solar and cosmic radiation, pulverizing the surface at a molecular level.

    So it’s not moldy, but it’s soon reduced to a state that’s not food either.
