    Q:

    ELI5: Why do running companies have to make men's and women's shoes instead of unisex?

    tgpineapple A:

    https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19767544\

    On average, women have different foot shapes that aren't just scaled down versions of men's feet (or vice versa). Running shoe companies tailor around the 'average' man or woman's foot, which follows these measurements because you want a better fit whenever possible. A man could wear a woman's shoe and vice versa, but it might not be proportionately correct.

    ViciousKnids A:

    Men and women are structured differently. If you ever look at ski and snowboard boots, you'll notice women's boots look like they have a bit taken out of the back. It's because a women's calf goes further down the leg than a mans and having that bit missing makes the boot fit better.

    This applies to gloves, too. Men's index and ring finger are the same size. Women's ring fingers are shorter than their index finger. So there is a point, if you'll forgive the pun.
