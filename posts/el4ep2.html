  • Chemistry Why does bourbon amplify the flavor of vanilla?

    NEW
    Q:

    Whenever you see recipes for vanilla milkshakes and such, its usually recommended to add some bourbon. (and i've tried it myself, its amazing) I don't really understand how bitter can make something already sweet, taste sweeter.

    7even2wenty A:

    Bourbon is not that bitter, it’s actually very sweet for a liquor, much sweeter than say a rye whiskey or scotch. To be labeled as bourbon, the predominantly corn-based whiskey must be aged in charred oak barrels. The charring of the wood allows the alcohol to better absorb the wood’s flavors. In the charring process, it degrades the oak’s lignin (a polymer found in plant cells) into its three chemical components: synapealdehyde, syringealdehyde, and vanillin, which imparts characteristic vanilla flavors.
