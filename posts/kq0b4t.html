    Mother_of_Brains A:

    I don't know if there's a detailed description of that, but for sure it will not be one chemical that triggers sexual arousal (aka being horny). My educated guess is that the stimulus (specially visual, touch and smell) is sent through your brain stem to the hypothalamus and limbic areas (basolateral amygdala most likely). Now, the amygdala is basically a GABAergic and gluramatergic nucleus, so these chemicals (GABA and glutamate) will be released in the amygdala to signal the valence of the stimulus (if it's good or bad). From there, the information will be sent to the appropriate brain area to control behavior, in particular the prefrontal cortex and hippocampus. In the mean time, hormones will be released by the hypothalamus, probably oxytocin (attachment hormone) and some stress hormone like adrenaline. The signal from the amygdala will also probably make its way to the reward system of the brain, where dopamine and serotonin will likely be release to indicate a positive reinforce and the emotional correlate of feel good. That would be my guess.
