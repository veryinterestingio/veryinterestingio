    Q:

    ELI5: Why is it when men ejaculate it will sometimes be a high pressure squirt whereas other times it just dribbles out?

    lucky_ducker A:

    Semen has three components:

    seminal fluid - thick, sticky white stuff produced in the seminal vesicles

    prostatic fluid - thin, clear, slightly bluish, slimy and viscous fluid produced in the prostate gland

    sperm - actually a tiny proportion of the ejaculate, produced in the testicles

    All three are mixed together at the last minute when ejaculation occurs. Their exact proportions depends on many factors, but in general:

    the longer since the last ejaculation, the more seminal fluid is available

    the longer time that sexual stimulation has occurred, the more prostatic fluid will be available

    Ejaculations with a high proportion of prostatic fluid are thin and slippery and tend to sort of explode out of the penis.

    Ejaculations with a high proportion of seminal fluid are relatively thick and tend to just sort of ooze out of the penis.

    With age, the quantities of both fluids diminishes, and the force of orgasm with it, resulting in most ejaculations being a bit underwhelming. Still feels good, though.
