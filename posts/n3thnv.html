    SematarySativa A:

    Aside from what everyone has already posted about different lists and formats and timing, the "best sellers" are reported to the NYT by the merchants. So, let's say that some big-name brick-and-mortar shop buys too many of a certain title. They need to get interest in this title so they can move the merchandise. So they report that this title sold a bunch of copies, thereby placing it on the list, thereby drawing attention, so they can make their money. So basically, that book is a best seller because someone said it was a best seller. This of course doesn't apply to all books.
