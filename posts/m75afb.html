    ELI5: Why does the government make you pay more than you should in taxes but then gives you some of that money back as a tax return?

    The government doesn't make you pay more than you should. You decide how much to withhold from your paychecks to cover taxes. If you withhold too much then, when you actually file and the government sees how much you've paid vs. how much you owe they'll pay you back the difference.

    If you don't withhold at all then you sure as hell won't be getting a rebate, lol.

    EvenSpoonier A:

    Strictly speaking, they don't make you. You can set any amount of withholding you want. The W-4 and other worksheets they use to determine the proper amount of withholding do tend to err on the side of caution -interpreting "caution" to mean that they try to avoid withholding too little- so people often do wind up withholding too much. But you don't have to follow the W-4.
