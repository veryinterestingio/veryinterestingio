    ELI5: How is it that I have never googled anything about glasses, just went to the Optometrist earlier today and later got my first ever glasses commercial on YouTube... the same day?

    arlondiluthel A:

    I would assume you took your smartphone with you when you went to the optometrist. Your phone's location reported to Google's servers as having an extended stop at an optometrist, so their algorithms assumed you had an optometrist appointment. Many people don't go to the optometrist unless they feel like they need to get their vision checked.

    theSchmoozer A:

    Google (and other companies) will employ a range of technologies (and combine them) to create an accurate and continually updated assessment of your purchase intent. It's possible to connect data from multiple services and types of hardware even if you're not logged in. Some examples of what is data mined:

    • Geo location from your phone. They can tell if you visited a car dealership, for example, and deduce that you're about to make a big purchase. Why is this important? Because they'll spend way more money trying to influence you about a car purchase now that they know it's imminent. The way it works out is that they end up spending $1 on you before targeting but $10 on you after. Except it's less overall. Instead of spending $1m spamming ads at random times, or in random places hoping to catch whatever walks by, they can spend $250k on a smaller chunk of people who are way more likely to buy. Lower overall spend, more trying much harder per person, because they get great returns.
    • Search history from your browser. Obviously this will yield a metric fuckton of data about you, where you work, what your interests are, and your problems, medical info, and of course, your fappin' habits.
    • Microphone speech translated from your phone AND computer AND alexa, etc. This one is easy to test. Google it.
    • Transaction info from iwallet/android pay or whatever it's called. They can infer from this your income level and home address to within half a block.
    • Different lists from technologies like the above, but purchased from other collectors. You can compare two overlapping lists of consumers, validating duplicates and filling in the blanks when not a duplicate. Even if your data is anonymized it's possible to connect "person x who lives on Broadway ave and makes 100k/y and likes to go to starbuck's every morning before work"
