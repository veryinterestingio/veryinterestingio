  • Biology Christmas question: 'A bad lobster in a dark cellar' - what does it mean???

    NEW
    Q:

    I am rereading Dickens' A Christmas Carol. There is a line that says, 'It was not in impenetrable shadow as the other objects in the yard were, but had a dismal light about it, like a bad lobster in a dark cellar.' What does this mean? Do lobsters have bioluminescence when they start to go bad? Would they glow for some other reason? Do they glow at all? Can anyone shed any light on this?

    Thank you!

    feedmahfish A:

    It's likely he's referring to bacterial bioluminescence which has been known for quite a long time (hence its description in the book). It is reported often for rotting shrimp, and I wouldn't discount it for Lobster.

    This article talks about one species called Vibrio harveyi that is luminous in rock lobster.

    https://link.springer.com/content/pdf/10.1007/s42995-020-00037-z.pdf

    CalibanDrive A:

    Bioluminescent microbes, which are naturally found in sea water, will grow on rotting shellfish if the conditions are just right. The pale blue light they produce is very faint and can only be seen in a very dark place, like a cold storage cellar. Glowing lobster is lobster that has started to go bad.
