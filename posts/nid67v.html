  • Technology if/when all cars are electric, how much effect will this have on traffic noise?

    NEW
    Q:

    This might be a broad question but even taking a particular country or city as an example, how much effect would there be on traffic noise?

    hallonlakrits A:

    Quote from https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC7558663/

    In general, road traffic noise in urban environments is generated by vehicles and is mainly due to the friction of the tires and the noise of the engine. At speeds of over 30 km/h, the noise of tire friction overcomes the noise of the engine [8]. However, at low speeds, the predominant noise is that of the engine.

    Its quite surprising how low speeds it is until road friction starts to dominate. But there are areas of the city where 30km/h could be the speed limit and there would be less noise, especially cars accelerating from red lights etc. Most EVs do generate some kind of humming sound.

    Noise near a highway would probably not change at all.
