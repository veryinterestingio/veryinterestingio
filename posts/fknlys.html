    Q:

    ELI5: Why does your side hurt while you're jogging, when you've recently eaten something?

    swollennode A:

    Your intestines are trying to move the food that you just ate down the track. By you running, you're tugging on the intestines while its trying to contract and move the food. Therefore, you feel visceral pain.

    Leucippus1 A:

    The honest answer is we don't actually know. What you are describing is called a 'side stitch', which you can google and find a lot of information about. A lot of information but no explanation for what causes it and no universal solution to stopping it. My experience has been that as you get in better shape, your side stitch will become wholly less prominent. Sometimes, when I get one while running, the solution is to actually run much harder and faster. I suppose it is like breaking your finger when you have a gunshot wound, you introduce a different pain so your mind ignores the other pain.

    RikerAlpha5 A:

    When you run (especially at a speed that is “hard” for you), your body starts diverting energy away from non essential functions to power your muscles and breathing. It’s a primal “flight” mode. One of the non essential things is digestion. So that ham sandwich you ate stops being moved along the digestive track and becomes a heavy weight in your stomach/small intestines causing discomfort.

    The same thing will happen in your large intestines, but since there is a readily available exit...

    Also, this is why runner use energy gels. They are readily absorbed without the need for mechanical digestion.
