    Q:

    I’ve heard of people smoking CBD in pod systems and dab pens, is it the same thing as THC?

    EDIT: Thanks everyone for all the feedback, it is really amazing.

    woooo3 A:

    Cannabis contains over 100 cannabinoids, of which some of the most popular and talked about are THC, THCA, and CBD. All the cannabinoids together produce the high you feel when you smoke flower/concentrates, THC and its counterparts like THCA (a lot of flower contains more THCA than THC, hence the term "total THC") contributing the most.

    CBD (cannabidiol) is a non-psychoactive cannabinoid, meaning it doesn't produce an alteration in state of mind, or "high," that THC produces. CBD is extremely useful for aiding symptoms (pain, anxiety, nausea, can stop shakes in Parkinson's patients even, it really is incredible) without producing a high.

    What's commonly referred to as THC is known scientifically as Delta-9 THC. Strains of cannabis (have been specially bred to) exist with very little THC and very high concentrations of CBD. Most flower you get at a dispensary is high THC, low CBD.

    CBD and Delta-9 THC can also be derived from hemp, an entirely separate plant from cannabis. Hemp has a long list of uses, but the most useful today in my opinion is that it can be used to make biodegradable plastic. Hemp flower containing less than 0.3% Delta-9 THC is legal in all 50 states, even though it looks and smells just like pot. This is why cops have trouble determining the legality of flower sometimes.
