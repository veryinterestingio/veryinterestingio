    Q:

    ELI5 Some internet pages (mostly articles) show maybe 1/3 of the text and include button "read all" to reveal whole text. What is the purpose of such decision, is it to make it seem that people "tend to" interact with the page?

    varialectio A:

    They get statistics and feedback on readership for one. For example, if you don't click, you might have just come from a search engine list and found that it wasn't what you were looking for. If you do, it shows you were interested in the article, so their future advertising has you down for that subject.
