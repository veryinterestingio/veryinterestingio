    jordangoretro A:

    Think about carrying big sink tub of water. It keeps shifting around, water on one side, then on the other, some spills over. You have to walk really slowly and keep adjusting your arms to keep it still.

    Now think about lifting a heavy tray with two hands. Its heavy, but you hold it to your chest, tuck your arms in, and you easily carry it to the table.

    An unconscious person is like the tub of water. Flopping around, first a little heavier here, and a little heavier there. It takes a lot of more effort for you to keep adjusting your muscles.

    A conscious person is like the tray. Solid, not moving, and once you adjust, you just hold that position and start walking.
