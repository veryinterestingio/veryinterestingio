    kodack10 A:

    It is a common misconception that fossil fuels are derived from animals. For the most part it comes from plants, algae, microbial organisms. When the organic material becomes buried under sediment, over time the heat and pressure increase and the organic materials break down into simpler forms, a kind of hydrocarbon soup we call crude oil, natural gas etc.

    Depending on the environment it can also turn into peat instead of petroleum. Peat could eventually form coals and tarry petroleum like substances if given enough time, heat , and pressure.
