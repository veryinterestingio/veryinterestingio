    Luckbot A:

    They block "sodium channels" and inhibit nerve cells from releasing Aspartat and Glutamat. That basically slows down the chain reaction of signals being transmitted.

    Epileptic seizures happen when a nerve chainreaction starts to cascade out of control, so slowing them down makes seizures more rare or even prevents them entirely.

    tybirdbuf A:

    Not sure today's 5 year olds would get this, but maybe 80's and 90's... Anyway.

    Think of the brain like a lightbright. When you put the pegs in the right places you can see a picture. The brain is the same, when working right it can think of things, move your body, etc.

    Now imagine you put a peg in every hole on the lightbright. No image, just a lot of jarring colors. That's a seizure, the whole brain is working at once, so no correct information is getting where it needs to. Some times you get a lot of random movements, sometimes people just kind of zone out for a minute.

    Anti-seizure meds make it harder for the brain to work so that only the real messages are created. In my lightbright example, it would be like if I put a few sheets of paper over the screen. Sure you can still put a peg in every hole, but it takes much more effort, so why not do less work and only put the pegs in to make a picture.

    Old_Investment2295 A:

    Hello! It depends on what drug is used since different medicines have different mechanisms of action.

    We have to remember that seizures are hyper-excitable states. So anti-seizure drugs are designed to control the excitability or the spread of excitability in the brains of people who get seizures. There are channels in our brains called voltage-gated channels which can be targeted to control the excitability. On the other hand, there are drugs that can enhance the natural inhibitory mechanisms in our brain. Think of it like using the built in breaks in our brain to help slow it down. And there are other drugs that will affect how the neurotransmitters or the chemical messages are released.

    Hope that helps!
