    itspobjoi A:

    Pizza in itself can have a high mark up. Meaning the cost to make it is a fair amount lower than what it's sold for.

    Basic pizza base is just flour, oil, yeast , water and sugar. Sauce is a mix of tomatos and seasonings. Then toppings vary depending on preference and place.

    All in all, bought in bulk these ingredients quite cheap.

    Let's say pizza can range from $1 to $3 to make and is 8 slices. Then because of it being NY and cheap eating, say they sell 80 pizzas during lunch period. Making $640 from a $80-240 spend. On the cheaper side you have $560 profit which can be put to wages, maintenance etc. Then when you consider opening hours, they probably sell more than 80 pizzas a day. It's enough to keep a business running.
