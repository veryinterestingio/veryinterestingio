    readweed88 A:

    I think your question is about how two things are related to symptoms 1) Infectious dose, and 2) Infected cell types and tissues/upper vs. lower airways as primary site of infection.

    There's been research on both (a few examples below), and reframing your question with the above points in mind might yield better results in literature searches. (i.e. it may help to convert "surface contact" to low infectious dose and lower airway infection to get more results.)

    For aspects of your questions that are not really specific to SARS-CoV-2 but to how infected cell types/tissues are related to the primary source of the virus's spread, maybe you'll find more looking further back in the literature or at basic texts (vs. studies) using field-specific search terms (e.g. "pharyngitis" for sore throat, "olfactory dysfunction" for loss of smell)

    Viral inocula influencing disease manifestations, mentions that in "viral infections in which host immune responses play a predominant role in viral pathogenesis, such as SARS-CoV-2, high doses of viral inoculum can overwhelm and dysregulate innate immune defenses, increasing the severity of disease." https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMp2026913

    Hamsters with infected with higher dose of SARS-CoV-2 had worse outcomes https://www.pnas.org/content/117/28/16587 and https://academic.oup.com/cid/article/71/16/2139/5848814

    Evidence that cells in the nose are key entry point for SARS-CoV-2 also may explain COVID-19 associated olfactory dysfunction https://erj.ersjournals.com/content/56/3/2001948

    Also recommend the r/COVID19 weekly questions thread for getting responses with cited studies and not personal hunches.
