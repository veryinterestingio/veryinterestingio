    Apostate_Detector A:

    The way freshwater accumulates on small islands depends very much on the geological, hydrogeological and geophysical conditions. Vegetation affects underground water salinity as does rainfall history. Even the very act of them pumping out their pit will cause a potential change in underground water salinity dynamics. Salt water doesn’t desalinate because it passes through porous rock or sand, so any freshwater on the island usually gets there via rainfall and is stored in the underground aquifers. When you get saltwater intrusion it’s because saline water moves into freshwater aquifers due to pumping out of the fresh.

    Oznog99 A:

    There is no basis for a belief in a "treasure", and there's no manmade "flood tunnels".

    The original story of a "there is treasure buried here":

    1. there is no extant evidence of any of this story... no one knows what happened to these remarkable things in the story
    2. the story really isn't possible. Pirates did not bury treasure. Why would you leave a marker specifying to anyone that you'd buried it there? And you can't dig on Oak Island very far at all. If you did bury anything deep, it's not safe. Salt water will come in.

    The original "money pit" was a sinkhole, very common in the area. "layers of log platforms" found- well, you don't build platforms when digging a hole at all. You shore up the sides, but that's not this. These are just logs sucked into a sinkhole.

    The "flood tunnels" are not manmade. In fact there really isn't such a thing as a manmade "flood tunnel" and it would not be possible to build. The flooding of excavations is going to happen because of natural water flooding- Oak Island is unusual geology of having a fantastic depth of glacial till (gravel) over bedrock and it does create unusual flooding of excavations. Again, why you could not bury treasure there.
