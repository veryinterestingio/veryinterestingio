    Q:

    ELI5: Externally, human beings are symmetrical (two eyes, ears, arms, legs, etc.), but our internal organs are asymmetrical (heart, pancreas, and stomach to the left, liver and gallbladder to the right, one of each, etc.). Why is that?

    valeyard89 A:

    Most animals evolved from a common ancestor with bilateral (left-right) symmetry. Basically like a worm, they had a mouth and anus with a tube connecting them. All our internal organs have since grown out of pockets and indentations of that original tube.

    When we have 2 of something, they're symmetrical internally... Think kidneys, lungs, brain hemispheres, and even your tongue and uvula are central. It's really when there's a single of something, and we just evolve a) where something is protected (your heart under its ribcage), or b) just where things fit.

    A bit like your car. They're designed so the parts basically just go where they fit.
