  • Why do books have a bunch of blank pages at either the beginning or end?

    Q:

    ELI5: Why do books have a bunch of blank pages at either the beginning or end?

    Edgelands A:

    Book pages are laid out in what's called a "leaf" which is usually 4 sheets of paper folded and bound together like a zine, so this makes 16 pages. A leaf is then stacked on other leaves and then bound together at the spine. If you look at a book from the side, you'll see the stacks of leaves.

    If your book doesn't reach exactly a divisible of 16 in page number, these become extra pages. Also, some "pages" are tools used to bind the stack of leaves to the cover.

    jaa101 A:

    Part of the reason is that books are made up of signatures, often of 16 pages each. If you have a book with 161 printed pages, that means you need 11 signatures with 15 blank pages at the end.

    A 16-page signature will be a large sheet of paper that has 8 pages printed on the front, 8 pages on the back, and is then folded thrice and cut twice to become part of the book. It's done this way because it's much faster and simpler to print a few large pages rather than many smaller ones. Printing presses are big.

    16-page signatures are called octavo; oct means 8 because they end up with 8 sheets of paper, each printed on both sides. See how the paper is folded.
