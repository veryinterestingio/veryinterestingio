    Amhinkey A:

    It’s called “pandiculation” and is a natural physiological response we’ve developed in order to introduce fresh blood/oxygen to our extremities after a period of rest. Similar to the way dogs stretch and yawn after laying for a long time.

    https://somaticmovementcenter.com/pandiculation-what-is-pandiculation/ does a good job explaining it

    Prokolipsi A:

    Your muscles sleep with you. Stretching helps to not only wake up, but to also recalibrate your muscles. The movement activates a part of your brain responsible for rest, which gets your blood flowing. Stretching also tests your limits. You’re giving your muscles a test and (hopefully) they get an A+. If your muscle responds and prevents you from over-extending it, it’s awake! As for the reason it feels so good, it’s most likely your body’s response to returning to a normal state.
