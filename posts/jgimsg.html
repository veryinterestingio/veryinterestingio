    Siske1995 A:

    Less mass hitting the ground at lower speeds = less damage potential.

    Another factor that I haven't seen talked about is tension. Adults might tense up more reflexively. A rigid object hitting the floor will be more damaging than a limp object hitting the floor. Think of this like punching a wall vs. a big pile of jello. The wall is rigid and will accumulate the impact in the spot where you hit it. The jello will diffuse the impact by allowing the shock to spread.

    You made the assumption that kids can fall and be fine and adults wouldn't be fine. This is an assumption, not fact. Your observations might reflect this, others might not.

    Then the psychological factors come into play, adults might complain and focus more on the pain, generally believing themselves more prone to injury, while a child might not have that belief as of yet.

    The wear-and-tear component is overblown here, more likely a combination of several factors.
