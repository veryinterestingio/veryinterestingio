    Q:

    ELI5: Why does 24fps look absolutely fine in movies, but terrible in games?

    Varonth A:

    Games produce 1 still image each frame.

    Cameras record a certain timeframe per frame filmed.

    That means a single image of a game is extremly sharp, and you need a lot more frames for you to not notice the sharpness of each individual still image as they will start to blend together the more are displayed each second.

    Meanwhile due to the timeframe used in cameras movement of actors will blur within the image, so less frames will still blur better than rendered frames of a computer game.

    Just look at this still image from the Kings Men church battle:

    https://i.ytimg.com/vi/z7-tkkcC6d0/maxresdefault.jpg

    You see how most people are blurry? Because they moved while that frame was shot. And the next frame will have them blurry in slightly different positions. Your brain will then see a pretty fluid scene when viewed with a decend speed, which 24 frames per second is.
