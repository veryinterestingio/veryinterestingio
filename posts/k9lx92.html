  • Biology Why is it so easy to doze off on the couch, but maddening difficult to fall asleep in bed?

    NEW
    Q:

    So when I’m on the couch and decide to “rest my eyes”, I’m out like a light. Yet I’m just as tired going to bed then I toss abs turn for hours. Why?

    StimulateChange A:

    You might be suffering from conditioned sleep anxiety - associating the bed with stressful activities or thoughts. If the couch is often where you relax and you bring worries or work to bed (physically or mentally), you'll have a hard time sleeping in bed.

    Responses to this problem are called "sleep hygiene" (you can start here CDC - Sleep Hygiene Tips - Sleep and Sleep Disorders). It can often take some tweaking to get these habits to stick and be effective.

    Also remember- if you can't fall asleep in 15-20 minutes, it's always better to get up and do something relaxing (e.g., reading in soft light) out of bed. Otherwise, the longer you wait in bed, the more you associate the bed with anxiety, which exacerbates the problem!

    Source: Clin. Psych Ph.D. with training in this area.
