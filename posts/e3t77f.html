    An adhd-brain has less dopamine. Dopamine is a molecule that does a lot of things. One thing it does is to make us goal-driven and focused. Dopamine also makes us feel good; sex, eating, drugs, gambling and generally, just accomplishing stuff increases dopamine.

    How is this decrease in dopamine connected to the behavior of adhd? People with adhd will go very far to increase their dopamine levels compared to a "normal" person. This is where the impulsivity of adhd becomes apparent: It's a condition which makes it much harder for the individual to regulate their behavior. Drug abuse is very common. This is a quite simple explanation, but perhaps not ELI5: "the PFC is critical for regulating attention based on relevance (i.e. its meaning). The PFC is important for screening distractions, sustaining attention and shifting/dividing attention in a task-appropriate manner. The PFC is critical for regulating behaviour/emotion, especially for inhibiting inappropriate emotions, impulses and habits. The PFC is needed for allocating/planning to achieve goals and organizing behaviour/thought. These regulatory abilities are often referred to as executive functions. In humans, the right hemisphere of the PFC is important for regulating distractions, inappropriate behaviour and emotional responses. Imaging studies of patients with ADHD indicate that these regions are underactive with weakened connections to other parts of the brain."

    askingforafakefriend A:

    Most answers here are talking about lack of dopamine as a settled reason for the cause of ADHD - it isn't.

    Such an answer conflates a scientifically proven treatment for the disease with a theory on it's cause - these are two different things and only the former is known to be true. Put differently, a lack of dopamine is not proven to be the cause even though increasing dopamine can help.

    This confusion is similar to the serotonin hypothesis for depression which similarly isn't standing up to scrutiny as a simple lack = causation.

    The real answer is there are a lot of theories and treatments but we don't clearly know the cause.
