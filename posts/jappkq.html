    Q:

    Eli5: saliva is a "weak acid", how is it good for your teeth?

    thejoeymonster A:

    Strong enough to break down the gunk on your teeth but not strong enough to get through enamel. What gets through your enamel is in the bacteria that live off the gunk on your teeth. So if you can keep the bacteria down, with general cleaning and no acidic sugary drinks them saliva can do the rest.

    busybody_nightowl A:

    It’s actually not strictly acidic. Saliva is ranges from slightly alkaline to slightly acidic. And I mean slightly. It’s usually right around 7 on a pH scale.

    It even has calcium ions in it, which help maintain enamel. It also contains enzymes, good bacteria, minerals, mucus, and immune cells. That helps prevent harmful bacteria from multiplying and secreting acid that wears away at your teeth.
