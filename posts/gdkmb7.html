    Q:

    ELI5: Our houses remain in shape when lived in, but once they're abandoned, why do they deteriorate so rapidly?

    OccassionalReddit194 A:

    Depends on the building material, but in general once water starts getting in a building will start to deteriorate fairly rapidly.

    Social factors accelerate this - people break windows of abandoned houses (either just for vandalism or to break in and see if anything is worth stealing), letting the elements in, and things go downhill from there.

    In a slightly more immediate sense - unconditioned spaces left alone (say 1 year timeframe) are very susceptible to mold getting a foothold, which can require fairly extensive remedial work before they’re safe for human habitation again.

    BogBirdBingle A:

    Because when people live in a house, they usually notice problems like leaks, or animals getting in, and usually fix them. They also do maintenance that can prevent those problems from happening in the first place, such as cleaning roofs and gutters, repainting when paint peels, and so on.

    If someone just lived in a house but did absolutely none of those things, the house would deteriorate almost as rapidly as an abandoned house.

    I say almost because just by keeping windows and doors closed most of the time (which even the most careless person would probably do), you’ll prevent things like animals and extra moisture getting in, which will slow down deterioration somewhat.
