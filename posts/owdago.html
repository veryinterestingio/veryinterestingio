    Q:

    I had witnessed several cases for birds of different species casually standing on one leg. It is always confused me...the equilibrium is not supposed to be maintained right?

    BIGGESTKEKW A:

    I think this is because of something known as "rete mirabile" that I learned from a podcast on Birds

    This is basically a characteristic for birds to minimize heat loss. The arteries that transport warm blood into the bird's legs are near the veins that return "colder blood" to the bird's heart.

    By standing on one leg (1 foot tucked up) a bird reduces by half the amount of heat lost through its bare limbs.

    different reasons would be simpler things like Muscle fatigue, comfort, or maybe it's just sleeping

    SharmaShaurya A:

    Because the veins also cool the arteries, the bird's feet are closer to environmental temperature and thus don't lose as much heat as they would if they were at body temperature. And by standing on one leg, a bird reduces by half the amount of heat lost through unfeathered limbs.

    MJMurcott A:

    Flamingos stand on one legs due to them often feeding in soda lakes and the toxic nature of the water. Soda lakes are normally formed from volcanic craters or rift valleys which are filled with water and rich in sodium carbonate and other chemicals. The lakes are toxic to many animals, but algae, brine shrimps and flamingos can thrive in the conditions. - https://youtu.be/j20XEOlUf5Q
