    funwithallama A:

    Your body has built in protection mechanisms to keep you from getting hurt. Some drugs can short circuit those mechanisms and let your body do things you are normally prevented from

    A very intuitive example is that your jaw is strong enough to bite through your fingers. But if you were to try, your brain would tell you it’s a terrible idea and stop you.

    Similarly, you can lift heavier objects under the influence of drugs or adrenaline in stressful situations because your brain senses or is tricked into thinking that your life is in danger, and at that point losing a finger or breaking a limb matters less than staying alive. So in a survival situation it’s worth losing the protection mechanism.

    HelloImJustLooking A:

    Your brain makes movement by sending signals (action potentials) along neurons to your muscles. These signals tells the muscle fibers in your muscles to contract, which is what physically causes the movement.

    Your muscles are really strong, as in, they will break your own bones if you fully contract every single fiber, so your brain has taught itself to limit these signals.

    What adrenaline or drugs can do, is override this "don't hurt yourself" feature of your mind. In a scary situation harming a bone is usually better than the alternative.

    No one knows how the brain works, and especially not a drugged or psychotic one, but it wouldn't be a stretch to say that such a mind can be really scared. Scared minds produce adrenaline, and that can make your muscles contract like crazy. The answer to your question could therefore be, that drugged or psychotic people can be confused and filled with adrenaline, which makes their muscles work on turbo mode!

    BTW, when you touch an electric fence (not that you should!) the electricity only heats you up slightly. Electricity mimics the brains action potential, tricking your muscles to contract whether you want to or not. When people are thrown backwards after getting shocked they are not actually getting thrown, they jump involuntarily because of the electricity!
