    SillyOldBat A:

    There are more sensitive nerve endings in facial skin than say, the middle of your back. But both the nose and ears have an added potential: skin stretched tightly over cartilage. Cartilage is covered by a fine layer of "skin", the perichondrium, which is very sensitive. There isn't much space for the inflammation swelling of a pimple to go without pressing painfully on the cartilage. A bit like getting kicked in the shin, the skin gets bruised and the sensitive layer around the bone too, without a meaty buffer. Much more painful than getting kicked in a squishy spot. Inflamed perichondrium is super painful too, if things proceed that far.
