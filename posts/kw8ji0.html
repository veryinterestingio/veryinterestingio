    carpediemwtf A:

    See Cambridge Analytica Scandal for more paranoia. Data scientists can uncover hidden patterns using algorithms, which can be used for good or bad. Knowing how most inventions go, it's mostly used to benefit the company that the scientist works for. Recommender systems would look at your history of purchases or chats with friends and use them alongside a character profile to suggest products. But when you understand that these people also design the UI, you can also appreciate the fact that they could build nefarious designs like different prices for different people. This would be at the lesser end of the evil and most of it is already happening, for eg. in the ticketing websites. At the other end is building echo chambers and feeding misinformation based on political agendas.

    The larger issue is that power corrupts, maybe not today but one morally bankrupt president or CEO or project manager and you will have 1984 in real life.

    Laerson123 A:

    People are talking about ads, but there's a lot more than that.

    It is not that some company is interested exactly on your data, but when we have a big collection of data, paired with the right computer algorithms, we can get a lot of hidden information, and ads are only a small part of it (this video gives some really cool examples (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8pHzROP1D-w), like anti-theft systems in cars that use the way you sit as a fingerprint, algorithms that can detect the odds of you causing an accident, etc)

    The big concern with how companies deal with your data started with the cambridge analytica scandal, where this firm used facebook data to promote the election of fascists governments around the world.

    TheAngryPenguin23 A:

    Besides what a lot of other redditors have mentioned about targeting advertisers to you, the other thing about your data is that it allows platforms to figure out how to keep you hooked on the platform. Let’s take Facebook as an example. If you like someone’s posts a lot or spend a lot of time on someone’s profile, they’ll start sending you notifications whenever they post and even tease you with part of the posted content. But when you open the notification, it’ll just ask you to open Facebook to see the content. This encourages you to continually use their platform. Reddit does this too.

    Since this is ELI5: you like Jill because she is pretty. Facebook knows you like Jill because you keep looking at her pictures. Jill just said something cool. Come to Facebook to find out what she said. You doing something else? Nah, here’s another reminder that Jill is saying something cool. Please come to Facebook.
