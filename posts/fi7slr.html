    Fenriradra A:

    Simply put, we've already had plenty of news a month or two ago that the virus was sourced from a wet market in Wuhan.

    Wet markets themselves are basically mass butcher shops. Animals aren't always kept in the most sanitary conditions, often kept in cages & crates stacked on top of each other. A sick animal at the top is likely spreading all kinds of nasties to animals below them.

    The thing is, it's not entirely about the condition they keep animals at wet markets, or the variety of animals they keep there. It's about having enough food to feed their population. Without wet markets, China would probably roll back several decades, to a time when they were facing huge issues with famine & starvation, simply because food would become scarce.
