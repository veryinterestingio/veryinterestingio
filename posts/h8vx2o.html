    Q:

    ELI5:How after 5000 years of humanity surviving off of bread do we have so many people within the last decade who are entirely allergic to gluten?

    incruente A:

    We don't know. However, one strong theory is that, for the VAST majority of human history, bread was not made with commercial, single-strain yeast. It was made with wild yeast or, occasionally, yeast from beer making; broadly, what we would now call sourdough bread. These cultures contained many microorganisms, and they break down the nutrients in flour much farther than do conventional commercial yeasts.

    It should also be pointed out that many cultures did not treat wheat bread as their primary foodstuff, historically. Beans and corn, rice, and cassava have all been staple crops of large groups of people. Even "western" cultures have often relied heavily on barley or rye breads.

    In this whole discussion it should also be noted that gluten intolerance and celiac disease are not the same. Gluten intolerance triggers short term smyptoms (diarrhea, bloating etc) but causes no long term harm to the body. Celiac disease in the other hand is an autoimmune disease and not eating gluten free will cause serious (up to lethal) permanent harm to your intestine in the long term.
