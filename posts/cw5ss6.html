    Q:

    ELI5: why does frozen food which is already cooked need to be heated to a certain temperature before eating?

    sithelephant A:

    If properly frozen, rapidly after safe cooking, it does not. There is nothing unhealthy in this case in eating a completely frozen meal. Improper freezing can mean you need to get it to 'piping hot' throughout to kill any residual bacteria.

    fearthejet A:

    Food Scientist Here:

    You reheat for a few reasons:

    1.) Obviously, flavor/sensory/experience. Most people don't like the texture or sensory aspects of a frozen chicken. So they cook it. Cooking can also give you a "browning" effect. This is when sugars or other components get heated up and add flavor. Think of a sausage on a grill.

    2.) Safety: while not all bacteria grow in frozen conditions, some do. Listeria is an example. It grows slowly, but sometimes people keep things in their freezer for long periods of time. Listeria, or other pathogens/spoilage organisms, can come into contact with the food after its cooked and before or after freeze. Cooking ensures it kills them off

    3.) Some foods are just meant to be eaten hot!

    Hope this helps!
