    Q:

    ELI5: How is Google Photos able to give you unlimited storage completely free?

    SimilarReception A:

    Many reasons :

    1. You're not actually going to use that much space. Google can advertise "Unlimited space", most people will only use a fraction of it.
    2. If your read carefully the Terms of Service, it's not really "Unlimited". There are conditions. But it will feel unlimited, cause the limit is way beyond what the average user does need.
    3. You're the product. You get free storage, they get free data about you, that they'll turn into personalized advertising.

    It wouldn't be free and unlimited if Google had no way to make big bucks out of it.

    seannytothemax A:

    Because they can combine the images with data taken at time of capture to build up their visual recognition machine learning capabilities, which will be the future of advertising (think of the power of providing contextual ads based on what you see). It also draws your deeper into their ecosystem.

    The thing about Google is...

    Everything Google does is to empower its primary business, which is advertising. Android, the Pixelbook line, the photo apps are all cost-centers (meaning they are business units not concerned with turning profit) and their main focus is just creating a more robust ecosystem to draw people to gather their data (to 1) further empower ad display algorithms and 2) to ensure better targeted ads) and show them ads that they will click on.
