    Q:

    ELI5: Why can’t we hear anything when we are asleep but can wake up to an alarm or a loud enough noise?

    breethebird A:

    Your ears are still receiving the sound, but the auditory cortex in your brain chooses to ignore it, otherwise your sleep would be disrupted multiple times every night.

    It’s not actually just the volume that triggers you to wake up. It’s whether your unconscious mind interprets that sound as a potential threat, for example if your bedroom door opens quietly it could certainly wake you up if you don’t expect anyone to enter your room. Your brain, without you being consciously aware of it, thinks, “that sounds like it could be a threat. I better wake up and check it out.” A loud or abrupt sound will startle you and trigger your fight or flight response, shocking you into consciousness in the same way.
