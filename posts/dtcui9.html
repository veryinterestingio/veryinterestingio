    Q:

    i understand how we're able to "see" memories in our mind's eye, but how is it that we're able to "hear" memories and be able to recall people's voices and sounds? does this have something to do with the mind's eye too?

    EDIT: it's been great to read all your responses! i've learned a lot, cheers for taking the time to read and reply!

    Derekeys A:

    This is a critical skill for musicians to learn. It’s called Audiation or Aural Image.

    Most books call it ear training, but really it’s mind training.

    In the same way you can visualize something in your mind, you can also hear something in your mind.

    Think about the physical complexity of singing. Matching a pitch means that the vocal folds need to stretch to become JUST the right length to vibrate at that frequency. It’s not like when someone goes to sing, they’re consciously moving those vocal folds to just the right position and then finally make some sound.

    My expertise is in vocal anatomy and vocal health as a college professor for 5 years now and whether through memory or good audiation, hearing with your mind is not voo doo or something mystical, it is a real skill that can be honed.

    im237 A:

    Memories are not so much replaying video, like you would on a computer. In everyday life your sensory organs feed boatloads of raw information to your brain. Brain filters through the unimportant stuff and interprets the data as sight, hearing, taste... When you form a memory all you are doing is storing this sensory data together. And when you go to recall it you are just "re-experiencing" the sensory data. *

    However human memory is garbage. Things get corrupt, lost, and made up all the time. Every time you recall a memory, the very act of recalling it can alter it slightly, or swap details with another memory, etc. *

    ockhams-razor A:

    We don't "see" or "hear" the external world directly either.

    Our brain is like a computer, it gets data from the eyes and the ears.

    It takes that data and creates a world that you see and hear in your mind.

    So the real world is already created in your mind using data that comes from your senses.

    When you see and hear memories, it's the same thing... but it's using stored old stale data from your memories instead fresh detailed data from the outside world.
