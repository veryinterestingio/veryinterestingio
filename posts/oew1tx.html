    m4rc0n3 A:

    The phrasing of your question ("heated to the point where it physically grows") makes it seem as though you think that the hypothetical cube of metal will suddenly increase in volume when it reaches a certain temperature. Just to be clear: this is not the case. The cube would gradually expand as its temperature increases. It's a continuous process. In fact, this is how most non-electronic thermometers work: the thermal expansion of a material is used as an indicator of temperature.

    rocksteady77 A:

    The coefficient of thermal expansion is what is used to describe the rate at which something grows or shrinks with changing temperature. This property, itself often temperature dependent, is measured in °C^-1 or °F^-1 , and is either volumetric or linear, either giving the % increase in volume or length due to temperature change. It is used a lot in engineering where temperature is a consideration.
