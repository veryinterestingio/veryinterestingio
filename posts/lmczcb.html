    shadowbred A:

    It will if you have some type of scarring, as those tissues (collagen) are not the same as the original skin tissues.

    That said, it is fairly rare that your fingerprint is altered beyond recognition. I worked in corrections when I was much younger and the computers even then could get accurate matches off of even the most mangled hands. We commonly fingerprinted people who worked in the poultry industry and whose hands had been bit, scratched, etc to a great degree. It does change their print but typically the pattern is still recognizable by the computer on a clear print. Possibly less so by human analysis.

    I fingerprinted thousands if not tens of thousands of inmates coming in and out of our facilities and not once did I see the machine make an inaccurate match regardless of the damage. There were a few rare cases where it couldn't match an old record but they could analyze and update it manually if there was a problem.
