  • Physics How big does an enclosure have to be to have its own weather?

    NEW
    Q:

    I have read in the past of large caves or even stadiums that have their own weather because the immense volume of gases in the enclosure. So, imagining something like a terrarium bottle or something along those lines that you leave in the sun, how big would it have to be/how long would it have to be there to develop a micro-climate?

    rehcsel A:

    You really need to have a definition of micro-climate, which doesn't really have a standard one. For example the ability for it to rain can be done in an incredibly small container. Take a clear plastic to-go container from your favorite restaurant and poor some hot water in it and close the lid. Then put a bag of ice on top of it and you can see it rain.

    Do you want the ability to have currents based on differences in temperature? You can do this with a large pot on your stove, fill it with water and just put a corner of it over a flame.

    Maybe you think that using an outside heating or cooling device is cheating, well you could do all this with just the sun (another outside heating device) and some cloud cover (another outside cooling device) however it's really slow.

    There's no definition and really it's just marketing for how big something could be.
