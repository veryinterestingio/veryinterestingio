    NEW
    Q:

    One thing I've noticed having dry skin is that when it gets really dry, like from being out in the cold or excessive hand washing, is that applying moisturizing lotion can be painful. The pain is generally short lived, but it's still odd to me that doing something good for my skin causes pain.

    Why does this happen?

    The things in the lotion, when applied to raw or cracked skin from drying, irritate and cause the stinging sensation.

    Skin damaged from drying out can be thought of similarly to a scrape. There's a bunch of small abrasions from the dried layers of skins losing flexibility and cracking slightly with movement.

    It depends on your skin, but it’s most likely the alcohol content. It could also be other ingredients irritating your skin. Personally, I can’t use skincare products with alcohol or fragrance.
