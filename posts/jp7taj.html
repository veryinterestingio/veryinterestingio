    MarcusHelius A:

    They are attracted to light. If they fly into a room that is brighter than it is outside, they will not want to leave.

    An easy way to get flies to leave a room (during the day) is to switch off the lights, pull the window shades down and leave the only window opening as the only source of light.

    Charyou-Tree A:

    You are much more likely to notice a single mosquito inside your room because:

    1. The usual value for "Mosquitoes in room" is 0. You would never notice the 1 fewer mosquitoes outside.

    2. You spend a lot more time inside than outside.

    3. Mosquitoes want to be wherever you are, to drink your blood.

    4. If there are lots of them outside and none inside, it only takes 1 to figure it out and get in, so there are many chances. If you're trying to get that 1 back out, there's only 1 way for that to happen.
