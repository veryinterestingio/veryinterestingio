  • Physics Do we know about any objects that travel at large fractions of the speed of light?

    NEW
    Q:

    We know that many particles travel at the speed of light, and sometimes I hear descriptions such as "these particle travel at roughly 0.5% of the speed of light" (paraphrasing, obviously). Do we know of any particles or phenomena that travel at, say. 50% of the speed of light, or some significant fraction?

    fuubar1969 A:

    Most of the replies talk about subatomic particles. If you're looking for high speed bulk matter:

    • There's a neutron star that spins at 24% c (716 revolutions per second, https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PSR\_J1748%E2%88%922446ad
    • A pair of colliding neutron stars produced a matter jet at 97% c made of heavy elements (gold, uranium, etc) larger than Earth. https://www.skyandtelescope.com/astronomy-news/superfast-jet-neutron-star-smashup/
    • A blazar (highly active quasar) released a matter jet at 99.9% c larger than Jupiter. https://www.space.com/694-blazing-speed-fastest-stuff-universe.html

    thedufer A:

    Depends on what you mean by "objects". Photons move (somewhat by definition) at 100% of the speed of light, of course. Gravitons are only theorized, but would be expected to also move at 100% the speed of light - at any rate, gravity propagates at the speed of light. These are massless particles, though, which might be less interesting.

    Neutrinos have mass, so we know they can't move at the speed of light, but the amount below the speed of light at which they move is smaller than our margin of measurement error, so we don't actually know how fast they go. We do know it is at least 99.9999999% of the speed of light (that's a deviation of 10^-9 , if you're looking for where that number came from on the Wikipedia page).
