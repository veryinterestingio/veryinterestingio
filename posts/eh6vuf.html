    Q:

    ELI5: what’s happening in your body when you’re hungover that makes you feel so crappy?

    DrAlbee A:

    There is still a lot of debate regarding the causes of hangovers and it is challenging to study.

    There are a few factors that are thought to be the main contributors to feeling so crappy.

    1. Your body has an inflammatory response to the alcohol, inflammatory responses don't feel good
    2. Alcohol alters the levels of different neurotransmitters in your brain and this takes time to return to normal
    3. Alcohol damages your mitochondria (part of your cells that make energy).
    4. The metabolites of alcohol (chemicals that your body breaks alcohol down into), particularly acetaldehyde, are toxic. Acetaldehyde is usually broken down really quickly so probably has minimal impact for most people, unless you are genetically less able to break it down (its build up in the body causes 'Asian Flush' in some East Asian people for example)

    Edit:

    I should have included something about dehydration up here. Dehydration definitely contributes to how you feel in the morning but it is not solely responsible for the actual alcohol hangover.

    This is the reason hangovers can still occur with appropriate hydration and why they don't go away simply by rehydrating.

    It's generally considered that the effects of dehydration often occur concurrently but should be considered separately to the effects of the alcohol.

    Jbota A:

    Generally it's a combination of things.

    Dehydration is part of it, not just lack of water but your electrolytes get all out of whack. Eat a banana and drink some OJ.

    Ethanol breaks down into nasty stuff like acetaldehyde. This is rough on your liver.

    Ethanol suppresses REM sleep so you don't get as good of a rest as you might think.

    DanielagainDaniel A:

    Combination of many things: Dehydration + low electrolytes
    Bad REM sleep
    Inflammation

    But, what a lot of people are afraid to say is that a hangover is acute alcohol withdrawal. This is why drinking the next day cures a hangover more than almost anything (Hair of the dog). A weird side effect of hangovers I get is sometimes referred to as “the fear” or really bad anxiety a day after drinking. Drinking acts on GABA (inhibitory NT), so you’re somewhat feeling the over-compensation from your body on that effect.
