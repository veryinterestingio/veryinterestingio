    inspectormac9 A:

    Nobody really knows, and it's only actually been quantified recently. Humans, as you noted, do lose strength relatively quickly, losing 54% of their strength in 90 days of bed rest, even with a well balanced diet. Bears on the other hand only lose 29% of their strength after 110 days of hibernation, so without any food intake whatsoever. These numbers come from a 2007 study reported in the journal Physiological and Biochemical Zoology. There is thought that it may be due to lowered body temperatures, slowed and altered metabolism, etc, but nobody really knows the answer.
