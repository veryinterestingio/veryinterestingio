    Gnonthgol A:

    Firstly there are multiple sets of redundant cables. So if one fails the elevator will just stop where it is at, the failed cable will get replaced, and whomever inspected it last will get fired for not noticing. Even if all cables were to fail the cables are connected to brakes which grab on to rails on either side of the elevator shaft. So as long as the cables are pulling the car these brakes will be disengages but if the cables stop pulling the car upwards the brakes will engage and stop the car in place. Even if this fails there is a buffer at the bottom of the elevator shaft which will stop the car even if going full speed so that people inside the elevator will not get killed. All these systems are inspected and tested regularly.

    turniphat A:

    Other people have mentioned cable elevators, but most buildings < 5 stories have hydraulic elevators. They are raised by a large piston. Every time they go down they are free falling, but the rate the oil can leave the hydraulic cylinder controls their descent rate. So there is no possible way they can fall any faster.
