    Q:

    ELi5: Why do people get crooked teeth?

    Thechosendick A:

    Genetic factors such as big teeth from one parent and a small jaw from the other can cause this.

    Environmental factors such as thumb sucking cause the palette to form around the size of the thumb instead of widening naturally. Poor oral hygiene also causes decay and erosion, which forces some teeth to fall out allowing space for other teeth to move, turn, etc.

    OttermanEmpire A:

    Not sure if any other actual dentists have answered but some of these comments are wildly inaccurate and sound kind of like conspiracy theories to me.

    The real answer is pretty simple, it can be anything from the mandible or maxilla not being long enough to accommodate all of the permanent teeth in the respective arch or it could be something like an imbalance between the muscles in the cheek and the muscles in the tongue during development. There are many, many, many factors that can influence arrangements of teeth in each arch, so there is no single explanation as to why teeth come in incorrectly sometimes.
