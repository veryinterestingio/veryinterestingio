    so in theory, if you had a telescope which was big and strong enough to focus that light, you could see the dinosaurs.

    Claint_Rigel A:

    Theoretically?

    You would probably require a telescope, a dish of maybe 87 million light years across of material. Provided you just managed that without your telescope collapsing on itself by gravitational forces, then you would probably have to wait about 60-70 million years to receive the information back at the center. Such a project focused on us would be visible in the sky.

    So maybe you could watch, but we would know right now you were watching us 100 million years ago before you see the dinosaur 70 million years later.
