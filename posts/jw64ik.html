    lankymjc A:

    The moon was very important for the creation of land-based life. Tides are caused by the moon rotating around us, and having patches of land that keep changing between submerged and dry helped develop the earliest versions of amphibians. This provided an important stepping stone towards permanently land-based flora and fauna.

    fliberdygibits A:

    The question may not be the one you want to ask. "Is the moon necessary for earth's survival?".... no, the earth would change drastically if the moon just went away but it would likely do just fine.

    Would life that is here NOW be affected if the moon just vanished? Yes, quite a bit. The moon has a very small effect on earth's rotation and orbit but a much larger effect on it's tides. Life on earth would be impacted but might survive.

    Could life have arisen on an earth with no moon? Not exactly life like we have now but likely yes. The solar system still has a number of comet catchers to reduce the chance of impacts. I don't think tides are strictly necessary to development of life.
