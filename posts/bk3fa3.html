    Q:

    ELI5: Scientifically speaking, how do we generate the voice in our head that says all of our thoughts?

    Dicktremain A:

    It is a little bit complicated, and still being actively researched.

    The basic concept is that when you are reading or thinking about something, your mind actually does almost all the same things as if you were going to speak it. When you read this sentence you mind is actually doing 90% of the same things as if you were going to speak it.

    Older studies found that the larynx is making tiny mico movements when you hear the voice in your head. Newer studies have found that the mind anticipates the noises you make when you actually speak, and thus filters them to avoid sensation confusion. When you read or think in your voice the mind is making that same anticipation reflex which is why you hear it in your own voice.
