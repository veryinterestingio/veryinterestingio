    Q:

    ELI5: As babies we learn to drink from a bottle while lying down, but as adults we easily choke on a small sip of water. At what point do we lose the ability to drink while lying down?

    serendiputopia A:

    Drinking from a hole in the nipple where the stream and amount of liquid is regulated is much easier than drinking sips/gulps of water while lying down. When you drink water lying down, you are increasing the chance of aspirating water into your lungs, which increases the risk of aspiration pneumonia (serious for the elderly). Other risk factors are having the water go into your sinuses or Eustachian tube.

    Source: RN who works closely with speech therapists and swallow studies for patients with dysphagia.
