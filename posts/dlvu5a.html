    Nettiqua A:

    During a stressful situation whether something environmental, such as a looming a deadline or persistent worry about a serious matter it trigger a cascade of stress hormones Such as your heart pounding and breathing quicken. Muscles tensing, sweating and that butterfly in stomach feeling

    The combination of these reactions are called the fight or flight response because it evolved from prehistoric times as a survival mechanism, enabling people to react quickly to life-threatening situations.

    The exact "feeling of your gut" is from your adrenal glands and stomach muscles during the fight-or-flight response, and that’s partially what causes the butterflies.

    Mrinvent0r A:

    When you are stressed, anxious, or scared about something, your body enters a “fight or flight” state, and optimizes it’s bodily functions to prepare for the perceived incoming threat. It does so by increasing heart rate (preparing you to run away if you need to), and other similar things. It also shuts down some of your bodily functions in order to preserve energy for the upcoming perceived threat. The digestive system is one of these functions. The “butterfly in the stomach” feeling/mild nausea is a result of the digestive system being shut down.
