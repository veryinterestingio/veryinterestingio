    Q:

    ELI5: When someone "buys a town" like in the plot of Schitts Creek, what do they actually own? Aren't homes, businesses and land privately owned by the people living there?

    A:

    When towns become abandoned the county or municipality ends up owning most of the properties because unpaid property tax. If they auction all those properties at once and one person buys them all, they effectively "buy the town" even though they probably don't own 100% of the properties.

    Joker4U2C A:

    3 things:

    • There are towns, adjacent to large industrial or agricultural concerns where most things (shops, schools, rentals, the mill/farms/plants) are owned by a single entity. These are exceedingly rare things in the US and even worldwide today vs. 100 years ago.

    • Even in "normal" towns a significant portion (say many of the rental properties and a strip mall) could be owned by a single entity. This is probably the most common thing but you aren't really buying everything, just the ownership of a few key things.

    • "Buying the town" a la Schitts Creek is more a creature of fiction.
