    Q:

    For instance i know bees and ants will carry their wounded (either away from threats or just in general) but do they try to nurse them back to health or euthanize them? I'm thinking more along the lines of injury than symbiotic cleaning of parasites some fish do. although i'd love to hear all about them too.

    Rhenic A:

    Yea ants try nursing their injured back to health. They "lick" the wounds to aid recovery.

    They also don't pick up every wounded ant, only the ones that can potentially be saved.

    BarnabyWoods A:

    Some populations of red colobus monkeys eat charcoal to counter the toxins in leaves they feed on. This is a learned behavior that is passed on from mother to child. They're known to steal charcoal from humans.

    Not exactly medicine, some would argue, but a natural process, yet there seems to be an interventional aspect, guidance.
