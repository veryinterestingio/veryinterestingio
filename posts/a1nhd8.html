    Q:

    Both humans and other animals seem to go grey around their mouths before other parts of their body, why is that?

    Dopamine_Deficiency A:

    Melanocytes, which produce pigment, are unevenly distributed in the body. As an organism ages, melanocytes become less effective and produce less and less pigment. Regions that have less melanocytes go grey first.
