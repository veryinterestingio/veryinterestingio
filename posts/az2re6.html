    Q:

    ELI5: Why does covering yourself up with bedsheets to force yourself to perspire when you’re sick actually makes you feel better afterwards?

    PublicChalupa A:

    Couple things to address in this question...

    As many have stated, raising the core body temperature is a hallmark of the body's immune response to an infection. The reason your body does this is because to an extent, it does help your immune cells fight off the virus or bacteria while also making it less hospitable for the germs as well.

    Now, what does it mean to have a fever....Your brain normally has an internal thermostat set to 98.6 F (37 C to the rest of the world). If you get too warm, you sweat (the sweat evaporates and cools you off) and when you're too cold, you shiver (muscle contractions to general heat). When that thermostat is adjusted to a higher temp in the case of a fever, it essentially confuses the rest of your body. It's important to realize when it comes to core temp, your body is very fickle...even a single degree or two change can make you feel very unwell...shakes/chills/sweats/etc.

    A fever is when your internal thermostat is adjusted to a higher temp, say 100F. That new set point can be rather confusing to the rest of your body. You may feel the urge to wrap up because your chilly and shivering but then a second later feel warm and sweaty...most likely a lot of back and forth.

    As I and many others mentioned, an elevated temperature can help your immune system but it also hurts your body. For one, it makes you feel awful (see last paragraph) but it can cause actual cell damage and lead to complications such as seizure (very bad) if the fever gets too high.

    Now I'd like to address the question of should we take medicine to curb the fever/will that make the infection worse. Over the counter drugs like tylenol (acetaminophen) or those in the class of NSAID (advil aka ibuprofen or aleve aka naproxen to name a few) can be used bring down a fever. It is generally accepted by the medical community that the risks a fever can impose outweighs the small benefit it make have in helping fight the infection. Your immune system is an incredibly sophisticated, complex network that will keep fighting with our without a fever.

    Hope this helped clear some things up...I know this is ELI5 and probably got a little long-winded but I did my best to keep from getting too technical.
