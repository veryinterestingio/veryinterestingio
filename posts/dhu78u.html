    CaptainJackWagons A:

    Many of the comments are using the taste or perceived value as an answer which doesn't really address why it tastes good in the first place, so let me give it a try. Spices are a natural defense mechanism of plants to kill any small pests or bacteria that might eat them. The reason they taste so go in the first place is because the concentration isn't enough to harm any large animal like a human, but it will kill harmful bacteria when we eat it. For the same reason, it also makes a great preservative, which is useful in warm climates where ice is hard to come by.
