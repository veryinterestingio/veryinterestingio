    Wormsblink A:

    Octopus have muscles too, but while mammals have mainly linear muscles, octopus have mainly circular muscles. This paper describes it well, although in very scientific terms: https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fcell.2016.00010/full

    Since the muscles are arranged circularly around the tentacle, there are no large fibrous strands to give the same texture as mammal meat.

    Edit ELI4: muscles in mammals are fibres. The fibres stretch and relax to change shape and move the body. Muscles in octopus are rings. Stretching and relaxing also changes shapes.

    Fibres and rings have different texture. One is grainy, the other is smooth. Thatâ€™s why when you eat octopus it feels different.
