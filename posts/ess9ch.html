    funwithallama A:

    First off, a lot of animals have wonky teeth! Some short snouted dog breeds have such terrible teeth the molars rotate 90 degrees because there’s physically no space to fit them. Horses often have malocclusion where the molars of the top and lower jaw don’t line up properly. That can cause severe problems because their teeth continuously grow, and if they don’t get filed down properly they can get painful spikes.

    This brings me to the main point: evolutionary selection. An animal that doesn’t have properly lined up teeth will likely have more difficulty eating, making it less fit for survival and less likely to pass down its genes. Humans have basically 0 evolutionary pressure, and someone with an overbite etc has no disadvantage over someone with perfect teeth. Hence passing down the genes to wonky teeth.
