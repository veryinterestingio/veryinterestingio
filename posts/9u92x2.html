  • Biology Why do bright spots remain in your vision after looking at something bright?

    NEW
    Q:

    And why do you still see the spots when you close your eyes?

    Aric00 A:

    When light hits the back of your eye (the retina), each photon causes a change in a protein that then Cascades down signals to compose vision. This protein then has to be converted back to the original state to accept another photon. This happens extremely fast, but when I looking at bright light the conversion of back to normal can't occur fast enough. The process of converting the protein back to normal is what causes the spots to remain.
