    Q:

    ELI5 why do Olympic divers shower after each dive wheb they are just going to dive again?

    sammerai1238 A:

    The pool is very cold. If you go from room temperature to really cold water, your muscles tend to tighten up. This is bad for divers, because they tend to need flexible and loose muscles to do what they do. A warm shower can help to relax those muscles again in anticipation of the next dive.

    zephyrzenizzle A:

    The diving pool is typically warmer than swimming, so it’s not as cold as racing temperature. The reason for showering / hot tub is to keep the muscles from cramping.

    Cheffmiester314 A:

    I've been to many college diving pools while playing college water polo. Most high end diving pools are warm, like warm bathwater. The cold comes from getting in and out of the water and the air temperature making you cold. Being cold can cause muscle tightness and leads to injury. I highly doubt Olympic pools are cold. Also the jet of water is used to help see the water line and break up the surface tension of the impacting diver.
