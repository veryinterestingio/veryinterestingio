    Eli5: is there a difference, in terms of cooking, between the shiny and dull sides of foil?

    fergunil A:

    Contrary to common belief, the difference in texture of both sides of foil is not a functional one, but a technical one.

    When we manufacture foil with the current tools and processes, one side is dull and one side is shiny, but it has absolutely no impact on its use, and is not designed that way or another, its just an artifact.

    Shiny up or shiny down, you're using it right

    nom_of_your_business A:

    There is no difference between the two side functionally. The reason the two sides have a different finish is due to how thin the foil is. They need to run two sheets through the machine to get the correct thickness and maintain structural integrity throughout the process. The shiny sides are the outside and make contact with the rollers that move the material. The inside matte is caused by very minimal squishing of the two layers.
