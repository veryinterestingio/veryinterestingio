    ElI5: What is a QAnon?

    mugenhunt A:

    A conspiracy theory that began on 4chan, reportedly coming from an anonymous source with "Q level security clearance." The basics of the conspiracy theory is that everything Trump does that looks like he's dumb or incompetent is actually part of a grand plan to save America by defeating a grand conspiracy of child molesting liberals, who use the basement of a pizza parlor in Washington DC as their secret base. That the evil conspiracy was trying to harvest a chemical from the brains of children so they could make a youth potion, by scaring the kids and traumatizing them.

    The conspiracy theory moved to the 8Kun site, and continued to get more and more people convinced that there's a secret liberal conspiracy that only Trump could defeat. When Trump lost the election, and seemingly proved the entire conspiracy theory wrong, the people who bought into it got deeper and deeper into conspiracy theories about Trump secretly winning, that he'd use some sort of loophole and when it appeared that Biden was going to be inaugurated, take over and arrest him and lead America in a new golden age.

    We have at least two Congress members now who are openly believers in this conspiracy theory.

    When President Biden was inaugurated today, a lot of the QAnon supporters have not taken it well, some realizing that they've been lied to for the past few years, others trying to claim some way of believing that there is still hope for Trump to become President again.

    PaxNova A:

    Picture a problem at your local combination Pizza Hut/Taco Bell. Say an oven catches fire and everyone has to evacuate. The fire marshal enters the building and notices burnt remains of towels and scorch marks up the sides of the oven. In his expert opinion, there was grease on the side that caught fire and wicked up to the towels, causing a greater conflagration. That's trusting in experts.

    Now picture the same issue, except people dislike the fire marshal. Someone says "Wait a second, wasn't he supposed to be there earlier in the day for a routine inspection?" Yes, he ran late. But people start "connecting the dots" and mistakenly realize that the fire marshal must have set the fires. Him not being there was too coincidental. It must be like the movie Backdraft, where firefighters set the fires so they can show up as a hero! Now we have a culprit and a motive. Case solved. That's QAnon.

    They provide "evidence" like the fire marshal being late, and tell you to follow your instinct. Since you don't like the fire marshal and assume the worst, that's where your instinct takes you. It's an alternate reality game, except it plays you instead of vice versa.
