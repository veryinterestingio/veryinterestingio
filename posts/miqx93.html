    Like I’m about to RAID you and your entire family, why aren’t you running away?

    You’re incomprehensible to them.

    You’re a million times their size, taller in comparison than our largest skyscrapers.

    Imagine if you were blind and deaf and Godzilla was stomping around somewhere. Would you even know?

    BurnOutBrighter6 A:
    1. Ants are incapable of fear of anything. They don't have emotions.
    2. They can't think ahead or imagine or predict anything. Those things require much more brain (specifically a frontal cortex) than ants have. So what you're "about to" do is beyond anything an ant would consider. If they saw you with a can of RAID**, they'd think "cylinder". Not "that is a container of poison and I'm in danger of it being used on me in the near future". They can't think like that.
    3. **They can't even see you, or the RAID. We're so big we're not even detectable to their senses. They can't see thousands body lengths up to our face or the can of RAID we're holding, they're nearly blind.

    TLDR: Ants aren't even aware of you, and if they were they're too simple to understand the threat anyway.
