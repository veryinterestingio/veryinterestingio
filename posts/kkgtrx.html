  • Why is the world record at 4 × 100 metres relay faster than the world record at the 100 metres multiplied by four?

    NEW
    Q:

    The world record time at the 4x100 metres is 36.84 while the world record at the 100 metres is 9.58. How is it possible that they run faster while having to pass a baton?

    bggtr73 A:

    Because after the first leg, each of the runners is already moving when they get the baton - they are getting a running start for their leg verses starting from a standstill.

    Gnonthgol A:

    In a 100m race they have to start at rest on the start line. It takes a while for the runners to get up to speed. However in a relay the runners are allowed to start behind the relay points and get up to speed before they are passed the baton. So they will already be at speed for the entire 100m.
