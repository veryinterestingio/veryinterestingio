    Q:

    Do the veins arteries tear too? How would they repair themselves?

    Compact_cranky_pants A:

    They are formed again through angiogenesis. This is the same process that occurs as an effect of exercise, or tumor growth. Damage to the vessel begins a cascade of hormonal signaling, first to clot and stop the bleeding, later to remove the clot, and then to begin the process of angiogenesis.

    Yes, the blood vessels within a bone will be severed in the event of a displaced fracture. It's sort of a little-known fact, but bones actually bleed when they break. And yes, they either heal or are replaced later, as another user has already pointed out.

    This comment will be less science, but an addition for medical protocols where I am. It is possible to die as a result of trauma to the tissues in your bones. In my unit, one of the protocols for medivac-ing a person was if they had two fractures on different extremities, regardless of severity. The chance of dying from that is way, way higher than you would think, and in the event that one or both is a compound fracture, it can turn into a medical crisis in the time it takes you to get someone out of a wrecked vehicle, let alone to the hospital.
