    ELI5: Why does it feel like such a release to scream? What is physically happening when we scream out to “release” everything that is bottled up?

    Physically, screaming causes us to tense up, and when the scream is done, your muscles noticeably relax. However, it’s much more of an emotional release, when our brains often interpret as a physical release as well.

    Sungillee33 A:

    I’m fairly certain that screaming has more to do with tension/mindfulness. Think about this: A cathartic scream is typically not going to happen in a super relaxed position (i.e. your other muscles are already to tenses up). In an emotional scream your muscles tense a little bit harder, like when someone’s crying turns to sobbing and they hold onto someone more tightly. The sudden jolt of tension acts like a progressive muscle relaxation for your whole body; it causes the brain to recognize how much tension there is and the scream is kind of the external symptom of what’s happening in your nervous/muscular systems (gotta reduce the tension). Consequently, after the scream all of your muscles feel more relaxed and the brain releases chemicals that tell your mind that it can relax a bit too.

    Source: I’ve got a doctorate in psychology.
