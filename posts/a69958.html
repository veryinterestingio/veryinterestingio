    Q:

    ELI5 how do a small ant find its way to a sweet food on top of the shelf’s and manage to call his fellow ants and invade the whole area in manner of 24hrs?

    adamfox1028 A:

    Ants leave a trail of chemical signals called pheremone wherever they go. There are a few different types of pheremone including one that tells other ants "this way to yummy food", so when an ant finds a food source, she will eat her fill and head back to the colony, leaving a chemical "road" for her sisters to follow back to the food.

    Something_Syck A:

    Ants communicate via chemical pheromones

    When the first ant goes up your pantry it leaves behind a chemical trail that serves no purpose other than to guide it back to the nest

    once it finds food, it goes back along this trail but now it leaves a different pheromone that says "hey dudes there's food here come grab it while it lasts!"

    Other ants rapidly pick up and follow the "food is this way" trail which quickly leads to the massive swarm in your pantry where you left some food out.
