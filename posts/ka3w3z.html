    hunybuny9000 A:

    TLDR: alcohol dries out the mouth which raises the risk of infection in there. Alcohol helps reduce staph on the skin which reduces the risk of infection when breaking the skin.
    Finally one I know the answer to! You always hear about the microbiota we have in our guts, but rarely hear about the microbiota on our skin. There is a whole community of microbes on our skin, including staph. But skin does a great job of keeping it out of our bodies, so it’s just a natural part of the community. When you get an injection, you’re obviously creating an opening in the skin, and alcohol does a pretty good job of killing off that bacteria in that area, thus preventing any infection.
    Edit: just to clarify, staph isn’t a huge concern in the mouth. If you were to wipe or swish with alcohol in the mouth, it would further dry the tissues in there and that leaves you with a higher risk of infection. Saliva is constantly washing over the mouth and has properties that reduce bacterial growth.
