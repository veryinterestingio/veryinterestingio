    Q:

    ELI5 How do radio stations know how many people are listening to them?

    DirtyChito A:

    There are companies who do polls and use the data they collect plus statistical analysis to determine a rough estimate of listeners. It's not all that much different than TV and Nielson where they send boxes to certain demographics to collect data on what they watch then extrapolate it based off that demographics population density.

    In other words, they make an educated guess.

    BadSanna A:

    In addition to surveys Neilson uses a technology called a Portable People Meter. Basically they pay people to wear this pager like device that detects subaudible codes broadcast along with the radio signal. This allows them to determine what radio or TV station you are listening to. Neilson has been doing something similar with TV since the 50s where they pay people to attach a device to their TV to determine what they watch.
