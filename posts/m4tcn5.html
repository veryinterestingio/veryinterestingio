    Chel_of_the_sea A:

    On the level of whole societies' birth rates dropping as their wealth increases, this is a well-studied phenomenon called the demographic transition.

    Within countries, this is a much weaker effect than stereotypes would potentially lead you to believe. The poorest households in the US have about 50% more children than the richest, but that's partly because having kids tends to plateau a woman's income (and thus drop her household out of the highest brackets). From the very poorest households to the middle class, the effect is pretty small: 66 kids/1k women in the poorest households to 60/1k women in lower-middle-class ones (i.e., poor people only have about 10% more children than lower-middle-class ones). It's only once you get past that into two-income households with professional women that the number drops off.
