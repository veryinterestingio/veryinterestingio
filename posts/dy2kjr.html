    KneeDeepThought A:

    It's not just sadness. Tear ducts react to any strong emotional state: tears of joy, tears of rage, etc. It's an evolutionary response to your external environment in times of upheaval. Your body tries to protect itself in case of some potential injury in all the drama. In this case, by flushing your eyes out as a shield against incoming contaminants. Doesn't make a lot of sense in our modern society but it kept our ancestors' eyes healthier.
