    It's to do with the dive reflex of mammals. Most mammals, if their nose gets wet while they hold their breath, their bodies respond by moving oxygen to the brain and heart, assuming they are in water and need to preserve oxygen.

    colkerns A:

    Mammalian dive reflex. When water touches your face, your body instinctively drops its blood pressure and prepares for deep breathing and underwater exertion. More distinct in marine and riparian mammals but 100% still active and measurable in humans.
