    WSp71oTXWCZZ0ZI6 A:

    In most jurisdictions, generally speaking you don't have to report a crime. E.g., if you passed by a junkie on the sidewalk doing drugs, you wouldn't have to report it to the police (I'm guessing almost everyone would just keep on walking). There are some exceptions to that (mandated reporters) but they typically don't involve drug use.

    When the documentary is released, the police could choose to open an investigation based on what they saw. If things were really serious, theoretically they could subpoena the documentary-maker the provide them with names or raw video footage or testimony or whatever.

    It would be a big hassle and I'm guessing most police departments wouldn't really give a shit if some random person did drugs in a documentary, unless there were more serious crimes involved.
