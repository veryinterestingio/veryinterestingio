    ELI5: If I'm supposed to stay out of the pool during a thunderstorm because lightening could strike the pool and I would die, how come everything in the ocean isn't dead when lightening strikes the ocean?

    Most discharge occurs along the water’s surface. Most fish are beneath the surface and aren’t affected; whereas as a person in a pool, you have an extremely high probability being at or near the surface. NWS Lightning & Fish

    Luckbot A:

    The electricity spreads in all directions. The further you are away from the hit the less current reaches you. At the entry point several million Ampere hit the water. But just 1 meter away the same current is already spread over an area of 6.28m² (half a sphere with radius 1) and that area grows quickly so only a small fraction of the current hits you when the further you are away.

    In a pool you can't be far away from the lighting strike, so you better leave the water.

    Also the smaller you are the less of the current hits you, so small fish survive more easily than a human.

    TorakMcLaren A:

    The ocean is pretty big. A pool is pretty small. Plus, the stuff in the ocean tends to be underwater, whereas you in a pool need to go above water.

    When lightning strikes a pool, the water gets heated up a lot from the electricity. The ocean doesn't get heated in the same way because there is a lot more water to absorb that energy.

    Lightning tries to find the best path to Earth. This often involves striking something higher, like a building or statue. If you stick your (wet) head above the water, it becomes an ideal path for the lightning to go through as it's a slightly shorter distance. But aquatic creatures are underwater, so aren't extra-likely to get hit.

    audigex A:

    The electrical charge dissipates (spreads out and gets weaker) after it hits the water - it's only really dangerous within about 50 metres (150 ft) on the surface of the water. A pool is generally smaller than 50m, so anything in the pool is at risk.

    The ocean is MASSIVE, though, so most fish and animals will be far enough away to be safe. Any fish that are too close to the lightning strike will die, it's just that there won't be many of them in the danger zone.
