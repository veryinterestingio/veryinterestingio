    arglwydes A:

    Seán is an Irish name and is properly spelled with a fada, the diacritic over the 'a'. It's commonly left out when you're not writing in Irish though. Bean is an English surname and follows different orthographical conventions.

    Oddly enough, the word sean (without fada) means 'old' and bean means 'woman.' A sean bhean or sean ḃean would be an old woman.
