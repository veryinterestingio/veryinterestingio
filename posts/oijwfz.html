    Q:

    ELI5: Why does your mind try to prevent you from exercising or working out when that activity usually results in an endorphin rush and positive feelings?

    legs_bro A:

    Our brains are hardwired for survival, yet we’re living in an environment where survival is almost guaranteed. In the short amount of time that humans have gone from having to struggle for survival to being almost guaranteed survival, our brains/bodies have not had time to adapt to this environment. This is bound to create some unnecessary or problematic behaviors.

    Because our brains are hardwired for survival, we tend to want to preserve as much energy as possible and eat foods that will give us a lot of calories to feed our body’s energy stores.

    Exercise burns calories, removing energy from our bodies. Our brain doesn’t like that because our brain is adapted for an environment where less energy means less chance of survival. Even though we know that the grocery store not that far away, the primitive survival portions of our brains dont think of it like that

    It’s the same reason why so many people overeat and eat sugary, fatty foods even though that makes them feel worse in the long run. Sugary and fatty foods are the easiest sources of calories. And again, even though we know that these sugars and fats are bad for us, the primitive survival parts of our brain are only thinking about trying to consume and preserve energy.

    Edit- as someone pointed out, fats are not all bad for you. There are healthy fats and in fact, fats are actually necessary for a well rounded diet. The reason I mentioned fatty foods as bad is that its so easy to overeat on them. Think about foods like pizza, burgers, bacon, etc. They may be low in sugar but they are still not good for us in excess. And it’s very easy to eat them in excess compared to say, chicken breast or brussel sprouts.
