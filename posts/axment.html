    kaldarash A:

    The short answer is no, but the long answer is yes.

    The discrepancy is an interpretation on what you are actually asking. As others have noted, putting sugar into the water source won't make the fruit sweeter, so that's why the short answer is no. But the things you put into the soil, the availability and quantity of nutrients, the microorganisms, will all alter the flavor of the final fruit.

    Actually, one of the most common items used to make fruit sweeter is dolomitic lime. This isn't a cure-all for making sweet fruit, but soil tends towards the acidic side and many fruits grow better with a neutral pH. Also an acidic quality to the fruit certainly takes away from the perceived sweetness.
