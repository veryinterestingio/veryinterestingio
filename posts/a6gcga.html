    Q:

    ELI5: Why is it so hard to forget movie spoilers, but so easy to forget more important stuff?

    Oaden A:

    You don't generally really forget important stuff, you just don't remember it at the correct time. Its rare that if you forgot a meeting, that you have absolutely no clue you should have been in it, after someone reminds you. Once you get triggered, you remember.

    Now in case of movie spoilers, you always get triggered to remember it at the worst time. The moment you go see the movie. Giving the impression you never forget a spoiler, but always forget a important meeting.

    mbergman42 A:

    You remember things better if you can make logical connections to other things you know, or if you get an emotional reaction to the fact.

    Movie spoilers often happen after you’ve heard preliminary info about the movie. Hearing the spoiler connects the dots in your head, it’s like dropping something onto a single strand of spiderweb or onto a full net—the net connects in more ways and the spoiler “sticks” better.

    Plus, you’re pissed that someone spoiled it for you. That emotional reaction helps you remember.

    Compare this to, “There’s a budget meeting next Tuesday and we need those numbers by Monday.” Good luck remembering that dry data—unless of course it fills you with fear, of course.

    apatheorist A:

    Contextual memory is a bitch.

    You remember the spoiler because you're watching the movie.

    Just like how you remember lyrics when the song is playing.
