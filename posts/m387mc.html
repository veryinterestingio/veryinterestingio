    CrateDane A:

    Bear in mind the outside influence of light is usually used to entrain an underlying oscillation, not to generate it in the first place. So there will likely still be some biochemical oscillation going on, just no longer getting synchronized to the day/night cycle.

    Then there's another layer as to how the circadian rhythm affects behavior - just because the internal clock says it's morning doesn't necessarily mean the animal will react the same way if it's still dark (polar night) as it would if it was light.
