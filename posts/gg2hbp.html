    jayaram13 A:

    It's a fairly simple question with a complex answer.

    In a normal healthy human who hasn't been taking antibiotics, the typical composition of poop is as follows:

    70+% water Of the rest, 20-50% is dead gut bacteria Remaining is bile (processed dead cells), undigested food and fiber

    Now if you don't eat unnecessary food, first to die would be the gut bacteria (unless you eat enough to feed it too). If you eat enough to feed it, it's volume would stay constant.

    Your body would still breakdown and rebuild constantly, voiding bile - which also contributes to poop mass.

    Please note I've simplified and not mentioned side effects of undereating to keep it ELI5.
