    Supernyan A:

    When you work out, your body is basically increasing the amount of blood flowing all around. Things are opening up, and blood is getting where it needs to go very easily because your muscles also help squeeze the blood around. Also your brain is drugging you with endorphins which help you to return to calm after working out.

    When you are stressed to the point that your heart rate is increasing, none of those other muscles are helping. You're just sitting there making your heart work harder. None of the blood vessels are opening, so there's just so much more force required to push all that blood just with one little heart. Also your body is flooded with cortisol, a corrosive hormone that causes fight or flight feelings basically. You want to run away, and your body is preparing for that, but you're not going to run away, so it hurts.

    spiritlessspirit A:

    When we are mentally or emotionally stressed our veins constrict/narrow which restricts blood flow.. which causes a whole string of things to happen.

    When we stress our bodies with exercise, it opens the veins and makes blood flow easier.
