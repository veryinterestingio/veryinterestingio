  • Biology Other than the Inuit sled dogs and Carolina dogs, are there any pre-Columbian domestic dog lineages left in the Americas? Additionally, is there much in a way of dog landrace formation in current Native American reservations?

    NEW
    Q:

    Looking at wikipedia, there's been a number of extint breeds due to European contact, in North American neck of the woods...kind of curious as to whether there's much information available on existing dog populations.

    LetsGoooat A:

    It's commonly assumed that breeds like Chihuahuas and Xolos are pre-Columbian, because they're depicted in pre-Columbian art and described in accounts from early European explorers. However, this is not supported by the latest DNA evidence. This study looked at mitochondrial DNA from 71 pre-Columbian specimens and nuclear DNA from 7, and found essentially no contribution from this population in modern dogs, even breeds like Chihuahuas, Xolos, and Carolinas. The one place the pre-Columbian genome is preserved is in canine transmissible venereal tumor, which infects dogs worldwide.
