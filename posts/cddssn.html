    NEW
    Q:

    ELI5: Why are there so many testosterone supplements advertised for men but no estrogen supplements advertised for women?

    ughhhhh420 A:

    None of the "testosterone supplements" that you see advertised actually contain testosterone. Instead they contain a variety of inert ingredients that are implied to boost testosterone production (though they never explicitly claim to boost testosterone production as they're not legally allowed to).

    As to why those supplements are marketed but fake estrogen supplements aren't? Testosterone is classified as a performance enhancing drug by the World Anti-Doping Agency because it boosts athletic performance.

    Casual bodybuilders get tricked, just like you did, into thinking those supplements are some legal form of testosterone and take it because they think it will increase their testosterone levels and muscle mass.

    Estrogen is not a performance enhancing drug - it does just the opposite and so there isn't any demand for it among casual athletes.

    There are pills that contain actual testosterone, but you can only get those with a prescription and you're not going to get a prescription if your only need for them is to help you workout.

    1000yearsofpeace A:

    Estrogen supplements are advertised to women for postmenopausal symptoms (brand names include premarin and estrace). It's controversial because there are risks to hormone replacement, including increased breast cancer risk, but there are also benefits for some women.

    I bet you could find ads in Good Housekeeping magazine or something like that. Advertising is targeted, so those not in that demographic might not come across ads that are aimed at age 60+ women.
