    Q:

    CosineDanger A:

    CDC page on variant proportions

    Alpha still exists in the U.S. but is only about 2.9% of cases as of most recent update. It has been on the decline for a while. Delta is 93% of cases and rising. Beta and Gamma also exist in small amounts.

    Alpha has not been seen for months in many other countries.

    I think it's important to talk a little about what these Greek letters even mean. Not all samples of Delta are identical; it is a virus lineage and a Variant of Concern. Many variations do not warrant a Greek letter. Theta variant was downgraded to Alert for Further Monitoring.

    If it seems hard to understand all of these variants and what they do, the WHO and CDC are trying their best to apply labels to an ever-changing evolutionary soup of viruses so of course it's confusing.

    Does the original still exist? If you download coronavirus genomes (which anyone can do, for free) you will see small differences between samples even in 2019. Evolution never stops.
