    Q:

    ELI5: Why does thunder sometimes "clap" short and sharp vs others "roll" shaking for extended periods sometimes even getting louder as it goes?

    scr3wballl A:

    If it is a direct cloud to ground strike then usually you get the single shorter sound, versus horizontal cloud to cloud or multiple strikes in a row can give the constant rolling thunder sound.

    OrbitalPete A:

    It's mostly to do with your distance from the strike. THe closer you are the more directly the sound reaches you. The further you are the more terrain, atmospheric layers, buildings, etc the sound can reflect from and refract through. This leads to a lengthening of the perceived sound. It's also worth nothing that quite often strikes occur in clusters, and you can get an enhanced rolling effect which is actaully the overlapping of multiple strike sounds.

    If you go to www.lightningmaps.org - this will show you strike locations and estimates of the propagating sound waves - and zoom all the way in on current active storms you will see lots of locations experiencing overlapping sound waves from surrounding strikes.
