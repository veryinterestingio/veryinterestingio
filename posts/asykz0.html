    Q:

    Mars rover Opportunity was built to last 90 days, and lasted 15 years. Voyager aircrafts were built for five year mission and lasted 41 years. Please explain if these crafts were over-designed or Nasa just got lucky?

    aggasalk A:

    Suppose they built it so that they could estimate the rover had a 1 in 1000 chance of failing by the 90th day - basically a guarantee (99.99% chance of making it 90 days). With that probability of failure, the chances of making it to the 5000th day would be something like 94.6%, so it's basically just a consequence of making that 90 day target as absolutely solid as they could.

    namer98 A:

    NASA absolutely overengineers the hell out of all their stuff. There isn't really a retrieval and fix option if anything breaks. With Opportunity in particular, it was assumed dust build up on the solar panels would kill the battery a lot quicker. But the dust was blown away by the wind, which wasn't entirely expected to happen. This was the luck part.

    FiveRandomWookies A:

    I’m just an undergrad engineer, but from what I understand NASA builds their projects with a minimal expectation for what needs to be accomplished. In other words, Opportunity only needed to survive 90 days to complete what was expected of it. So it was designed to survive a minimum of 90 days given very serious conditions.

    But conditions were more favorable for the rovers than the initial expectations, leading to longer survival periods.
