    ELI5: If skin constantly regrows, why do burn marks not disappear?

    sebonzakurai A:

    it depends on the degree. Regeneration only happens on the basal layer of the epidermis. Usually 2nd degree burns burned skins layers that goes beyond the epidermis. As a result, the regenerative layer is burned and cannot produce new skin layers anymore.

    Skin has multiple layers - some regenerate faster than others. Damage to the outermost and fasted growing layer will not leave a scar, but damage to deeper and more slowly growing layers will.

    donh- A:

    There are many types of skin tissue, but there is only one kind of scar tissue.
    Scar tissue tends to knot up. That is the scar. It sits there, and can bind.

    If you put long slow pressure on scar tissue it will relax and blend in with the surrounding tissue.

    Example: I had a botched operation on my forehead, think multiple stab wounds to the same location along with a ballbat hit. Wore a tennis sweatband for most of a year and the scar is almost impossible to see.

    Point: scar tissue is scar tissue and looks like same. Unless you learn how to blend it.
