    minnesconsinite A:

    with knuckles its a simple hinge joint that goes forward and backwards and there are only one or two muscle attachments per knuckle.

    with the neck, there are a lot more muscle attachment sites and a lot more joint surfaces and facets that can crack. cracking yourself is not in and of it self bad but i've seen plenty of people give themselves mild forms of whiplash where your neck just locks up after cracking it. most of the time its because they overstretch one part of their neck and then crack it which causes a mild muscle strain

    Additionally, where people usually need the crack, in the junctional areas of the spine (where the curve switches) is not very easy to crack yourself so you end up cracking the middle hypermobile part and not the part that actually needs it.

    TLDR: no, but you can lock your neck up on rare occasions
