  • Biology Eli5, Humans are painfully fully aware of their own mortality. Are there any other creatures in the animal kingdom that are capable of complex enough thought to know that they're gonna die someday?

    NEW
    Q:

    Do animals know that they can die? Or do they simply react to threats simply on instinct?

    ryschwith A:

    There are some animals that at least seem to understand what death is when it occurs: elephants and most corvids (crows, ravens, magpies, etc.) have exhibited behaviors that seem to be basically funerals. But that's something of an assumption on our part, because we can't really know what those behaviors mean.

    And that's really the problem with answering your question. We have very little understanding of cognition outside our own species and very few avenues to learn more about it. There are tantalizing hints that the mental life of animals is more robust than we currently know, but nothing that even approaches the lower limit of scientific certainty.

    So really the best answer I can provide to your question is: maybe, but we can't currently state with certainty that any of them do.
