    Q:

    Eli5: The human brain’s storage capacity is unknown, but generally accepted to be very large. Hypothetically, what happens if the brain’s storage space is full?

    katatafisch A:

    Information is saved by the formation of connections between nerve cells. When you learn something new, new connections between nerve cells are formed. Over time synapses that aren't used deteriorate and make space for new ones and the information they held is forgotten. So basically you always forget unimportant stuff you don't need anymore to be able to remember new information.

    Nerve cell is kinda like a dumpling that with noodles. One neuron has long nudles and a short ones The long one is called axon and the short one is called dendrite. Typically neuron has many short noodles. When we learn something these short noodles grow something like small thorns on them. If we learn smth very complex the thorns can be much bigger and not look like thorns at all. They can look like truffles or other mushrooms. These truffles have proteins inside that we call engrams. When we dont use some knowledge these proteins break down and thorns shrink. This is how we make room for new material. So the memory capacity is not infinite, its plastic. We can learn and forget.
