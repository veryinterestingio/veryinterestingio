  • Biology Why does the feeling of having something stuck in your throat last after you have already swolled the object. i.e) a pill

    NEW
    Q:

    When you swallow a pill and it gets stuck for a second, the feeling sometimes lingers even after you swallow again. It feels like there is something stuck in your throat when in reality you have passed the pill.

    Ohzza A:

    When something gets stuck in your esophagus it's still being forced down by muscular contraction, so with the friction that keeps it in place it's creating scratches and irritation.

    So even after the pill's down the feeling you have associated with it being there (said feelings of scratching and irritation) are still there and there aren't enough specific nerve endings to distinguish that from the pressure of an object lodged there.
