    ekac A:

    Yes. It is a physical object, just very small. The round surface is phospholipid - an oily substance. The spikes are proteins, chains of amino acids in a particular coding sequence. That sequence determines the reverse-sense sequence the protein targets to hybridize with, the receptor in your lung cells.

    It literally is an oily droplet that uses surface anchors to attach and fuse with the membranes of your own cells. When it fuses, it dumps genetic material in your cell. That material gets into the nucleus through a ribosome, and the nucleus translates that genetic material instead of your own, using DNA polymerase - or in this case it reverses the RNA back into DNA as an initializing step because COVID is an RNA virus. [[Some have pointed out that I mixed taxonomic kingdoms of viruses here (orthonavirae and paranavirae, they're both RNA viruses) - Coronavirus uses messenger-RNA instead of reverse transcriptase. EXCUSE ME GUYS!]] This produces more viral particles, and eventually kills the cell doing so. Particles move on to the next cell. On and on until you're coughing spores of colonizing oily droplets, then die. Spores colonize the next person. Rinse, repeat.
