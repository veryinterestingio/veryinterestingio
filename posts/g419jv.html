    WallTheWhiteHouse A:

    "Grassroots" describes when the people in a community start a political movement by themselves, without any outside help, like the democratic or republican party for instance. It describes a movement that has many small roots, like a field of grass, rather than one large source of power, like a tree. Grassroots movements are somewhat rare, but can be very powerful. Occupy Wall street and the Tea Party were both grassroots movements.

    AstroTurf is a brand of fake grass, so astroturfing is when someone tries to fake a grassroots movement. A large political organization might buy fake twitter accounts to tweet their hashtag, organize "local" protests all across the country. The idea is to make their political position seems like it's popular with the general populace of the country, even when it's not.
