  • Why do people get tongue tied in social situations, and how does one improve their social skills?

    NEW
    Q:

    I'm very curious, it's why I'm so fine with not knowing everything, because I know I'm learning a lot on the way.

    I love my curiousity. One second I'm researching about x and the other y. I like that I learn about different things all the time, be that through books or online through documentaries.

    BUT Even if I know I'm right, when it comes across to explain my view I seem to forget my facts, freeze up, and then come across as some idiot who clearly doesn't know what she's talking about.

    I'm very awkward, I start doubting myself and my knowledge, I can't maintain eye contact, use proper body language or even accept criticism without inadvertently getting defensive.

    When the person is listening, I freeze up and sort of detach myself (it's really weird), I can hear myself talking AND see them looking (maintaining eye contact therefore listening) but it's like I'm not there. It gets so bad I can feel my heart beating so fast and I start stammering. It almost feels like a panic attack.

    WHY does this happen?

    P.S: I'm based in the UK

    DucRider A:

    As others have stated, this is classic anxiety. What has not been stated is that biologically, the reason for your symptoms is that anxiety is your body activating its fight-or-flight mechanism. When in fight-or-flight, certain parts of your brain and body are shut down to allow other parts to work more efficiently. Your speech center is one of those parts that gets shut down, while your reasoning remains active. If you have overactive anxiety, it can cause a myriad of health issues because your body is in fight-or-flight mode more often than it should be.

    cloudwatcher2019 A:

    You're trying to put on a show, but you did not do the rehearsals.

    When you see people eloquently telling stories, making speeches, arguments, comedy, interviews - all of those things are rehearsed, practiced over and over.

    What you need to do is, prepare an advance answer in your off time, so when you do need to converse, youre just copy-pasting information, instead of having an impromptu oral essay.
