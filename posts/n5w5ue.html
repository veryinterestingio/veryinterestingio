    ELI5: why do we immediately not wanna do something we were going to do, when someone tells us to do it?

    diagnosedwolf A:

    This is called psychological reticence reactance. Basically you see the command as a threat to your freedom, a trap, and immediately rebel against the idea. It’s a fascinating area of psychology.
