    Q:

    How come only about 10% of people are left-handed and not 50%? Why does nature "prefer" the right hand to be the dominant one?

    varenne0 A:

    We don't really know. Research has been pretty inconclusive about this matter, and there does not seem to be a genetic component, as in several twin studies, even identical twins will have mixed handedness. Here's a TED-Ed that explains what we know about handedness so far:

    https://www.ted.com/talks/daniel\_m\_abrams\_why\_are\_some\_people\_left\_handed/transcript?language=en

    I believe it is about \~5 years old though, so hopefully there have been further studies done into the matter since then. I wish I could help you out more!
