  • Why is COVID-19 so highly feared with 2,900 deaths, but not the flu which kills sometimes hundreds of thousands worldwide each year?

    NEW
    Q:

    I don’t know enough about COVID-19’s vectors, resilience or lethality to really understand the fear over it. Clearly it has world leaders worried but I don’t understand why the flu is not as commonly feared or discussed.

    Xalteox A:

    The flu kills about 1 in every 10,000 people that get it.

    COVID-19 kills 200 in every 10,000 it infects.

    People compare the general death tolls without putting it into context of how many got it. Also we are not done with this virus yet, it will spread and kill a lot more.

    There are strains of the flu that do kill at the rate that COVID-19 does and on rare occasions outbreaks of those do happen, but they are taken just as seriously as this years outbreak is, the last time this happened was in 2009 with the H1N1 influenza virus.

    Additionally since this is a highly contagious strain of a virus family that does not commonly infect humans, there is a fear that may potentially become a recurring event in human history like the flu. If it spreads enough, there is a possibility that it will become a disease that stays around in the centuries to come with enough infections to mutate a strain resistant to past vaccines like the flu is.
