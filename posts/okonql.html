    pseudocultist A:

    There are two types of nerves in your body, myelinated and unmyelinated. Myelinated nerves are wrapped in a protective sheath, and can transmit signals much faster with higher fidelity. When these nerves are injured, the pain is immediate, usually sharp, intense. Whereas unmyelinated nerves transmit much slower, the impulse “creeping” its way to your brain. When these nerves are injured, the pain is slow, often dull, broadly aching.
    Your balls are full of unmyelinated, slow nerves. So that’s the sensation when injured.
