    Q:

    ELI5: How does a petrol pump know a car tank is full and to stop?

    the_original_Retro A:

    It's a pretty clever little thing.

    The end of the gas tank nozzle has a separate small tube in it that connects into the gas pump handle. You pull the trigger, and that tiny tube starts sucking in air while the larger hose goes about pumping gas.

    That little tube is connected at the upper end to a pressure sensor which cuts off the gas flow when it's triggered. As long as the end of the little hose is clear to suck in air, the pressure stays the same and gas will pump. But the second the pressure goes up, the sensor says STOP.

    As you fill up, eventually the gas level in your tank climbs so it's now covering the tiny hole. Having to suck liquid instead of air is a little harder, and that change from one to the other instantly changes the pressure and causes that sensor to fire to cut off the gas.

    So the gas always stops when it reaches a certain level, and the odds that we get more tragic Zoolander scenes are greatly reduced.

    PossessedHamster A:

    The tip of the pump nozzle has a small hole that essentially sucks fuel back into the pipe as a sensor. This is not enough suction to pull fuel being dispensed but rather when the tip of the nozzle is submerged. This means the moment your fuel level touches the nozzle and any fuel is sucked into that tiny hole, the sensor detects it and the pump cuts flow.

    This is why you might get it cutting out if the flow splashes back onto the nozzle as you start pumping and then you have to restart pumping.

    Added tip: Only fill up in the morning or at colder times of the day as fuel is more dense when cool thus you effectively get more fuel for the same quantity pumped.
