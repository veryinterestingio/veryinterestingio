    bonyponyride A:

    Taste refers to receptors on your tongue, which has receptors for saltiness, sweetness, bitterness, sourness, and savoriness. Flavor is detected in the nose. So if you drink a soda, you'll notice the sweetness of the sugar and the sourness of the phosphoric acid right away, but the rest of the sensory experience happens in the nose once you breathe.
