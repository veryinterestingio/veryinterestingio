    Q:

    ELI5 : why is coffee almost never found in portioned bags like tea? Why are loose coffee grounds/instant coffee favoured?

    TheRarebitFiend A:

    Coffee begins to oxidize rapidly after grinding. The more air you allow to get to it the more oxidizing (change in flavor) occurs. When you grind it up and keep it in a big bag or container less of the coffee gets exposed to the air, basically just the bit on top. When you separate the coffee into a bunch of smaller portions you’re giving each bag of coffee more opportunity to oxidize. This only matters if you use the coffee relatively quickly after it’s been ground since even if packed relatively tightly it will still oxidize.

    This hasn’t stopped people from producing single Serb serve options, there are several bag and single pour over solutions to choose from as well as the (in)famous Keurig, but these delivery systems are pretty universally viewed as producing inferior tasting coffee.
