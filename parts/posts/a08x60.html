    synchroswim A:

    You have muscles in your eyelids that keep them open. Some of these muscles are skeletal muscle and can be controlled consciously. Some of these muscles are smooth muscle and are controlled unconsciously - it's the same kind of muscle as is in your stomach and intestines. When you have more "awake" hormones like adrenaline, the smooth muscle in your eyelids contracts and your eyes stay open without much effort. When you have more "sleepy" hormones, the smooth muscle relaxes and the only thing left holding your eyelids open is the skeletal muscle. It's not used to doing all the work by itself, so it gets tired fast and your eyelids droop.
