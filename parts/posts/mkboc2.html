    stanitor A:

    It isn't realistic at all. It would be very rare for you to get stabbed in a way that causes significant bleeding, and for that blood to end up inside the airways or intestines, which is what it would have to do to get you to throw or cough it up. You might get small amounts, which you would cough up with mucus and saliva, but this wouldn't look like the pure blood you see in movies. Stab wounds in the major vessels around the lungs/throat/intestines would bleed into the chest, on the ground, or in the abdomen. I have never seen it happen like it does in movies.

    Source: surgeon who has done lots of trauma work
