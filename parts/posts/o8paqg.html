    Penny_Traiter A:

    Honest hard to fake signals are common in nature. One example is stotting, the deliberate jumping by antelopes in front of lions. This behaviour signals two things: I've seen you. I'm really fast (so don't bother). This saves both species time and trouble and thus the signal and it's reception can be selected for.

    TheDevilsAdvokaat A:

    Stotting is a thing.

    The idea is by showing how much energy and speed they have, even in the face of death (EG a charging tiger) they may convince the animal not to even try, to break off the pursuit.

    Then again I wonder if it's like a super-fast cat stretch; an instant way to prep the muscles for extreme action.

    ImprovedPersonality A:

    Some species of birds intentionally draw a predator’s attention to lure them away from their nest or mate. This can (obviously) be an evolutionary advantage if it helps protect offspring while still allowing the taunting bird to escape.
