    MyNameIsRay A:

    Cable company is just providing you access to the network's content. They manage the box, the wires, the distribution, etc.

    They need to pay networks licensing fees in order to broadcast that content to you. Of course, that means you're paying the fee, plus profit.

    TL;DR: Your bill is service costs+licensing costs. You're paying for access to what the networks provide, not buying the content directly.

    You can just buy the content directly, it's called a "box set". Alternatively, a lot of networks sell direct access. EX: HBO Now (which costs the same as adding HBO to cable).

    Dodgeballrocks A:
    • Broadcast TV is free in the US.
    • Ads that air on broadcast TV pay for the production and distribution costs.

    • The same company that sells the ads built and maintain the equipment that broadcasts the signal.

    • Cable TV costs money because a new company built the system of equipment and cables that gets the signal to your house.
    • When you pay for Cable TV, most of the money goes towards maintaining that system. Only some of it goes to cover the cost of the programming.
