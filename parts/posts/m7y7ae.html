    SuprMunchkin A:

    Some good answers here already. I wanted to add two more:

    1) Boxing is hard work and it's easy to over-exert yourself and become exhausted. Throwing a light punch can be a way to get your opponent to tire themselves out by wasting energy blocking or dodging.

    2) A light punch can also be a feint: throw a light punch to the face, and when they block that, it can open up their defenses for a more powerful follow up, maybe to their ribs if they bring their guard up too high (as an example).

    AgoraiosBum A:

    Boxing is exhausting. Don't underestimate how tired they can be, especially in later rounds.

    But also, little light jabs can keep your opponent away from you or disrupt an attack. It's easier to maintain your defense when you don't put everything into a punch. When you do, if your attack doesn't go right, you can really open yourself up for a counter-punch.

    So it is also a way to get your opponent to react and change their stance while still having a careful defense and preserving the opportunity to throw a harder punch at the right time.
