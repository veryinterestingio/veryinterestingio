• ELI5 why a tubal ligation (getting her tubes tied) is 100% covered by my insurance, but a vasectomy comes out of pocket until my deductible is met?

    NEW
    Q:

    I understand all plans are different and other plans may cover a vasectomy at 100%, but mine does not.

    I called my insurance company to find out what's covered for each thinking they'd probably be about the same. Nope! The more invasive and riskier procedure is fully covered, while a "little snip" comes out of pocket first before they start to pay.

    Not looking for any political diatribes, just wondering if anyone can explain why what seems to me (and I could be wrong about it) like a simpler, safer procedure wouldn't be covered like the tubal ligation.

    EDIT: Just adding that this is in the U.S. since I know people from other countries may wonder why someone is asking this.

    bguy74 A:

    The rationale is fairly straightforward. If a women gets pregnant, that is a health issue for the woman - the insured person is the person's whose health is protected. When you get a vasectomy as the insured person, your health is not protected. It's 100% risk (procedure) and no prevention (for the insured party).

    The insurance company incurs no obligation for health costs of the person you make pregnant, just of the insured themselves.
