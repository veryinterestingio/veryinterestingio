    NEW
    Q:

    In an age where information was only passed by mouth and IDs were only science fiction (which didn't exist as well), how could the king (for example) exhile someone and make sure the guards actually know and remember how the guy looks to prevent him from coming back?

    Gnonthgol A:

    They could not. They had to rely on guards or other citizen to recognize them. For a small relm this was not a big problem as most people knew about each other. However there are plenty of criminals who just ended up changing their name and fled to another part of the country where they were not as recognized. Even returning home when people had forgotten about them and continued living out the new identity. But this in itself was a punishment.
