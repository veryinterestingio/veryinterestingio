    Q:

    ELI5: Can someone explain what COPPA is and why they have the power to fine youtube creators $40,000+?

    sunsparkda A:

    COPPA is a law, intended to protect children under 13 from people gathering their personal information without parental permission. Violating the law allows the FTC to sue the violator for up to \~$40,000 per offence.

    The FTC have decided to treat channel operators on Youtube like they were running a website on a host. Since Youtube collects data of viewers and serves ads based on that data, if a person who's making content for children doesn't mark the content as for children, those children's personal data is being collected without parental permission. The FTC is treating Youtube as offering a service to the channel owner.

    It's a very legally dubious stance that came out of a settlement between Youtube and the FTC, passing the buck for most of the responsibility to channel owners.
