    Q:

    ELI5 - Why can’t a bunch of diabetics get together and start a nonprofit insulin manufacturing company? Isn’t it pretty old tech?

    TehWildMan_ A:

    Human insulin itself isn't a excessively expensive drug: many pharmacies offer it for something like $30 a vial.

    It's the newer analogues of insulin, which provide much better control of blood sugar levels and are easier to use, which are far more expensive, mainly as many of them are still under patent.

    DonJuansTaco A:

    It would taken tens of millions of dollars to get a drug manufacturing line up and running. Specialty equipment, highly trained engineers, regulatory specialists, plus general costs of doing business. Getting a medicine to market, even one already off-patent, can take years.

    Cheap insulins already exist. But the more effective, modern insulins and delivery systems are still under patent by for-profit companies. There's no incentive for that much money to be spent to not turn a profit.
