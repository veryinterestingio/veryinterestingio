    BobbyP27 A:

    In the UK when a new style bank note is introduced, after a period where both old and new are in circulation, the old style is withdrawn from circulation and no longer recognised as legal tender. For a time ordinary banks will exchange them for you, but after a while even they stop, and you have to go to the Bank of England if you want to exchange them for newer ones (the Bank of England itself will honour any note, even ones 300+ years old, but really old ones are probably worth more as collectors' items).
