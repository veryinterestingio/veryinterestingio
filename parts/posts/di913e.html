    tee2green A:

    ELI5: psychedelics reset your thought process and your consciousness to be like new again. It’s like restarting a computer.

    ELI20: Michael Pollan does a good job summarizing one theory on this in his book “How to Change Your Mind.”

    Psychedelics break down your Default Mode Network (DMN) which is your brain’s typical process for interpreting the information your senses are receiving.

    Your DMN is your typical sober state of consciousness. It gets very well-worn to the point where you develop your instinctive interpretations of information, which is good and bad. The good is that it helps for survival. The bad is that your get susceptible to falling into negative thought loops (ruminating thought loops about the past (depression) or worrying thought loops about the future (anxiety)).

    Psychedelics dissolve the DMN so that your brain functions like new again. The sensation is like being reborn and seeing things for the first time. Many people find that they appreciate things like a child does, including mundane things that the DMN would ignore as not important for survival. Suddenly, you’re mentally rejuvenated.

    It’s kind of like when you go on vacation in a foreign place. Suddenly, very simple tasks (go to the store, use transportation, order food) become fascinating in a new language and new culture. Hence why taking psychedelics is likened to “taking a trip.”
