    aptom203 A:

    Gallium and Mercury can form alloys with aluminium.

    They are also liquid at, or slightly above, room temperature which allows them to form alloys without being heated significantly.

    Aluminium is highly reactive, but it is so reactive with oxygen that its surface forms a protective coat of aluminium oxide which is very unreactive.

    Gallium-aluminium alloy does not form this oxide, so as the alloy forms, aluminium atoms are attacked by oxygen in the air, freeing up gallium to go on forming an alloy and exposing more aluminium to oxidation.
