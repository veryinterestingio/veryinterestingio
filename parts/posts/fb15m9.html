    Q:

    ELI5: When you "catch" a sneeze before it happens, what's happening inside your nose?

    ColeWRS A:

    Sneezing is triggered when something that should not be there enters your nose. Specifically, the hairs and layer of mucus in your nose. When this happens, chemicals are released that cause the nerve cells in the nose to become irritated. Once this happens, signals are sent to the brain to trigger the pharyngeal and tracheal muscles to force air through the nose with the hope of removing whatever is causing the irritation.

    I could not find supporting evidence of this, but knowing how a sneeze works, it is likely that when you "catch" a sneeze before it happens, you are likely catching one of the steps in between, probably the signals telling the pharynx and tracheal muscles to activate.
