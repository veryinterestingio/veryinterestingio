    Fruit522 A:

    Times have changed, of course, but traditionally cartoons are syndicated which is a fancy way of saying that the cartoon signs a deal with a company that negotiates publishing with newspapers/other forms of media. These middlemen could be compared to agents or marketers in other industries and they often represent more than one client.

    Individual cartoons can have an impact on the public’s view of that particular strip, but usually it is up to the paper’s editors to choose to cancel their contract with the syndicate.

    The advantage to doing it this way is that the artist is free to focus on their work, and let others get dirty with the negotiations. The downside is that the artist is in effect paying someone to do the negotiating and may not have much control over where their work appears or how much they get paid
