    Neusch22 A:

    When you're going to bed your body and brain have been working/thinking/going all day, so it takes time for everything to shut down into "sleep mode."

    The opposite occurs when you wake up, your body is in "sleep mode" already so it's much less work to just go right back to sleep, unless whatever woke you up really startled you to the point where your body isn't in "sleep mode" anymore.

    I'm sure this varies from person to person as well, depending on how easily their body/brain can settle down and get to sleep.
