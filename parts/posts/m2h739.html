    RobusEtCeleritas A:

    The coordinate time diverges as the object approaches the horizon, so an observer at infinity never sees the object cross. They just see it asymptotically approach the horizon, and redshift until it's no longer visible.

    But the proper time remains finite. So if you're falling into the black hole, you always see your own time passing normally. At some point before you reach the singularity (which you will definitely do in finite proper time, as long as you cross the horizon), you will be ripped apart by tidal forces.
