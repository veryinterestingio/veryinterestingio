    Q:

    ELI5 Why do McDonald's fried turn into cardboard approximately one hour after they're cooked?

    Mr__Furious A:

    The frying oil removes moisture from the outside and creates a crispy shell. The moisture that is inside is now trapped and heats the inside to a fluffy consistency. This makes a crispy outside and fluffy inside... the perfect french fry.

    After as little as 8 minutes outside of the oil, the moisture inside starts to redistribute through osmosis, rehydrating the crispy shell, making the fry soggy and limp.

    If you were to put fries into a refrigerator which is a very dry environment, the moisture is lost and you get the "cardboard" fries.
