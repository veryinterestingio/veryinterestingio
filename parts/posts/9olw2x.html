    Q:

    ELI5: Why is it so difficult to stop peeing halfway while it's so much easier to control your bladder before you start?

    Kaelestius A:

    When pee is in your bladder, it is kept behind two valves. The one closer to the bladder (a) is automatic and the one further down your urethra (b) is "semi-automatic" - you can consciously control it with your pelvic muscles. Opening this valve consciously to start to pee automatically opens the one further up, which will not (usually) close until the bladder has emptied.

    The force of the urine coming down your urethra after valve (a) opens is too strong for you to easily stop with your secondary (b) valve.

    The feeling you get when you need to pee is pressure on the top valve, (a).
