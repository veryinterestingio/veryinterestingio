    Q:

    (ELI5) Why do flies rub their front legs together when they sit somewhere?

    Baktru A:

    They are cleaning their legs from any dust particles or other debris that have ended up on them. If look at flies closely you'll notice that not only do they rub their legs together to clean them but you'll also see them rubbing their legs over their heads and wings to clean those.

    Flies aren't big so dust particles sticking to them have an influence on how stable they can fly.

    kindanormle A:

    Aside from cleaning themselves, the behaviour has to do with pollination. Flies, like bees, are often pollenators. Flies will collect and/or deposit pollen on flowers that are beneficial to them. This helps both the fly and the plant. The pollen can gather on their legs at one flower, and the instinct of rubbing their legs to clean this pollen off is what allows them to pollinate another flower.
