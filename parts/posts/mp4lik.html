    Q:

    ELI5: Why does picking at a scab/wound feel so good?

    xr_tech A:

    There are a looooot of nerve endings in the skin, and they relay all kinds of stimulation information to the brain. When skin is healing, those nerves in the area send signals to the brain that there are changes taking place (aka healing). The brain interprets this as itching.

    Also, when we are injured, in one part of the process the body takesv to recognize and start repairs, hystamine is released. This is the same chemical that signals allergic reactions, also causing itchiness.

    Just like with any itch, our bodies reward us for scratching it. This is the one time you don't want to scratch though! Resist the temptation in order to prevent infection and scarring.
