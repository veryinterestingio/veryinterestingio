    Q:

    What happens when you “get the wind knocked out of you”?

    Alterego1911 A:

    Google:

    Getting the wind knocked out of you can be terrifying, but it's not life-threatening. It happens when a sudden force is applied to the abdomen or back, putting pressure on the celiac plexus, resulting in a spasm, or 'freezing,' of the diaphragm muscle.
