    There are protective esters that serve as sunscreen for the plant, effectively filtering out the spectrum of ultraviolet-B Ray's. Depending on the plant species and the climate around it, chlorophyll is accumulating and being utilized while also producing proline (for example) to help protect the plant against extreme wavelengths it's being exposed to.

    squidjuggler A:

    MARIJUANA is exhibit 1!

    Marijuana plants evolved in the high desert-mountains of Afghanistan and Ethiopia - really intense radiation. So they evolved a sunscreen, glands that produce THC, to protect their leaves and flowers from the sun. That is why marijuana growers are known for really hot lights really close to their plants - the intense radiation encourages more growth of the THC-glands.

    This THC-sunscreen coincidentally became a discombobulant, a psychoactive that confuses animals. And so now humans have selected for marijuana plants that produce a lot of more-psychoactive THC-glands.
