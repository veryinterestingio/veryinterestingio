    ELI5: Why can cows and sheep crap in fields where they eat without getting sick, while faecal matter lying about quickly leads to diseases like cholera in humans?

    bod1888 A:

    Cattle have a natural aversion to feeding around their own dung. This can lead to the formation of taller ungrazed patches of heavily fertilized sward. These habitat patches, termed "islets", can be beneficial for many grassland arthropods, including spiders (Araneae) and bugs (Hemiptera). They have an important function in maintaining biodiversity in heavily utilized pastures

    that_other_goat A:

    Animals do get sick from fecal matter being about so it's a bit off a misconception. They don't get sick because of a farmers work.

    ​

    What happens is twofold.

    ​

    In pens:

    They move and the farmer mucks up (grabs the poop) and they don't eat where they poop. The aforementioned poop is deposited in a compost pile for use or sold.

    ​

    In pasture:

    ​

    The animal doesn't poop where it's eating it grazes and then is moved to different pasture once the plants are eaten. The poop falls on the already eaten parts additionally they often use agricultural fencing to divide up the aforementioned pasture land to keep the animals in a specific area. For some animals dogs can be used as well.

    ​

    Additional steps depend on what's going on as there are numerous styles of agriculture. The common thread is the poop is not wasted.

    ​

    If this pasture is a fallow field: it's often spreed and allowed to rot in place.

    ​

    If the poop is to be used as fertilizer in a different field it is gathered by the farmer and composted. The compost is then spread by machines. Raw manure cannot be used on many crops especially root crops.

    ​

    If the poop is going to be sold it is gathered by the farmer and either composted on site or sold as is to producers of bagged manure or anyone else wanting to buy poop.
