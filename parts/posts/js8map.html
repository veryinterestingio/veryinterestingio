    Q:

    ELI5: Why is it during a weather change (like going from 70s to 50s or other direction) why do our sinuses flip out during the change and maybe a day or two after but when the weather stabilizes your sinuses are fine?

    parkerwe A:

    Sinus problems are caused by the level of air pressure inside your sinuses being different from the air pressure outside. When the temperature changes outside the air pressure also changes as they are directly related.

    The pain from you sinuses is the air pressure inside your head not be equal to the outside air pressure. In periods of stable weather or gradual change you won't ever notice this. Things that affect the outside air pressure drastically can out pace the bodies ability to equalize. Particularly changes in altitude (like driving through mountains) and big swings in the weather.
