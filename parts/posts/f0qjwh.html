    HopelessHamsta A:

    What are you referring to? Spaced armour, composite armour or surface hardening of plate armour?

    Spaced armour works by having an outer plate disrupt a penetrator, the fragments of which are then stopped by the inner armour. Spaced armour can also reduce the effectiveness of HEAT rounds by causing them to detonate at the wrong distance.

    Composite armour is made from multiple layers of different materials with different properties, which can be more effective than homogeneous armour, especially against HEAT rounds.

    Surface hardening of plate armour gives a hard outer surface which can damage penetrating rounds, and a softer innermost layer which can resist shock and spalling.

    Takancha A:

    It's not so much that there's different densities, it's more that there's a gap--can be small, but a gap. This allows the armor to crumple just a tiny bit. This increases the time it takes for the force to happen, dispersing the impact of the force. It's kind of similar to older vs newer cars--older cars were rigid, and if you got in a head on collision, the force would go mainly to the people in the car. At around the 1960's, cars were made to deform, increasing the time it takes for the collision to occur, which is much safer.
