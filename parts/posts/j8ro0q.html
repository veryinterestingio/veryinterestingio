    Q:

    ELI5: If the odors that our bodies emit mean they are doing their job properly, then why are their smells so offensive to us?

    AgentElman A:

    Humans dislike smells that they assume are related to disease. Feces and other bodily wastes can cause disease - so we find those smells bad. It causes us to avoid them.

    This is a culturally acquired thing.

    I work around a number of professional market gardeners. They get seriously dirty and sweaty every day. I do not find their body aromas offensive in the least. They take normal care to shower after a day's work in the hot sun. None of them wear deodorant, why bother? They are male and female, makes little difference. They often drink a gallon of water in a day, sweating it all out.

    When I bring this up the online reactions range from shock to disgust. You guys haven't even sniffed them yet. The aversion to body aromas is a learned, cultural characteristic. Also a very modern one. A normal healthy person who is sweating and has normal hygeine smells pretty good, actually. Take an actual sniff of one sometime.

    marmiteinthepan A:

    This is partly a societal construct and what we consider "offensive" can change. For example the perfumes from the early 1900 were very musky and funky often using whale blubber. They fell out of fashion because of puritanical ideas about smelling of sex being bad.

    That led to the whole smelling clean phase and the eventual fresh citrus scents that blew up in the 90s as they mimicked cleaning products.

    TLDR: Puritans decided smelling like sex is bad and now we consider BO bad
