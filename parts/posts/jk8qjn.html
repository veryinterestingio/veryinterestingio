    Q:

    ELI5: When you take cookies out of the oven they are so soft that if you move them too quickly they’ll fall apart. If you wait a couple minutes, they’re much more stable. What’s happening in that two minutes?

    MrFunsocks1 A:

    Butter/fat cools and hardens. Bonds between starches and proteins (from the flour and egg) are more mobile with more heat. Steam is released as the cookie cools so there is less moisture content. Chocolate chunks also resolidify. Basically, the cookie becomes more viscous/solid in the way that everything else becomes more viscous/solid as it cools.

    ToastyNathan A:

    2 things

    1: it is continuing to cook. When food comes off the heat, it doesnt stop cooking. It continues to cook until its temp is at a relativley low temp. The cookies are continuing to cook on the hot pan as well.

    2: The starches and sugars that were looser when cooking begin to harden up when cooled down.
    So dont get em browned in the oven. If they are brown on top when you pull them out, they will be burnt on the bottom.
