    loserfaaace A:

    It's hard to say as far as adults are concerned. Probably? However, there is proof that young children and infants, when deprived of touch, will stop developing mentally and physically. It is called 'failure to thrive'. This happens most commonly in third world orphanages where caretakers do not have the time and resources to hold and comfort the infants, but you can also see it in neglect cases all over the world. Even if a child is fed and diapered and cared for in other ways, it can not develop without human touch and interaction.

    Switch_n_Lever A:

    Yes.

    Consider oxytocin, the so called "hug hormone" which is released during prolonged physical contact with others. Let's say you do not have any such contact, your levels of oxytocin would decrease, together with whatever else that hormone may affect. However, oxytocin is not only related with the touch of other people, but pets can have the same effect.

    Here's an interesting article about the effects of oxytocin in mice: http://blogs.discovermagazine.com/d-brief/2019/02/14/mice-love-hormone-oxytocin-warmth-cold-isns/
