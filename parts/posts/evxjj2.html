    Q:

    When sending robots to other planets, how do they make sure not to include Earth microorganisms?

    mfb- A:

    Complete sterilization is not possible without destroying the spacecraft in the process. They assemble them in clean rooms and sterilize each surface as good as reasonably possible to keep the number of bacteria/spores/... low. The environment of space kills most that remain.

    NASA uses 5 categories of missions depending on the target and the mission profile. https://sma.nasa.gov/sma-disciplines/planetary-protection

    Their Mars rovers actively avoid regions that are expected to have liquid water close to the surface to reduce the risk of contamination. "Martian Special Regions" - https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/reduce-red-tape-for-the-red-planet-report-says/
