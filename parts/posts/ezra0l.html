    MoiMagnus A:

    The hypothesis of your question feels wrong. I feel there are as many of both, and that you are just more picky to call a cold day in summer a "winter day" that you are to call a hot day in winter a "summer day".

    I mean, I don't know where you live. But where I live (Lyon, France), in winter it is usually 5°C (40°F), and we call it a "particularly hot" when it is 20°C (70°F), and in summer it is usually 30°C (85°F) and we call it "particularly cold" when it is 15°C (60°F). An both of them happens, and it didn't feel to me like one was more frequent than the other.

    [Excluding global warming that makes winter of nowadays slightly hotter in average than few decades ago]

    Even moving through France, I never had days in Winter that were like hot days in Summer. The only reason why some days feel hot is because I'm not in Summer clothing so 20°C in winter clothing feels hot.
