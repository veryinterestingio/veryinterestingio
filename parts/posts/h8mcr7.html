    Xkingworm A:

    I went to a music conservatory and took a music neuroscience class that deals with music related phenomena like this. There isn’t a lot of specific detailed research when it comes to things like this because it does magical things to our brains, but there are a lot of memory related phenomena that music causes. One theory I’d suggest is that since music uses a lot of your brain all at once when listening and especially when playing, music is quickly embedded into your memory. An especially catchy tune that is easy to remember in combination with using up lots of your brain at once is a perfect recipe for short term storage in your brain. This is why memorizing things like the constitution in song is more effective. It essentially drives the information into your brain by using more of it at once through music. It’s like filling a bottle with a funnel rather than pouring water above it and hoping it gets in. Because it gets in faster and more efficiently, that music will be embedded into you short term memory strongly and you’ll be singing it in your head until your short term memory gets rid of it.
