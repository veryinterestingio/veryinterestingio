    black_brook A:

    The texture of those foods and eggshell is really quite different. It just so happens that the English language doesn't have words to simply capture the difference, and the word "crunchy" applies to both. The fact that you are asking this question reflects how much language itself affects your perception.

    frogan_red A:

    All your other foods are actually, you know, food. An eggshell — which is texturally very different from a smooth, creamy egg — is not food, obviously, and that, and the extreme texture contrast, is the likely culprit. Doesn’t taste like food, doesn’t smell like food, and it’s crunchy where crunchy is not at all expected.
