    demonspawn79 A:

    Please ignore u/HorsefuckerJim, he has absolutely no idea what he is talking about

    During REM sleep (the part where you dream) your body stops regulating body temperature. That's why most people will need a blanket to keep their body temp up at night. It also stops or slows down a lot of other non-essential functions like digestion (why it's not good to eat right before bed). If you're waking up sweating after a few hours it means you've either got far too much bedding or there's a possible health problem. Night sweats are a common symptom of menopause, hypoglycemia (diabeetus), some cancers like lymphoma, hormone imbalance, or some neurological conditions.

    It is not normal to wake up often covered in sweat. If this happens to you regularly go see a doctor.
