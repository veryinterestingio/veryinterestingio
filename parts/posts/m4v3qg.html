• Why do people blur out their license plates in pictures when they are visible in public to anyone that wants to look?

    NEW
    Q:

    ELI5: Why do people blur out their license plates in pictures when they are visible in public to anyone that wants to look?

    Gnonthgol A:

    You are not hiding the license plate itself as that is pretty much already in the public. What you are hiding is the link between the license plate and the rest of the information about that picture. A picture usually contains a lot of information such as when and where it was taken as well as lots of contextual information. By publishing that image not only are you linking that license plate to that information but you are spreading it around for the entire world to see. And some people might object to this.

    kowie650 A:

    To avoid being caught up in any open source intelligence. It’s a way of tracking and identifying.

    So I’m selling a car on kijiji / Craigslist / fb marketplace etc. I post a pic and now my contact phone number, postal code (zip if your American), my plate and car are all now publicly open and linked.

    Driving around is just a car and plate but not anything else (not residence nor phone number). That info is only available to police if they run my plate.

    Some awesome stuff happens on r/osint ; it’s amazing to learn what we’ve lost in privacy and how easy it can be to link people to pictures. Never take your privacy for granted.

    Mopperty A:

    Some good answers hear, I will also add this although it may be specific to the UK:

    If you steal a car, you can put fake number plates on it to avoid detection. The best way to do this is to copy the number plates of a car with the same make, model, year and colour.

    People will even steal a car and swap the plates over with another car. This leads to innocent people driving around the car they own with stolen plates on it and being pulled over for driving a stolen car.
