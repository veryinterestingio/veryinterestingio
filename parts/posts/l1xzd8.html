• How come we "flip" Japanese names when speaking English, but not Korean ones?

    NEW
    Q:

    When speaking English I've noticed that when we refer to Japanese names, they are "flipped", i.e. we refer to them in the Western name order (given name-family name) as opposed to the Japanese order (family name-given name). E.g. the Japanese Prime Minister is referred to as Yoshihide Suga as opposed to Suga Yoshihide.

    This doesn't seem to be the same for Korean names - for instance, Kim Jong-un is referred to as Kim Jong-un.

    So why are Japanese names flipped, but Korean names not?

    Such_Newt_1374 A:

    Answer: it's a standard introduced during the Meji era in Japan as a way of catering to westerners and "modernizing" (i.e. westernizing) Japan. When a Japanese name is written in western characters specifically they will then put the given name first and family name last, but only when written in western (Roman) characters.

    Worth noting that in 2019 the government reversed policy on this and is now encouraging the traditional name order in all circumstances.

    tsuchinokoko A:

    When Japan modernized with the Meiji Restoration, they essentially took ideas from all sorts of culturally western societies and combined them as they saw fit. This included the military, their banking system, and in this case, general culture. Along with stuff like new clothes and hair styles coming into the picture, flipping the names was another thing that began in order to help Japan best mesh with western countries & societies. They were quite keen on modernizing and being seen in the same echelon as western world powers, and decided that the cultural changes were needed alongside technological ones. Another factor, is that in the Korean language, the family name and given name are something of a "package", called an ireum. There are many more nuances regarding naming conventions in Japan due to the Meiji Restoration, but Japan's willingness to become more Western in the global eye and Korea's "ireum" concept are a couple of the primary reasons.
