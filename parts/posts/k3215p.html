• Biology eli5: Why does your skin become dehydrated when exposed to large amounts of water?

    NEW
    Q:

    This is something that has always perplexed me, mostly cause I’m a dumb guy. So, when you shower or bathe for long periods or go swimming your skin is dried out. Why does a lot of water dry something out? Shouldn’t it do the opposite?

    natabun A:

    There is a special protective layer of emulsified sebum (skin's natural oils) and water on everyone's skin, called the acid mantle. The acid mantle prevents trans-epidermal water loss and keeps the skin moist and at a pH of about 4-5, but when that layer is compromised it causes the skin to lose it's water content and become dehydrated. Water's natural pH is around 7 (neutral), so long term exposure to water causes an alkaline reaction with the acid mantle and throws the balance off. This is also why especially after taking very hot showers or baths, or even washing your hands constantly, can cause your skin to feel very dry. When soaking in water, and the acid mantle becomes compromised, your skin essentially endures osmosis with the surrounding environment!

    Source: I'm an esthetician.
