    NEW
    Q:

    ELI5: why do all nursery rhymes have a dark twist in them?

    Downtown_Leek_1631 A:

    Because before the industrial revolution and modern medicine, it was a lot easier for people to die from things like injury, disease, and natural disasters, and a lot of nursery rhymes came out of getting children ready to face their own mortality.

    I don't remember where I read this, but 'we told our children stories full of blood, then took out all the blood and wonder what happened to the stories.' The idea that children need to be sheltered from the horrors of reality is relatively new in the grand scheme of things. I'm not saying parents should traumatize their children, depictions of violence and gore usually aren't traumatic for children anyway. What I'm saying is most censorship is unnecessary at best.
