• Medicine A vaccine is 94% effective. What, exactly does that mean?

    NEW
    Q:

    Does that mean a vaccinated person, exposed 100 times, will be fine 94 times? Or does that mean the severity of the resulting disease is reduced by 94%? or does that mean that 94% of subjects are 100% immune for this disease for a period of time?

    Rannasha A:

    It means that during the trial period, there were 94% fewer cases in the vaccine group than in the placebo group.

    So if you would two equally sized groups with either the vaccine or a placebo and in the placebo group 100 would fall ill, in the vaccine group only 6 would fall ill.

    notthatkindofdoc19 A:

    Effectiveness is not measured by a clinical trial; it is measured by "real-world" outcomes. A clinical trial measures efficacy. For the average person, the difference between these terms probably doesn't matter too much, but it is really important to remember that they are not the same thing. Effectiveness is almost always less than efficacy, and we would generally not expect these vaccines to reach 95% effectiveness. Here is a good overview of these terms from a WHO presentation. Here is another explanation from the NYTimes.

    The vaccine is 95% efficacious. This means that the vaccinated group experience a 94% reduction in the measured endpoint as compared to the placebo group. The primary endpoint for most of these studies was lab-confirmed (PCR) symptomatic covid.

    swill128 A:

    In epidemiology it means 94% fewer “events” than the control group, given equal levels of exposure. Sometimes you see increased numbers of “events” after the intervention due to there being more exposures.

    In this wonderful world we live in, we allow the company running the trial to define the “event”. In this case the vaccine does not prevent infection, just a reduction in acute signs and symptoms.
