    UncleWeyland A:

    Drosophila melanogaster, (the fancy-pants name for the species of fruit fly studied for over a century by geneticists) sure do! They can both get tired from flying around or climbing, and they also have a "down time" very similar to sleep in humans.

    In fact, the genes that control the sleep cycle (circadian rhythm) were discovered in Drosophila, and the geneticists who identified them won a Nobel prize for their work. These genes are so evolutionarily important that they are very similar in humans!
