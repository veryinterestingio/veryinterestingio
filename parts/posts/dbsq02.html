    NEW
    Q:

    ELI5: Why don’t businesses use more convert means to transport their cash instead of using, for example, a Brinks truck which practically screams “I have a ton of money or other valuables inside”?

    drafterman A:

    As a general principle of security, you don't rely too heavily (or at all) on obscurity. This precept is often stated as "the enemy knows the system." After all, someone has to know how the money is being transported. And once one person knows it, other people will eventually know it.

    But it's not like it would be that hard for any random person to figure this out.

    "Hm. Armed guards are putting tons of money bags in that Toyota Corolla. I wonder if they're transporting their cash now or not?"

    Only 25 to 35 armored trucks are robbed each year, compared to thousands of bank robberies. In terms of risk management, those are pretty good numbers for armored trucks. I don't think you're going to improve that by making them regular cars and hoping people don't notice.

    Haort A:

    Because covert doesn't help if people know what to look for. Plus, have you seen them fill an ATM? It's obvious in any car. The whole point of an armoured car is to make it as difficult to steal anything from the vehicle, and easier to investigate if it is. It's very difficult to penetrate, has constant video surveillance, armoured guards, etc.

    Plus, if someone steals an armoured car, it's pretty easy to track. First up, it's big and obvious, and secondly, they have GPS tracking.

    Ralfarius A:

    A lot of businesses do transport covertly. A large number of fast food type places, coffee shop chains etc do daily deposits by having someone count the cash out of the till drops and carry it to the nearest bank.

    They do this to save the cost of professional transportation. The other, bigger reason is that daily drops keep the cash in store at the absolute minimum.

    Combined with timelock safes that have secondary doors which don't open except for a small window during the day, a robber can only make out with whatever is in the cash drawers and if they're greedy enough to wait for the town delay of a safe, a few hundred dollars worth of coin rollers & small bills.

    Larger single location businesses like grocery stores handle too much cash on a daily basis to make this viable so they rely on secure transport.
