    sifsand A:

    Structural integrity. It's a cross rib to ensure that the pressure combined with the high temperature doesn't cause it to break. It's not really two chambers, it just looks like it is.

    jxf A:

    In plastic lighters, it's not so much that it's getting divided but rather that it's getting structurally supported. The divider is a web of crisscrossed struts that reinforce the integrity of the lighter, whose contents are under a lot of pressure.

    You would never want, say, a lighter to explode if you left it outside in the hot sun. This is what prevents it from doing that.
