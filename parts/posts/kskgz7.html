    Q:

    Eli5: Why are numbers on a telephone keypad set out differently to a calculator keypad?

    aricelle A:

    So back in the 50s, AT&T swapped from rotary to touch tone phones and they needed a pattern for the new keys. So they ran a study and tested 15 different layouts. Oddly enough, people preferred the telephone layout over the calculator layout. So that's what they went with.

    Further reading (including pictures of the extra patterns) -- https://thenextweb.com/syndication/2018/12/31/heres-why-telephones-and-calculators-use-different-numeric-keypads/

    _sadme_ A:

    Warning: slightly off topic.

    I was watching several YouTube videos about mechanical calculator and adders (such as Comptometer), so don't treat me as an expert in this field.

    Anyway, those devices have a common layout that was somehow forced by the design principles that they were using. There are separate sets of keys 1 to 9 for units, tenths, hundreds etc., but the common pattern of arranging keys was bottom-up (1s are closest to the user).

    Early models with single set of digits (0 to 9) used a layout that we're familiar with.
