• Eli5: Why is it "Attorneys AT law" instead of "Attorneys OF law"?

    NEW
    Q:

    Eli5: Why is it "Attorneys AT law" instead of "Attorneys OF law"?

    TokyoJokeyo A:

    The use of "at" often coincides with something practical; the Online Etymology Dictionary offers the example of a churchgoer being "at church" while a tourist is "in the church."

    An attorney at law is someone for whom the law is his profession. This isn't true of all attorneys: "attorney" means something like "representative"; the phrase "attorney at law" is opposed to "attorney in fact", someone who represents your interests but not in court, like when you give a relative "power of attorney" in case you are incapacitated. Today we usually say "agent" to avoid a potentially misleading use of the word "attorney."
