• Biology Are there diseases which can transfer from plants to animals?

    Q:

    There are quite a few diseases which can transfer from one type of animal to another. COVID-19 being one of them. This got me wondering, are there diseases which can transfer from plants to animals?

    Whatthemuzi A:

    It's rare but it can happen. One particular example I can think of is Pseudomonas aeruginosa infection. It's a Gram Negative bacteria causing a soft rot in plants and in humans it is most commonly associated with Pneumonia but it can invade any tissue.

    Gibberella A:

    A fungal example of this would be Aspergillus flavus. It’s best known for contaminating maize and peanut fields. Normally it’s already a bad time because it produces aflatoxin, the most carcinogenic naturally occurring compound as far as I remember, but it can also infect your lungs if you are immunocompromised.

    nichachr A:

    How about from insects to plants and back? Huanglongbing disease in citrus is an example of this. Candidatus libericbacter ssp. bacteria in the host psyllid infects the tree which then reproduces the bacteria and future generations are also infected allowing for further spread.

    hott8bitaction A:

    Zygomycosis, In particular infection from black bread mold (Rhizopus spp.) is a opportunistic infection that can occur in immunocompromised humans. This is the mold you see on berry type fruits and bread obviously. Route of infection seems to be inhalation of the spores when you go to smell if the food went bad.
