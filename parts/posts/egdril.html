    Delanoso A:

    Grams of sugar in one 12oz can of Coca-Cola: 39

    Grams of sugar in one cube: 4

    39/4 = 9.75 cubes in a can of Coke or .8125 cubes per ounce. Let's hope you're drinking Starbucks size coffees because otherwise you're getting more sugar in your coffee than you are in a Coke. (1 cube per ounce for a standard 6 ounce cup).

    pseudomota A:

    Coca-Cola and similar soft drinks are very acidic. Acid is what makes things like vinegar and lemon juice taste sour. Cola is almost as acidic as pure lemon juice. Because of the acidity, cola would taste really sour if it wasn't for all the sugar. The sweet and sour tastes balance each other out and you don't notice just how sweet it is.

    source on pH (acidity level) of drinks https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4808596/
