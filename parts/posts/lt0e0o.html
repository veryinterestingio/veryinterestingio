    PhotographNo7485 A:

    This can only happen when the photon interacts with something else, and it's impossible (and frankly meaningless) to tell if the photon after the interaction is the same one or a different one with lower energy. If a wave bounces off a wall, is it still the same wave? Maybe so, maybe not. A wave isn't an object in itself, but rather a moving pattern of excitation in a field.

    RangerSoul A:

    Can a photon release a portion of its energy?

    Yes. For example Raman scattering. Photons passing through a Raman active medium are inelastically scattered, leaving the molecules of the medium in an excited (usually ro-vibrational ) state.
