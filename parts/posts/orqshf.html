Some medication sometimes prevents the automatic valve from opening at all and this can be very painful and life threatening.

    1stDegreeBurns A:

    As with many aspects of the way in which the body runs, it’s helpful to think of this in terms of cavemen and how this system would have impacted/benefitted them. Imagine you are a caveman/cavewoman and you rely on hunting to get food, and shelter to stay safe from predators. When your body tells you that you need to pee, then you just go pee somewhere that you think it’s safe to be vulnerable for a few minutes. Now imagine you’re in a location that you don’t think is safe, obviously you’re not going to drop your trousers, you’re going to start looking for somewhere safer. The same works if you’re in the middle of hunting something and don’t have time to stop. After a while, it’s become clear that you either can’t or won’t stop your current task (hunting or searching for shelter), so it’s now in your body’s best interest to help you finish whatever task you’re working on so that you can go pee. To this end, hunting or searching for something can be difficult when you’re in mild pain because of a full bladder, it can distract your mind, making it hard to focus, and it can also make it hard to move because of the pressures you put on your bladder when you walk/run. So after a while of telling you that you need to pee, your body essentially recognises that you can’t pee because something more important is happening, and so it silences the need to pee so that you can focus on this more important task. This only lasts for so long, however, as eventually this need to pee will return when your body is letting you know that you seriously have got to pee right this minute or else you are going to start doing proper damage to your body. The same thing happens in terms of hunger. If you go a while without eating you’ll feel hungry, push through this for a few hours and you’ll stop feeling hungry, but several hours later you’ll feel even hungrier again.

    Tldr: When your body needs to pee it lets you know with various signals. If you push through this then your body recognises that you have bigger fish to fry, and will dampen the signalling, trusting that you’ll remember your bladder is full when you next get a chance to pee. Eventually your body gets fed up with waiting and reminds you that you seriously need to pee soon, and will continue to do so because at this point you’re possibly going to damage your body if you hold it in much longer.
