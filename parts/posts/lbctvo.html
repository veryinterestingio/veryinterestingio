    goldenkicksbook A:

    Before my son had to anaesthetised for an operation the doctor explained that just before he went under, his body would jerk almost as if resisting, but that it was nothing to worry about. He explained that when we fall asleep very rapidly our brain has the same reaction as when we fall and causes us to instinctively reflex to try and prevent the fall.

    bethbed A:

    Something interesting about these “jerks” is that once you are able to sleep through them, that is when your body is finally truly asleep.

    If you have a baby (or toddler/child) who has trouble falling and staying asleep, it’s recommended to hold them until they jerk. Once they are finished jerking (especially with babies and kids, the jerking can last for a few minutes) you can finally put them down in their crib/bed without worrying about waking them. This was the trick I LIVED by until my baby was about 3. Otherwise he would wake up the second you put him down and you’d have to start the whole process over.
