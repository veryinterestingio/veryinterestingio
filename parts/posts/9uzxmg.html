• Technology Why does hitting a flashlight when the batteries are low keep it working for a short time?

    NEW
    Q:

    If a flashlight is getting dim or having trouble turning on due to low power in the battery, it's common to bang on the flashlight a few times and it will start working. The same goes for batteries in a TV remote, except for banging on the batteries, just running your finger over them and making them roll in their slots back and forth seem to make the batteries start working again.

    shleppenwolf A:

    Corrosion on the battery contacts creates resistance that reduces the current delivered. Vibration can wipe off some of the corrosion.

    Black_Moons A:

    Most batteries stop working due to buildup of non conductive material on the internal plates of the battery or the plates falling apart.

    Hitting them knocks some of this build up off, exposing plate. Or potentially rejoining cracked plates.

    It is not generally due to external corrosion, as batteries and battery contacts are often plated with metals that do not easily corrode like nickel. Hence why only nearly dead batteries suffer from this and you'll find a brand new battery never needs to be hit.

    Chris_Hemsworth A:

    As an aside to the corrosion aspect, warming up batteries in your hand can give you a small burst of power as well. Temperature can help to create charge between the anode and the cathode, although does so at the expense of the batteries lifetime.

    If you want to make your batteries last longer, store them in the fridge when not in use.

    Source: Used to work in a battery lab.
