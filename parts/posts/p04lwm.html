    RelicBeckwelf A:

    They generally didn't. Though it depends on how bad your eyesight was. If you could see mostly clearly you likely wouldn't be in the military except for time of crisis (they usually didnt take the disabled or infirm). Though with a lack of automation if you learned a skill you could do by touch, such as weaving or pottery and the such you could likely find work. Most would have stuck near family for the support (and since the average person didnt do much traveling anyway)

    The_Thunder_Child A:

    The main issue with poor eyesight in the modern world are tasks that require reading to be able to perform them.

    Most people back then didn't need to read to do their daily tasks and so less than perfect eyesight didn't pose a problem.

    TeamShonuff A:

    The Barrow, Alaska Vision Study by Dr Francis Young and his associates in 1969 concluded that primitive societies where the people did not read or engage in substantial close work have virtually no myopia.

    That said, even if you're really nearsighted, you can still spend your day managing chickens or making clothing or firing pottery.
