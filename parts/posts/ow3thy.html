    Q:

    ELI5: What’s the difference between Ibuprofen, Acetaminophen, and Asprin?

    Ineffable_Mango A:

    They all work differently.

    Ibuprofen reduces inflammation and swelling, which reduces pain by taking pressure off of sensitive nerves.

    Tylenol directly acts as an analgesic to reduce sensitivity of the nerves themselves.

    Aspirin works similarly to ibuprofen. It also can thin the blood, which is why it is frequently given to people to prevent or help treat heart attack and other clotting issues.

    Notagunner96 A:

    Acetaminophen acts directly within the brain to reduce pain and fever.

    Ibuprofen reduces formation of chemicals that cause swelling and pain, as well as fever.*

    Aspirin does reduce some of those same chemical formation but also prevents the action of other chemicals that cause platelets to stick together. It’s effects vary depending on the dose. It’s effects on platelets are permanent (for the life of the platelet, not the person).

    *Ibuprofen can also have some temporary effects on blood clotting too which is why you shouldn’t take it or other NSAIDs if you’re about to have surgery or are taking blood thinners.

    AlwaysPixel A:

    Acetaminophen crosses the blood to brain barrier, where Ibuprofen doesn't.

    Acetaminophen will tell your brain to respond to pain differently, Ibuprofen will go to the part of your body that's hurting and reduce the pain or inflammation there.

    Acetaminophen toxicity is bad for your liver, Ibuprofen toxicity is bad for your kidneys. Acetaminophen also shouldn't be used with alcohol, because both of those mixed together will really strain your liver.
