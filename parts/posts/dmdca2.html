    Q:

    ELI5: Why can eating far too much pineapple make your tongue bleed? What makes that happen? Furthermore, if you say in a tub full of pineapple, would your whole body bleed?

    NoYellowSnow A:

    In the same way pineapple juice can be used to tenderise meat by breaking down the surface proteins, tongue (and on other mucous membranes in your mouth) is also subjected to the same enzymatic action.

    The enzyme is bromelain.

    It's actually a self-defence mechanism for the plant, evolved in order to deter/kill predators by dissolving their mouthpieces and digestive tracts.

    Once the surface proteins are dissolved, the acidic nature of the pineapple is then what gets in and causes the irritation and bleeding for some.
