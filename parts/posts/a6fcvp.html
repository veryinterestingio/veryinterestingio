• Biology Do organisms living in complete darkness (caves, deep water, underground) have a circadian rhythm? Most organisms do, but does it ever appear in those that have no access to a 24 hr cycle?

    NEW
    Q:

    Do organisms living in complete darkness (caves, deep water, underground) have a circadian rhythm? Most organisms do, but does it ever appear in those that have no access to a 24 hr cycle?

    mirshafie A:

    Yes, at least some of them.

    Darkness is not the only thing that changes on a 24-hour cycle. Close to the surface (up to 1 m depth), temperature also fluctuates on the same cycle. So worms definitely have a circadian rythm.

    Subterranean animals such as mole rats that live deeper than that also exhibit circadian rythms.

    As for deep see creatures, some seem to exhibit bicircadian rythm based on the lunar cycle, and others a circadian rythm based on the patterns of their prey.

    As caves are the most arrythmic environments on the planet, cave animals exhibit the least circadian patterns of all, but some vestiges seem to be there.

    slothhands A:

    It's important to note that light is not the cause of the circadian rhythm, just a way to adjust it. The circadian rhythm is controlled by a protein and it gets feedback from various sensory systems to adjust the the time cycle. The time cycle set by the rhythm varies between animals, in humans it's about 26 hours naturally, but it recieves input from our sensory systems. For instance, there are specialized photoreceptors in our eyes that feedforward directly to the part of our brain that controls the circadian rhythm. As far as neuroscientists know, every living thing has some sort of "sleep" cycle independent of specific sensory inputs, regulated by a protein.

    Walrad_Usingen A:

    Do parasites count? Some species of Plasmodium, which causes malaria, have a circadian rhythm. P. knowlesi has a 24-hour cycle although the most lethal version (P. falciparum) has a 48-hour cycle, and P. malariae has a 72-hour cycle.

    These live in the blood, so obviously it's complete darkness.
