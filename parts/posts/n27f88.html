• Earth Sciences When was the last time in Earth's history when the land area was evenly divided between the two hemispheres?

    NEW
    Q:

    I read recently that some cartographers of antiquity believed that the as yet undiscovered landmasses in the southern hemisphere must balance the northern hemisphere's in terms of area. While obviously untrue today, when was the last time in Earth's history when this was actually the case?

    yerfukkinbaws A:

    Based on Ron Blakey's global paleogeography series maps, it was some time in the later Triassic. Land area (not counting submerged continental margins) was close to even between the northern and southern hemispheres during most of the Jurassic and Cretaceous according to these maps, though, with only a slight excess in the north. Possibly within the error range of the reconstructions.
