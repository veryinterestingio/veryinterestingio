• Biology Why does it often feel like "work" to start something (e.g. a new show, or a shower) when we know we'll enjoy it once we've started?

    NEW
    Q:

    Inspired by a post on r/gaming

    Why does it sometimes feel like starting a new thing or task is so much effort even if we know it'll make us happier or more relaxed?

    Like when you're exhausted from a long day and you want to take a shower, but you feel too lazy to go through the whole process. If you manage to force yourself in, you frequently feel loads better and don't know why you ever felt like you didn't want to shower in the first place.

    PM_YOUR_MANATEES A:

    Most people are reflexively resistant to change, even when that change could or will have a positive effect on them.

    Change involves the possibility of negative risk, causing you to lose time or resources. When people evaluate their situation, they often feel safer avoiding negative risk entirely than pursuing a positive risk/opportunity.
