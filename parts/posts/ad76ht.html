    How do the Chinese send signals back to earth from the dark side of the moon if it is tidally locked?

    karantza A:

    Before landing Chang'e 4, they launched a relay satellite named Queqiao that stays at a point past the moon where it can see both Earth, and the far side of the moon.

    radome9 A:

    There is a satellite orbiting the second Lagrange point beyond the moon. From that point it can see both the Earth and the far side of the moon, and can therefore act as a relay.

    It's "far side", btw. The dark side is the side that is not illuminated by the sun. Some pinhead will be along shortly to argue that "dark" means "hidden" and that both dark and far side is correct. Screw that. Dark means dark and far means far. Mixing them up just leads to confusion and misunderstandings.
