    I had my Lenovo laptop plugged in all the time for almost 9 years, used many hours each day. After about 7 years I did a battery reset and the battery manager showed that it still had 95% capacity.

    Lots of confusing replies here, mostly because there really is seemingly conflicting info that some people are only presenting one side of.

    Yes, keeping it at 100% all the time is worse for battery life than keeping it at 30-70% all the time. In other words, cycling it is much worse than floating at 100%, which is in-turn worse than floating at 70%. Basically in order of best to worst the order is:

    oddbehreif A:

    To maximize your laptop battery lifespan, avoid topping it off and fully draining it. Between 40% and 80% is optimal; this is true for all lithium batteries. Check your laptop BIOS settings for an option to limit maximum charge level (I set my Zbook to 80% and just keep it plugged in).
