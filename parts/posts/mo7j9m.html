• What's the difference between a herb and a spice?

    NEW
    Q:

    Why is salt and pepper for instance classified as spices, while sage and basil are commonly known as herbs?

    Edit: OK salt is a mineral Marie, not a spice

    the_dreaded_triptych A:

    “Herb” is generally used to refer to leaves and stems of plants. “Spice” is typically used for ingredients derived from other parts of the plant, like seeds, pods, or bark.

    blipsman A:

    Herb is the leaf of a plant, spice are seeds/pods/bark. So basil, cilantro, sage would be herbs but a peppercorn, coriander seed, cinnamon would be a spice.

    jmz8675 A:

    Someone else posted this, and I can’t remember who, but collectively they are all “seasonings”. Herbs are made out of the leafy parts of plants. Spices are made from other parts of the plant, but not leaves. Like roots and seeds and such.
