    ELI5: If slouching is so bad for our back, then why is it so much easier and more comfortable?

    peanutbutteryummmm A:

    You slouch because your paraspinal muscles (among others) get tired, so it feels better in the short term to do that. The problem is that you’re then just “hanging out” on your other soft tissue structures (discs, ligaments, joint capsules), which get strained over time (think of pushing past an elastic barrier to a plastic deformation, aka tearing). Eventually you would start to break down tissues that you want to be healthy, and you will start having pain.

    fudgeyboombah A:

    Slouching is not bad for you in moderation.

    Slouching for 14 hours a day is bad for you.

    The problem is that human bodies are designed to move. We’re not designed to be continuously still in any pose, be it lying down, standing up, or in a semi-recumbent position - slouched or otherwise.

    A slouch is a position of rest. In and of itself there is nothing wrong with it. But as we have increasingly sedentary lifestyles, our rest-to-activity ratios change. We stop using our muscles enough. With that comes degradation. Our bodies need to move to function properly - our joints must constantly swing or else they stiffen, our muscles must be used or they atrophy, we can’t even properly move our blood around our bodies without moving our limbs.

    If we don’t lift ourselves out of the “rest” position and use the muscles that we are resting, it becomes the habit to never engage them and they atrophy. That is a significant problem and it causes dysfunction and pain. It means that other muscles need to work harder to do things like lift your head or move your arms or support your torso because you’re not using the muscles you should be using to hold up your chest and head. These muscles should be “on” for most of the day, with short “slouch breaks”, not the other way around.
