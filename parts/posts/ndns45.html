• Why do farmers cut down trees in livestock paddocks?

    NEW
    Q:

    I'm not looking to start an argument, I've just always wondered what the reasons are for this?

    Would love to hear from some farmers directly.

    thelastcreatorKB A:

    Often they dont keep fields full of livestock year round and rotate what they do with each field to keep the ground healthy. Sometimes they will let the grass grow tall, harvest and bale it then put cows in after. Trees block light, and get in the way of tractors.

    Flaxenfilly23 A:

    Grass does not grow well in shaded areas. The more of the land that is in full sunlight, the more it will be able to produce forage for them to graze on. Which equals less hay/grain requirements to keep them in good weight.

    jakeyy9306 A:

    Worked on farms for a while when I was younger. We used to have a 4 year cycle of paddocks, 3 years of crops and the 4th being a rest/livestock year. Trees are a pain to avoid with tractors and seeders/sprayers

    enjoyoutdoors A:

    I live in an area where agriculture is in decline, so to speak.

    You can, in theory (there are a lot of ifs and buts to deal with) get a government grant for keeping livestock that keeps a pasture open and grassy.

    Because it contributes to biodiversity, and a few other reasons.

    However, you can sort of fail at it, like one of my friends did. The government did some fly-by's and took aerial pictures, where they swiftly discovered that his pastures had waaaay too many trees to count as a pasture. Grant revoked.

    Well. Until he got rid of some more trees, of course.
