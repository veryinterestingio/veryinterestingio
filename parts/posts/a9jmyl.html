    Very basic explanation, but testosterone in the bloodstream converts to DHT, or dihydrotestosterone, which is responsible for genital growth and arousal as well as male pattern baldness among other things. Some of the testosterone in the blood converts to DHT; the more testosterone, the more DHT can be produced.

    Juswantedtono A:

    There are receptors in your sex organs and sexual circuitry in your brain that respond to testosterone by ramping up those organs’ response to sexual stimuli. There’s probably no special reason testosterone was chosen for this task as opposed to any other molecule, but that’s the molecule we and most other species evolved with. The general purpose of having a masculinization hormone is to create the necessary sexual dimorphism between the males and females of the species (estrogen being the equivalent feminizing hormone in females). In addition to contributing to sexual functioning, testosterone also develops other sexual traits like deeper voice, stronger muscles, and increased body hair in men—traits that advertise their maleness to the rest of the species and help them fulfill their male role.
