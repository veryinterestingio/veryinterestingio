    Not all kinds of cells will uptake the LNP. LNPs are formulated for uptake in fairly specific cell types that are being targeted. These vaccines are delivered intramuscularly and will have uptake in muscle tissue in white blood cells near the intramuscular target site, but also in hepatocyte liver tissue, via blood routes.

    It would likely be most cell types that come in contact with the mRNA liposomal particles, so in the current mRNA covid vaccines mostly muscle cells since it is an intramuscular injection.     TTEchironex A:

    It's an intramuscular injection, so the muscle cells will make the proteins. Basically any cell capable of producing protein that the RNA ends up in will make the proteins. Injections like this induce highly local expression of the gene in question, so it's not like the RNA is gonna float away and be picked up by some organ. It'll be mostly uptaken by muscle cells and maybe some fat/epidermal cells if it leaks towards the surface. Muscle is a great choice because the cells are long lived and produce huge amounts of protein, and a single muscle cell can even have multiple nuclei, so are basically protein factories. Other formulations like the russian vaccine use a virus for delivery and unlike the RNA vaccines, that will actually float away and be picked up by cells all over the body. But the virus they use for delivery will have a fairly specific set of sells it can enter, so only those will be producing the proteins at the end.

    Red2016 A:

    some cells simply don't have the capability to transcribe/generate new proteins. Red blood cells don't even have a nucleus or ribosomes (which are needed to generate proteins).

    I havent read into the details of these covid specific mRNAs but my guess is that the LNPs are best absorbed by certain cell types which can then transcribe the mRNA. since the vaccine is injected intramuscularly, the muscle cells would be the prime target for mRNA/LNP absorption. Cells that are able to take up the LNPs wont be able to distinguish between "human/self" vs foreign/vaccine mRNA so they will be transcribed indiscriminately. the mRNA leads to the generation of viral particles which helps the body develop antibodies to the virus. So when you're infected with the real virus, the body already has antibodies ready to fight it!
