    Q:

    ELI5: Why do we usually wake up a minute before our alarm clock goes on?

    DupeyTA A:

    Your brain recognises patterns and has an internal clock. If you set an alarm, it's probably because it's a continuous thing you need and or it's something that you consciously deem important. As your brain is cognisant of time, your brain will attempt to wake up in time to perform that task, which you have changed from "meeting at 9" to "wake up at 7:30".

    vanZuider A:

    Selection Bias. When we wake up and go to sleep again after 1-2 minutes, we don't remember it in the morning. We only remember those episodes where we can't fall asleep again - either because the thing that woke us up actually demands our attention, or because the alarm happens to ring one minute later.
