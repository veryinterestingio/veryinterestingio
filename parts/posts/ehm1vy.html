• Biology Why does cilantro taste bitter like soap to me?

    NEW
    Q:

    Any time I have cilantro i can always taste it out of the dish it was put in. I asked my friends about it but they said its suppose to be kind of like a zest flavor to foods, but it just tastes super bitter to me. No one else knows what I'm talking about when I bring it up to them.

    KitsuneRisu A:

    Expanding on the other guy, yes. It is genetic.

    Cilantro and other members of that family including carrots, celery or parsley have a specific chemical that acts as a deterrent to wild predators eating it. Basically, it tastes bad to animals so they leave it alone.

    Humans have generally evolved OUT of being able to taste or detect that flavour, but once in a while someone will be born with that genetic code (The OR6A2 gene) that allows them to taste the bad chemical.

    That is you and also me and nearly everyone in my family.

    Likely, if you hate the taste of cilantro, you will also find celery and carrot tops (just the tops) very bitter as well.
