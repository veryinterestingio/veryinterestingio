• Biology Eli5: Why do lemons have seeds but limes don't?

    NEW
    Q:

    Whenever I'm in a restaurant, limes never have seeds, but lemons always do. Why?

    Lupicia A:

    Persian limes (not Key limes) are seedless. They can produce fruit without being fertilized. They are generally grafted instead of grown from seed.

    Limes like these at the grocery store all perhaps come from the same "tree" sliced into bits and surgically attached Frankenstein style to different plants. Freaky if you think about it.
