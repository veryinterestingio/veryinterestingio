    rizlakingsize A:

    It can and has happened. Look up Mira - a star just shooting through space leaving a 13 light year long trail. A black hole has also been found that was ejected from it's galaxy. Sometimes instead of merging they'll circle each other and one accelerates enough to be ejected from the other's gravitational pull.

    CosineDanger A:

    Hypervelocity stars

    You can't do a proper slingshot maneuver with just two objects. You can do a hard (hyperbolic) turn with just two, but you need a third object to gain speed relative to the primary. It doesn't matter if the primary is a black hole.

    At least some hypervelocity stars start as binaries where one partner becomes trapped circling the black hole and the other is yeeted out of the galaxy at ~0.2% of the speed of light.
