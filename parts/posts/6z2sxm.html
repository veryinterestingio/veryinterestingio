    Q:

    ELI5 why sedans are the choice of the average person in the US, whereas in the U.K. it's hatchbacks?

    blipsman A:

    Larger sedans -- which are Americans' preferences -- offer similar storage space to smaller hatchbacks, but the benefit of being in a more secure trunk (boot). Americans drive larger vehicles because they cost less (no VAT or other high taxes), taxes/registration is based on broad class (passenger car, truck) rather than engine displacement or other more granular determination. Also, our roads and towns were built for cars, so no tight spaces where small cars are necessary. Now, we're actually seeing small SUVs like Honda CR-V, Nissan Rogue surpass even the most popular sedans in sales, which trades the cargo security for higher driving position and AWD capabilities in bad weather..

    Grimsteed931 A:

    Speaking as someone who has lived both in the US and the UK, my theory would be that for whatever reason pick up trucks are nowhere near as ubiquitous in the UK as they are in the US, therefore hatchbacks offer considerable advantages over sedans for transporting unwieldy items from time to time such as TV's, stepladders, etc. This was certainly one of my considerations when choosing a vehicle there. Here in the US though almost every family probably has access to a pick up truck for occasional cargo use and therefore sedans for regular use offer advantages such as a quieter operating experience and an ability to lock valuables out of sight in the trunk.
