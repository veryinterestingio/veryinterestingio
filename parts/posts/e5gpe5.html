    Q:

    ELI5: If the eye focuses with muscles, how can/why can't those muscles train to work without glasses again?

    chemical_refraction A:

    Eye doc here: Think of the eye as a complete system for bending light. The goal is to have the light in focus on the back of the eye (retina).

    The main focusing factors are the curvature of the cornea (fixed/doesn't change), the curvature of the lens (not fixed/can add (+) power only), and the length of the eye (decides if the other two are doing it good enough or not enough.

    If you are near sighted that is a way of saying your entire system has too much power for the length of your eye...which means you have built in reading (near power) so we use minus powered lenses to correct that light to focus on the retina. In a near sighted person the lens in the eye is relaxed because adding even more power would just make things more blurry.

    If you are far sighted the eye is under powered for the current length of your eye, so adding more (+)power is needed, which the lens in your eye can do...in other words the lens can act "like a pair of glasses" for a far sighted person...except straining all the time like this can cause headaches and eye strain.

    The ability of the lens in the eye to bend is deteriorated over time regardless of "muscle strength" which is why it wouldn't help to do eye exercises. Eye exercises are beneficial to folks who can't bend their lenses as well as others in their young age group (younger than 40)

    The reason LASIK works is the front curvature of the cornea is reshaped to match the length of the eye so things are in focus for the distance. To see things up close the lens in the eye still needs to bend and flex, which is why after age 40 those people with LASIK still need to wear reading glasses. (Reading glasses give the (+) power needed that the lens bending used to give but no longer can)

    twotall88 A:

    Edit4: Most importantly, what I put in my original post and 2 edits didn't tell the whole story. Please, follow this link for a better, in depth explanation from an eye doctor /u/chemical_refraction.

    TL:DR ELI5: Exercising your eye muscles only works for far sighted people that are younger than 40 years old and cannot bend their lens as well as they should. The muscles you would be 'exercising' only affect the lens of the eye (only a potion of how you focus) that deteriorates as you age. It also would not help astigmatisms

    Basically, the muscles have their limits. The muscles stretch the lens of the eye to pull the light into exact (it has very sensitive tolerances) focus (near/far) onto the retina. The lens only accounts for 25-35% of the focusing power of your eye. You can make minor gains by 'training' these muscles and making them stronger but then you bounce against the limits.

    There are no muscles that stretch the cornea (as far as I can tell) which accounts for 65-75% of your eye's focusing power. The cornea is also where an astigmatism (misshaped cornea) lives so you cannot fix an astigmatism by doing eye exercises.

    Edit: apparently astigmatism can live in the lens as well. This makes sense but TIL.

    Finally, as you age your cornea and lens will become more dense/rigid and you cannot stretch it as much so your ability to focus diminishes (why we have bi- or even trifocal glasses).

    Source

    Edit2: ridged =/ rigid

    Edit3: People have brought up the fact (and they aren't wrong) that nearsightedness and farsightedness has to do with the overall shape of the eye (long/short).

    fox-mcleod A:

    Myopia (farnearsightedness) is the eye being too long for light focusing. The muscles on the lens pull it to shorten the focal length or relax to elongate it. The muscles can’t train to relax even further than all the way, so farsighted people remain far sighted.

    Presbyopia (nearfarsightedness) usually comes with age and is the lens losing is stretchyness. So no matter how you train the muscle, they can’t fix the fact that that the lens is no longer stretchy.

    Astigmatism is the misshapenness of the eye that caused a distortion in the visual field. So the muscles which pull in the lens can’t fix that either.

    But yes, other smaller defects can get fixed by the muscles adjusting.
