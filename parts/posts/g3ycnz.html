    Q:

    ELI5) Why is it that most times we feel the need to pee with a full bladder and other times we have the same feeling but only have to pee a little bit frequently in the span of an hour when hydrating? Why doesn’t our body just wait until our bladder is full to give you the signal?

    MyOther_UN_is_Clever A:

    The bladder is actually inflexible when full, it cannot stretch to accommodate more urine. If it's truly full, it's in danger of bursting. Generally, your body starts bugging you to pee at about 30% full.

    If you are drinking water and frequently urinating, it's because your body is reacting to the excess water by processing it quicker than normal to expel it.

    If you are peeing just a little bit, it can indicate you are dehydrated but your kidneys are processing a lot of waste. Many people notice something like this after taking vitamin B supplements or eating certain vegetables like asparagus.

    Also, urinating frequently can be a warning sign for diabetes. This is similar to above, but your body is trying to remove the extra "sugar" from your blood. A simple home test for diabetes is to drink a full glass of orange juice and at your next urination, check for a sweet smell coming from the toilet. People with maids/cleaning service are sometimes diagnosed by the maid noticing the sugary scent from the toilet(s).

    Pressure on the bladder can also cause an urge to go, such as an animal or child leaning on it. Pregnant woman often go more frequently as the baby develops and gets larger.

    Men can also end up with a strange condition if they ejaculate and pee too closely together, causing a sensation of needing to pee despite an empty bladder. Because the urethra is used for both, there are muscles activated for ejaculation to prevent urine from coming out. Those muscles can get "confused" when both happen within a short period of time (varies but usually within 10 minutes). The muscles can be relaxed by taking a warm shower or with a heating pad, focus the heat in the lower abdomen where the bladder is.

    There are a lot of possibilities and reasons, and some can indicate a medical issue. If you don't think your situation is typical, you might want to ask your doctor about it. If it's really as simple as "sometimes you pee more than other times" it can just be as simple as "you may have been asleep or distracted long enough to distract from the sensation so your bladder could fill more until it became more pressing."

    edit: Clarified "inflexible" meaning. Here's a medical report of a burst bladder from a blockage, preventing urination, for those who are curious. It can also be caused by binge drinking. https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5849873/
