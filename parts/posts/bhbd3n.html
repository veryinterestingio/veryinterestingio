    Q:

    ELI5: why is scalp skin so different from other skin?

    Derpy_Derpenstein A:

    The skin structure of scalp is basically the same as other parts of the body. However, since it has more lipid glands and sweat glands compared with other parts of the body, it becomes dirty very easily from sweat and sebum. Scalp has a lower barrier function and therefore it has a lower ability to maintain and replenish moisture compared to skin on other parts of the body. Therefore, it is more likely for skin problems to occur on the scalp than on other areas of skin.

    AncientPunykots A:

    The function of the scalp is to protect the skull and brain from injury. As medical students, we learnt the layers of scalp from top to bottom as follows: S - skin C - Connective tissue A- aponeurosis L - Loose areolar tissue P - Pericranium

    Basically the skin is attached to the aponeurosis with the connective tissue. Aponeurosis is a sheet of tissue that connects the forehead muscles and the muscles at the back of your head. Loose areolar tissue allows the skin with the aponeurosis to slide over the skull.

    Fun fact - this is why some people can move their whole scalp front to back.

    Another fact - native Americans used to “ scalp” the whites to prove to their chiefs their kills! They did this by cutting the scalp off at the level of the loose areolar tissue and the scalp can easily be peeled off in seconds!

    We surgeons use the same technique to “scalp” patients to expose the skull for various surgical procedures !!
