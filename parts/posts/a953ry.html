• Why is it, when you say a certain word so many times it starts to become alien and almost unfamiliar to the point where you can hardly understand it?

    NEW
    Q:

    ELI5: Why is it, when you say a certain word so many times it starts to become alien and almost unfamiliar to the point where you can hardly understand it?

    paolog A:

    It's called "semantic satiation".

    The reason it happens is that the neurons of your brain that recognise the meaning of the word essentially "tire out" and so the word stops meaning anything until they've had time to recover.

    Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semantic_satiation
