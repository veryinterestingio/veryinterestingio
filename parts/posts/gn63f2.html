    gamel35 A:

    Basically, the tutsi minority (~10-15%) was the ruling class of the kingdom before colonization. Traditionally they were herders with a strong military tradition.

    The hutus were the majority, but divided and traditionally farmers.

    Then the Belgians came, with the Catholic Church. When they left the country for real (early 1990s), they left "democracy" behind them. So the tutsi knew they would never be able to take the power back, because when you impose "democracy" in a country where the society is organised around your ethnicity, the biggest group always wins.

    So the civil war started, and the genocide is only the final act of years of fighting.

    The International Criminal Tribunal for Rwanda investigated but failed to prove the premeditation of the genocide. It also didn't investigate those who took power after the genocide (RPF, ~ tutsi rebels led by the still current president kagame).

    RegularBIrb A:

    At the time Rwanda was predominantly made up of two groups: the Hutu, who made up around 85% of the population; and the Tutsi, who made up around 14% of the population. Decades before during Belgian occupation, these groups became social classes, with the Tutsi being the minority rule. The Tutsi were given higher paying jobs, better education and many more benefits, but importantly, everyone had to have ethnic ids and lists were made up of everyone. At this time there were many riots and a lot of tension between the ruling Tutsis and the Hutu. But when the Belgian’s left in 1962, power was given to the Hutu - the long oppressed majority. Between this and the genocide there was still unresolved hate for the Tutsi and there were many riots and purges of the minority group.

    In 1994, Rwanda was under a one party, far right, Hutu government and there was even more tension then every between the Hutu and the Tutsi. On April 6th 1994, the presidents plane was shot down and everyone died. The Hutu government and people blamed the Tutsis and within hours all of the capital was shutdown with barricades and roadblocks.

    The genocide was planned, and within hours Hutu militia groups began efficiently and systematically killing every member of the Tutsi population.
