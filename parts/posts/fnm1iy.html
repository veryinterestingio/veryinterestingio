    NEW
    Q:

    The instructions recommend staying upright for 30 minutes afterwards, why is that? I wonder why don't I see this with things like antihistamines or painkillers. It is a 200mg iron fumarate supplement, if that helps.

    TheBioloG A:

    Some medicines - such as iron supplements - can cause ulceration of the oesophagus if they do not pass cleanly into the stomach.

    Lying down after taking an iron supplement can increase the chance of this occurring so it is advised against for a period of time.

    cmillhouse A:

    Certain pills can cause severe esophageal irritation even to the point of an ulcer if they get lodged there instead of moving to the stomach. So things like iron, doxycycline, bisphosphonates (osteoporosis treatment) can all do it, so it is generally good practice to take water to flush it down and let gravity help by being upright.

    Source: am doctor.

    PS You should know there is evidence that oral iron is better absorbed by being taken every OTHER day instead of daily. Seems counterintuitive but it is related to a hormone called hepcidin in the small intestine. I would recommend taking only every other day or MWF
