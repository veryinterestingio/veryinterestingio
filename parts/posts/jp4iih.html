    Override9636 A:

    Some bugs' lifestyles essentially breakdown into Eat -> Grow -> Mate. They eat until they grow into their larval form, then spend 90% of their energy transforming into their sexually mature form, and then spend their last 10% of energy mating, and laying eggs. After that, they're done. Some mature stages don't even have mouthparts to continue eating and living, so if they don't get eaten by something else, they just slowly die afterwards, and their genes get spread to the next generation.
