    drzowie A:

    People born male develop the "cro magnon bulge" brow ridge: a prominent ridge across the face just under the forehead and supporting the eyebrows. Women in general don't have that bulge -- the forehead rises basically straight up from the optical orbits.

    Once you start noticing it, you'll never un-notice it.

    TooBusyToLive A:

    There really isn’t one main feature. The other answers point out some main features that are more masculine or feminine, however think of a dog vs cat. Name one feature that distinguishes them. You could come up with something, but really what happens is your brain is looking at a lot of things and making a judgment in a fraction of a second that what you’re seeing is a cat. Before you notice the whiskers, you know it’s a cat. It’s the same in this case. It’s your brain recognizing a pattern (and making judgements often without you knowing it when some feature doesn’t fit the pattern), not a single feature

    PinkWhiteAndBlue A:

    There are a lot of feature seen as masculine or feminine, there's not one thing you can pinpoint it to.

    In general having soft features, small nose, wide eyes, large lips, round jawline, round hairline, small head, and a round forehead are scene as feminine. Some of these features can be changed with hormone replacement, so it's not purely skeletal.
