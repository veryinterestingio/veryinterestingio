• Biology Are there "deep-lake" creatures at the bottom of lake Baikal ?

    NEW
    Q:

    I know that deep-sea fish and arthropods adapted to the extreme conditions of these depths and are really weird looking, bioluminescent or transparent. Are there creatures like that in the depths of lake Baikal ?

    R97R A:

    I’m aware of some “deep-lake” animals, such as Comephorus (aka the Baikal Oilfish), which possess similar adaptations to some deep-sea animals. There are also examples of “deep-lake gigantism” (see: Deep Sea Gigantism. Apologies for the Wikipedia link, but I feel it’s a good primer for beginners), but these are less giant squid and more “Gammarus the size of your finger.”

    While Lake Baikal is very deep, it’s important to note that it doesn’t reach the depths of the abyssopelagic and hadopelagic zones, where most of what we’d consider “deep-sea” animals reside.
