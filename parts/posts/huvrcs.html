    Q:

    ELI5: why does your arm short circuit when you hit your elbow?

    ToxiClay A:

    Running very close to the elbow joint is a nerve called the "ulnar nerve."

    If you whack the bottom of your upper arm in a particular way, you'll jam the nerve up against the bone and cause a burst of sensation and motor impulse to run down your arm into part of your hand.

    Ilovephysics123 A:

    Because motor reflex arc,similar reflexes occur in other parts of the body as well for example when the doctor hits your knee with a hammer, we call that one as the "Knee-jerk" reflex, it is the result of the excitation of the ulnar nerve,which sends an electrical impulse down to the inter neurons which lie in the gray matter of your spinal cord through an afferent neuron,these signals do not go upto the brain to get a motor activity done because it takes too much time,it's like there's a fixed protocol to be followed and these should be done in the least amount of time which the spinal cord does but the brain takes more time to do,imagine having your hand on a hot vessel and taking more time to feel that it's hot and you need to take your hand off it,that's why it never goes to the brain,the reflex arc completes itself without the brain involved.After the spinal cord's interpreted what protocol is to be followed when you've hit your elbow (like getting a shock), it sends an electrical impulse conveying the message down to the effector through an motor/efferent neuron, this electrical impulse is eventually converted to a chemical impulse at the end of the synaptic knob of the motor neuron into chemical messengers such as epinephrine and non-epinephrine which tell your body to give you the short circuit/immediate shock.
