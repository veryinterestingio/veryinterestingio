    Eli5 How can Tesla be the most valuable car company in the world whereas it was almost bankrupt last year?

    OgFinish A:

    Vehicle manufacturers are fined in many states if they do not produce enough EVs. Tesla, who produces only EVs, sells their extra "credits" to these manufacturers. In the previous four quarters, they made over a billion dollars doing something that essentially cost them nothing. That led to them posting a profit for four quarters, which (among other things) made them eligible for the S&P 500, which caused people to buy more stock, which caused their valuation to go up, and so fourth.

    They also had the 7th best selling sedan in Q4 2019.

    AgentElman A:

    Tesla being almost bankrupt last year was an accounting view. Tesla had spent almost all of its cash just before its cars came off the line and could be sold to bring in income. The cash was spent on the materials and costs to produce the cars.

    Once the cars were finished and sold Tesla had plenty of money.

    If Tesla had needed more money it could have borrowed it. The appearance of being near bankruptcy was actually excellent cash management that was spun as a problem for the company by people who wanted to target the company.

    Tesla may not be worth what investors are currently paying for it, but it also wasn't close to going bankrupt last year.
