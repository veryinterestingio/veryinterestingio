    Taurius A:

    As long as one can eat with their mouth, the tongue will maintain their muscle tone so to speak(pun not intended). What can diminish due to not speaking are the laryngeal muscles. Without them, the vocal cords can't contract to produce sounds. There was a famous case in China of a man who lost his voice after not speaking for 12 years.

    suid A:

    The tongue is not what makes the sound when you speak: it just modulates it. And you exercise your tongue thoroughly when eating, for instance, or drinking. (I.e. it doesn't just sit there like a slab that's being dragged around your mouth..)

    As for the vocal cords, maybe just a little. But coughing, etc., also gives them a workout.

    sashafurgang A:

    In addition to other replies, the vow of silence is not “one size fits all”.

    Some communities have a hard vow where they don’t talk at all. Others have a softer version where they discourage most idle chit-chat but it’s still ok to have occasional conversations between members. Some only have a vow of silence during certain times of the day. In addition, many communities would still pray and/or sing out loud several times a day. So the likelihood that a member’s vocal cords would lose tone to a problematic degree is very unlikely.

    ETA regarding your edit: if someone were to take a hard vow of silence and get absolutely no practice making the sounds of their language for a very long time, then it is entirely possible for them to lose the muscle memory that allows the articulators (tongue, lips, glottis...) to hit the exact right movements to make the right sounds. They wouldn’t be unintelligible if they tried speaking again after a few years, but they would sound rusty for a few days/weeks. Kind of like someone who hasn’t practiced their second language in a while. You would mostly notice it in the vowels, because those are more fluid and the tongue needs to make very precise movements to modulate the voice correctly. You probably wouldn’t notice it in the consonants at all as those are simple discreet movements.
