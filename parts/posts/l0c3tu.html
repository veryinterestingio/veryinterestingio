    If we didn't eat sugar our whole lives our teeth would last a lot longer even with brushing.

    BurnOutBrighter6 A:

    Thousands of years ago:

    1. Acidic foods (and drinks!!) were not eaten every day by most people (or at all)
    2. Refined sugar was not eaten in huge quantities every day by most people (or at all)
    3. The diet contained much more rough fiber (plants, raw veggies, seeds) so there was a lot of scrubbing action even without brushing. Wild animals don't brush their teeth and are largely fine for this reason. Pets benefit from a rope toy they can sink their teeth into to replace this natural scrubbing from their "normal"/wild diet.
    4. "thousands of years ago when brushing teeth wasn’t a thing" Dude the Egyptians were brushing their teeth with chewed twigs literally 5,000 years ago. Ancient Greeks, Romans, Arabs and Indians cleaned their teeth with twigs too.
    5. To answer the 1st part, they weren't "meant to" but yes, tooth problems were still a thing. Here's a description of a dental filling done with beeswax 6,500 years ago. Here's a record find of a cavity being painfully dug out using stone tools 14,000 years ago. Anthropologists actually use the oldest-found cavities to determine when a society invented agriculture, because of the link to eating carbs, which are otherwise very scarce in nature.

    Not really. Our teeth lasted longer because we didn't eat refined sugar. We ate natural foods not processed foods. So our teeth lasted longer. Similar to how wild animals have teeth that usually last a while.

    However, even thousands of years ago, there were teeth problems. There is evidence we have uncovered of dental work being done on people even in ancient times.
