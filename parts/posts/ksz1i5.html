    usrevenge A:

    Your frontal lobe basically turns off when you sleep. This is the part of your brain that helps you form a picture of reality.

    This is why dreams can be fantastical. Your logic/reality center is turned off.

    Your brain is sorta repairing itself when we sleep. It's actually very active while sleeping but it's, in computer terms, saving all the files for the day it thinks it needs saving. It's defragging, and it's stopping processes that aren't needed.

    goos3d A:

    It’s my understanding that sleep is the way our brains get rid of the trash that builds up throughout the day. It’s pretty much a filing system that determines whether or not what we learned from today has been useful enough to use again later, or if it’s going to be forgotten and thrown out as useless information. These memories are filed and sorted during sleep and end up being interpreted as dreams.
