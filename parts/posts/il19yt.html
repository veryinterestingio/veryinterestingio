• ELI5. How can games be so “addicting” and work not feel the same - even though work yields real life results and games are just virtual. How can one incorporate the rapid reward system into work?

    NEW
    Q:

    How can it be that I’ll even pay my real hard earned money in a game just because it’s so addictive, but one is “ a waste of time” and the other helps me progress in life but is a chore?

    Logically it seems silly to be so immersed and devoted to a game, but wow sometimes I can’t sleep without just “one more level”. One more level just leads to one more level. How come work isn’t as addicting, wed all be rich !

    realultralord A:

    When I play a video game, no one tells me I have to. No one is expecting any results. There are no real life consequences when I fail or just decide not to.

    When I work, it can be fun sometimes, but sometimes it isn't. I can't just say "Nah fuck it, I don't like this level." and leave it. At work I have to perform, otherwise there might be bad consequences.

    weeddealerrenamon A:

    games are like pure sugar for our reward centers. Real life accomplishments are always going to take more work and pay off slower. I'm wary of any system to "gamify" work, since I think quality work shouldn't be about immediate reward, it should be about dignified pay and the nom-rapid, long-term satisfaction of doing valued work within your community. When a job sucks, adding video game elements to work only distracts from these deficiencies.
