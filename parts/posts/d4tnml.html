    ELI5: Why does spicy food burn the mouth and tongue but not the throat?

    MurderTron_9000 A:

    Technically it does, we just have more receptors for pain in our mouths. Because the chemical capsaicin, which is responsible for that, doesn’t actually burn, it just uses receptors in your mouth to fool it into believing it is being burned, and they happen to only affect certain receptors. We also know it does cause that reaction the whole way down due to the fact that too much gives you an upset stomach, which will result in the body trying to eject it either by ridding your body of it the usual route by very quickly passing it through your intestines, or vomiting.

    medium_finger A:

    Because it’s not burning anything? You are just tasting an ingredient called capsaicin.

    Also why water achieves nothing and could actually make it worse. Water doesn’t neutralize capsaicin, instead it ends up spreading it all around your mouth to even more receptors.

    Foods that have some acidic element will neutralize capsaicin (dairy, citric juices, vinegar, etc).
