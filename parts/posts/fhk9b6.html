    starfishgirl1991 A:

    The Federal Reserve buys US treasury bonds.

    A US treasury bond is an IOU from the government. When you buy a bond, you are giving the government a loan. When the government buys that bond, they are giving you your money back.

    When the Federal Reserve wants to inject cash into the market, they buy bonds from banks. When the banks have more money, they are able to give out more loans to people and businesses. This cash then flows down into the rest of the economy. This action also lowers the interest rates that people have to pay when they take out loans, which increases investment.

    Upeeru A:

    Most of the money takes the form of bond purchases.

    The treasury issues bonds, these get bought by people as an investment. These bonds are often sold to third parties before they mature (can be cashed in). The Fed becomes that third party, spending cash and buying those bonds.
