    Eli5: If caffeine blocks the chemicals that make you tired, how does coffee sometimes you more tired than before?

    nerdmeetsworld A:

    Caffeine blocks the chemicals that make you feel tired from being detecting your brain, however, it doesn’t replace these chemicals (adenosine).

    These chemicals continue to build up and when the caffeine begins to wear off, the chemicals flood the receptors leading to an increased level of tiredness, much more than you felt prior to consuming the caffeine.

    TellMeHuMI A:

    There are a couple of factors going on here:

    Dehydration: Caffeine is a diuretic, it makes you urinate more water than you take in. More often than not when you're tired after drinking coffee, your actually just dehydrated.

    Tolerance: If you use caffeine often you'll end up needing more and more to maintain the same level of wakefulness. You could be taking a smaller dose than you need and could just enter right into the caffeine crash, without a high.

    General Body Weirdness: The body is a super complex thing, there are probably a hundred other answers to your question. The body is weird sometimes, does fucky shit.
