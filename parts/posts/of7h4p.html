    Livesies A:

    Combination of reasons:

    Biggest in my mind is cost as others said. Petroleum is cheaper. Often it's cheaper to make new plastics than to recycle - specifically Styrofoam.

    Material properties also matter. There are many types of plastic. There are six types of unique chemical structures and properties just in the recycling program with many more. Some properties can be imitated, and when prices match or customers prefer the non-petroleum version it gets used.

    Shelf life is the last hurdle. Petrol products will last for as long as needed on a store shelf. A biodegradable plastic needs to maintain integrity for shelf life or life of use (non food packaging) but still degrade when disposed. It isn't easy to control when that happens.

    Progress is being made though. Small purchases add up to incentivize companies to change what they use. As the non-petrol products gain popularity, they'll get more funding to perform better.
