• Biology If pimples are formed from clogged pores, how come we don't get huge pimples if we get adhesive like superglue on your skin

    NEW
    Q:

    I was working on a project the other day and spilled super glue all over my hands. It ended up taking a few days to come off and I had this random shower thought yesterday. The glue wasn't able to come off so intuitively, isn't the glue clogging the pores on my hands? What's happening here?

    Mmedical A:

    Pimples happen when the sebum glands below the surface of the skin get clogged and then bacteria cause an infection.

    Your superglue may or may not have blocked the gland, but there was no bacteria, so no infection.
