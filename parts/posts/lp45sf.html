    ELI5 Why is it a bad idea to fall asleep after a concussion?

    WithEyesWideOpen A:

    It isn't. The new recommendation is to have someone check in on you every few hours and make sure you are breathing normally. The reason they used to have people wake you was to check your mental faculties and make sure you weren't having a brain bleed. Sleeping actually helps heal after a concussion and it's very important.

    tsuuga A:

    This is outdated advice. The reasoning was that you wouldn't be able to notice their symptoms getting worse, because you're not slurring your speech/having balance issues/etc when you're asleep.

    The current advice is that if you can walk and talk normally, and your pupils aren't dilated, sleeping is fine. If you do have those symptoms, you should see a doctor. In that case, they may recommend waking the patient up a couple of times during the first night, to check that symptoms aren't getting worse.
