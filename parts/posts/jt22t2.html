• Biology how does saline mix (salt and water) helps with healing sores such as wisdom tooth extractions or episiotomy?

    NEW
    Q:

    I have had wisdom tooth extraction, navel piercing and episiotomy. In all instances my doctor/administrators said to soak the area with a saline mix. What's the benifit of doing that?

    NotoriousSouthpaw A:

    At the cellular level, concentration gradients are critical for cellular function. Saline is isotonic with human blood, meaning that it has roughly the same concentration of salt and water in solution as blood does- so there is effectively no difference between the two.

    This means saline doesn't disrupt those concentration gradients by sucking water or electrolytes out of your cells or forcing them in. This is great for wound irrigation, since you can wash away dirt and bacteria without damaging your cells as they heal.

    Chyndonax A:

    Salt and water don't actually cause wound healing directly. It just keeps the wound clean and stops it from drying out. Basically it provides an ideal environment for the wound to heal as opposed to healing the wound. Salt is mixed with water, at a very specific ratio, because otherwise the cells would have their own ratio of sodium to water disrupted which would lead to saturation, dehydration and electrolyte imbalances in which case so long good wound healing environment.
