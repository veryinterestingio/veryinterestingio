    ELI5: Why is that it’s so much easier to run outside naked in temperatures that are much lower than the freezing point of water but jumping in an ice cold lake, that is technically warmer than the air outside, is so much worse?

    Turbotheworld A:

    Water conducts heat better than air. So when you jump in the water it is taking heat away from your body way faster than air would.

    Mecxs A:

    In cold environments, heat is transferred from your warm body to the cold air/water around you. That transfer happens when the molecules of your skin collide with the molecules of the air/water. Each collision robs some of your heat.

    The air is colder, so robs more heat per collision. But it's also MUCH thinner, so there are fewer collisions. Water is a bit warmer, but has far more collisions robbing heat, because the water is hundreds of times more dense than the air.

    It's the same with cooking. To cook a chicken breast, you can put it in boiling water at 100 C, or an oven at 200 C (or more!). The oven, despite being hotter, usually takes longer to cook the chicken. Again, that's due to the fact that the air is less dense, and so there are fewer collisions, and therefore heat transfers more slowly.

    Our bodies don't actually feel a temperature, it feels a sense of heat gain or heat loss.

    Air isn't very good at absorbing heat from things so you don't feel as cold as quickly

    Water on the other hand is a great conductor of heat so it takes the heat away from your body really quickly.

    Fisicas A:

    While density and conductivity are factors, the most important consideration is water’s heat capacity (also called specific heat). Water has a heat capacity of 4.186J/K•g while air has a heat capacity of ~1J/K•g. This means it takes less water to steal the same amount of heat from your body as air.
