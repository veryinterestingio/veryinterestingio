    zoinkability A:

    This is how a doc described it to me (I get super hungry a couple hours after eating, probably a mild hypoglycemia):

    When you eat food, it gets broken down into glucose. Your body then produces insulin, which prompts your cells to take up the glucose and bring your blood sugar back down to the proper level. If your body (like mine) keeps making insulin a bit longer than needed, your blood sugar goes lower than the ideal, making you feel super hungry.

    If you eat at this point, you start again and the cycle continues.

    Alternately, if you power through the hunger your body will stop making insulin, your blood sugar levels will stabilize, and you won't feel starving again until you are "really" hungry -- that is, the readily available sugars in your cells are genuinely depleted.
