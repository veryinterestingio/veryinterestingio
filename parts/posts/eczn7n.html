    jussumguy2019 A:

    The emotional stress of the breakup causes the body to release a flood of catecholamines, like norepinephrine and epinephrine, cortisol is also released and can act as a potentiator to these hormones, essentially making cells hyper responsive to them. These hormones predominantly effect the heart by whipping it to work harder (beat stronger and faster) and making it harder to work (constricting blood vessels, including at times the blood vessels that feed the heart itself). This can be so severe at times that it leads to heart failure, heart attacks, and arrhythmias. Takatsubo cardiomyopathy is the disease that results from severe emotional trauma’s effects on the heart, also called broken heart syndrome.
