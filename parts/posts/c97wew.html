    Nobody has stated the technical answer yet.

    Prolactin.

    This is the chemical on the flip side of the dopamine coin.

    Dopamine is often associated with pleasure(location+receptors type in the brain determines it's function), but it has a bigger role: motivation. Dopamine increases the 'wanting' of something. Food, sex, movement, etc.

    Prolactin serves as the brakes. Once you ejaculate, prolactin is released and you no longer want it as much (in this case, sex. You might want food, which is controlled by dopamine in other parts of the brain along with other complex hormonal systems).

    There are theories as to why men and women differ in the dopaminergic/prolactin response.
