    Q:

    ELI5: How come people who stutter have difficulty saying some words but say other words with ease?

    trenchcoatler A:

    I stutter and the one answer my therapist has: Nobody knows. Sometimes I can talk all day without trouble. Sometimes some specific words are hard for me. Some days I can barely speak and every second word gets stuck in my throat.

    It's also not black and white what triggers a stutter. I can be nervous as balls giving a presentation to a room full of people and I talk just fine just as I can be relaxed at home and not get out a single word talking to my girlfriend.

    It is very, very weird. Symptons differ from stutterer to stutterer. I have problems with explosive words, those that start with your lips closed (mother, pen, bridge) or those that start with a vocal (auto, open). Some others may have problems with words starting with s or ch.

    However. To every stutterer out there: There is no cure, you have to accept it and to live with it. You dramatically ovetestimate how much it bothers other people. They usually dont care and will have forgotten you and your face like the other 200 people they saw that day. Carry your stutter out and don't hide or avoid it. Nobody gives a shit and so shouldn't you either.

    My stutter came in at age 16 when reading along in a play in class. I chose a small part, and when it was my turn to read aloud I stopped on the word noble. Couldn't get the word out no matter what. My friends were concerned for me because up until that point I never stuttered.

    Also, I stutter when reading numbers mostly with nines and eights. At meetings when I say 1800, and start to stutter I have to say one thousand eight hundred, or 200 away from from 2000.
