    NEW
    Q:

    How do harvester ants prevent the seeds they harvest from sprouting and destroying their cache chambers?

    FillsYourNiche A:

    It seems to vary by species:

    • With some species they don't bother and remove sprouting seeds from the nest (Carroll, 1973; O'Dowd and Hay, 1980; MacKay, 1981).
    • Messor barbarus destroys the radicle of intact seeds (Moggridge 1873; Bequaert, 1922). The seed radicle is the first part of the seed that germinates (Britannica).
    • Pheidole diffusa, Pheidole providens and Solenopsis geminata dry damp seeds outside (Sykes, 1835; Jerdon, 2009)
    • Pogonomyrmex occidentalis and Pogonomyrmex badius use depth within the nest to stop germination (Lavigne, 1969 Tschinkel, 1999; Tschinkel, 199a)
    • It's been floated seed density also prevents germination (Inouye, 1980; Grundy and Burston, 2003). Though after nest abandonment or failure some seeds do germinate (MacMahon et al., 2001)

    Tschinkel and Kwapich (2016) have a great review of Pogonomyrmex badius which covers everything I've mentioned and a little more, we use some of the same references. I'd give it a read.
