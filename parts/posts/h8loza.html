    1kschmeckles A:

    Screaming is an extreme manifestation of the fight or flight response, which is a series of mechanisms our body unchains when in danger or distress, triggered by the hormones adrenalin and cortisol, among others.

    When we feel we have run out of options to overcome a dangerous threat, screaming is the last resort, its goal being either alerting a fellow congener so they can help us survive or hoping the strident noise will scare off or deter the predator.
