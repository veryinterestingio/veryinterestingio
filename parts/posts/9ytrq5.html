• How does the entire city of NYC not have bed bugs?

    NEW
    Q:

    I live in NYC, and ever since having my own bed bug scare over the summer and doing tons of research, it blows my mind how bed bugs have not infected the entire city.

    Of course I know it's not exactly bed bug free, but knowing just how easily they spread and how much contact people are having with each other every day, I just don't see how it's possible that anywhere is safe. Thousands, honestly millions ride the public transport every day - surely some of these people are carrying bed bugs. Surely they hop onto others. Thousands to millions of people going to movie theatres, restaurants, Broadway shows - how are all these places not infested, and therefore not infesting everybody else who visits? I'm just wondering if there is any explanation.

    newtypexvii17 A:

    So I live and am a real estate agent in NYC. One thing I can assure you is that no matter how shitty the landlord they take bedbugs very seriously. The loss of potential rent and fines if they do not attend to the matter immediately scares them enough to action. Also when ever you rent an apartment they have to disclose any bedbug issues and confirm eradication if it happened in the last year or so.
