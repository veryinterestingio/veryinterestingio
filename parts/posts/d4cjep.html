    dobbs_head A:

    If you think about it, absolute pressure doesn’t crush people. Differential pressure crushes people.

    Think about diving. You can dive pretty deep (w/ gear) and survive just fine under a lot of absolute pressure. You are just pushed from all directions, so it balances out.*

    But if you get a barrel of water dropped on you, it can kill you while exerting significantly less pressure. It’s the pressure differential that crushes you.

    So for bacteria and the like, mostly if you step on them they experience it like a quick dive. The fluid around and in them pressurizes, but doesn’t shear or crush them.

    *Surviving absolute pressure increases has a limit. Your proteins only function properly in a specific range of absolute pressure, so you can only dive so deep. Gas transport in your blood also doesn’t work correctly at too high a pressure.
