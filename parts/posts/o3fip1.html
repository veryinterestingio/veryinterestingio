    Eli5: why is space so dark if the Suns in space?

    Heerrnn A:

    Imagine shining a flashlight up into the sky at night. It will still look pretty dark. Now imagine holding a sheet of paper in front of the flashlight. It will be super bright.

    Space itself can't reflect the light from the sun. Hope this was a simple yet understandable explanation!

    dobbykins A:

    The sun is actually very bright in space, but only if you're looking straight at it. The reason that it is "bright" from every direction on Earth is that sunlight is scattered by the atmosphere, so it can reach you indirectly from every angle. There is no atmosphere in space, so only direct light reaches you.
