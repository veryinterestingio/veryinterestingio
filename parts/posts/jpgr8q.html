    TheASCIItype A:

    You would see equal numbers of electrons flowing from and returning to the source, out from the negative and into the positive wires, respectively. They hop across valence orbitals through a lattice of metal atoms that make up the wires. They also move surprisingly slowly - the rate at which electric pressure is felt on the other end of a wire is very fast, but the electrons themselves only move around 10mm/sec.

    Inside the battery you would see electrons hopping off one electrode and onto a molecule, destabilizing it and causing part of it to rearrange in a fashion that transports an electron through the electrolyte. Generally speaking, charging the battery would have the same effect but in the opposite direction, re-forming the original molecules in the electrolyte.
