• Physics How would a compass needle react to a magnetic monopole?

    NEW
    Q:

    Would an observer be able to tell the difference? How?

    lmxbftw A:

    A compass needle follows the field lines where it is, it's a local measurement only. It can't tell you the global shape of the field in a single measurement. A compass needle doesn't know whether it's in a field generated by a monopole, a dipole, a quadrapole, or whatever moment you want to pick. It just says "the field line right here points this way". To tell the global shape of the field, you would need to take several such measurements in different places, at which point you would be able to tell if you had a monopole or a dipole by the curvature of field lines.

    It's worth noting that magnetic monopoles that you could walk around with a compass to measure field lines don't exist in nature.
