    Q:

    ELI5 Why is every book I pickup "The New York’s times 1 bestseller. It’s like all of them have that

    FargoPelz A:
    1. The New York Times now maintains eighteen different best seller lists, and there are even separate lists for hardcover releases (more popular, more publisher investment) and paperback releases (less popular books still get on a list, and only have to compete with other less popular books).
    2. In order to use the mark "New York Times Bestseller", a book only has to exist on a list once, at any position, at any time. Getting on the list is more about marketing for a single spike in book purchases all at once than about sales in aggregate.
    3. Your book must be released in the US to qualify for the list, so English books published in other nations are excluded. If you purchase books in the US they are more likely to be from a NYT list.
    4. If a book is on a New York Times bestselling list, it is probably heavily marketed to you both before and after this accomplishment, meaning you are much more likely to pick that book than a random book.
