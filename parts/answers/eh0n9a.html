Question: are you sure that the women really have very thick legs, or is it just that they seem thick because they’re so much thicker than the upper half of their body? Most elite runners I know are pretty damn skinny. The less excess body fat, the less weight they have to drag along with them in the race. However, women genetically have more body fat in their thighs and hips than men do. This will be present in women no matter how slender they are, unless they get so skinny that they are unhealthy, which would make them slower and then they wouldn’t be elite runners. But these same women won’t have excess body fat in their upper body—there’s not the same genetic predisposition for that. They also will have less muscle than a man in their upper body. The net result is that elite women runners will have thinner upper bodies than elite men, and larger hips and thighs than elite men, making their legs looks relatively thicker than elite men’s legs, and even disproportionate as compared with thin women who are not athletes.
