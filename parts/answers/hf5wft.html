Charyou-Tree A:

Your sinus cavities are connected to your nose and mouth by narrow channels. If those channels get blocked, pressure can build up in the sinuses causing pain. If you increase the pressure in your nose by "blowing" with it plugged, you can force a little air through the blockage, opening the channel, and allowing the pressure built up in the sinus to release. However, it can also make things worse if you manage to force even more air in there that also gets stuck and just adds to the pressure.
