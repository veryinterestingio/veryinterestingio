These are actually all generally wrong. Artists can generally chose to release to apps like Tik Tok and instagram (my record label allows me to mark those selections, or choose to unmark them). I’m assuming labels have deals with those apps for previously released songs and everything as well, so they still make royalties. You can still get a copyright strike if you post a song as an actual post, not a story (as that’s run through Spotify).

TLDR- Tik Tok and music on Instagram stories are already monetized by the labels. Places where you create content independently from the app and use music are not monetized by default and result in copyright strikes for that reason.
