SlickMcFav0rit3 A:

Normal mRNAs in your cell have something called a poly A tail, a string of adenines that are added during the maturation of the molecule.

The general consensus is that the tail is between 100-200 As long. When the mRNA is set to be degraded, it goes through a tail shortening process until it's 10-15 As long and is then (usually) sent to be degraded.

Any RNA floating around in the cell without a poly A tail won't get turned into protein. Viruses obviously want their mRNAs turned into protein so they either genetically encode the tail or use some other trick to get translated into protein.

SARS2, like most coronaviruses, is using the strategy of genetically encoding its tail.
