Singular_Thought A:

It is very unlikely we could ever deplete the supply of titanium.

https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titanium

Titanium is the ninth-most abundant element in Earth's crust (0.63% by mass)[22] and the seventh-most abundant metal. It is present as oxides in most igneous rocks, in sediments derived from them, in living things, and natural bodies of water.[5][6] Of the 801 types of igneous rocks analyzed by the United States Geological Survey, 784 contained titanium. Its proportion in soils is approximately 0.5 to 1.5%.[22]

I can speak for the FnB industry in some measure where substitutes are being sought and used. My hunch is that if other industries find substitutes, running out of TiO2 wouldn't affect as much.
