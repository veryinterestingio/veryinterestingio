dukesdj A:

Mars is not thought to have a fully solidified core. It is just that it is stably stratified to convection (there is debate as to how). There is a theory that the Martian dynamo may turn back on (basically this assumes that there is a small temperature gradient through the core but as the surface cools this gradient increases thus becoming unstable to convection). So it is possible we dont need to do anything other than wait and Mars will get a magnetic field again.

jswhitten A:

Probably not, but it wouldn't be necessary anyway. If we terraformed Mars, its atmosphere would last at least a hundred million years, so there's no danger of it losing its atmosphere on any time scale that we care about. Also, while a magnetic field might reduce atmosphere loss due to sputtering, there's reason to believe that loss is negligible. Even if Mars had a magnetic field like Earth's, it would lose atmosphere nearly as fast as it does now.
