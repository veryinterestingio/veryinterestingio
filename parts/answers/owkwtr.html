Not meaningfully for most decay modes. Radioactive decay occurs in the nucleus which only impacts electron orbital levels on the hyperfine level and vice versa. This would be a miniscule impact as can be seen by comparing the hyperfine structure to the energy of nuclear decay. The exception to this would be the electron capture mode of decay where an electron must be available to fall into the nucleus.

RobusEtCeleritas A:

For decays which don't involve the atomic electrons (everything except electron capture, internal conversion, and bound-state beta decay), it won't have any effect on the half-life.

For decay modes that do involve the electrons, then in principle the difference in electron wavefunctions can influence the half-life for the decay.

Most notably, if a given nuclide can only decay via electron capture, if you remove all of its electrons, it can't decay.
