Lets say you're stressing about your weight. You spend a good portion of your day thinking about how you should lose weight, how you've been putting it off, how it's going to get worse if you don't do something about it now, etc. This primes your brain on the topic.

You meet up with your friend Phil for lunch. He makes a decadent menu selection and mentions: "I know I should be watching my weight, but I'm going to treat myself this once." Since your brain was already thinking about all the reasons why you should lose weight, it's rather easy to rattle them all off on Phil. In fact, Phil is a much easier target to vent those frustrations on since telling someone what needs to be done is worlds easier than doing so yourself.

As another example: You're cheating on your spouse. Because of this, you're extra mindful about all the techniques for hiding an affair. Your spouse then announces she's going out with her friends later, and this makes you more suspicious than you would be otherwise because you thought of using that exact excuse to cover up your cheating.

Mental priming is one possible pathway for projection to occur. I understand it would be rather difficult to test if this is in fact the case, but it's what I find most plausible.

I think it's worth noting that the thought "This bad thing I'm doing must actually be pretty common" helps sooth guilt over doing the bad thing and create the perception that the activity is more prevalent than it actually is.

There's only one person on the planet whose head you can look into; yourself. Now, imagine you observe certain internal factors manifesting themselves in certain ways in your behaviour. Now, imagine further you see others display that behaviour. If you don't have any deeper knowledge on all the factors that might be in play when a person displays that specific behaviour or simply don't think about it too hard, naturally, the most obvious assumption would be 'must be for the same reason as with me'.

You only draw conclusions about the world from things you know and, if you're a chronic cheater and your spouse suddenly starts acting all secretive, what do you think what possible explanation would come to your mind most immediately? Probably not that they must be planning a birthday surprise.

I think it's a mistake, however, to assume that all forms of projection must always be direct results of the 'projector', if you will, trying to deny a flaw in themself or to distract from it. It's really just an outgrowth of our constant efforts to understand others by checking against the database of our own experiences.

Projection doesn't even always necessarily involve any negative traits at all.

Say someone tweets about their mother having passed away and receives messages of condolences. But, as it turns out, it was more of a celebration tweet than one done in mourning since the mother in question was an abusive piece of shit. There you have an example of the exact same psychological mechanism. The person going "so sorry about your mother. thoughts and prayers" simply inferred from their own experiences what is most likely. Or, more simply put, they projected their own love for their own mother onto this other person who they had no reason to think doesn't share that particular trait.
