The vagus nerve, one of 12 cranial nerves, runs from the brain all the way down through the thorax. It's name essentially means "wanderer," as it has many branches that innervate many parts of the body, which is kind of strange, right? Why is a cranial nerve coming directly down from the brain and traveling into the chest? Idk about the evolutionary relevance, but the observation is that this nerve has a lot of cortical input, meaning it's firing is based heavily on how we are feeling and thinking. So yeah, you feel a sensation in your chest, and you also feel a lump in your throat because the vagus has a branch that straight up just turns around and decides to go back up the body and innervate your larynx (voicebox)
