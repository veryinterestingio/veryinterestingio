Ghosttwo A:

Not necessarily. Consider that boiling waste in a closed container or irradiating it sufficiently would yield the expected smell, but there would be no life. Smells typically come from molecules with a couple dozen atoms, while microbes will be made from billions if not trillions of atoms, all in a generally specific configuration. Antiseptics can damage the microbes quite effectively while leaving the scents unscathed. That said, cleaning to the point that there's no smell is a better indicator that most of the microbes have been destroyed.
