Antiperspirants use aluminum salts, which react with your sweat and basically form a gel-like substance that physically plugs up your sweat glands. That's the white goo that you will see in your underarms if you use antiperspirant. You eventually either wash it off or it will slough off with your dead skin cells over time.

As far as the sweat that cannot make it out of the blocked pore, it just gets reabsorbed into the body.
