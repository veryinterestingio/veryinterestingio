PotatoPog87 A:

On the way there is all new experiences. Your brain is paying attention and makes a note to remember it.

On the way back you’re rehashing stuff so your brain is more on autopilot and only logs things you didn’t see on the way out.

Repeat the route often enough and your brain barely bothers to register that you made the trip - see “highway hypnosis” where people can safely complete a journey by car and at the end of it have virtually no memory of anything that happened on the trip. Once your brain is familiar with the route it just ignores the specifics of each journey unless there’s new information that important or majorly out of the ordinary - if there’s a forced detour because a road is closed that you need to know for the next time, or if a semi truck has a spectacular crash. That sort of thing. But uneventful familiar journeys - not worth the neurons.
