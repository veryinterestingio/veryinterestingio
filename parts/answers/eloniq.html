Think of it this way: ever since you were a child, you were experimenting with the way you interact with others - when something works, you get a reward -, think of a child who cries and gets a candy bar to stop crying. Eventually that kid will learn that crying = candy. Now, apply that same logic to more complex social dynamics: You do someone a favor, you get something back. That person's friend doesn't like it, you lose some. Eventually that becomes "person has friends = loss". So after a life time of similar experiences, you start feeling entitled to your rewards, which makes you ignore the harm you're causing through a false sense of fairness and reciprocity. The factors that can keep you from getting that reward are usually the same, so the "abuse tactics" people develop will end up being the same as well, since they work best.
