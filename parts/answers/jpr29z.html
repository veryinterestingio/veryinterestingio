Male homo sapiens unlike other apes have androgen sensitive hair.

Basically our hair follicles are sensitive to male hormones. That is why in puberty we start to grow beards. The hair follicles on our lower faces get the hormones and become active.

The paradox is that after puberty the follicles on the scalp become sensitive to androgens but they become dormant instead of active. The exact rate and location of the dormancy depends on your specific genes but generally it's from the front and crown on what's called male pattern baldness.

Loss from all over simultaneously is rarer and not male pattern, this could be genetic or environmental.
