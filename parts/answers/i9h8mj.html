Well, a sunburn is basically damaged skin by UV light that becomes inflamed due to the damaged cells. And as always, when something is inflamed, it's warmer than the rest of the body, caused by the small blood vessels in the tissue that become dilated, allowing more blood to reach the injured tissue. Hence why it becomes red and warm, a lot more blood flows through the area during the healing process.
