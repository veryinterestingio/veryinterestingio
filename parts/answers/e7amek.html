The sinuses and nasal passages drain during the day when you're upright. When you lie down, blood and fluids go more towards your head throughout the night, making you more congested. A cold = more mucous production, so more in your nasal passages. Add dehydration to the mix (since you're not drinking all or most of the night) and mucous thickens, making it more...unpleasant.

Alextangfastic A:

As it gets darker outsider / later in the day, your body has a lower level of a hormone cortisone and a higher level of the hormone melatonin. Cortisone suppresses inflammation. As the levels of cortisone in the body decrease the cold and flu symptoms tend to worsen, because the suppression is reduced. Most illness that is the result of inflammation will tend to have symptoms that worsen over night and "get better" during the day.
