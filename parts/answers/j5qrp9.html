To those saying you don't experience it, you likely do. It's similar to the "falling sensation" when you're falling asleep and your body jerks up. It's in the same category of involuntary muscle movements called myoclonus. Some people have it happen when wide awake. There really aren't any external triggers.

Perfectly healthy individuals experience it all the time, and is just a random firing of motor neurons from signals in the nervous system. You can think of it like a random little spasm. Sometimes it involves more muscles than others, but it all links back to a central nervous system "misfire" thus the slight tingling feeling when the contraction occurs.
