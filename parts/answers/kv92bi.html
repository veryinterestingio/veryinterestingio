alose A:

To provide a little more information.: The servers are called SMSC. At least the one I supported had Web and application front end servers with a database backend. All SMSgoes through the SMSC and looks for routing information the final destination of the SMS. If it is not handled locally, it gets handed off to a clearing house Verisign. If it is handled locally, the SMSC communicates with the telco switch to see if the phone is available, if yes, then the SMS gets handed off and delivered tot he phone. If not, then the SMSC will try again later.

So until the SMS is delivered, it lives in a database.

Also, while most of the infrastructure of the SMSC was standard servers, we did have specialized network bridges to communicate over the Telco SS7 network directly to the Telco switch. A Telco switch is the modern implementation of the old telephone switch boards you see in old movies.
