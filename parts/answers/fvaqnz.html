Humans and animals do, to a large extent. Egg laying chickens, for example, have been shown to effectively regulate their calcium intake - if provided with regular feed and limestone (a calcium source), they will eat the regular feed during the day and the calcium in the evening, which is when their body needs it the most to form the shell of the egg they lay in the morning.

There have been studies utilising what’s called geometric framework analysis which show that humans eat to satisfy their protein requirement - so you will keep on eating until you have the protein your body needs, regardless of the carbohydrates and fats (and hence energy) that come with it. Your body also craves rich energy sources - sugars, easily digestible starches, and fats. This in combination with a sodium appetite (which I think all vertebrates have) can explain why food like potato chips can be so addictive.

I think the more you pay attention to what you eat and notice how it makes you feel, the better you will be at identifying what your body needs. Educating yourself on the nutrient values of food and what different nutrients do for you might also help - although it’s hard to tell if that’s a bit of a placebo type effect or not.
