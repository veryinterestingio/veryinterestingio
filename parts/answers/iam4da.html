Petting is just slightly different grooming behavior which is basic social mammal behavior to keep your coat clean. It also feels good, social animals like being touched by members of their group and also have stimulus responses like how it feels good to have your face or neck stroked by you partner. It's an important part of bonding in basically every social mammal.

I can tell you about goats.

When a baby goat nurses, its mother nuzzles its back just ahead of its tail. The baby goat's tail wags in response.

I have raised a lot of baby goats on bottles. These four were orphans:

https://i.imgur.com/NxjwAj7.jpg
