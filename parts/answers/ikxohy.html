The minimum age was lightly debated (there is also minimum ages for house and senate members) and in the end the writers of the Constitution enacted them without too much dissent.

The general for and against the min age arguments aren’t very insightful or interesting either. Basically some argued that they don’t want to stop a person from holding the role due to their younger age, and that young people can and have excelled in high raking govt roles, others said they want this person to have had experience gained over time as a person in the US and skills from being older. The older crowd won out.

To add on, minimum ages for parliament was a thing in England at the time (21) so setting a minimum age was a pretty normal idea that was already in use elsewhere in similar systems.

There was no discussion of a maximum age only a minimum so there is nothing to say on that.
