It’s because of the hormone cortisol. Your body releases more cortisol than usual when under stress. When you don’t sleep, your body releases cortisol to deal with the tiredness, so you still maintain mental clarity and can get through your day. Cortisol effects last the entire day, about 12 hours, and can make you moody, hungry and anxious. It can disrupt your sleep cycle and mood for days.

Applejuiceinthehall A:

It could be because of when you are waking up. Sleep is controlled by two systems, the sleep-wake homeostasis and the circadian rhythm. The sleep-wake is pretty simple the longer you have been awake the sleeper you get and the longer you have been asleep the less sleep you get. The circadian rhythm uses hormones to keep you awake when you start to get sleepy because you have been awake too long and it keeps you asleep when you have been sleeping too long.

Generally, a typical rhythm has adults being most sleep between 2 am and 4 am and 3pm to 4pm. If you are an early bird/night owl subtract/add an hour or so (only about 20% of people are true night owls or early birds). If you are a young adult or teenager then your sleepiest times are longer and later too.

If you woke up during these sleepy times that can make you groggy but that should go away after a hour or two. If it doesn't then you probably aren't getting enough sleep over all or you might have an underlying issue like a thyroid issue.
