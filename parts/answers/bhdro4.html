Gazpacho_Catapult A:

People have already discussed the variation in "imagination", I'll simply chime in to point out that the brain is highly modular. The part of your brain responsible for processing visual information is completely distinct from the part responsible for comprehending words. So reading while creating imagery is possible because the two functions are being performed by different areas.

As for why we are able to do it, self-defense. If we can visualise a dangerous situation and experience it hypothetically, we can develop a better defense, should we ever encounter it, as well as understand and predict the various outcomes; should I fight or should I flee?

Tedonica A:

Not everyone gets this sensation. It has to do with your ability to see images and recall them afterwards, even being able to manipulate them in your mind. This ability is known as spatial intelligence, and it's something everyone posesses to some degree. Some people are more naturally gifted at it than others, and it can be honed and trained.

I personally have a very strong spatial intelligence and have sometimes even confused my memories of a book for memories of a movie. Another interesting personal anecdote about spatial intelligence is that it can be expanded into four or five dimensions, although with much difficulty as there is nothing one can observe that would hone that ability.

For more reading: https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spatial_intelligence_(psychology)
