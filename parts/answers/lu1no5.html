beingtall A:

There are maybe 6 genes that are only found in humans. That's about 0.002% of the human genome. But the answer is more complicated than that. What makes us human is not just the genes but how they are regulated. We share 98-99% genes with chimps but when looked closely the genomes are all filled with structural rearrangements. This results in a very different regulatory system in both. So the actual similarity may be 60-70%.
