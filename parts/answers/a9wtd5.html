Even if you have sunblock on every three hours and wear a hat, the water reflections get under the hat and you usually get some measure of mild sunstroke after four hours or so. The biggest impacts are top of the head where you don't usually apply sunblock, the back of the neck, the forehead and the front of the neck down to chest.

If you're in the water, you often don't even . notice the amount of sun affecting you.
