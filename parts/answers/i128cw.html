TommmyThumb A:

Alcohol tricks receptors in your skin into releasing the same Nerochemical trigger as when you are being burned. So your body reacts by telling you it hurts. Alcohol actually doesn’t make a wound heal faster. It can damage the tissue and cause the wound to heal more slowly. However, the alcohol will disinfect the wound, so if the wound was dirty it may be a matter of the alcohol slows the healing to a lesser degree than the bacteria would have and gives the appearance of speeding up the process. It is not recommended to put alcohol or hydrogen peroxide on a wound though.
