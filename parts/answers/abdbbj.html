At microwave wavelengths you get naturally occurring masers in dying stars and around supermassive black holes. They also occur in solar system objects, but I'm less familiar with those. They are hugely important for calibrating the distance scale of the universe, and gave provided one of the most accurate rungs on the cosmic distance ladder.
