There's no one singular source of a definitive answer, but the sign itself is called the "Cuckoo sign".

Cuckoo is acknowledged as a slang for crazy, going back to the 1900s America, seemingly because of the sound the bird makes. -- source: https://www.etymonline.com/word/cuckoo

​

Similarly, the notion of "having a screw loose" equates to being crazy, born from textile machines in the 1700s not operating properly if they have a loose screw -- source: https://www.bloomsbury-international.com/student-ezone/idiom-of-the-week/1278-have-a-screw-loose/

​

There's no well documented evidence on how these idioms got paired up specifically with the hand gesture itself, but that seems to be the origins.

​

Anecdotally, I had a psychology professor who told us that the gesture came about due to excessive hair twirling being a common activity of people diagnosed with various mental illness, but I was never able to find a source which supported that theory.
