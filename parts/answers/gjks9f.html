Pistro A:

In part, it has to do with the fact that a twisted position is a position in which there is the least amount of tension between muscle pairs (e.g. biceps and triceps). It's obvious with small muscle pairs, for example your fingers always curl when relaxed, but it's not so obvious with large muscle pairs and the reason is that with small pairs tendon tension overcomes the force of gravity and forces of friction even in a relaxed state, which doesn't happen with large pairs except for some specific situations. For example, you can observe it while being underwater where the force of gravity is negated by buoyancy, and you can also see it when watching astronauts on the International Space Station which is in a perpetual freefall.
