Physical geographer here. The changes would be much smaller in Scotland compared to the tropics. Precipitation is entirely driven by the westerlies transporting moist air to you. However, runoff would decrease because trees increase what's called evapotranspiration, which would mean reduced risk of flooding. I don't remember the numbers but it's in the general area of 10-15%, which is a lot.

Temperatures would become more stable and generally slightly cooler, but probably just a few degrees.

You also have to remember that the effects are mostly local (except runoff ofc. which is important for everything downstream), on the order of kilometers I think.

By far the most important benefit of reforestation would be ecological as the heathlands are extremely poor.
