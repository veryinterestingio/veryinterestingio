malatropism A:

When a gas is hot, it’s molecules are vibrating/moving around super quickly. When a cooler gas is introduced, the hot gas wants to share its energy with the cold one, so the hot gas encourages the colder gas’ molecules to vibrate faster. The hot gas will vibrate slower because of this, but all the gas wants to be the same temperature/energy level.

With the rapid change in vibration speed, some of that gas gets compressed together. Gas doesn’t like to be compressed, so the molecules move away from the source of the pressure. Several layers of compressed gas will do this very quickly, which creates the ‘sound waves’ you’re familiar with.
