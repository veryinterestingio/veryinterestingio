curiousmadscientist A:

The Evaporation rate is expressed in mm/day, and ranges from ~1mm a day to ~110 mm/day (for worst conditions, warm water with cold air). This linked article is for a pond, but works as a rule of thumb, IMO

Lets assume 10mm/day (from example calc below) and multiply by area as required. 

1 mileSq = 2.58999 kmSq 

Evaporation over 1 mileSq = 2.58999 * 10^6  m^2  * ( 0.010 m )

                                   = 25890m^3 per day. 

                                   = ~25,890,000 Liters of Water per day or ~6,839,000 Gallons of Water per day

The paper linked below explains the influencing factors, and gives us the equation: 

E = 0.0331 V (e – es) [1 – 0.03 (T – Tw)] 24 h day-1 (1)

where E is evaporation rate (mm/day);

V is the wind speed in the lowest 0.5 m above the ground (km/h),

e is vapor pressure (the equation is setup to use the English system unit mm Hg),

es is saturation vapor pressure,

T is the atmospheric temperature above the water surface in C, and

Tw is the water temperature in C.

http://tornado.sfsu.edu/Geosciences/classes/e260/Evaporation_From_Water_Bodies/EvaporationFromOcean.pdf

An example (From the paper) to show range:

Suppose the vapor pressure is 15 mm Hg,

saturation vapor pressure is 15 mm Hg,

wind speed is 25/km h,

air temperature is 29 C,

and water temperature is 27 C. Here's a global oceans water surface temp map, so you can check for your location

Inserting these values into Equation (1) (leaving off units since they cancel) gives E = 0.0331 X 25 (30 – 15) [1 – 0.03 (29 – 27)] 24 h day-1 = 11.66 mm/day
