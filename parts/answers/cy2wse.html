IminaNYstateofmind A:

It really depends on what lifestyle changes youre referring to. Smoking? Quitting smoking at any age drastically improves lung function within days-months. The risk of cancer from the time smoking remains, however. Heavy drinking? The liver can recuperate. The risk of oral/throat cancer remains. Exercise? Your heart will thank you. However, for some people with severe heart issues, exercise can actually kill them. Someone in their 30s typically would be fine starting an exercise regimen. To my knowledge, exercise will not reverse the damage done to the heart’s blood supply (coronary arteries), although it will prevent plaque buildup and improve the heart’s supply of oxygen overall. Eating better? Losing weight will prevent so many diseases including diabetes, high blood pressure, etc. Mental health? Always a good thing to address to prevent further damage, but years of a stressful life will have certainly taken its toll on the body. In summary, it is never too late to make changes. Some things cannot be undone, so the earlier you make changes the better!
