TakingCareOfBiscuits A:

Most of these old films were hand-cranked at around 10 or 15 fps to save money on film. Modern broadcasters intentionally speed them up to 24 fps or 30 fps so we don't see the jerky motion, and they figure the Benny Hill chase style animation is an acceptable compromise.

Thanks to high tech frame interpolation tools (SVP is one that works on desktop) it's possible to reconstruct the correct frames in between to simulate 30 fps, or even higher, so if you look around on YouTube you can find HQ high frame rate videos constructed from 10 fps movies that look "normal".

That said, some older movies were in fact cranked at much slower speeds as artistic or industrial experiments. I don't know where they are, but they do exist. The single one I know of is a WWII D-Day landing video produced for Winston Churchill or FDR (one of the two) that was intentionally filmed at 48 fps so they could get a much higher quality showing back in the 1940s, and it looks amazing. This video is on YouTube at its original 48 fps frame rate.
