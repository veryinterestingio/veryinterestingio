That_Biology_Guy A:

I'm not sure how universally this can be applied across all birds, but there are certainly some species with clear evidence of favouritism. In fact, in some cases this extends beyond just preferential feeding to active infanticide. See Zielínski 2002 on storks for example, or this video (warning: not really graphic, but potentially disturbing). In other species, there is often harsh competition between offspring for food, which again may rise to the level of siblicide (see a review by Mock et al. 1990). This is actually the norm in masked boobies, which lay two eggs but only have one offspring reach maturity, as can be seen in this rather overdramatized video. The evolutionary dynamics of this system are quite interesting, and I recommend checking out some of the work on it by Anderson 1990a and 1990b. So in summary, there are definitely at least some species where parents are perfectly willing to sacrifice one offspring to help the others, though there may be exceptions where this is never observed too.

Natter_Jack01 A:

It isn’t nice to find an abandoned nest. Birds often abandon nests if they feel threatened, eg if they see predators nearby. Some birds breed in pairs, either for the breeding season or until one dies. If one dies they have to form a new pair and start a new nest as they can’t feed the chicks alone, their next brood has a better chance. If food is a problem, they do feed the strongest offspring first. It sounds morbid but it is survival.

Westerdutch A:

Weaker birds will stop shouting for food before stronger ones when starved so they will get less food and die before their stronger siblings. This will continue until there's enough food to go around for the baby birds that are left.

Finding the entire contents of a nest dead under a tree all of a sudden is more likely the work of a playful neighborhood cat.

When female sharks have two offspring in the womb, one will more likely kill/consume the other. And I know of several breeds of dogs that will either shun certain offspring until the point of starvation and death, or down-right eat their young to ensure the best outcome of the other offspring. It kinda sucks and is morbid, but it makes sense, and is probably the reason that we humans exist today.
