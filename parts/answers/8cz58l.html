kouhoutek A:

Most jurisdictions have laws that allow people who witness serious crimes to use appropriate force to intervene. If you grapple someone running from a police officer, that is likely going to be ok. If you hit them in the face with a baseball bat, not so much.

Also, police officers enjoy what is called qualified immunity. If they make a mistake in the course of performing their duties, they are usually protected from civil repercussions. You, however, are not, and if an innocent bystander got hurt due to your actions, you could be civilly responsible.
