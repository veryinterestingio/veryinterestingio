Taira_Mai A:

A professor of mine (my advisor and chair of the Psyche department) said this "If you are in your bed and you are not sleeping, get out and do something else."

A flaw in our modern way of living is that we do things that make us associate the bed with not sleeping, things like:

  • reading in bed
  • watching TV
  • Cell phone use in bed

So if it's not anxiety, it's that you've (on a subconscious level) told yourself that bed =/= sleep but bed=other stuffs.

His advice to get out and do something else has worked for me. Another helper is to re-arrange your bedroom. If you have been anxious, ill or just had a lot on your mind, your bedroom now has "cues" that could trigger those memories and again no sleep. Until you conk out on the couch because it doesn't have those cues.

If you’ve something big in your life that’s taking up multiple days of consideration, you might lay in bed completely comfortably and still be devoting 50% of your brain energy towards it and now that thing is all you can think about.

Then you lie on the couch, and your body takes 20% of your attention and focuses it on parts of your body that are ‘uncomfortable’.

Now maybe your brain has something smaller it can focus on, and you can relax to the point where you’re asleep.
