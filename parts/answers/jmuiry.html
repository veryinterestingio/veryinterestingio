rammo123 A:

Ignoring the mental aspect of it, literally how you are seeing yourself can change. Is it a mirror reflection or a photo? Photo focal lengths can greatly affect your proportions, making you look uglier. Mirrors are naturally more representative of your looks. But on the other hand certain photographs can be very flattering, hiding parts of you you don't like, enhancing those that you do.

I'm fat, but I'm also very tall. I look OK in the mirror, because from my perception my broad shoulders are front and centre, and my belly is kinda hidden down low. In photos though it's reversed, because people are usually shorter, so are usually taking them slightly upwards from their perspective. This exaggerates my belly and makes me look bottom heavy.

Lighting also comes in to it. Are you squinting looking into the sun when you take the shot? Are looking down, creating a double chin?
