A:

Some great answers here already regarding the ‘pivot point in relation to the barrel and the hand’ but I would just like to add something about human anatomy.

Up is the direction your elbow naturally wants to bend if you are holding a hand gun in the normal way.

Hold your arm out straight like you are holding a pistol and see what direction your elbow naturally wants to bend without rotating.

For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction, or, bullet goes forward, gun goes back.

If the gun moves back with enough force, your elbow will either flex, or break, the path of least resistance is to bend at the elbow, which naturally moves the forearm and hand, up.

In some films, you may see the bad guy hold a gun rotated sideways instead of upright, in this case the elbow can’t flex upwards.

If you were ever to fire a gun like this in real life, it would not go up, it would follow the bend in your elbow and probably smack you in the face, don’t try this at home, or anywhere lol.

collin-h A:

Imagine you’re hanging upside down holding a gun. If you fire it it will kickback in the downward direction (down relative to gravity; not to your feet).

This is a clue.

The direction of kick back is influenced by how you’re holding it.

Generally, you’re holding it on the bottom side of the gun, so the kickback, while generally equal in all directions on it’s own, is influenced by the force you’re applying to it by holding it on the bottom side.

If you had a rifle and, instead of using your hand to hold the bottom of the barrel, you put your hand on the top of the barrel, the kickback wouldn’t be as strong in the upward direction.
