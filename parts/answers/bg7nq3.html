ChimTheCappy A:

The shortest answer is yes. Animals that hibernate do so in response to both shorter days and colder weather, so any hibernating animal with access to the outdoors and natural sunlight would feel an urge to put on weight, and then fall into a state of torpor, the sleepiness that most of us confuse for true hibernation. It's not a voluntary choice on their part, so unless you put them in an environment where they never experience seasons or weather, they would always get ready to hole up once fall rolls around.

Hope that helps!
