It’s actually a smell. When IV medication is injected, it travels through the blood stream to the lungs and some of the volatile components that are present, are breathed out. It’s the same as if someone hand injects air bubbles through an IV. As the air travels through the blood vessels in the lungs, during air exchange, the bubbles are breathed back out.

I can taste the heparin that is frequently added to saline flush syringes. My nurse compared it to people having shoulder pain during a heart attack. The brain is getting the message that something strange is happening and doesn't know quite how to tell us about it. I found that hard candy or gum masked the sensation easily.
