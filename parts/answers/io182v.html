Whales & dolphins evolved from land animals; they adapted their existing lungs to living in the water and that was good enough to allow them to survive with out evolving back to gills.

The fact that sharks & whales both exist show that neither gills nor lungs is strongly preferred for survival; lungs provide far more oxygen and allow whales & dolphins to sustain their warm-blooded metabolism and speed, gills don't provide enough for that but do allow continued submergence.

PhyterNL A:

About 55 million years ago a certain land mammal, very similar to a hippopotamus, began to make a living near and in the ocean. Over time, a period of about 5 million years, this species diverged and diverged again, evolving into a multitude of semi-aquatic mammals adept at swimming and making a living in the ocean. Fast forward another 10 million years and these species had diverged even further, evolving rudimentary flippers and fins. In total, over a period of 35 - 45 million years these hippopotamus-like mammals, who carved a niche out in ocean foraging, led to the evolution of modern whales that we know today. And that's why aquatic mammals breathe air, because they were once land mammals.

jxsn50st A:

Whales and dolphins are mammals, not fish. Their ancestors were land-dwelling mammals that eventually adapted to life at sea.

The fact that whales and dolphins look so similar to fish is an example of convergent evolution - basically organisms with very different ancestry evolving similar morphology, in this case fins and hydrodynamic bodies, for the same environment.
