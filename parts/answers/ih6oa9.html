Compared to the size of rocks in the earth's crust, human consumption isn't very big.

Total marble production is roughly 10 million tons per year, about 3.8 million m3. That's a cube of marble about 160m on a side. That's a big rock, but it's not even the size of a decent sized sports stadium, let alone a mountain. And we've literally got mountains of marble.

And the total production includes a lot of marble for applications outside of large dimensional mable like we use for tiles and countertops.

Marble is metamorphosed limestone, there's a lot of marble in the world, there are some entire mountains made of limestone

You're worried about counter tops so run the numbers. If we give everyone (even the children) their own 5 meter x 1 meter x 5 cm thick countertop so its a chonker then each countertop needs 0.25 m^3 of marble, times 7.7 billion people and you're at just 1.925 km^3 of marble. That's not even 2 full 1km x 1km x 1km cubes stacked on top of each other, that's a fairly small mountain if turned into a dome.

Its difficult to really conceive of the scale of parts of the Earth. All those countertops amount to 5.4x10^12 kg of marble, but even if we suppose that just 0.0001% of the Earth is marble then we only need to mine 0.0001% of the available marble to meet our needs of giving everyone a fresh countertop. The Earth clocks in around 6x10^24 kg which is really too big for your mind to grasp
