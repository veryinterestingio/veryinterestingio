Sharlinator A:

This is called pair production and can occur if the interacting photons have a center-of-momentum energy of at least 1022 keV (the mass of an (e^(-), e^(+)) pair). This means gamma rays, just like (e^(-), e^(+)) annihilation produces gamma ray photons.

One theorized astrophysical process that involves pair production is a so-called pair-instability supernova, where the energy density in the core of an extremely massive star becomes high enough for pair production to occur in large quantities. This is very bad news for the star and everything around it.
