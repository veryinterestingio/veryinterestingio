Arfamis A:

I did an essay on mongoose resistance to alpha-neurotoxins (a component that causes paralysis in some snake venoms, there are other components of venom that cause blood thinning, for example) and in the particular example of alpha-neurotoxins snakes and mongooses are both highly resistant because the post-synaptic receptors that alpha-neurotoxins attack in these animals have adapted to not bind to the toxin but still allow acetylcholine (the neurotransmitter) to bind normally.

So in short, at least in the case of paralysing snake venoms, a snake is resistant enough to not do notable damage to itself. There's a really diverse and wide range of venoms and toxins used in different snake species so I imagine some snakes are less resistant to their own venom than others.

EDIT: Although I should clarify that beyond a healthy interest in toxicology I am by no means an expert, so obviously please do correct me if anything I said is blatantly wrong.
