hgismercury A:

Like someone else said, it's something called desert pavement. It's when smaller grains are removed by wind and pebble to rock sized stuff gets left behind. The color difference happens because the top layer of particles receives all the sunlight, which causes color changes, called desert varnish. The Nazca lines are made by simply brushing away the varnished stones on the surface. Here's the other big point: they've been maintained over time. The same varnish process would change the color of the exposed material, so over the centuries people have gone out with brooms and exposed the lighter layer underneath.

Regarding the new find, slope processes (gravity driven) must have worked on the material that makes up the image, but I don't have an answer for how people have missed it for so long.
