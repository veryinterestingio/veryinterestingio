Yes, particularly if you are being intentional and specific about the word "eating" The result can be what's called refeeding syndrome

( https://www.medicalnewstoday.com/articles/322120.php ) where your body is unable to process or absorb nutrients properly. Since the kidneys shut down first, your ability to balance electrolytes is severely impaired.

Typically small amounts of sugar water are given first, then later if the starving individual begins recovering, solid food can be reintroduced.

Other than that, as far as I know there's no strict amount of time that you can measure by for how long any given individual can starve before they are unable to recover. Their individual vitals and conditions need to be monitored.
