Doctor here.

When trauma to the brain occurs, several things can happen.

The direct injury to the brain is called a coup injury. This is damage to the brain at the point of impact (the trauma to the head causes the brain to impact the skull).

You can also get a contrecoup injury - this is when the opposite side of the brain then bounces against the back of the skull.

Either of these injuries can damage brain tissue. This can be in the form of swelling of the tissue or of something called "diffuse axonal injury" - micro-shearing of nerve cells.

In any event , the injury to the central nervous system more or less "overloads" the system and higher level functions - like consciousness - shut down.

There's more to it than this, but this suffices as a basic explanation.

First I suggest reading the response by u/deecaf, it explains the impact related knockout.

There are also nerve-related effects depending on the location of the strike. Impact stimulation of the trigeminal nerve (see link for location in the face) can cause a drop in heart rate and changes in blood vessel constriction that cause fainting—and when you faint after getting punched, that’s a knockout.

This second effect is related to how the rear naked choke or triangle choke of MMA can cause a fighter to “go to sleep”. The target there is the vagus nerve and carotid sinus in the neck. The reflex effect of dropping the heart rate is the same, and combined with constriction of the neck — where blood is trying to get in and out of the head — is what causes the knockout.

Edit: Forgot to mention that disruption of the inner ear from impact is another cause of knockout.
