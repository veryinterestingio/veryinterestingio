Bonsai is basically two kanji meaning tree or plant and tray or pot. Any woody barked tree can have bonsai techniques applied. The goal is to give the tree a sense of age, and this is done by developing the root structure (nebari) the trunk, and ramification in the foliage.

To achieve this shape, techniques of wiring, pruning, and defoliation are used. One also wants to build the soil nutrition in the pot and promote beneficial bacteria and mycorrhiza.

In short, bonsai trees work just like their full sized counterparts but they are kept compact due to the restriction of the pot and the techniques applied. In fact, if I took a younger tree in a bonsai pot, and put it into the ground, it would grow to be a full sized tree.
