Xelopheris A:

Your bank immediately puts the money into a giant holding account to be transferred during the giant end of day batch transaction. The other bank will have to process the incoming money and put it in the appropriate accounts accordingly. As a result, the retailer doesn't actually see the money from your sale for several days.

When you do a refund, the same is true in reverse. The retailer will see their money disappear instantly, but it will be in a banking buffer for a little while until it gets deposited into your account.

The reason for all of this is that banks want to manage as few inter-bank transactions as possible. Having every $5 transaction between banks have its own link just adds a lot of room for error, and doesn't really add any value on the banks side.

kouhoutek A:

Money is removed from your account immediately, but it doesn't reach the vendor immediately. It first goes through a clearinghouse process where all bank transactions are bundled up into a few large transactions, the money is moved around, then the transactions are unbundled.

This takes a few days in each direction, so the vendor has to wait until the process is complete, then issue a new transaction that in turn will take a few days to process.
