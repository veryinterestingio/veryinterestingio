lessens_ A:

Most of the replies have focused on number of individuals, where humans are easily rivaled by small rodents. However by biomass humans are a candidate for the greatest mammal population ever. Currently 36% of mammal biomass is human, a remaining 60% is livestock, and only 4% is wild mammals. However, cattle probably have humans beat by a nose: they embody 0.61 Gt C compared to 0.6 Gt C embodied in humans, though the calculations are rough. It's unlikely there's ever been a time when only a couple mammal species so dominated the biosphere, so it's hardly out of the question that the situation is unprecedented.
