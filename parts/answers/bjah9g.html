During the day, the cells in your brain have a lot of work to do. Each cell is like a little factory working hard to build proteins and shuffle ions around and help you be you; coordinating every plan you make and piece of info you retrieve and breakfast burrito you pick up to eat with massive efforts. So much work! In the process, like any factory, some trash builds up that doesn't get taken out. The cells are simply too busy! You have breakfast burritos to eat and people to recognize!

At night, your brain (pineal gland what's good!) coordinates a hormone cascade to help get you relaxed and sleepy and off to dreamland. Once you are asleep, the liquid bath that your brain floats in all day becomes the star of the show. Your brain goes into dishwasher self-clean mode! Lots of swishing and sloshing, extra production of the liquid sweeping through the brain. This is when your brain cells can take out their trash and make sure it gets on and out and that proteins out for garbage don't gum up the works. This is just one reason that skipping sleep may make you feel off; your brain did not get to take out it's trash and dump it into the cerebrospinal fluid trash river! The same fluid that steals away toxins and trash proteins also nourishes the cells, getting them primed for all their work the next day!
