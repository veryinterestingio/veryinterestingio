I'm not from a medical or scientific background but I am from a therapy and counselling background.

The sense of relief is the negative emotions that were affecting you being processed through you talking about and experiencing our emotions.

To put it more simply, when we experience our emotions instead of running away from them, they usually go away by themselves.

AwakenedEyes A:

Another part of the answer comes from neurobiology. Our right brain hemisphere records emotions: how we felt because of an event, everything your body and senses recorded.

But not the explanation of the experience. Only the left brain hemisphere can interpret and make sens of it. By talking, by using language and telling someone about it, you literally put words to it, allowing the left brain to reconcile the experience with the left brain.

Once both memories, the implicit (right, emotions) and explicit (left, language), are in sych, you have processed the memory and this allows you to move on.

Many forms of counseling is based on that principle.
