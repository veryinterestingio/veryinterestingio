Lithuim A:

Alcohol and water are mutually soluble. Like salt or sugar, alcohol (specifically ethanol here) molecules will stay in the liquid solution indefinitely.

Contrast that to most oils, which are not water soluble and will separate into distinct layers based on density.

Sanfords_Son A:

Water and ethanol are miscible, meaning they mix in all proportions. It also means they will not separate or form a distinct layer no matter how long they sit.

Having said that, here’s an article describing a study that showed alcohol and water never completely mix on a microscopic level:
