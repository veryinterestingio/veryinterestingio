Japan had an industrial strategy after WW2 to invest in education and businesses that would lead to creation of high-tech jobs. This was aided directly by US funds in the early years but even more helpful was openness of the US to import Japanese products at tariffs that were significantly less than the tariffs that Japan placed on American imports for much of the postwar period, starting at a time when American companies dominated most industries including the electronic and automobile fields. It was important for the US to help build up Japan as a potential bulwark against communism in Asia. But at the same time Japan took the proceeds of its sales from imports and wisely kept investing to make their technology better. By the time that trade conditions became more fair between the US and Japan, US products in many areas had already fallen behind Japanese products in quality and price. It took a lot of hard work and shrewd decision making on the part of Japan.
