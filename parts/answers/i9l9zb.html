Nerves don't feel things in the way you are describing. They act purely to transmit a physical sensation, in this case, of light touch. The pleasant emotion you are referring to is a construct of the brain. The nerves from the penis end up stimulating multiple areas of the brain associated with feeling good. They also act on other areas of the brain that go on to stimulate the various processes of orgasm and ejaculation.

Incidentally, there are other nerves that purely transmit the physical sensation of pain and many other types of physical sensation. These pain nerves will stimulate areas of the brain associated with negative feelings.

The relationship between emotions/feelings, and the physical sensations we connect to them, is the job of the brain, not of the nerves themselves.

As for why those glans nerves end up stimulating pleasure centres of the brain more than say, touch sensitive nerves in the arm do, that's down to the evolution of the brain. It was evolutionarily beneficial to make sex feel good, so that organisms want to do it more!
