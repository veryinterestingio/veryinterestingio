LSD is a molecule that is almost like seratonin, but not quite. Seratonin isn't just "the happy hormone", it's a really common molecule used to communicate between neurons. LSD molecules bind to the same receptors in your brain, but because they're a little wrong, they cause your neurons to work kind of wrong, and it results in weird conscious experiences.

It can result in weird sensory hallucinations (visual and auditory), and that's what's usually depicted as trippy rainbow prism stuff. But it also affects other stuff in your brain (which can be positive, or negative). So you shouldn't take it expecting just a fun light show.

LieutenantDan710 A:

LSD more often than not does not make you "see rainbows", movies and TV have dramatically skewed many peoples impression of the drug and what it does. The hallucinations produced by LSD more warp your perception of depth and shape as well as make colors more vivid and vibrant, imagine sitting calmly in nature and watching it breathe while the colors of your surroundings are more intense than usual.

Yes if you take a considerably large dose of LSD it can produce much more intense hallucinations, but standard doses that people who know what they're doing take will not make you think you're walking on Mario karts rainbow road.

A lot of confusion about this also comes from people who say "well I/my friend took some acid and it was nuts and did all these crazy things" and more often than not the "acid" they took is some off-brand research chemicals sold on paper as "LSD". The majority of real, high quality LSD vanished from production in the 90s when a major lab was busted, now adays unless you're well connected you are not buying real LSD. Movies, TV, and research chemicals have now mislead many people about what LSD is like.

If you want an ok example from a movie of what real LSD is kind of like watch the tripping scene from Taking Woodstock (https://youtu.be/4XHySKsPa3M), take note of how at about 3:00 the images on the tapestry above them seem to shift shapes and colors but not in an overwhelming way.
