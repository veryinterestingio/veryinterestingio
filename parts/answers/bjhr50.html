Onetap1 A:

Until the start of the Industrial Revolution, nearly all the trash decayed. No-one discarded metal, it was too valuable. Plastics didn't exist. Clothing was natural fibres (wool, cotton, linen, silk,etc.,) and they decayed. Archaeologists dig up crockery, a few lost metal artefacts, broken tiles, shards of glass, bricks, some grave goods, remnants of stone structures, bones; nearly everything else has long gone. The rare archaeological treasures are the few things that didn't decay.

The Industrial Revolution was discovering ways to make stuff cheaper and faster, stuff that was resistant to decay. Plastics, concrete, glass, metal, painted stuff, toxic chemicals. We made a lot of stuff. The resilient garbage is mixed up with the bio-degradable stuff.
