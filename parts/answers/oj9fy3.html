Only your literal brain itself doesn't have pain receptors. The scalp and muscles outseide the skull do, the meninges (layers of support tissue around the brain) do.

Your brain shrinking slightly from dehydration and therefore pulling away from the inside of your skull can cause pain.

You brain swelling from an impact/injury/infection/tumor and pressing against that same lining tissue can also cause pain.

Neck tension carried into scalp muscles can cause headaches too.

Etc.
