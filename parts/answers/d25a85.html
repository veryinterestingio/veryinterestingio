These games aren't random.

Many of the components are intentionally designed to lock a portion of the board, forcing players to "play the board" until areas are "free". This gives the player the illusion they have "choice" to "clear" a board when they really don't.

It's also why most board clears, once the developer has tried their best to extract cash from players, offers the highest points with the most rewards.

This psychological effect is why millions can't stop playing these types of games. They "reward" players into believing they have full control, but as long as the game throws out "freebies" such as "unlimited lives" and "power boosts", players continue to repeat the pattern.

Most are completely unaware the game is rigged from the start. Even if they did know, I doubt they'd stop playing simply because most people play the game to kill time.

gamerize A:

Every level you played has finetuned so that the number of turns you have is 1-2 less than what the majority of population needs to finish. This makes the each play of the level be on that edge of either failing by 1 turn or passing it with 1-2 turns left max.

If you suck at the game you will always end up needing just a couple of turns to finish the level. Most players will try again, but some will pay just to skip frustration and that is what they count on to make money.
