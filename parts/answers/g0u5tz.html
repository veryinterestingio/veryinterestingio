Drachefly A:

It does, if it's in thermal equilibrium. If it is monochromatic or any other non-blackbody distribution, then no.

Of course, it will quickly attain this distribution if there's something not perfectly reflective in the box, but that violates the assumptions.

Or you could wait a thermodynamically large period of time for extremely slow photon-photon scattering to thermalize them. Unless the photons happen to be high energy gamma rays, in which case you won't have to wait that long.
