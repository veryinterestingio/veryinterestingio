alphaMHC A:

I just want to say that in a bit of a contrast to what /u/zeiandren said, insect brains are complex enough to have neurons interacting with one another, and those interactions (especially in parts of the brain called mushroom bodies) form memories.

How those memories (like aversion to certain smells) persist through metamorphosis is still a mystery. We have very impressive maps of neuron-neuron interactions in insects like Drosophila, so we are learning a lot. The latest I've seen on the subject was a paper from 2018 that says that interactions between those neurons may be altering the gene expression in the neurons, setting them up to continue remembering after they're put back together in the adult form.

Alternatively, there is also some evidence that not ALL neuron interactions get broken down during metamorphosis. It is a tricky and difficult thing to study, but people are working on it.
