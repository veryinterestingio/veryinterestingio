haysoos2 A:

The tracheal tube that connects the blowhole to the lungs has a sort of a nozzle attachment, like a vacuum cleaner hose, that slots into the internal passage to blowhole. This allows them to swallow while underwater without aspirating water into their lungs.

It does mean they can't breathe through their mouths though, so something like a golf ball plugging their blowhole would be very serious.

If they swallow something too large, it also has the possibility of dislodging their breathing tube, and making choking on their food or accidentally breathing water a possibility. It's rare, but it does happen.

Some whales have also learned how to voluntarily pop the tube out, allowing them to do tricks like blow bubble curtains. It's a learned skill for them, like the ability to burp on command.
