Hi. I am resident in thoracic surgery. I have 24 hour shifts around 6-7 times a month. After regular 8 hours I continue with 16 hours in emegency surgical ambulant. Sometimes I continue as a thoracic surgeon consultant for the whole hospital. There are also days, when i have to continue next day on the thoracic department, especially if we are low on staff.

But why you ask? In our hospital it all depends on the number of doctors on the department. Shifts have to be covered. For example there are only 4 thoracic surgeons in our hospital. And those 4 surgeons have to fill a whole month of shifts, plus regular work in daytime. And the only option to do that, is being there a whole bunch of hours. Departments with fair number of doctors do not have those crazy hours. They work in 8 hours daytime or 16 hours afternoon/night shift with free day afterwards

I do not have problems with those terms, because adrenaline keeps me going. It only sucks when I get home, adrenaline gets depleted and all I can do is being vegetable on the couch. Thank god that my lady understands this.

ELI5 answer: Number of doctors on the department dictates how many hours will each one have to cover. Many doctors= less crazy hours. Few doctors= shitload of crazy hours. There is no way around it.
