This is actually a misconception. You don't get vitamin D from the sun. You make vitamin D from cholesterol in its inactive form. Inside your cells, this inactive form gets subjected to UV light which is provided by the sun and then gets activated. That's where the whole "vitamin D from the sun" idea comes from. Other vitamins might require activation chemically through enzymes. So we either get them from food or we synthesize them from other molecules in our body.

miladymondegreen A:

By definition "essential nutrient" means a nutrient that our bodies cannot synthesize. They are essential because we can grow deficient without digestive intake. Some other non-essential nutrients that can be synthesized within our bodies (like vitamin D) are biotin, cholesterol, and vitamin K. Biotin and vitamin K are synthesized in the intestines, cholesterol is synthesized in the liver. There are also 11 non-essential amino acids that our bodies can synthesize from carbon skeletons: alanine, arginine, asparagine, aspartic acid, cysteine, glutamic acid, glutamine, glycine, proline, serine, and tyrosine.
