_Foy A:

Your eyes "see" with light-sensitive receptors at the back of your eyeballs.

There is other stuff between the receptors and the sources of light that you "see" when you look around.

For example, when you close your eyes you are essentially looking at the inside of your eyelids, if you shone a flashlight on your closed eyelid you would see red light because it's going through the blood and flesh of your eyelid.

You may also be seeing "floaters", which are small thing insideof your eyeball that casts tiny shadows. You can also sometimes see your own white blood cells (trippy, I know).

So, essentially, your peepers are simply peepin' more than you thought!
