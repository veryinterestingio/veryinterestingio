Soramaro A:

OP's premise is flawed. Though OP may subjectively feel they have vivid auditory imagery and poor visual imagery, there are individual differences among people with respect to how vividly they can engage in imagery across the senses, and expertise plays a large role:

https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/j.2044-8295.1994.tb02536.x

Note also that forming a mental image involves reconstruction, and there are far more rules that you can use to reconstruct a song (even rules that you don't realize that you know, e.g., the 8-note octave in Western music, which determine the notes that combine to make familiar chords) than there are rules that you can use to reconstruct visual images of real-world objects.

I'll also point out the curious association between the hippocampus, believed to be critical in supporting your episodic memory system (i..e, your built-in Go-Pro) and olfactory imagery (imagery of smell).

https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fnhum.2012.00285/full
