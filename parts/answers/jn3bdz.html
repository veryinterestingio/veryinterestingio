jerichojerry A:

B vitamins were discovered by their deficiency. In the early 1900s we discovered that beriberi was a processed grain eater disease. Eating only polished rice gave it's eaters watery stools and distended bellies and exhaustion. Adding back in some elements of the grains reversed its effects. Around the same time it was discovered that other wasting diseases with similar presentations (exhaustion, rashes, abdominal bloating) were also cured by adding yeast extract into the diet. The medical community had known for awhile that citrus reversed scurvy and a more comprehensive theory around what exactly was in these foods that treated disease. The theory was developed that there was some "vital amine" that treated particular diseases. Vitamin C was isolated. Around that same time, thiamine was isolated. It was then discovered that isolated thiamine didn't treat all wasting nutritional defects and there was a bit of a race to isolate the rest. The remaining vitamins were named in the order of their discovery.

It should be noted that all the other vitamins have different forms too, its just that the b vitamins are essentially different molecules rather than one parent molecule with different functional groups or redox states, like vitamin D or C
