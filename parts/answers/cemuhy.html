So, here's what's fun. Whenever we experience something emotionally significant, it gets "bathed" in hormones that strengthen our memory of that event.

This is fantastic for fun memories of playing in the mud, baking cookies with Grandma, first kisses, weddings, babies. etc. But our brains don't differentiate.

If something really insignificant happens- but we have a strong emotional response to it- our brains still act accordingly. The hormonal "bath" strengthens the memory, making it easier to recall. This is part of the reason why we're so good at remembering embarrassing events, awesome moments, harsh break-ups, etc. They're all emotional, and they've all been reinforced by our brains' recall mechanisms.

It's not always terribly helpful for modern times, but you can imagine how effective it would be for our ancestors. "OH SHIT, I'm being chased by a tiger again! What did I do last time this happened?!?" -This is what our brains are trying to accomplish, but the end result is you recalling that time you farted in 3rd grade, next to a cute girl. As you're trying to fall asleep before a final exam. Brains are great.
