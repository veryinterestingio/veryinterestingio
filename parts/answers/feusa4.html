Whilimbird A:

Several reasons!

-If you’re using your horn to fight with (say, in competition with another male), that means you’re going to be smacking your head against the thing you’re fighting. Two horns gives better coverage and makes it less likely you’ll miss and hit with your actual head.

-Two horns means you have a spare if one gets broken or grows in deformed.

-Animals tend towards symmetry, and two horns is easier for that than one.

​

You might notice that one-horned animals generally either use their horn for something other than fighting (narwhals use their ‘horn’ -really a very long tooth- as a sensory tool!) or have thick enough horns that you can’t really fit two of them side by side (rhinos).
