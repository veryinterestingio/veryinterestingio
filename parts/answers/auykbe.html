If the trucks start at the same time, from the same place, and travel with the same velocity (speed and direction) then they both arrive at the end point at the same time.

The only difference in the trucks is their cargo. This data is irrelevant, assuming the trucks have the same velocity. It's not a question of inertia, drag, air resistance etc. Even if we assume those things are different, your teacher stated they are moving at the same speed. (Even if the cotton has more drag, lets say, it doesn't matter. If the speed is the same, then it is the same. Force, inertia, mass, density are irrelevant.)

Simple Newtonian physics.
