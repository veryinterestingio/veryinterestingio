Because companies don't usually report rent payments to the credit scoring agencies (Transunion, Experian, Equifax). Same with most utility bills (phone, electricity, etc.). If your landlord does report your payment history to the credit companies then it can count in your favor but I don't think that's too common.

And of course if you're late on payments that can get reported to the credit agencies and negatively impact your score.
