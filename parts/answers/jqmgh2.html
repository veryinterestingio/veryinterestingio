The nerve in charge of sensing sound vibrations at your ear drums is connected with the nerve that sends signals to and from your teeth. Sometimes signals can get a little wonky so you can feel sounds in your teeth.

(If you want to know more, you can look up the mandibular division of the trigeminal nerve, auriculotemporal nerve, and inferior alveolar nerve).

trinite0 A:

This has nothing to do with resonance. It is a psychosomatic response to sounds that your body associates with dental pain -- things resembling the sound that might result from biting a rock or stick other hard, tooth-breaking object. You will notice that in some cases, merely thinking about the sounds causes the reaction. Your brain has learned to automatically associate certain sounds with tooth pain, and does so for the (actually, pretty sensible) evolutionary reason that things which cause dental pain are very bad and should be avoided. So the vicarious pain response is autonomic and unconscious.
