jelder A:

In a dark room, turn on the faucet (slowly enough that its clear) and shine a flashlight into the stream. See how the light bounces around and draws a spot of light at the bottom of the sink? That means some of the light didn't go straight through the column of water.

The same thing is happening when clothes or a surface gets wet: some of the light which would normally be reflected by the dry surface gets deeper into the wet surface, so you don't see it getting reflected back to your eye. This makes it look darker.

GrandMoffTarkan A:

The clothes appear darker if you're on the same side as the light. If the you are on the other side of the clothes from the light source, the wet part will appear brighter.

The reason for this is that light can move through water, so when water gets into the fibers of the fabric it creates little passages for light to get through. When the light can pass through the cloth, less light bounces back to your eye so the spot appears darker. Super dark materials use this effect, but instead of using water they use carbon fibers to create a light trapping forest
