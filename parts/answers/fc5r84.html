zeralesaar A:

The brain is very energy-hungry -- it is responsible for something like 20% of bodily energy consumption despite being around 2% of body mass in a typical adult. Like all organs, the brain needs to be fueled, and actively engaging a cognitive task will increase the brain's energy expenditure from baseline, meaning there is less available elsewhere.

EDIT: for clarification, the 2%/20% figure is for resting metabolism as far as I have read. This review article examines studies of brain energy consumption, cognitive tasks, and blood glucose -- you should read that; it will do a better job of elucidating the basic idea than I can.
