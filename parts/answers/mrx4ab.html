methanalmkay A:

Well pretty much any plant can grow roots in water, it's just that plants that aren't woody grow them much easier and faster. Most woody cuttings need some rooting hormone to help them grow roots in water, but it's still possible. Willow contains lots of indolbutyric acid (which is an auxin) and salicylic acid which both help in growing roots. Some people use willow extract to help other cuttings root as well.

PatientCamera A:

The process you describe is called vegetative propagation, or sometimes referred to as cloning. While some plants do this naturally with little to no assistance, with the right plant hormones and technique you can get a vast amount of plants to propagate this way.

DecentChanceOfLousy A:

Growing viable roots from any part of the plant, when detached, is a very complicated process (as with everything that involves growing new body parts or structures). Plants that need it to spread and survive had selection pressure to keep those mechanisms working. For any that didn't have that selection pressure (that didn't need this behavior), either some part of the complex process broke through random chance or it was actively selected out if it conflicted with some other trait that was better for survival.
