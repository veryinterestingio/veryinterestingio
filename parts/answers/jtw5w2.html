Hold a piece of paper with a dot on it, at an arm's length. Look at the dot. Now keeping your eyes focussed on the dot, bring the paper gradually closer to your eyes. Your eyes will get crossed and you will feel physical strain. You know why? Eyes have muscles too.

https://aapos.org/glossary/anatomy-of-the-eye

These muscles have to be exerted harder when you are focusing on something at a closer distance. The father away you look, more relaxed these muscles get.

Staring in the void is essentially looking into infinite distance, which relaxes the eyes. You are not focussing on anything specific so your lens is also not under stress.

Additionally, your brain is not registering anything specific, because this viewing is aimless viewing. So, it relaxes your mental faculties too.
