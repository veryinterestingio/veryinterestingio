No. Decomposition is a biochemical process. That means it requires the presence of other chemicals (namely oxygen in the air) and other biological life forms (bacteria and other macroscopic scavengers).

You might partially decompose as a result of the bacteria in your own body, but only for a very short amount of time before they, too, die or go dormant.

Decay changes when in a vacuum. A body free-floating in space would be exposed to a large amount of radiation and the composition of the body would change as the organic compounds break down into their lower constituents.

A body shielded from radiation would be well preserved, however.
