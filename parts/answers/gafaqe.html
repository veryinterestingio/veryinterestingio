ifoosh A:

The reason is your anus and rectum have a very robust vascular supply (arteries, veins, lymphatics) compared to other parts of the body. This allows for your white blood cells to get to the infection easily, attack it quickly, and clear the debris quickly so bacteria cannot grow unchecked. That being said, peri-anal abscesses (pus-filled infected regions around the anus/rectum) are definitely a thing that require surgery to drain. Not so fun.

Source: Am a surgery resident
