EggsAndMilquetoast A:

Most antibiotics work by disrupting bacterial synthesis, whether it keeps them from forming a cell wall, generating proteins, or replicating their DNA, and so it's pretty easy for bacteria to develop resistance to that. But as someone else said, it's hard to develop a resistance to something that completely poisons and obliterates bacterial cell walls and viral capsids, whether or not it's actively dividing. It's like the difference between human skin evolving to be darker pigmented to protect us from the sun's radiation versus developing skin that would protect us from an H-bomb.

Also, we over-sanitized things even before coronavirus. A little extra lysol or hand sanitizer at this point is like a drop in the ocean.
