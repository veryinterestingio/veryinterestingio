Humans laugh because we are very very social animals.

And before language was developed, humans and our ancestors still needed ways to “communicate” socially. Besides your grunts, roars, shouts, fights, and social hierarchies, laughing was another way of non verbal communication.

Laughter naturally builds bonds between two individuals laughing at the same time, so it helped up build deeper social bonds with one another. Laughter is also a way to acknowledge and diffuse awkward/embarrassings/Tense situations. Laughter is a way to basically go “hey man I know you might be angry or irritated but ah it’s fine just laugh about it”

So laughter helps keep those social bonds form breaking down soon.

And yes, other great Apes laugh too, tho many are silent laughter. But gorillas, chimpanzees, orangutans all laugh in similar ways to us

Source: Documentary “First Man” available on curiosity stream that follows humans historic evolutions
