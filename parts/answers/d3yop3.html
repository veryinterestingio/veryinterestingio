littlesoubrette A:

Suicidal ideaton can be a coping mechanism, as weird as that sounds. As someone who has personally struggled with this, there is a weird sense of relief to consider that this moment of pain and angish might have an end point, even a self-inflicted one. For me, having suicidal thoughts was the only way I could survive, they were the hope of being without pain.

And as others have said, the fight or flight (or freeze) response takes place in a different part of the brain. It's an uncontrolled biological response to a threat, totally separate from the areas of the brain responsible for suicidal thoughts.

When I reached out for help and learned some better skills for coping with my emotional and mental pain, the desire to die slowly went away. When I was able to get help and heal, it was easy for me to want to live. I never actually wanted to die, I just didn't want to be in pain anymore. If anyone is struggling and feeling as if death is the only thing that will take the pain away, please believe me when I say that it is possible to have a full and meaningful life that you enjoy living, even if you can't imagine it now.
