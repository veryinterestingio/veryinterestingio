Could be congenital, could be an injury. If one of your nails is different from the rest then it is probably one of those two.

There are a lot of diseases and deficiencies that can display symptoms in your fingernails but if you had something like that it would most likely be expressing it in all your nail beds.

Seraphenrir A:

You can think of the nail as a modified hair shaft. Deep at the base there is a "root." When this root gets damaged by trauma, picking, infections, etc., it is no longer able to produce perfectly symmetrical and smooth nails.
