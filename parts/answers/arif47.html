Skin has different layers. The outer layer, the epidermis, is the thinnest layer.

All of the melanin that makes skin turn dark is in that outer epidermis layer. In the linked photo, the turf burn stripped away the epidermis and all of the dark melanin with it, leaving the pink dermis exposed underneath.

Melanocytes are located in the epidermis. In a turf burn, the outer part of skin is abrased, leaving the dermis exposed. Since the dermis has no melanocytes, you donâ€™t see the skin colour.
