onexbigxhebrew A:

ELI5 Since we all HATE ads and tend to skip it or ignore it at best

I work in marketing management, have been very successful in digital marketing and can start by telling you your proposition is based on an incorrect assumption. Many people do watch ads, and many click through; it's often a matter of the right person being hit with the right one, and across multiple channels. The better a company's targeting, the better an ad's likelihood of reaching the right target and being fully viewed. I have plenty of regional Youtube ad campaigns where full-view rate is over 50%. Some are 20%. Many are lower.

It's also important to remember that reddit is very counterculturalist from an internet use perspective, and commenters are a poor sample of the general population. They're also critical and adversely reactive to advertising, and naive enough to think it "doesn't work" on them. Redditors being vehemently anti-ad is a demographic and, moreso, psycographic phenomena.

Outside of that, think of that one time you finished an ad. That's what we look for. That one person of many at the tipping point for interest in the product out of hundreds. Now multiply that by millions of views and impressions.

TL;Dr We get the views, people watch ads more than you realize.
