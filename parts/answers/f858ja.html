deep_sea2 A:

On the last segment, spectators see the runners going from left to right. This is how we read in the western world, so anti-clockwise running makes more sense as a spectator, at least during the last segment of the race.

Also, most athletes are right leg dominant. A dominant right leg makes it easier to run anti-clockwise because your right leg is always on the outside.

imperfectchicken A:

Psychology is part of it.

Left-to-right is generally perceived as a "good" or "progressing" movement. Right-to-left is generally perceived as "bad" or "awkward" movement.

https://nofilmschool.com/2016/02/left-or-right-why-characters-lateral-movement-screen-matters-film

Try watching a show where the bad guy approaches from the left, or the innocent and gullible character is on the right, or even the same race in a mirror. It's...well, weird. Talk shows and interviews use the same psychology, depending on who the producers want you to focus on.
