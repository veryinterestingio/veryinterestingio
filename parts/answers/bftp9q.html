onsereverra A:

imersial's response is great – just wanted to chime in that another way to think about it is that fevers work on the same principle chemo/radiation treatments do for cancer. namely, they're bad for all cells; we use those treatments in the hopes that the cancer cells will die before the healthy ones do, but chemo/radiation can and do also make patients really sick because they're harming the healthy cells as well. the same is true on a different scale for fevers and bacteria! when it works right, it kills off bacteria before your healthy cells are harmed, but the same thing that makes it bad for bacteria makes it potentially bad for you as well.

RolandDPlaneswalker A:

From a medical perspective, fevers are really used as a measure of the bodies response to the pathogen rather than something dangerous that we need to correct. It’s only at very high levels that things get dangerous. That’s due to a set functional range for our own proteins, which deform when the environment around them changes too much.

When I was on my peds rotation, the head of the department asked me why we were treating this patients mild fever (100.6). The answer? Because it makes us (the doctors and the parents) feel better.
