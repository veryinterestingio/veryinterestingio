afcagroo A:

When you learn something or make a memory, your brain actually changes a bit. New synaptic connections are made, or are strengthened by the interactions of neurotransmitters and synapses. You create a new neural "circuit". These can be of variable strength.

But you are constantly experiencing things that you don't need to retain. They either don't form a new circuit that is strong enough to last long, or never get passed from short-term memory into long-term. The connections are not made or are too weak to persist.

Memories must be occasionally accessed to persist, or they can decay. There's also evidence that accessing a memory destroys or damages it, and it must be re-written. We don't remember things, we remember memories of memories.

ItsSchlieffenTime A:

A thought is not a static thing, its a process, a continually changing event.

- In our brains, a signal takes the form of neurons activating other neurons. Compared to an electrical impulse, this is a slow process (but still very fast compared to pretty much anything else).

- So when you are 'mid-thought', you are at a point where many of your neurons are activated, and they are each about to activate other neurons.

- A person with a certain thought in their head will continually change the way they are thinking about it. They will consider it from different angles, and in different contexts.

- Eventually they will get distracted by something else, and the thought will stop circulating in their head.

- Asking 'where did the thought go' doesn't really make sense, because the thought was an event, not a thing. It's like asking 'where did the weekend go'. It didn't go anywhere, it's just over.

mostlygray A:

That thought is silently discarded if you're not paying attention. If you're paying attention, it goes into short-term, if the thought is valid, it gets moved to mid-term memory, if it's worth remembering, it goes to long.

Bits and pieces of short-term get re-filed as long-term immediately, thus deja vu. Sometimes short-term is never touched and you only get long-term but it is disconnected until you find the puzzle piece.

If you're reading, but thinking about something else, sometimes it's really just silent discard. You might remember a bit of the gist but no details.

Think of it like a firewall doing a silent discard. There's a record that the packet was discarded, but the content is meaningless.
