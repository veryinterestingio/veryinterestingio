When you consume a lot of salt, your kidneys need extra water to process it. It gets that water by making you feel thirsty and drink more water, and holding that water in reserve for your kidneys.

Your body actually handles both saltwater and salty food in the same way. You are only in danger of dehydration if you don't have access to regular water after. If you drank seawater followed by regular water, you'd retain water and be fine, if you ate a bunch of salty food without drinking after, you'd risk dehydration.

UnlovableBitch A:

You have two kidneys, they are in the lower part of your belly, sticking to your back from the inside. They clean your blood, like a coffee filter catches coffee grounds. Your kidneys are very effective, they clean lots of waste materials from your blood. Now you can't clean the mud on your bicycle without water. Similarly your kidneys needs water to clean your blood.

One of the things the kidney remove from your blood is extra salts.

When you eat salty foods, you add extra salt in your blood. Your kidneys will now need some extra water to clean it, which is why you'll feel thirsty. You drink water. Now your kidneys are working and they're slowly cleaning all the salt using the water you drank. Until all the cleaning is done, your body will keep the water inside, to help the kidneys clean properly. That means you retained the water.

In case of drinking sea water, it's the same process. You drank sea water which added lots of extra salts in your blood. Your kidneys need water to clean the extra salt. You'll feel thirsty. Now if you drank one cup of sea water, your body now needs one and a half cup of normal water to clean up all the extra salt in your blood. This is where the problem arises. If you don't have one and a half cup of normal water available, your body will use its own personal supply of water to do all the cleaning. Since your body does not have much stored water inside, you'll feel dehydrated and more thirsty. So you see, only cup of sea water made you dehydrated. That's why if you drink enough quantities of sea water, you can die of dehydration.
