It’s not that the presence of higher oxygen levels in itself leads to bigger insects. At least not directly, it’s not like insect steroids.

It’s that insects themselves don’t use oxygen like mammals and other (land) animals you generally think about do, they don’t breathe with lungs.

Instead they breathe through little things called trachea, you can read more about that here: https://askabiologist.asu.edu/how-insects-breathe

There’s a lot of info about that on the internet, but the important factor is that it’s less effective at exchanging large amounts of oxygen than lungs. That doesn’t matter for tiny insects though, they don’t need lots of oxygen.

For bigger insects, though... that’s important. It means they can’t get enough oxygen to fuel their bodies.

So it’s not that higher oxygen makes bigger insects, it’s that lower oxygen limits their size.

There were still tiny insects filling tiny insects niches in higher oxygen environments. It’s just that bigger insects could survive so they competed with smaller animals in those niches as well (like birds or rats).
