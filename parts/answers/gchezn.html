ogdeloon A:

As a classical singer, severe asthmatic, and meditation enthusiast I can attest that breathing in all the way through your stomach, which engages your diaphragm to take a breath, makes it feel more complete and satisfying. If you want to master this type of breathing, I would research Buteyko breathing or take some voice lessons for the purpose of strengthening breath support. A fun fact: as fetuses in our mothers’ wombs, we couldn’t effectively breathe focusing on the expansion of the chest, due to being in the fetal position. When we grow up and start walking upright, it’s more convenient to take smaller breaths, so we naturally adapt to focus on the expansion of the chest. If you want to try for yourself, just put yourself in fetal position for a long enough time on your knees with your chest facing downward (kind of like child’s pose.) Put your hands on your lower back, you’ll know you’re taking the right kind of “tummy breaths” (as I have lovingly named them for my voice students) when you can feel the area expand on your inhale.

If I might add a different point to the table here. While the top answers are correct, there's is also another possible factor to what you're describing. Anxiety and high stress levels can cause your regular and deep breaths to feel completely unsatisfactory, even though your body is getting ample oxygen. You could breathe 5 deep breaths in a row while following the instructions of breathing from the belly, but maybe only one or two of those breaths are going to feel "complete". Just ask yourself if you're feeling particularly down or stressed lately, it could be the cause.
