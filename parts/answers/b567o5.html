quats5 A:

You don’t use all the oxygen you breathe in; you actually exhale a good bit of oxygen, too. This why CPR works: you’re breathing out enough oxygen to keep someone else alive. One deep breath in can last you longer than you think, especially if you are fit and trained — physically and mentally — to maximize it.

Divers often hyperventilate to force as much oxygen into their system as possible before holding that last extended lungful of air. They also typically are fit, so that their bodies use oxygen more efficiently. They also learn how to recognize and delay responding to carbon dioxide buildup signals (if you breathe out that carbon dioxide waste gas, you lose valuable oxygen). And swimming in cool to cold water also kicks in oxygen optimization effects in the body as well.
