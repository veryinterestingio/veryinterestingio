Printing presses can fit 8 or 16 pages on one press form (sometimes called a press sheet signature). If the book page count is not evenly divisible by 8 or 16, the blank pages are added to the end of the book.

sahesush A:

Book pages are printed in a way that typically prints 16 book pages per large page. If your book isn't divisible by 16, then you're going to have some blank pages in the front or back.

internetboyfriend666 A:

It has to do with how books are put together. Pages in a book are put together in groups called signatures. A signature is groups of pages folded in half and assembled together. Each signature in a book will always have the same number of pages and it's always even. If you have a book with 10 32-page signatures, your book has 320 pages, but there might not be 320 pages of content in your book. If there are only 316 pages of content, you have 4 blank pages.

ky1-E A:

This is because books are printed on a big sheet of paper, called a signature, which is then folded to make 16-24 pages in the book. So books can only be printed with pages that are multiples of 16 (or something else, depending on how large a signature they started with). This means there will be left over pages at the end of the book if the book isn't an exact multiple of 16 or whatever they used. Most editors would typeset the book so as to reduce the number of blank pages, but sometimes there are one or two that just have to be left blank.
