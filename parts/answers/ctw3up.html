internetboyfriend666 A:

Because our skin has many more sensory nerve endings than our insides. If you think about it, it makes sense. From an evolutionary standpoint, there's no benefit to having highly innervated internal organs, since there was nothing we could do about internal injuries until very recent human history. Your skin is where you want the most sensory nerve endings, because your skin is your first line of defense against external injures which are things you can actually avoid and maybe try to fix.
