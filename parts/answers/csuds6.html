Depends on what you describe as 'too long'.

If over the course of a day or two, form what I read from Matthew Walker's "Why We Sleep", there is basically two things that come into play. However, since this is ELI5, all you need to know is that our circadian rhythm doesn't care whether or not we had fallen asleep last night. It will convince you to be at full wakefulness all the same at certain times and this is what is your 'second wind'.

If over the period of what is becoming an exceptionally long time, as another user said, we start producing adrenaline as a response to the stress the body is feeling.
