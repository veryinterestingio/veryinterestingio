baloo_the_bear A:

Coughing does aid in clearing the airways of particulate debris and mucus. It is a reflex mediated in part by the vagus nerve (CN X). However, coughing is violent and forceful and too much can be detrimental.

In many respiratory viral illnesses, the skin lining the airways is a target and port of entry. These skin cells can slough off the airways and mix with mucus and get coughed out of the lungs. However, when the skin sloughs off, it can expose nerve endings called J-fibers. These J-fibers can stimulate more cough, leading to more skin cells sloughing off and more exposed J-fibers and the process builds on itself in a positive feedback loop.

Additionally, coughing can be so violent that a person can break ribs, dislocate joints, suffer a pneumothorax or pneumonediastinum, or any number of unintended consequences.

Suppressing cough is typically done for comfort, and to allow the skin lining to calm down and heal after a viral illness, and to prevent any unwanted sequelae from uncontrolled cough.

Cough suppression is not without risk however, and can put a person at risk for secondary bacterial pneumonia or any number of other lung diseases. The choice to treat cough is a risk vs benefit calculation.

If there's something new tickling the back of the throat then a good cough reflex is a great thing. It's all about airway protection. Nothing should have gotten past the epiglottis down near the vocal folds, so let's create a big old squeeze and a forceful push of air to get rid of it. Protects the lungs. This is a good idea, and keeps you alive. Things in the lungs lead to infection and the blocking of things you would rather not have blocked.

The 200th time it happens that day though, well there wasn't likely actually something down there that woulda caused damage to the lungs, instead what's now happening is just repeated forceful events at the layrnx. And they can be really damaging AND self-reinforcing. A cough causes damage which causes sensation in the throat which makes the person feel the need to cough again... and so they again slam their vocal folds together and...

It causes all sorts of voice problems and growths, we don't want it to become a habit.

A cough or two is good. 200 is damaging, and in a vicious-cycle that can cause yet more damage and more coughing...
