MontiBurns A:

The other commenter is wrong. The idea with fighting in hockey is that its a way to for players to police each other. You have your skill players, your Wayne Gretzky's Sidney Crosby's ECT. These are the guys that score goals.

You have your role players, your defenders and your less than superstar players, and you have a few enforcers, your big guys, whose job it is to protect their players.

Now, when anyone from the other team lays down a big hit on one of your star players, even if that hit is legal, you can expect the enforcer to come out onto the ice on that guys next shift, and pick a fight with him. The message being, "hey, you hit our guy, you're gonna have to face our big guy.". In theory, this self policing discourages games from getting too chippy.

Keep in mind that guys are on skates, so it's not like they're getting their full weight behind a punch, it's mostly upper body stuff, but I'm sure it still hurts like hell.

Whether or not it's essential is up for debate. All amatuer levels it's illegal, including the Olympics and college.
