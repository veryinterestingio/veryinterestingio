Andrew5329 A:

If I recall correctly the Pfizer/Biontech mRNA went from platform IP to final version in the span of a weekend once China made technical information about the Coronavirus like it's genome public. Honestly the basic principles of vaccine design for either aren't rocket science.

The Chinese took whole unmodified virus and inactivated it with beta-propiolactone, a chemical which has been a default for inactivated vaccines since the 1950s/1960s.

Moderna and Pfizer copy/pasted the sequence responsible for coding the spike protein (the infection mechanism), printed off the messenger RNA for that sequence, and mixed it with a lipid based formulation to protect the fragile mRNA through to injection. The main substantive difference between the Pfizer vaccine and the Moderna vaccine is in the lipid formulation which makes one more shelf stable.

I'm being somewhat reductionist, but that's the design process in a nutshell. Pretty much everything beyond that is scale-up and safety /efficacy testing. China/Russia's main 'advantage' in rollout is that their governments were willing to grant approval once they passed early safety trials even without meaningful efficacy data.

In the West we're a bit spoiled for choice, and we wanted to go with vaccines that actually worked the best which means inefficiencies as we wait to see which cantidates we need to mass produce. Others are going to end up vaccinating whatever reasonably safe domestic option was available which streamlines the decision tree. And that shows through, it's why outside mainland China Sinovac's efficacy drops from a claimed 80% down to only about 50% in the hands of scientists without a finger on the scale.
