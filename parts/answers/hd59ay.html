Charcoal is dry wood. It is wood that has been heated without oxygen (to prevent it from burning) which removes absolutely all water and decomposes parts of the wood, leaving mostly carbon residue which is much more volatile than dry wood.

crazygringo A:

Charcoal *isn't* used to make fire starting easier.

For grilling, charcoal is used instead of untreated wood because it burns hotter and longer.

A charcoal fire *is* hard to start. You can't just light charcoal on fire, usually. Which is why people use lighter fluid or newspaper to make it easier.
