HCl stands for "hydrochloride". It is because the cetirizine molecule is formed into a "salt". There are a few reasons for choosing to use a salt instead of just the active ingredient molecule by itself; probably the two most common reasons are solubility (so that it can be more readily absorbed by the body) and shelf stability.

The active ingredient in such medicines are called amines, that are water insoluble. Converting the amine into a hydrochloride makes them water soluble, which means they dissolve in the gastrointestinal tract and are able to be absorbed into the bloodstream more quickly.

The HCl in such medicines refers to hydrochloride, not hydrochloric acid. Of cource, the HCl in the final compound is introduced by the reaction between hydrochloric acid (acid) and the amine (base), which produces the final hydrochloride (salt).
