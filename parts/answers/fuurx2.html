Lemesplain A:

You’re comparing apples to oranges.

Being overweight isn’t necessarily an eating disorder. Neither is being underweight.

However, there are several disorders that can lead to either being over- or underweight (anorexia is one such disorder).

Or, to put it another way: being obese isn’t necessarily an eating disorder, but it can certainly be the result of one.

apathyontheeast A:

Your weight/weight loss behaviors are only parts of diagnosing anorexia and bulimia. One key part of anorexia/bulimia is a disordered body image that drives those behaviors. For example: believing you're massively overweight to the point of delusion when you're actually skin and bones. This is common in a vast majority of folks with those conditions.

On the flip side, it's not really common in overweight folks - by and large, they're not out there saying, "I'm so tiny! Must eat more to be a healthy weight!"
