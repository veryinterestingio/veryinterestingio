Nuffsaid98 A:

No one is so fast that they can see a bullet coming and step in front of it. Bodyguards stand in front of their client and act as a wall or shield. An assassin who wishes to shoot the client can't get a clear shot. In most instances this means that they don't take the shot. Killing or wounding a bodyguard doesn't really help. The other bodyguards will swarm and cover the client and get them into cover.

Bodyguards put themselves in the line of fire in a general sense. They may and sometimes do get shot by accident because they are standing next to someone who is a target. They don't jump or dive in front of a bullet like in the movies. They are already standing in front of or next to the target usually.
