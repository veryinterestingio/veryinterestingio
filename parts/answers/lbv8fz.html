Treefiddyt A:

If I remember correctly, your body releases melatonin to help you go to sleep. It's pretty rhythmic. Your body will start to release it around the time you normally go to sleep. It will eventually peak and if you resist it ect the process starts over making it harder to go to sleep.

" In humans and most diurnal mammals, melatonin is secreted at night with a robust circadian rhythm and maximum plasma levels that occur around 3 to 4 AM. The daily rise of melatonin secretion correlates with a subsequent increase in sleep propensity about 2 hours before the person’s regular bedtime. The time before this secretion is the least likely for sleep to occur, and when it starts, the propensity for sleep increases greatly as the “sleep gate” opens.
