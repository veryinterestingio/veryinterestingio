DerCatzefragger A:

SOME chicken meat is white, but they've got dark meat too.

Well, cows are simply nothing but dark meat.

Red (dark) meat is packed full of myoglobin, which makes these muscles a "slow burn." These are the muscles that are constantly being used, at least a little bit. A chicken stands and walks around all day, so it's legs and back are dark meat, just like a cow and all of it's muscles that are used to maintain posture and just eat all day.

White meat is packed full of glycogen, which makes these muscles a white-hot flame. These muscles aren't used all the time, but when when they are needed, they have to work hard and fast (e.g. flying). This is why only the chicken's pecs and arms are white meat.

If cows were known to sprint full out for 30 or 40 miles at a time several times a day, then their legs and arms would be white meat too in order to power this high-drain, very energetic activity. But they don't, so they aren't.
