AlsoIHaveAGroupon A:

Quality is subjective, but fame isn't, and what's popular changes over time.

Beethoven and Bach became famous by composing types of music that is no longer popular. You won't hear a sonata on the top 40 countdown (if those even still exist? I'm old). In 2020, no matter how much skill you have, composing orchestral music will not make you famous like Kanye.

The "best" jazz trumpeter ever might be playing now, but they'll never be as famous as Louis Armstrong or Miles Davis. The "best" psychedelic/prog rock band ever might be playing now, but they'll never be as famous as Pink Floyd.

GauntletsofRai A:

Music is subjective, but in all art I think public consensus says whoever did it first did it best. New composers usually have a samey sound because a lot of it is used for movie scores and most movies are also kind of samey in tone and visual. Then you have modern composers like John Williams, who I'm pretty sure has taught his music to more people than Beethoven or Bach thanks to the popularity of Star Wars. His music is very well composed, but it is popular because of its exposure in pop culture. So I think that is they key here. Classical artists where popular in their time because they were the first to write music like that. They became the archetype. If you're not impressed by new music it's because:

  1. It's unoriginal or sounds derivative.
  2. It's honestly low quality.
  3. Nobody has given it a listen because it has low exposure.
