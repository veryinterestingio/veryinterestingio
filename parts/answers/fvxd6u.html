Jakewb A:

Volume is also a factor. A glass of water can be rapidly warmed up to be the same temperature as your body, and which point it stops feeling cold. If I poured a glass of water onto you (and somehow preventing it from all pouring off again!) it wouldn’t feel cold for very long.

That’s very different to if you get into, say, a cold bath or the sea, where there is so much water that your body can’t warm it up to the same temperature as you so it still feels cold.
