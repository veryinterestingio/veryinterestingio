105kglifter A:

Extremely low body fat is not healthy because it causes undue stress on your body and can lead to vitamin deficiencies, hormonal imbalances, and a stressful lifestyle due to constantly being food deprived.

That being said, having strong muscles and a healthy metabolism is absolutely healthy. Leaner people have better insulin sensitivity (aka lower risk of diabetes), lower blood pressure (aka lower risk of heart problems) and a well moderated hormonal profile (better sleep, better mood, higher energy levels, less risk of low testosterone which has been associated with all sorts of health problems). Being strong and physically capable is incredibly important to your health. You can have visible abs and a good physical appearance as a male even at 12-15% body fat. Will you look like a bodybuilder? No. Will you look good in and out of your clothes? Yes. Same goes for women who are around the 20% range.

The key is focusing on an active healthy lifestyle which incorporates all modalities of fitness (cardio and strength training) and wellness (proper diet, low stress, good sleep habits, etc). Fitness models are extremes, it is really hard to look like them, which is why their appearances are en vogue. They look different from the average person and it takes a lot of work to maintain an appearance like theirs.
