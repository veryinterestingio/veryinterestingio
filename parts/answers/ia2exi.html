The elevated heart rate during exercise is not inherently what makes it good, although it is a good indicator of how hard you’re working. There’s a bunch of other things going on at the same time; your lungs are working in unison with your heart to deliver oxygenated blood throughout your body. Moving is what an elevated heart rate is for.

The elevation in heart rate you’re experiencing while rolling on the couch after a few caps of quality MDMA is essentially increasing your resting heart rate. Your heart is working harder and pumping blood around your body (increasing your blood pressure), but you’re not moving in the way that you should be to increase your heart rate to this level.

Maybe you’re dancing at a club, and now both your heart rate and your blood pressure are higher than they should be for the movement that you’re doing.

In fit individuals this is probably not a huge issue (although high doses are a different story, as well as consistent use), but someone who is not physically fit could experience issues if they already have blood pressure problems. This can also be true for unfit individuals exercising too hard, too often and too soon.

Your body is a complex system of moving parts, and modifying one factor does not create a healthy whole.

Edit: I’m not a medical professional, so don’t take this comment as gospel. This is just my high-level understanding of the topic. It’s more practical experience in both areas (physical fitness and drug consumption) rather than formal education.
