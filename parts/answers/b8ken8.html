The throbbing is due to the slightly increased blood flow to that region of burnt skin. After the burn, your skin receptors become a bit more sensitive in that locality and so you tend to sense that pulsating blood flow easier.

The body sends more blood there to fight infection, begin wound healing, stem any blood loss, etc

johncmd22 A:

Third degree (full thickness) burns kills skin nerves- this makes it numb. These likely do not grow back. Cutaneous nerves often grow back but generally do not cause pain unless they malform into a neuroma

Burns destroy varying thickness of the epidermis (skin). This mounts an inflammatory response. This causes pain, redness, swelling.

Exposing an area to a heat source- dilates blood vessels in the area- causing more pressure which may cause throbbing and more pain. It can increase swelling as well, but unlikely in short bursts as described
