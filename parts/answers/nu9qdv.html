cara27hhh A:

It's an issue of balance. There's a lot of weight above the legs, and the legs aren't very wide or else then we'd look like a pyramid (which would be much more stable)

Your body gets around this by making microscopic adjustments every time you might fall. This is why babies fall a lot when they're first learning to walk, they haven't quite gotten it figured out yet. Those adjustments that we call 'balance' are little twitches of muscles you do without realising which keeps the weight of your torso above your feet, and that exertion of energy to twitch those muscles is what makes you tired when standing for a while. It's also why leaning on something for support helps

The head and neck are slightly more triangular, and it weighs less. Easier on the muscles to keep it upright. It does get tired and people still lean it on things though, like resting your face on your hands or a headrest
