iamagainstit A:

when you eat something, you actually experience three different senses:

  • Taste is detected by the taste buds in the tongue and includes the 5 options in you listed.
  • Most of the flavor comes from retronasal olfaction, where the volatiles chemicals come off the food and travel up your throat and into the back of your nose where you smell them.
  • Then there is the trigeminal sensations. This is a broader category of other sensations your mouth experiences, namely heat, cold, astringency, and fizzyness. This sense gives information on actual temperature, however there are certain chemicals, (menthol, Capsaicin) that trick your nerves into miss-reporting the temperature as colder or hotter than it really is.

So when you eat mint, your tongue will send a signal of sweet and bitter, your nose will send a signal for the spearmint or wintergreen flavor, and the menthol will make the trigeminal nerve send the cold sensation.
