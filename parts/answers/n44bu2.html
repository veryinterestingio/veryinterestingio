Yes, the ancients certainly did go on holidays in other countries and wanted to see the sights.

Pausanias' Guide to Greece was written in the 2nd century AD. He wrote travelogues to enable Romans to plan trips to Greece to “see the sights”. He had detailed directions, descriptions, and the myths associated with each interesting locations.

The Romans visited places in ancient Egypt in the same way we visit Roman ruins. We have their written descriptions, as well as their graffiti.

Herodotus, in the 430’s BCE toured many places in Egypt and wrote about them. He was likely there on business and enjoyed seeing things when time was available.

ImReverse_Giraffe A:

The peasantry used religious pilgrimages as vacations. They would often, yearly or bi yearly, go on pilgrimages to see some relic or site and use that trip as a vacation. They would travel from town to town sleeping in churches or other people's homes.

Nobility did it too, but they didn't require the religion aspect due to financial reasons. Also they more often than not stayed in the lords house.
