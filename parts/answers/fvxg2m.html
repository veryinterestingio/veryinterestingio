Often when you oversleep, you are waking up in the middle of a sleep cycle which causes a phenomenon known as " sleep inertia"

When you wake up naturally at the end of a sleep cycle your mind is ready to go, when you experience sleep inertia you are waking up in the middle of a sleep cycle and your brain is actually prepared for more sleep and not being awake and it takes significantly longer for your brain to come around

If you would like to plan your sleep so you do not wake up in the middle of a sleep cycle check out www.sleepyti.me

Sometimes it could be that you're mistaking the cause for the effect. When I get sick or have bad allergies, I tend to be very tired. So I sleep a lot, but I'm still very tired. But it isn't because I slept too much, it's because I'm sick. So you think you're tired because you slept too much, but really, both sleeping too much and still being tired are the result of an illness.

I'm not claiming it is the only reason, just a possible one.

Because as you get adequate sleep, your body is more aware of your deprivation. When you’re deprived, your body adjusts to working with less sleep, so you feel “less tired” vs sleeping well, when really your body and mind are working at a lower gear.
