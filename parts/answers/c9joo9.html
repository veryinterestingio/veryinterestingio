Humans didn't evolve to work in an office building, eat hamburgers and soda or any of the other thousand things we do every day in the modern world. Humans spent hundreds of thousands of years as hunter/gatherers, and we're exceptionally well adapted to that lifestyle.

Our starch and sugar rich diets are full of energy, but those starches and sugars also stick to your teeth and dissolve the enamel. Fruits and meat don't cause this problem nearly as much.

If you live in the city, you spend a lot of time indoors very close to lots of other people, so body odor and general hygiene cause a hell of a lot more problems than if we were all outdoors running around.

Speaking of running around, humans evolved to run a lot. That's literally how we would hunt, just chase a deer until it collapsed from exhaustion. But these days we drive to work, sit in a chair for 8 hours, drive home, then sit on a couch for 8 hours. Your heart isn't getting the exercise it evolved to deal with.

Also, keep in mind that you can eat poorly, not clean yourself, and never exercise, and still live to like 50. That's plenty long to breed in the wild.
