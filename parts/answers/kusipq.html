Car dealerships are like franchises and part of the rules is that you have to sell an exclusive brand. They don't want a dealership selling competing brands on the same lot.

Dealerships though are often owned by dealership groups which have multiple dealers for different brands owned by the same person. So if you don't like want Honda is selling, realize that the Toyota dealer across the street might be owned by the same person.

The exception is exotic car dealers. While they may be branded with a specific mark, they tend to sell all sorts of exotic cars. The reason being that these cars are such a specialist product that you have to sell more than one brand usually to make money. Also a person set on buying a Ferrari likely won't be convinced to buy a Lotus instead, with exotic cars the brand and image is often more important than the car itself.

Pure Lambo or Ferrari dealerships do exist but you'll only see them in certain place like LA where there's enough customers to warrant it.
