Anarch157a A:

There is one in the Southern Hemisphere. Emperor Penguins migrate further inland in early autumn (March) for breeding. After the females lay the eggs, the males stay there during the winter while the females go back to sea to feed. In spring, the females return to feed the chicks, while the males go to shore to feed themselves.

Happens idiosyncratically in some species like the Heermann's gull, which spends summer on the Pacific Coast of Mexico, then disperses north all the way to British Columbia. Probably in response to food sources. The west coast of North America has mild winters, so weather is probably not a factor.

Herzogs-Penguin A:

Polar bears move south in the summer to the shoreline and wait for sea ice to form then they go back out to hunt seals all winter. Summer is their lean times, they pack on the fat during the winter. They need the sea ice which is why arctic warming is threatening the species.
