Browncoat40 A:

There’s a couple big differences. The orbital astronauts fly a lot higher and much much faster than the suborbital astronauts. That means high forces for much longer. For reference, the Bezos’s flight was 10 minutes from liftoff to touchdown, and went to about 100km. A crewed SpaceX/space shuttle mission burns for 8-9 minutes, gets up to 400km, and is going along the earth far faster than it ever does vertically.

Second, handling zero-g for a few minutes doesn’t put much strain on your body. Staying in zero-g for weeks can really deteriorate your body, and cause other issues.

Last, it’s the difference between a sightseeing trip, and working for several weeks on a few multimillion dollar projects where simple mistakes could be deadly, and you have to train for dozens of scenarios if something goes wrong.
