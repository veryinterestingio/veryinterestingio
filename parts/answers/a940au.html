The law of gravitation applies equally to all objects near a planet. So if the planet has a moon of its own, we can observe the moon's motion and use that to determine its mass.

If the planet doesn't have any moons, then you're right that its mass is a bit of a guess. But the first time we send a space probe past it, it's like giving it an artificial moon: we can measure the motion of the spacecraft as it flies by, and use that to accurately determine its mass.

All "gravitational slingshot" maneuvers we've done so far have been on planets that either have a moon or that we've visited before.
