      • marcan42

        "Long polling" is a technicality of how the protocols work. The point is that once the TCP connection is established, the phone is no longer actually polling for data; once there is a notification, it's the server that will send a packet without having received any from the phone recently.

        (Of course there are keepalives too, but those happen on a slow schedule and do not drive the actual message delivery)

        I'm other words, the point is that the phone does not have to keep asking for notifications every second, just to get notifications within a second. It just tells the server once that it'd like any notifications to be delivered in the future, perhaps reminding it every few minutes at most, and then it does nothing until a notification comes directly, in real time, without any further questions required.
