    Waterfowl hunter here. Echoing the comment above. I see geese flying north right now. However I know they’re just going from roost to feed in the morning. In the evening they’ll be going south to their roost.

    If there’s hunting going on in your area, the birds will get accustomed to avoiding fields that are hunted and start only visiting fields and ponds that aren’t hunted because they’re safe. Then they’ll be following a flight path that looks very deliberate as if they’re migrating but in reality they are heading straight to roost or feed and not making eye contact with anyone.

    • MNEvenflow

      Geese (and mallards) will stay around as long as there was open water up to ~2 days prior AND their food source isn't too covered to feed from.

      So it's possible they'll stick around through super cold weather if their food source remains uncovered. Or it's possible they'll leave open water to head south of too much snow to eat.
