      There are many ways to do this. Many experiments are actually done with light. Circularly or linearly polarized photons. Clockwise, counter-clockwise, horizontal, and vertical polarizations form the basis states similar to 'up down x y' I mention above. We can create a single photon at a time, pass it through a special kind of crystal that creates two lower energy photons when one photon enters, and those two lower energy photons have to obey conservation of momentum properties.

      Another common design is nitrogen vacancies in diamond. If we very carefully control diamond growth with the right amount of nitrogen, every now and then an atom that should be a carbon in the diamond structure will actually be a nitrogen. This leaves an electron isolated in a physical location and we can manipulate that electron where it is. (I don't know this one as well, so pardon if I've made a mistake here).

      There are other designs like superconducting rings with a little split in them that can hold quantized magnetic field amounts, and so on that I'm even less familiar with, but there's a lot of ways to build a qubit (the name of these things)
