    • Year_of_the_Alpaca

      aka they had to reduce the sound quality on the digital copies to fit the entire album on a single CD

      This is nonsense. You can't fit more onto a standard audio CD by varying the quality- the spec is fixed at 16-bits / 44.1 kHz, uncompressed, and can't be altered.

      That in turn means a fixed bitrate, and therefore a minute of CD audio will always take up the same amount of space, limiting the maximum recording time to 80 minutes regardless (on a 700MB CD).

      Some CDs may sound better than others, but that's down to the quality of the mastering. Horrible-sounding, badly-mastered CD audio takes up just as much space as the best.

      Perhaps you're confusing CD with later sound formats like MP3 that allow variable levels of compression and different sample rates. DVD video is also compressed- you can squeeze an almost arbitrary amount of video on a single disc (though below a certain point the quality is going to get unwatchably horrible).
