    • jbert

      > This is why competition is essential to drive down prices for consumers

      Yes, but it's important to avoid being simplistic (please note: I don't mean you are, just it seems that a lot of people stop with this thought and I want to elaborate to avoid readers doing that).

      Competition can certainly stop price gouging. But it comes with a bunch of caveats. Pressure to reduce product quality, misleading advertising, time spent by consumers researching the market, buying quality brands and running down the quality while selling on a premium, outright adulteration of products, market manipulation/collaboration, etc.

      Unregulated and unethical competition is bad for everyone. You can choose to regulate the profits (so you don't need strong competition) or you can choose to regulate the competition (truth in advertising, product safety, monopolies+mergers oversight) but you can't have a healthy product space without regulation of some sort.

      Analogy: people probably run quicker times in races if they have other people running alongside them, also trying to win. They probably run slower if they have to worry about those other people trying to trip them up.
