      While hyperalgesia occurring at the site of injury is termed primary hyperalgesia, enhanced pain sensitivity in surrounding uninjured skin is termed secondary hyperalgesia. Primary hyperalgesia is contributed to by sensitization of peripheral nerve endings (peripheral sensitization), whereas secondary hyperalgesia is due to changes in the spinal cord and higher brain areas (central sensitization).
