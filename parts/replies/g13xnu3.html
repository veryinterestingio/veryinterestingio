    • Skitburd

      I think Bach's music, while certainly not a "slapfest" anymore, can really only be appreciated within it's place in the musical canon

      Yeah, Bach numerology is a complex and while fascinating probably coincidental study, but it's important to realize that the entire field of music theory was shaped around his writings. Like imagine if we based English Grammar off of Mark Twain's writing. There were other systems of music theory before him (Pallestrina slaps if you haven't heard him), but it's not the most risky thing to say that all classical music after Bach is derivative of Bach.

      Big deal right? It still sounds busy. I would recommend listening to some pre-Bach music, and then stepping back into Bach's music. Sure if you were to start from Medieval chant and slowly work your way up, every step up would feel like a breath of fresh air (Plainchant is another world of music imo), but Bach is the first thing that sounds comparable to real, modern, music. Within its historical context, Bach is a breath of fresh air.

      Anyway, here's an unsurprising hot take by someone who sat through a semester of Josquin, Leonin, and Hildegard to finally reach the end where he wept at the sound of Bach.

      Yeah altogether he's kinda boring. Even his contemporaries thought he was old fashioned, particularly scathing commentary especially coming from his son, CPE Bach.

      TL;DR from a historical context Bach is pretty darn good actually. Haydn did flex on him though
