    Fun fact, humidity used to be measured in Canada with hair. Typically straight blonde hair worked best. The hair would lengthen or contract depending on humidity and would be attached to a pen that wrote on a rotating cylinder.

    Now we are using electronic instruments. The temperature sensors in the field are compared against travelling standards, which are compared against regional standards, which is compared against a Canadian standard, which eventually gets back to the world standard.

    A similar thing is done with the pressure sensors.

    All are NIST traceable back to the source.
