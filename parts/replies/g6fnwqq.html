    Nice explanation. Some random, related, facts:

    1) The spinal cord can trigger muscle motions! These are reflexes, like letting go of hot things. The spinal cord says to let go before the brain can process what's going on. This isn't really memory per se but it's an adaption from evolution.

    2) Muscles don't have memory: the central nervous system has the memories. However, muscles do "remember" in two ways: 1) Their neurons can learn to fire muscle fibers all at the same time. This neural adaption helps people become stronger without necessarily gaining more muscle (three people pushing a car all at the same time is stronger than 3 people pushing a car out of sync.) 2) Muscles seem to "remember" when they used to be bigger. So, if you gain mass in a particular place, then lose that muscle mass, you can gain it back more quickly. In this way, your muscles "remember" they used to be bigger and can get bigger faster the next time.
