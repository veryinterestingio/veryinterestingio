    • isuphysics

      It actually does stand for a specific spec. This is so that you don't have one company just calling their stuff 5g because marketing.

      1g: Analog signals

      2g: Digital signals with SMS/MMS and Data up to 64kbps

      3g: Bandwidth of at least 144kbps.

      4g: Bandwidth of at least 2mbps (more generically: speeds that enable the use of multimedia, video, etc)

      5g: More focused on latency in order to make mobile a primary internet source. Increases the peak speeds available up to 10gbs. But 5g and 4g overlap greatly on usable speed range.

      There are more to the standards, but these are the basics of what a customer cares about.
