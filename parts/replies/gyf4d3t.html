    There are culinary truffles (Tuber uncinatum) grown on Gotland (Swedens largest island). There's also one called stjärnhovstryffel (Choiromyces meandriformis) that grow on the mainland of Sweden in and around a region called Stjärnhov (the culinary merits of this one are.... debatable).

    Then there are many other types of fungi in the truffle family that grow in northern Scandinavia that are either toxic or just inedible.
