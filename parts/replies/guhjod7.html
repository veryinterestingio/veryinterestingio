    Thirst is an evolutionary indicator that at some point in the future you will need to find water. It's is not an indicator that you must find water NOW. If that was the case we would never have become pursuit hunters. Our ability to sweat profusely to maintain core body temperature and at the same time tolerate up to severe dehydration allowed us to outlast our prey animals. Once the hunt was concluded we would then rehydrate.

    Unfortunately modern marketing makes a lot of people believe that you need to drink constantly or you will perform badly. So drink your Coca Cola bottled water!

    The truth is in real life activities often the most dehydrated athlete is the winner. They put aside the discomfort of thirst until after the event is completed. Several studies show the winners of marathon events are also the most dehydrated. So the people with the best performance are also the most dehydrated. Which blows up the suggestion that dehydration causes a decrease in performance.
