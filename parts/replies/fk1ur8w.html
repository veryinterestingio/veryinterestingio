  • parposbio

    You're taking that information out of context.

    First and foremost the source says, "On average a player will order appproximately 120 bats in a season." That does not mean a player will go through 120 bats in 162 games.

    Second, a key point you're ignoring is that baseball players take batting practice, hit off a tee, and do other hitting drills almost every single day. All of those batted ball events will degrade the wood quality/durability regardless of number of games that specific bat "appears" in.

    For example, a player could theoretically have four at bats in a game without ever recording a single batted ball event (0 for 1 with three walks and strikeout, for example). But that same player could have broken a bat in BP earlier in the day.
