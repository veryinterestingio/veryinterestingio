  • Rabid_Mongoose

    So, I grew up in Pleasanton CA, which was the first Silicon Valley before it got too expensive and everyone left for Palo Alto.

    Pleasanton was actually an agricultural town and a federal prison town (which changed form FCI Pleasanton to FCI Dublin) we moved there because my dad got a job with the county ag department.

    Essentially, there was cheap land. A bunch of farmers sold their land and got it zoned for businesses, but it literally sat vacant for probably 10-15 years. I'm not sure who was the first, but PeopleSoft moved in, followed by Cisco and Oracle and then the boom happened. Pleasanton got greedy and charged too high of rent and everyone moved to Palo Alto, which was much cheaper. It was a lot easier when BART moved into everywhere.

    I remember when Livermore was kind of a dump, and the Dublin wanted to be part of Blackhawk, but it was too poor and forced them to make their own city.

    I haven't been back in years, although my parents made out on the house. Their 130k house sold for 1.4 million.
