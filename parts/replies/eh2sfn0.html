    I can answer this. It USUALLY goes back to blood flow. Depending on the headache, caffeine can trigger or inhibit a headache. Blood vessels tend to open as a headache comes on and caffeine constricts blood vessels, this inhibiting a headache.

    But it’s REALLY easy now a days to over consume caffeine (especially when trying to break a headache). This causes a rebound or crash which can make a headache (especially a migraine) SOOO much worse.

    With food, the same as above can apply (about constricting blood vessels) but usually chemical imbalances can cause headaches and if you haven’t been nourished in a while, chemical imbalances are an absolute certainty.
