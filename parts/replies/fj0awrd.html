    • Mmedical

      Generally in pneumonia, unless it is involving both lungs or the person's lungs are already damaged, this inflammatory effect is helpful to resolve the infection. There are multiple examples of why does the body do this? Examples:

      • Anaphylactic reaction to a bee sting.

      • Heart continues to want to pump harder and faster despite a heart attack - injuring the heart more.

      • Persisting vomiting and diarrhea despite dehydration.

      • Compartment syndrome where the swelling from injury causes damage to the surrounding tissue because the fascia can not expand.

      These are outlying events for which the body has a usual compensatory mechanism - except the mechanism itself causes damage. The body triggers a reaction but there is nothing to limit the response.
