    • KageSama19

      Basically soap is hydrophobic on one end and hydrophillic on the other end. Oil is hydrophobic and will try not touching water, but will stick to other hydrophobic surfaces. Normally what happens when we clean with soap is the hydrophobic end sticks to the oil, and the hydrophillic end sticks to the water and is taken with it as it rinses over. With plastic, since it is inherently hydrophobic, it will more easily bond with the oil and not allow water to come between to two materials, so you need to slowly rinse layer after layer off the top.
