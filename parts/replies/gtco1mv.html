  • 1tacoshort

    Divers see this all the time. They dial in their buoyancy so that breathing in makes them positively buoyant and breathing out makes them sink (this isn't the effect, yet). If they breathe out and hold that until they've sunk a meter or so, breathing in isn't sufficient to become positively buoyant again and, even with full lungs, they keep sinking. It's a real dance to dial in and maintain your buoyancy -- especially as you change depths.
