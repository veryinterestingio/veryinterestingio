    • zekromNLR

      No, it does not, because in places like the UK you have a high proportion of vaccinated people.

      Say, hypothetically (so I do not get egg on my face by getting real numbers wrong) you have a population that is 80% vaccinated with a 90% effective vaccine. Say also that vaccinated and unvaccinated people are exposed at the same rate, and that exposure for an unvaccinated person always results in infection (probably unrealistic assumptions, but they make the math easy).

      Then out of 100 exposed people, 20 are unvaccinated and get infected. 80 are vaccinated, and 8 of them suffer a breakthrough infection, since the vaccine is 90% effective at preventing infection. This means there are then 28 total cases, of which ~28.6% are breakthrough cases.
