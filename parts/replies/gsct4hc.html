      The other commenters here are correct, but I’d like to add that if you’re working with heavily used and/or large surfaces like the kitchen floor of a restaurant, it’s best to use a brush to scrub the floor with the soapy water and use the mop to get up the water. For your kitchen or living room floor at home, just using the mop with soapy water is usually enough unless you have some stuck on residue. Some mop buckets also have two compartments: one that you fill with soapy water, and one that catches the water you squeeze out of the mop. This allows you keep dipping the mop head into the clean water without getting it to dirty, which means you can clean one or two more rooms without having to replace your soapy water.

    • stuckinmyownass

      You definitely rinse the mop every time.

      1) wet mop

      2) mop floor

      3) wet mop

      4) squeeze water out of mop

      Repeat until the water is brownish.

      The water carries the dirt; so squeezing water out of the mop after rinsing it helps remove excess dirty water from the mop and reduces the amount of dirt you put back on the floor.
