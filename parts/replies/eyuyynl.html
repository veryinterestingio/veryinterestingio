      • dale_glass

        A hotend takes cold filament on one end, and outputs molten plastic on the other. There's usually a fan cooling part of it, so that the transition zone is as small as possible. You want a very abrupt change from solid to molten. This is accomplished by cooling the upper half, heating the lower one, and having as thin of a connection between both as possible (the heat break)

        See this graph

        So, if you pull the power, the upper half is now cooled less effectively because the fan isn't working anymore, while the lower half is still hot. This can cause the filament to melt in the upper part. And then it can get stuck there.

        It's generally not a fatal problem, but it's annoying to have to disassemble it.
