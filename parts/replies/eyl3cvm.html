    It has to do with the concentration of rods. Rods are not concentrated at the center of the retina. Instead, they are found off center. Therefore, if you look slightly to one side of the star, its faint light will fall on a more light-sensitive region of the retina.

    To expand on this, the center of your eye has more cones, which are less sensitive to light, but are able to distinguish color. It is easier to see the fine color detail at the center of your vision.
