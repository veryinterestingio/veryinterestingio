    The sun is not a laser, that's correct, but you can make an incoherent light source spatially and temporally coherent. You don't need a laser to produce spatially and temporally coherent light.

    To transform an incoherent source into a coherent source, you can use pinholes and filters.

      You can make it coherent with pinholes and filters, but laser light is more than just coherent. The photon count statistics for laser light have much smaller fluctuations in intensity than coherently filtered sunlight even at the same average power.

      • CurriestGeorge

        Okay sure to make spatially coherent light take a good strong flashlight. Or use the sun as the source. For a small example use a cardboard box. Put a pinhole in one end and a matching pinhole in the other. Shine your light source through and what light makes it through both pinholes has to be moving in a very narrow path. Use a bigger box with holes further apart or smaller holes to increase the coherence of the light. This would be spatially coherent but you'd be getting all the frequencies that the source put out more or less.
