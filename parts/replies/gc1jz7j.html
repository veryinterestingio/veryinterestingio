  • khansian

    But you answered the wrong question. The question was why digital is sold at a higher price than disk.

    The reason seems to be price discrimination. People who want to buy digital have revealed that 1) they like the convenience of downloading, and 2) aren't interested in re-selling in the secondary market. As such, they should probably be willing to pay a premium.

    --

    As to the question you seemed to be answering. There is a general tendency of the market price -> marginal cost of production due to competition.

    But the market for video games is what is called monopolistic competition. It's where producers have a monopoly on their product, but they face competition from close. but imperfect substitutes. COD and Battlefield are competing products but not the same.

    A monopolist sets price equal to where marginal revenue is equal to marginal cost--because they need to recoup the high fixed costs associated with development. This is why video games, which have very low marginal costs in either disk or digital form, are sold well-above the marginal cost of production.
