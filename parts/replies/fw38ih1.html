      • gobblox38

        Yes, the promotion system is different for enlisted than for commissioned officers.

        A solder is promotable (for noncommissioned ranks) when a specific set of requirements are met, typically when time in service and time in grade are achieved. They can also be put on the promotable list if the leadership wants them on the list. The soldier then needs to make the points requirement. Depending on the state of the army, they may be promoted with or without completion of WLC, BNOC, or ANOC.

        Battlefield commissions are typically reserved during manpower shortages when replacement officers can't come in fast enough. The more traditional way for an enlisted soldier to gain a commission is the green to gold program (army). I'm not sure if there are similar programs in the other branches.
