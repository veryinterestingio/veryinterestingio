    So long as you do not burn or otherwise effectively decompose the old tree (i.e. you just bury it or construct durable objects out of most of it). This doesn't necessarily happen in practice with trees that have been cut down. Same is true of agricultural biomass - agriculture could be an effective contributor to the sequestration of carbon if we didn't burn the straw (unused stalks from cereal grains, usually) and other bi-products of large scale agriculture but in practice, this stuff is generally burnt and the products eaten so you just end up footing the carbon bill of the manufacturing and transportation that support it.
