    • Reginault

      Acid isn't the most important part of the puzzle, usually digestive enzymes do most of the work.

      To get acid tho, they either eat other things that have eaten the appropriate chemical somewhere down the line (predators eating organs rich in nutrients, herbivores eating a specific fruit or plant) or they have specific bacteria that "eat" part of the food inside their stomach on a micro scale and leave behind something that the animal can absorb.

      Mammals get a head-start from their mothers' digestive system, egg-born creatures are encased with a bit of nutrients to get them going. Animals that feed their young through regurgitation share enzymes/bacteria.
