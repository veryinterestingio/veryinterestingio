  • gnolema5011

    100% waking up in the wrong part of the cycle. A sleep cycle is roughly 2 hours. If you take a quick nap, and wake up 20 minutes later, you have not yet entered deep sleep. Therefore you feel rested and ready to go.

    If you sleep the entire two hours, by the time you wake up, your brain has left deep sleep and is in a state more ready to accept waking. There you feel rested and ready to go.

    If you wake up in the middle of the cycle, it’s like going from “mild coma” to “wide awake” without taking the time to slowly achieve wakefulness. This is why you feel groggy!

    TL,DR: if you’re going to nap, make it </= 30 min, or a full 2 hours, otherwise you’re gonna have a bad time.
