    Netflix measures a lot of other things, too. Including retention. So they may look to see if members who watch a show are more likely to stay members than those who do not.

    Edit: I'll also add another possible benefit. The movies and shows Netflix licenses have two drawbacks: they are often (maybe always) licensed for a limited time, AND have fees they have to pay to the owner, per view. (There may be exceptions there, but that's the general arrangement, as I understand it.)

    When Netflix makes their own show, though, it's theirs as long as they want it. And they avoid any ongoing licensing fees. They trade that for the production costs up front, but there's two benefits to that. Long-term, the subscriptions gained/retained can cover those costs and make a profit, AND it's much easier to budget for. Once they've made the film/series, no more costs. But a per-view licensing deal is ongoing and variable. Ongoing and variable is not loved by finance departments.
