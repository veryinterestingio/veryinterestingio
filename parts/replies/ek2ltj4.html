    a few corrections. I've been in about 15 credits to feature movies and animations.

    Not every single person is a union member. For example - almost 0% of digital special effects artists are union members. They work for what seems like ok wages but then you count the unpaid OT - of which there is a TON of, then they're often making min wage on an hourly basis. There are lots of non-union labor involved in movies.

    Post production doesn't make up the bulk of the production. Even on a fairly VFX heavy production.

    Marketing and advertising can definitely push in the tens of millions of dollars but are never computed or counted as part of "production". Marketing is huge but it's just not accounted together with production costs. It's totally separate.
