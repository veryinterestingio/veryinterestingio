      • haf_ded_zebra

        Brocas area controls speech and Wernickes area controls mathematical calculation so maybe also the names of numbers. I had brain surgery between those areas and I lost most of my nouns. I knew the people visiting me were my roommates from college but I had to say “I...know you...are MY friends, But I ..don’t ..KNOW...your NAmes...” I cried a little bit and they all turned red and told me their names. It was so sad. Then I would save things until my best friend/roommate came at lunch time, and hold things up for her to name. Fork. Spoon. Knife. U-TEN-sils. It was very frustrating, and even after people thought I was better, I mostly just strung together SAT words - words that I had memorized in the past few years- rather than more basic English words. Native language is stored i. the left side of the brain, memorized language takes a while to move over from the right side to the left.

        Weirdest thing was, my boss walked in while I was trying to talk to the doctors in very VERY halting english, and he greeted me in Japanese. I answered him easily and fluently. The doctor was so excited. The next day there were like 20 medical students around my bed. And he billed me $600.
