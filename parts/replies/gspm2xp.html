    To be pedantic, it's the common ancestor of North Indian languages - the bottom 1/3 of India speak Dravidian languages, which are unrelated - as well as Iran, and most of the languages of Europe - with the exceptions of Basque (which is in a language family of its own) and the Finno-Urgic languages (Finnish, Estonian, Hungarian, and some rare languages in a chunk of central Russia). And arguably Maltese - if you count it as a European language, it should be counted in the Semitic language family.
