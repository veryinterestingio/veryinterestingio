    • BiologyJ

      Yes but it’s less likely. A respiratory infection is looking for a moist area with a thin and active membrane. This happens in your lungs where gas exchange takes place. It’s also why respiratory infections can’t go through your outer skin. The lungs also have macrophages secreted into the fluid lining since it’s so common for viruses to attack there.

      A dry cough simply means that excess mucus production doesn’t happen to a large extent. Some viruses tend to cause a runny nose and large productions of mucus in the airway while others do not. It’s not so much this infection is lowering mucus production as it’s not necessarily raising it(though for some it will).

      A vaccinated person has antibodies (and B cells) that are primed to attack any infection that starts. For an unvaccinated person a sustained illness in your body has to do this from scratch which takes far longer. That allows the virus to replicate and enter new cells. Eventually the host will catch up and the immune system will attack the infection. In a vaccinated person that time is chopped off significantly and small infections are stopped before they get going.
