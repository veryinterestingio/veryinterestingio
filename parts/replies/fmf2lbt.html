    The thing I can't wrap my head around is that a denser universe would warp space time to a larger degree and so as the universe expands it becomes less dense and warps space time less and therefore things "seem" closer right??? Would it not be equally accurate to say that time is slowing down if?

    • pfisico

      No, a uniformly more-dense early universe does not have more curved space; if space is not curved now (aka "flat"), it's not curved then.

      You're thinking of things like black holes, etc, which are non-homogenous distributions of matter; when we're talking about the universe as a whole, we're looking at a homogeneous (or close enough to that) distribution of matter. You can think of it as that near a black hole you feel the pull in one direction, while in the early universe the "pull" from nearby mass would be in all directions, so things cancel out.
