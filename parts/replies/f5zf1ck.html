    • Boden

      Dawkins goes into a lot of detail in one of his books. I forget the name but there are a lot of details about how bats use sonar to sense their world.

      The quick answer is, they cant. When the information comes back from another bats chirp, the data is garbled up, it doesn’t make sense from the flying bat.

      Think of it like having sight with a lower framerate, when a bat is flying, it’ll chirp at a certain rate and get constant updates as to its and others positions. When another bat chirps, the first bat will “see” a frame that is out of place for itself and so gets filtered out.

      He also goes into detail of how bats catch bugs. It’d be really hard to catch a flying insect at night with a low framerate. What they do is increase thier chirp rate and therefor framerate to catch them.

      There was a lot more explained but I’d have to find the book to remember it.

        Most organisms that generate a sound or electricity for orientation will adjust a little when the frequency is jammed, like from a friend. They wouldn't use the neighbor's signal for their own orientation, but may use it to find the neighbor.

        This happens in weakly electric fish as well. They adjust their chirps a little when another chirp is too similar.
