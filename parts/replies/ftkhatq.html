      • cerrosanluis

        my professor in college was one of the leading experts in space debris tracking! they use normal ground-based telescopes and look for glint off of small objects-- that's the only way you see them. Then, you cross reference the location, time, apparent brightness (which can correspond to size and optical properties) and then plug that in to a massive database tracking historical data and propagating that forward in time! You can estimate the location of any object at any point in time by knowing its orbit.

        So most of the job is monitoring known objects and tracking orbit degradation, but the exciting bit is when you get a data point that doesn't match with any of your known objects. Then it could be a new one!
