    • InvisiblePinkUnicron

      As far as I understand it (which isn't very far), there's nothing that technically stops you from monitoring all cellular communication that's in range of your device. It would just be a matter of having the correct software. It's also possible there are firmware restrictions that prevent your phone from passing traffic not intended for it up into the OS or other software.

      The process would be similar to how you would sniff wifi or wired ethernet traffic. Some wifi adapters and most wired NICs can be put into "promiscuous mode" (wifi might use different terminology) and once in that mode, using the right software (such as wireshark), you can look at all of the network traffic in your area. Usually that will be stuff on the same switch or set of switches that your computer is plugged into or any wifi traffic within range of your antenna.
