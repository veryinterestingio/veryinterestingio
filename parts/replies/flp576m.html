    To add to this, colds spread easier in the winter time because of less humidity. Humidity causes resistance against the air you exhale. So when you sneeze or cough it doesn’t travel as far of a distance.

    • Fifferpush

      It's the other way around. Humid air is less dense than dry air. Warm air is also less dense than cold air, too, and that means less air resistance in warm or humid air. The physics of it is well studied, and it's called the ideal gas law.

      So we can basically rule out the idea that the cold slows in summer because humidity reduces sneeze velocity.
