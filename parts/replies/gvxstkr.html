    This is wrong.

    The air pressure simply isn't dramatically increased by the very small change in volume of the ball in contact.

    The scale of that effect is tiny compared with the same idea of pressure increase, except this occurs at higher order of magnitude in the solid stress deformations and elastic rebounding of the ball itself.

    More simply, your understanding of how the air pushes back is correct. That "pressure" is analogous to the stress in the ball skin.

    That skin on the ball is prestressed by the higher air pressure and therefore rebounds with greater energy.

    The air pressure stretches the skin of the ball, and like a guitar string when you pluck a more stretched string it has a higher pitch /energy.

    It's both the air and the ball skin, but in a very good bouncing ball it's the rubber that does most of the work.
