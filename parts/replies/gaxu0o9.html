      • irCuBiC

        This is not correct. The diuretic effects of alcohol are seemingly unrelated to its processing by the body, as the majority of it never ends up in urine.

        Alcohol is not filtered out, it's processed by the liver in several stages. The majority of it is ultimately turned into a acetyl-CoA, which then enters the Krebs cycle to be used as an energy source for the body, and then released as water and CO2. (This is where the calories in alcohol come from, in addition to whatever other calories come from the left over carbohydrates)

        You should still hydrate though, dehydration is one of many possible components of a hangover. (but rarely the only one)
