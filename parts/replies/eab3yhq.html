    • MisterSquirrel

      There is a certain intrinsic geometric harmony to the golden ratio, apart from the fibonacci spiral thing. So I wouldn't call it complete nonsense, even if the theories about its prevalence in art and architecture might be nonsense.

      It's the only way to divide a line segment such that the ratio of the whole length to the longest part is the same as the longest part to the shortest part.

      And only a rectangle whose sides are proportioned to the golden ratio can be attached to an adjacent square to form a larger rectangle proportionate to the original.
