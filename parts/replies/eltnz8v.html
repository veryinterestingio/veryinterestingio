  • That_Biology_Guy

    Yeah, I think this is pretty much the best explanation. Because seeds are nutritious but incapable of moving to escape herbivores, a lot of plants produce far more than are actually needed to maintain stable populations. In more formal ecological terms, these sorts of organisms are called "r-selected", and have very high mortality rates for early life stages which then flatten out (like the oysters on that graph). This is in contrast with "K-selected" organisms like humans, which tend to have fairly low mortality rates for most of our lives until we reach old age.
