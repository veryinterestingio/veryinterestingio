      Sea levels had been rising fast thousands of years before this proposed impact and the Younger Dryas event. I'm not sure how confident we can be about shorter periods given uncertainty in the data but much of it actually suggests the Younger Dryas was associated with the onset of a slower rate of sea level rise. That makes intuitive sense because the cooling of the high latitude Northern Hemisphere is likely to have temporarily halted or even reversed the deglaciation trend in that region, which was the source of most of the ice melt causing the eventual 120m deglacial sea level rise.
