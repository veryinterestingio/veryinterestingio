    I think heart failure in any context is going to be defined as a point when the heart cannot pump enough to meet there body's demand for blood flow. If the pressure gets high enough, the heart can't overcome that force to open the aortic valve, and blood stops flowing. Of course there is every scenario in-between as seen in the Frank-starling curve, which would result in various different cardiac output ratios.

    Pulmonary edema and CHF are the same thing in my book because CHF always causes pulmonary edema unless you have cardiac defect like a foramen ovale. Pulmonary edema is going to be the effect of chronic mild-moderate CHF. Total circulatory collapse is going to be the effect of severe CHF (like in the event of transfusing as much liquid as possible into a body)
