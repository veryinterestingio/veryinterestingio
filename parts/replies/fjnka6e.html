    • PraiseTheMetal591

      People with antibodies against other antigens such as c or e will react to O Neg as almost all O Neg is e and c positive.

      "O Neg" only covers a few antigens (O from ABO system and D from RhD system).

      Other antigens exist too and could be present. There are dozens of antigen systems but not all cause serious reactions. If the patient has been exposed to an antigen before then they could have an antibody against it and will have a reaction.

      Some of the serious* ones are C, c, E, e, K, k, Fya, Fyb, Jka, Jkb, Lea, Leb, S, s and sometimes M.

      *serious in this case is defined as our guidelines requiring that a patient be given blood negative for that antigen.
