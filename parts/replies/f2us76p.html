    We did that in a physics lesson once.
    It's a cool little practical to see what wavelengths of light are actually being put out by stuff.

    The trick to making it clearer is mounting a piece of cd at 45 degrees at one end of a tube. Cut a window at the cd end of the tube and cover up the other end, except for a small hole/slit. Instead of the reflection of a whole light bulb, you just get a small line/dot split into its constituent wavelengths.
