      • Lilcrash

        Blood clots from veins (where the pooling occurs) will clot either in the vein itself or in your lung. You get heart attacks from coronary embolisms which cannot come from veins.

        EDIT: Unless you have shunt between your right and left atrium (open foramen ovale) but that is rather rare.

        EDIT2: The open foramen ovale itself is not rare (~25% of adults have it), but paradoxical embolisms (that are caused by the right-left shunt) are rare (estimated at below 1%).
