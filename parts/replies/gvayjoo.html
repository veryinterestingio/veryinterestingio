    • loljetfuel

      It sounds like your college didn't actually learn what the immersion method is before implementing it.

      Immersion method teaching is not supposed to be refusing to speak in the students' primary language -- it's a technique that involves getting students to hear, read, speak, and write in the target language right away. Basically, teaching English speakers to speak Spanish using immersion looks like getting the students to speak simple sentences right away, saying things in Spanish before repeating them in English, and so on.

      Immersion method, in other words, would be "let's all speak as much Spanish as possible". It acknowledges that students won't be able to speak or understand much at first, but pushes them toward more.

      I'm learning German using the immersion method. My first classes were mostly English, and now that I'm more advanced, it's about 90% in German -- but my instructor will drop back to English to explain complex things we're not ready to discuss in German yet.
