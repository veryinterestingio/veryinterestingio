      They would have been actively chasing game, not really trying to get to the other side. The Beringia Bridge was something like 600,000 sq miles and the migration lasted for a couple of thousand years. A lot of folks, not referring to you specifically, have this image of folks standing at one side and seeing the other side with a desire to cross and that being their primary mission. The truth of the matter is somewhat less cinematic in that land bridges like this appear during global cooling periods where ice is formed, lowering sea level, revealing these bridges. Another effect of these cooling periods is that vegetation becomes scarce and herds are thinned and move to find new sources, and then nomadic people follow these herds. These two events combined is how you get mass migrations over long periods of time. It was a survival event that those in warmer climates didn't have to resort to as they were developing subsistence farming around this time (Neolithic Revolution).
