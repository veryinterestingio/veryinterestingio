    • hegemontree

      Just some background information about electromagnetic radiation penetration. Electromagnetic radiation suffers an exponential drop-off with thickness. For instance if 1 mm of bark blocked 90%, 3 mm blocks 99.9%, and 5 mm blocks 99.999%. So the difference in thickness between a radiation shield which will protect you for 10 years on average and one which protects you for 1000 years on average could easily be relatively modest.

    • silvanuyx

      A lot of organisms do have DNA repair mechanisms! I don't work on trees necessarily, but I do work on light responses in fungi. Photolyases are a pretty ubiquitous class of proteins that use light energy to repair lesions in DNA. There's also nucleotide excision, which straight up cuts out and replaces damaged DNA. Plants do have photolyases at least, and I would guess they have NER as well.
