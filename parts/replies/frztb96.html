  • brilliantretard

    This reminds me of a story that I've heard Martin Amis tell about Saul Bellow. Apparently, on his deathbed, Bellow was talking to someone who was visiting him, and after a lull in the conversation, apropos of nothing, Bellow asked his visitor something like, "Tell me--when people see me walking down the street, do they say, 'There goes a man' or 'There goes a schmuck'?" (Amis's wording, or thereabouts)

    As Amis said, here was "a man whose medals clanked when he walked." Among many other things, Bellow was awarded a Guggenheim Fellowship, the Pulitzer Prize, and the Nobel Prize. He is the only writer to date to have won the National Book Award for Fiction three times.

    If a guy like that can wonder if he's any good or not, anybody can. As you say, that's impostor syndrome.
