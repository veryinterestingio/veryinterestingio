    If you take a huge breath and hold it as long as you can, most of the air is still going to be in your lungs, especially the nitrogen which makes up about 78% of the air around you. If you were a whale and dove deep, much more of the air would get dissolved into your bloodstream and other tissues, including much of the nitrogen.

    Surfacing slowly will allow the air to come out of solution slowly. You will get itsy bitsy bubbles inside you but they will be carried back to your lungs by the bloodstream with no problem. But surfacing quickly will cause the air to come out of solution quickly, which of course makes bigger bubbles. The bigger bubbles are the problem. The bloodstream can no longer deal with them and they can destroy all kinds of tissues.
