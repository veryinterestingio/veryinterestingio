    • Twink_Ass_Bitch

      Any radioactive decay basically obliterates the molecule. The energy involved in a decay is orders of magnitude higher than any chemical bond. Remember that momentum is conserved, so if an atom decays and emits a particle that shoots off in a direction with momentum p, the daughter atom will shoot off in the opposite direction with momentum p (if more than two are emitted, the sum of the momenta all three is zero). Because the decay has so much energy, the daughter particle recoils with a lot of energy, ripping it away from the parent molecule. The parent molecule is likely not even in 'one piece' and could further break apart.
