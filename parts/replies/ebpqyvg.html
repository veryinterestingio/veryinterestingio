    • CommenceTheWentz

      The time lag depends on the distance to the object that it bounces back from. The pulse travels at the speed of sound, so the delay in the echo is used to calculate the bat’s distance from the target. As to how this applies to the mechanism I described, the first harmonic of the call temporarily opens a neural gate in the bat’s brain to be able to respond to the echo of the other harmonics for a brief time interval. If the bat doesn’t hear the echo in that time, it won’t hear it at all, since the neural gate will close again. In that sense, the bat’s sonar has a certain spatial range that it operates in
