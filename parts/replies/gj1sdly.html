      From the perspective of the moving object, the speed of light relative to it is always the same; so from the perspective of an static external observer, any gains in the speed of the moving object gets compressed further and further the faster it goes, and from the perspective of the object, the speed of light always remains the same "distance" away from its own speed; in no perspective the object ever actually reaches the speed of light, no matter how much it accelerates.

      It's sorta like a real version of Zeno's paradox; except instead of position it's speed. You can never accelerate all the way to the speed of light; first you gotta reach half of speed of light, then half the difference between that and the speed of light, then half the difference between that and the speed of light, and so on; you will never actually touch the speed of light, you will always be "twice the distance of your next step" away from it. ("half" being picked just as a illustrative example, it can be any value less than 1)
