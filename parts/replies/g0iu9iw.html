    In order to use the mark "New York Times Bestseller", a book only has to exist on a list once, at any position, at any time. Getting on the list is more about marketing for a single spike in book purchases all at once than about sales in aggregate.

    To add to this point a bit - the New York Times Bestseller lists don't track consumer sales, they track sales to book sellers. So if Amazon orders a bunch of your book but none of them actually sell then the book is still a New York Times Bestseller.

    Publishers can, and have, gamed this system by buying their own books. They then "ship" the books to a warehouse until actual demand for the books materializes.
