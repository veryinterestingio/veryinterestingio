      If you have done XANES before, you certainly have had more experience with x-ray absorption than I have. I mostly have done kinetic, mechanistic, and DRIFTS work with zeolites and other metal oxides for alkanol conversion. I have never had cause to do XAS nor have I visited a beam line, though others in my Ph. D. group have done so. I needed to know how the experiments worked and how to interpret the data to understand literature for my thesis, but I have never collected or analyzed my own data. It is selective for a particular metal center like XANES, but it is a Fourier transform of the extended periodic data following the initial XANES features, which are indicative of oxidation state and coordination. This requires a lot of line fitting and apodization (k-weighting) to give you clear peaks in the transform.
