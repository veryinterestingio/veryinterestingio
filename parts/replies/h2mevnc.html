    • prikaz_da

      Yes, a lot of live captioning is done with stenotypes. More recently, an alternative to the stenotype has gained some ground: speech recognition. It doesn't work the way you might expect it to, though—there isn't a computer recognizing speech straight out of a broadcast or courtroom proceedings. Instead, the captioner / court reporter / whoever repeats everything into a special microphone with an enclosure they can hold against their face. That way, it doesn't matter so much if someone in a courtroom is mumbling, or if a broadcast has loud noises in the background. The computer hears only clear speech with minimal background noise.

      You can see some of these special microphones here. It looks like this company's microphones sell for around $200–300.
