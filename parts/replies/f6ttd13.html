        Two hours in the kitchen meal prepping will save you at least 5 hours throughout the week I've found. The hard part is just doing it.

        Another trick I've learned is not to contort your lifestyle to a diet but find a diet that is already close to what you're doing. When I was in university, the way my days were scheduled made it optimal for keto for a few reasons, and I was able to stick to it because it was just a couple minor changes to what I was already doing.       • PaperClipsAreEvil

        I never got married or had a family, so that's a HUGE plus in my favor when it comes to fitness.

        If you're not already, try just walking as much as you can during the day. Maybe a five minute walk here, a five minute walk there. Buying a smart watch that measured my steps made a big difference in allowing me to see how much walking/running/physical activity I was getting in a given day and then to set goals on what I wanted to be getting. I walk/run between 18,000 and 20,000 steps a day five days a week now, but I started out with goals of more like 5,000 steps and slowly increased over time.

        Try to cut down on your caloric intake. I initially lost the majority of my weight through intense hiking and then kept it off just by eating less, cutting back on carbs, cutting out most refined sugars (stop drinking soda and or sugary drinks if you haven't already). I actually went back to a pretty sedentary lifestyle after the initial weight loss but managed to avoid bouncing back by eating only when I was hungry and never a huge amount. It's like the old motto says: Eat to live, don't live to eat.
