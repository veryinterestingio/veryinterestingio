    It's even crazier than that! Even euv is too wide a marker tip for some things. So what they do is draw a line, then move over a little and draw another line. Where the lines overlap is a little darker and thinner than the marker tip (you move over by less than the width of the marker). Sometimes you have to do this multiple times to get a dark enough overlapping line. This is called multi patterning.

    But! It's even crazier than that. At the risk of leaving eli5 territory, the marker isn't perfectly flat, it's more like a wave (but also not a wave, quantum physics is wack). So every time you draw a line, it's darker in some places and lighter in others. Because they act like waves sometimes, if two markers overlap, they interfere with each other (like how waves in water can get bigger or smaller when they hit depending on if the high or low parts line up or not). Of course, because of quantum physics it's a little weirder than that but close enough. What this means it's that you can't draw the line you want, you have to draw a different line that will look like the line you want after the waves all interact.

    Lithography is so freaking cool. Like some of the most extreme, on the edge of human understanding of physics and chemistry, wacky awesomeness.
