  • happyhappypeelpeel

    Even if you strip all of that away and run the bare EV motor on a table, it will still be extremely quiet. 50kW machine tool spindles spinning at 30k RPM are near silent on the high end equipment.

    The reason is basically as simple as: power tool motors - even in "expensive" power tools, are built poorly and cheaply, with loose tolerance and unsophisticated control electronics which can create audible resonances and torque spikes and such throughout the rotation of the rotor.

    There is no reason why a brushless motor sized for a drill couldn't run almost completely silently, gearing aside. Except for cost. Take a look at the cost of motors from Maxon or Faulhaber. Those are extremely high quality motors that can run for decades, and do it silently. Same with quality servo motors. But one of those motors (without the drive) will cost more than a nice cordless drill, and the batteries to run it, and a case to hold it all.

    Another example: cold saws are extremely quiet and can be safely run without ear protection. OTOH a Dewalt chop-saw will deafen literally half your neighborhood. One is on sale at Home Depot for $150. The other costs $4000.

    TL;DR: It's cheap and not enough people care enough to pay for it.
