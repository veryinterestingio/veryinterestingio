    Pluto is in an orbital resonance with Neptune, which means that they are locked in the 2:3 ratio of orbital periods. This happened because Neptune migrated in its early days as it was scattering the original Kuiper Belt objects. This orbital resonance keeps the two bodies from colliding despite being on crossing orbits, and makes Pluto's orbit more inclined and eccentric. As the previous answer said, there is a large group of Kuiper Belt objects that are in the same orbital resonance with Neptune, and they are called Plutinos.
