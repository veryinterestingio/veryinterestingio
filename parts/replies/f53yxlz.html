    This would make you like any other human. It's easier for everyone to form more-accessible memories when those things connect with the less-cognitive parts of our brain. If something is associated with your safety or well-being (like fear of injury or feelings of belonging), that's important in a different way than a social obligation. This is why people who study languages often work so hard to connect vocabulary and the like to personal experiences -- they want to make an emotional connection.

    You'd remember the conference times better if you imagine how hurt your spouse/kids will be if you miss them, for example.
