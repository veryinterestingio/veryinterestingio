• BigKlepa

    What I've done for a couple of hobby video games:

    "once you've loaded all the sounds, add 10% to the bar"

    "Once you've loaded the environment, add 50% to the bar"

    "Once you've got all the special effects, add 20% to the bar"

    "Once all the monsters and NPCs are in, add 20% to the bar"

    If all the sounds are loaded last, or take the longest time to load, my super basic crummy loading bar would be stuck at 90% for a long time.

  • KURAKAZE

    I agree this is the best answer. Other answers are correct also, but the big part people didn't mention is that there is no universal way of how the progress bar behaves - it is completely up to the programmer who made it.

    So different bars are doing different things in the background based on what the programmer decided.

    Overall the other explanations are pretty accurate in that the progress bar is not based on concrete time, and is heavily influenced by what the program is doing and how your individual computer is handling it.

    Some computers are faster than others in specific aspects so the same program won't run the same way in different computers.
