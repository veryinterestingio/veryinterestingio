    Most plastic can't really be recycled in the sense that metal can be, i.e. melted down and made back into the same sort of product. It can often only be downcycled, e.g. turning plastic bottles into a park bench or fleece, not back into bottles.

    Aluminum is a great material for recycling because so much of the energy used in making a new aluminum product is in refining the aluminum from ore. A used can is made of aluminum that's already been refined, so it's a great material to make a new can from.

    Glass, on the other hand, is a great material to reuse, because so much of the energy used is in shaping the glass into a bottle. If that bottle can be returned, cleaned, and refilled, it doesn't have to be remade.
