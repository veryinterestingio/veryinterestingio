    Kind of a strange answer, all of the answers we have are because of physics.

    To expand on gas giant core structure, one measurement we were able to do was measure the speed of our probes orbiting them. If they had a solid core, we would expect the speed to stay relatively constant because the density of the planet underneath the probe would be more-or-less uniform which would mean a constant speed.

    What we actually found was that the speed of the probe fluctuated as it orbited. This points to the core being a state of matter in between liquid / solid with larger fluctuations in density leading to variations in gravity and therefore variations in the orbiting probes speed.
