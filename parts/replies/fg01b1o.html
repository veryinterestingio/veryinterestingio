  • nichogenius

    Each muscle cell is one really long cell that runs the entire length of the muscle.

    I'm not a biologist, but this is almost certainly wrong. Yes, muscle cells are elongated, but they aren't the length of the entire muscle. The cells link together to form the muscle tissue. It's more like a chain-link fence that can stretch very far in one direction. As the muscle tissue stretches in one direction, the cells become more elongated and closer together. As the muscle contracts, the cells get shorter and wider, causing the muscle to bulge (like how flexing your bicep looks better when your elbow is bent).
