    • Cesum-Pec

      it was probably not young chickens. Young birds lay small eggs. The first eggs can be as small as a robin's egg. The eggs get larger as the bird gets older. You don't see small eggs in the store because those are sorted out. Consumers mistakenly believe that small eggs are inferior. Often small and misshapen eggs are sent to "breakers" which then send the liquid, frozen, or dried eggs to commercial bakeries or kitchens for processed foods.
