    this is a very important part of the answer a lot of people miss.

    basically, without a source of income from failure there is nothing to put the brakes on the hype train, if positive news was always good for stocks and negative news always bad it aligns profit incentives such that you shouldn't dig too deep and certainly never publicize bad news.

    one thing betting markets like stocks do really well is efficiently use information, but if you lack a mechanism for negative information to be used then you're really setting the stage for bubbles, even moreso than the nature of a financial market already does
