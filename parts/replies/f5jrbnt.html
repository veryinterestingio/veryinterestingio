    • bow_and_error

      Sorry mate, I think you’re confusing Pasteurized with Sterilized. Pasteurization reduces the amount of bacteria by a factor that is dependent on the type of pasteurization, whereas Sterilization kills ALL of the bacteria.

      For example, High Temperature Short Time (HTST) is the most popular method in the US, and will keep in the refrigerator for about 2 weeks. Ultra-High Temperature (UHT) pasteurization, most popular in Europe, is much more intense, and will give milk a shelf life of about 6 months. The downside to more intensive pasteurization methods is that is changes the flavor of the milk.

      With that in mind, if we sterilized milk (or killed all of the bacteria), we would have a longer shelf life, but it would also taste pretty bad.
