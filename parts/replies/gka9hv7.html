    • Terr_

      You can do puzzles indefinitely to "record facts", in particular the fact that A publicly gave up ownership of X bitcoins to B.

      What eventually slows and ends is the finding of "new" Bitcoins by their very first owner.

      How does one find new bitcoin? It happens whenever a certain part of the puzzle solution happens to look "pretty" in an agreed-upon way, like any number that ends in a series of unbroken zeros. The first person to randomly stumble across one is automatically the owner of the Bitcoin corresponding to that number.

      As the finite supply of "free for the finding" bitcoins dry up, people will switch from trying to find buried treasure to taking a percentage cut of the transactions they digitally stamp.
