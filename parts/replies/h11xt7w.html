        This is awesome. We did a study in college about polygraphs and our team was actually selected to perform some small experiments to test our methodology.

        We had 20 polygraph examiners participate. The premise was two fold, we asked them to review polygraph data from 10 separate polygraph sessions and interpret the results (they would rate their confidence in their assessment, no one self rated their assessment below 95%). Secondly they would be asked to perform an exam on a subject that was connected to their machine but seated in a different room with an intercom to speak to each other.

        Round 1, they actually examined 5 actual polygraph exam data files, and five files of people hooked up to the machine while they were watching different types of films and not being asked any questions. The examiners were around 30-45% accurate on the actual data. And of the data for the movies group just under half of the examiners said they were lying. So less accurate than a coin flip.

        The second round had worse results.

        When presented with these findings the reason we heard most from the examiners was that these subjects were not under the stress of facing criminal consequences! Which is soooo ass backwards! Like guys, this is at best a coin toss and you think that it somehow is going to become more accurate when people are worried about jail time?!? Ugh
