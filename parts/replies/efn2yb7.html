  • garyhopkins

    In commercial graphic arts, if you wanted to create an ad or a page of a magazine, you'd create the original, a "mechanical paste-up" made of all of the individual components. So you'd paste (or cement or glue) the headline, text, footnotes, etc. onto a sheet of heavy paper or lightweight board. After you lined everything up, you'd shoot it with a "copy camera", which would not only produce a single copy but, because of multi-angled lights, would eliminate all of the inevitable shadows of the individual pieces. And you could enlarge or reduce everything as part of the process.

    Copy cameras could be absolutely huge, since the focal plane might have to hold a 16x20-inch piece of photo paper. The camera back would be inside the darkroom while the rest of the camera would be outside. I once saw a copy camera that must have had an 8-foot long bellows, probably held 20x30 or even larger paper.
