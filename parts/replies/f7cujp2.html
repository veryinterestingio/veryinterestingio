      https://www.pnas.org/content/115/26/6697

      Yan et. al. Suggests a "caliper" model in which the length of the RNA primer is physically constrained by the Structure of primase. This study was carried out in an archaeal species, which shares both structural and functional homology to eukaryotic replication proteins.

      They found that binding of the 5'triphosphate at the initiation site by primase subunit PriX, an archaeal primase subunit that shares structural and functional homology with the N terminal portion of the C terminal domain of eukaryotic PriL, and binding of the elongating 3' end of the RNA stand at the catalytic site of archaeal PriS acts as the physical constraint to determine primer length. The RNA primer length is determined by the maximum number of nucleotides able to be accommodated between these two binding sites at different subunits on the primase.

      Figure 6 in the paper has the model if you want to check it out. I won't go into too much more detail, but that's the gist of it.
