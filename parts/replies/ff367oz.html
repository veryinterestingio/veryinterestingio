    • jeremypr82

      Am I correct that a person's diet will affect their salivary pH? If so, what foods would tend to raise salivary pH?

      • No, this is incorrect. You can't willfully alter your systemic pH with dietary intake. Your body works pretty hard to keep your pH at a constant level. The idea that you can somehow alter this process, or make your body more "alkaline" is a fallacy not supported by any science that I'm aware of.

      When you say that "it" makes your teeth more sensitive to cold, are you saying that calculus buildup does this?

      • No, using tartar control toothpaste prevents calcium deposits from forming in dentin tubules, which means more sensitive/responsive teeth. If anything calculus prevents this even further, which is why many people often have very cold sensitive teeth temporarily after getting excessive calculus removed.
