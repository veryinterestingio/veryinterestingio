    Vitamin deficiencies like scurvy, pellagra & rickets simply require a minimum daily dose to stop the disease.

    There are nutrients like leutein which are not classified as a vitamin but are needed for sight. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lutein#Role_in_human_eyes

    I assume it would be more like testing patients with various disorders, including healthy control patients, and recording their diets.

    People who came in with discoloration of the skin and eyes, along with recording an abundance of carrot in their diet would indicate that something in the carrots (Vitamin A or Beta Carotene) is leaving unhealthy deposits in the body.

    People with low amounts of Vitamin A or Beta Carotene in their diets showed more problems with eyesight, indicating a need in the body for certain amounts of it, leading to recommendations between the unhealthily low levels and the unhealthily high levels.
