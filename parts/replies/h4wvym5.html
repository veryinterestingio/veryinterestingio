      insoluble monosodium urate

      "Less soluble" or "relatively insoluble" would have been more precise; I'm trying to simplify and explain something that some folks have based their entire research career on, in a couple paragraphs.... :)

      Just to make it clear as mud, the actual mechanics of crystal formation are likely very different in vivo than in vitro, and the relative solubility of uric acid vs. monosodium urate (MSU) may not explain what's going on; recent research is looking at things like antibodies against MSU crystals promoting crystal formation, and some x-ray crystallography studies suggesting that the crystals formed in vivo don't have the same structure as in vitro MSU crystals.
