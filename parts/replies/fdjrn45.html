      • Hypothesis_Null

        Indeed. Though it looks more like a ball-point pen. Many thermonuclear bombs use multiple stages - it's called a Teller-Ulam configuration, after the two scientists who developed it.

        Typically there's just a primary, spherical stage (basically a boosted-fission bomb) which emits X-rays to fuse a cylindrical secondary. But I believe the Tsar bomb actually used 3 stages (with a duplicate of the secondary stage for the tertiary). They also thankfully removed the uranium tamper of the last stage so the bomb only produced a humble 50MegaTon yield, rather than the ~100MT is was capable of.
