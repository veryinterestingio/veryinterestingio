    • tj177mmi1

      It did, but at 16.7 psi, which is greater than the pressure found at sea level. They did this to test the pressure of the capsule on its own ("plugs out"), but at much greater pressure than would ever be experienced in space (I believe it was 5psi). Because of 100% oxygen combined with the really high pressure. the spark instantly ignited and burned of control.

      For all future Apollo flights, they pressurized the cabin with nitrogen and oxygen at sea level, and then bled off the nitrogen as they reached space to 100% pure oxygen at a much lower pressure. This was one of the challenges for the Apollo-Soyuz flight where only one hatch could be open at a time because Soyuz was using mixed gasses while Apollo was still using pure oxygen.
