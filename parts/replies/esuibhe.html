    Yeah, they are efficient, the CIP (clean-in-place) routines are set to run against the amount of time the equipment has been running (72hrs?), or not running(30 min?). Worked at a bottling line as well for a large winemaker and our crews would be hustling to get the CIP complete to make the schedule for the next tank of wine. If the wine gets to the pre-bottle testing and there was something off (label alc% doesn't match, high DO (dissolved O2)) etc they only had a window of about 30 minutes before the equipment had to be run through an additional CIP cycle, which meant a lost hour of production (600 bottles/min). Can't rush a CIP either, had a crew finish one early and the Production Manager made them do it over, figuring they must have skipped a step.

    We were incredibly fastidious about cleanliness and constantly tested for wild strains of yeast, etc, in our environment. Medical is a serious cut about this, they have even stricter protocols than food/bev industry
