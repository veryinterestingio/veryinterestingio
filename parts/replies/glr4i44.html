    This is spot on. As for why value can change in the blink of an eye: People's opinions can change. Value is a subjective construct. Your question is less about economics and more about human psychology.

    Edit: Note also that the entire reason money exists is to prevent it from being rapidly created or destroyed. Value of something specific can suddenly change. But if money is used as the common intermediate in all trade, then the value of money is tied to the average value of the market as a whole - rather than the value of any particular good.

    Dollars do not have fixed value. All currencies and assets, and good are in a constant flow of changing value. If a dollar had a fixed value, we'd still be buying cheeseburgers for 5 cents.

    In your example of buying a $1 stock, and then selling it for $100. Depending on when you bought and sold it. That $1 and $100 may buy you the exact same amount of bread or whatever.

    I call this out, because it gives the assumption that converting stock or whatever into cash, somehow locks in the value, and that is a dangerous financial misconception. There is a reason why portfolios are invested across many things. It is a better way to ensure your value stays more stable.
