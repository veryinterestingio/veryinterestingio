    I would guess that dichotomy effects such as these present more infrequently/mildly when viewing yourself in that way. We are very good at recognizing gray area in ourselves, we have a much harder time seeing gray in other people.

    It's a combination of only having access to the constant dialogue for yourself, and not having enough mental capacity to hold an idea of every person you meet in complex detail.

    We see people who are closest to us (if we are emotional mature) as complex people capable of varied responses, but it's much, much harder to do this with more than a handful of people. Hence the emergence of tribalism, in groups, nationalism, etc
