    • vrishark

      We don't usually measure the distance of objects or units using the Parallax method that are farther that 100 parsecs. The farther away an object is, the smaller the Parallax angle (measured in arcseconds), which would give a less accurate value. Hope this helps!

      • percykins

        Worth noting that the word "parsec" comes from the parallax method - it's the distance from Earth where the parallax angle of a star over a year will be one arcsecond.
