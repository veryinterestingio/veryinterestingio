    This is, at best, a sidebar comment for human-induced extinction, but I had to put it somewhere. The Tree of Ténéré was a solitary acacia tree in the desert of Niger, the last survivor of a group of acacia near a well. The tree was so isolated, the only tree within a 400km radius, that local superstitions grew about why camels didn't eat its leaves and nomads didn't burn its wood. It was the only standing object visible between the horizons on a flat sea of sand, and it was hit by a drunk driver in 1973.
