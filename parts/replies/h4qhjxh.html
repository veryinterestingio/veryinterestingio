    Just wanted to add that many organisms (including humans) do generate short bits of dsRNA in the course of the regulation of gene expression via miRNAs. miRNAs are transcribed and after a lot of processing, you are left with a 22nt piece of ssRNA which can (in conjunction with some proteins) base pair with target mRNAs, inhibiting their translation and/or causing them to be degraded. More than half of protein coding mRNAs are regulated in this way. Kind of a special case, since usually dsRNA is treated as a sign of viral infection, as you pointed out.

    Whole miRNA system was discovered quite accidentally by people doing basic research in nematodes and plants-just goes to show the value of funding that kind of stuff.
