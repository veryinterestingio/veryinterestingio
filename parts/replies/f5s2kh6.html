    • MoJoSto

      They're likely referring the structure of atoms.

      We typically think of our mass as the quantity that gives us "substance". Atoms compress 99.9% of their mass into a tiny dot (the nucleus) in the center of the atom that is 1/10,000th the width of the total atom. When atoms line up in a tight row, the vast majority of the resulting structure is empty space. In this sense, it's much like a solar system, with nearly all the mass compressed into the center (the sun) with just a few free floating bits in a mostly empty void.
