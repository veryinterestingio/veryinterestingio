      A. Producing water is easy. Producing glucose is easy. Producing a liquid high in water and glucose is tough since ideally milk should be more sugar rich than blood and osmotic pressure makes that hard. Since lactose can't pass the golgi apparatus membrane, that allows it to become the right consistency of nutrient rich and water rich. This allows it to be free-flowing enough that it can pass through the milkducts without much of an issue but still not be absorbed back into cell tissue. In more primitive mammals it could also be "sweated". B. The only animals producing a glucose solution use a modified digestive system, because making a flowing glucose liquid is a fight against osmotic pressure. As is storing it. And keeping it bacteria free isn't easy either. And there is no easy way of fixing that since you need your cells to be able to absorb glucose (or they would starve!). Milkproducing cells on the other hand don't have to be more advanced than sweat producing cells or other glands that exist in the skin.
