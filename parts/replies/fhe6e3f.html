    Mercator projection maps generally "touch" the sphere at the equator, but any line on the earth can be used to project a map. There are map projections that correct for distortion farther away from the center of the map. And a globe is a better representation, but it's not perfect because globes are usually spheres and our planet isn't - it's flatter at the poles and bulges a bit at the equator.
