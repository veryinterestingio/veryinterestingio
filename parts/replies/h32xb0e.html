      the stuff that's bad is not actually the virus itself, but the body's reaction to it

      This was a generalization I made. Of course the virus itself is bad. But not for most people. Varicella isn't one of the big bad viruses out there. The immune system can take care of it pretty easily, which is why kids aren't hurt too bad. Their immune response is optimal at fighting the virus. The adult's is overactive and hurts the normal tissue itself. The immuno-compromised individual's immune system, on the other hand, is under active. The virus cannot be brought under control by the host system and it's free to wreak whatever havoc it wants. The infection paves way to more serious complications ranging from bacterial infections on the skin to even pneumonia (I don't know how exactly the virus works so can't give u an accurate pathophysiology). Also, even in kids, sometimes the virus isn't completely eradicated. It later gets reactivated in adult as shingles. It all depends on how good ur immune response is.
