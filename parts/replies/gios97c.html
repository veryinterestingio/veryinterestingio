    • manVsPhD

      Pretty accurate! I’d only add that in nearly all cases the time reversal symmetry is broken due to an external magnetic field applied on the material. OP discusses a situation where the time reversal symmetry is broken by the material itself by internal magnetic fields due to how its spins are oriented but that is a pretty esoteric case in the field of topological insulators. When put this way the breaking of time reversal symmetry is the cause of the unidirectional dispersion of the edge modes and not the other way around (i.e. unidirectional dispersion causes time reversal symmetry breaking).

      The magic is indeed in how spatial and global symmetries change the topology and there is a lot of work still being done on that front (people nailed down all the global symmetries but the combination of spatial + global symmetry has not been classified fully yet for all possible space groups and global symmetries). Even then there is a practical challenge of finding physical systems that faithfully represent the simplified theoretical models.

      The existence of the one directional edge state is given by a very complicated mathematical proof that is called the bulk edge correspondence principle. It basically says 3 related things. 1. for a topological insulator a bulk topological invariant (say, genus) exists. 2. There is an edge invariant at the termination of said topological insulator. 3. The invariants of 1. and 2. must agree with each other (by math, not some happenstance). If you establish all 3 then you have a proper topological insulator. Most works don’t deal with explicitly establishing those properties because mapping to a known system where the proof has been established is sufficient.
