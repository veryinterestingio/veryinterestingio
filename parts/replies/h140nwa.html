      • readergirl132

        Honestly it depends. There’s actually a stage called the Honeymoon Period where white blood cells are actively targeting and killing beta cells, but there is still enough producing insulin that the difference is negligible. Honeymoon Period can last anywhere from days to years depending on an individual’s immune response, and ends when all beta cells are gone. After that it’s a diet of low carb, high protein/fat food and water until you die.

        Personally, after 10 years of experience, I become unresponsive when my blood sugar has been over 400 mg/dL for a week or so and completely comatose if left untreated for another 24 hours. Once I did actually die for a little bit (in the ER, rescued by adrenaline to the heart (twice)) and getting there from perfectly healthy took 9 days.

        Those dying kids could have been anywhere from days to 16 years old, because nobody knows when it’s gonna hit them.
