    • SilentHunter7

      You want to see something cool, look at drafts of the F-22. All the edges of the wings, tail and inlet are parallel with each other. They all have the same basic angle relative to the front. The flat edges act like the flat mirror, in that they'll provide a good return when they're facing dead-on to a radar. If your edges have a bunch of different angles, you get a bunch of directions where a radar can see you. So they made sure that there's only 3 or 4 exact directions a radar would have to be to get a good return.
