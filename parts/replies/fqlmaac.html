      This is for black holes moving in front of stars from other star systems - it's much easier to detect black holes that are in a binary. Microlensing events are rare but not super rare - we've seen thousands of them over the years, mostly from stars lensing other stars. Massive black holes should give off lots of very strong microlensing events, and we've got a big enough sample to run the statistics and basically rule out a huge unknown population of MACHOs.
