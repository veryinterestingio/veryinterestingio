    • veemondumps

      The problem with a teardown analysis like that is that its really only telling you how much similar components cost if you buy them from a wholesaler and assemble them into a board.

      That doesn't tell you anything about how much the company in question actually paid for the components because:

      a) The company isn't buying them piecemeal from a wholesaler

      b) Just because a component has similar performance doesn't mean it has a similar cost. Console manufacturers engineer their own components in which they take OEM designs and then gut a substantial amount of stuff that isn't necessary for what the console is doing.

      To use the Switch as an example, the Switch uses a custom Nvidia Tegra X1 chipset. The OEM reference documentation for that chipset shows that its an 8 core processor. When people started getting their hands on the actual processors they noticed that it only shows as having 4 cores.

      What happened was that Nintendo gutted 4 of the cores from the OEM design. Nintendo was the main buyer for the Tegra X1 chipset, so when Nvidia began producing Tegra X1's for its own use it just continued to use the Nintendo design, rather than go back to the original OEM specs.

      But if you were to go to Nvidia and ask for a wholesale quote on the Tegra X1 around the time that the Switch came out, the quote would have been for the 8 core version of the processor (which is what Nvidia was trying to sell to people other than Nintendo).
