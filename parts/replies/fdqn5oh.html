The issue was the altitude chosen at which to destroy a satellite as part of the test. The most disruptive anti-satellite test prior to that was conducted by the US at ~525km in 1985 with debris believed to have nearly completely de-orbited within a decade (although a single outlier was tracked all the way up to 2004 before finally losing orbit). China chose to conduct its 2007 test at an altitude of ~865km, high enough that most of the debris will remain in orbit for decades to centuries.
