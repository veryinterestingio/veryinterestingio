    I think an important issue that goes undiscussed too often is the looming demographic transition in the developing world. We need to make sure the social norms change before modern medicine penetrates, or we’ll have a global baby boom the likes of which have never been seen. The planet can’t support the population of the developing world quadrupling over the next century.

    I think the reason the issue isn’t discussed is that any viable solution requires the temporary cessation of medical aid to developing nations, which sounds like a monstrous thing to do. We want to feel like we’re doing the right thing in the moment, even if it’s harmful in the long run.
