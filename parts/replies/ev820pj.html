  • onetimerone

    That's a basic x-ray tube. Cathode with molybdenum filaments inside a focusing cup. An electron cloud is formed at said filament with the cloud size being determined by the selected miliamperage. The anode side is normally a tungsten target (molybdenum in some applications). The formed electron cloud is accelerated across the tube in a vacuum, which is a function of applied Kilovoltage. When the electrons collide with the target x-rays are emitted. The deceleration of the electron is called Bremsstrahlung or breaking radiation referring to this effect.
