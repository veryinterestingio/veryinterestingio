  • drumsripdrummer

    N(t)=1*.5^(t/5) This represents the amount of caffeine in your system.

    DN/dt=-ln(2)/(5*2^(t/5)) This represents the rate that coffee is leaving your system. Notice how it depends on t, so it isn't constant.

    Another way to think about it;

    At t=0, you have 100% of the caffeine.

    At t=5, 50%.

    t=10, 25%.

    t=15, 12.5%.

    t=20, 6.125%.

    t=25, 3.0625%.

    You lose 50% of the caffeine in the first 5 hours, and roughly an additional 47% in the next 20 hours. Clearly the rate must be changing.
