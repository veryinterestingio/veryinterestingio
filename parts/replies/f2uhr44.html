    Also, many people forget that our DNA is fundamentally coded by four nucleotides. ADENINE and GUANINE (A and G) are PURINES. CYTOSINE and THYMINE (C and T) are PYRIMIDINES.

    A always bonds with T, and C always bonds with G.

    When DNA is copied, it is unzipped by the enzyme HELICASE and another enzyme (DNA POLYMERASE) zips along and reads one strand of the double-stranded helix and synrhesizes the corresponding "correct" complementary strand. So if it reads A-A-T-G-C-A it will make the corresponding T-T-A-C-G-T. If one of these single letters is changed then the wrong complementary pairing will be synthesisuzed and will forever be passed down incorrectly to all cells whose DNA descends from that one cells. Luckily, most genes are coded by codons (groups of three nucleotides, many of which will still code the same thing even if one of the base pairs is altered) . Furthermore the vast majority of these nucleotides and codons code for junk and inconsequential DNA whose anticidons don't encode for mRNA at all or if they do it often is mRNA for unimportant proteins.

    So a lot of mistakes have to be made that don't affect important coding regions of DNA, and the don't result in immediate apoptosis/self destruction of the cell, and are not propagated far and spread to other cells, tissues, organs, etc.

    The body is surprisingly well adapted to minimize the negative effects of things like this.
