    So the skin cells need to absorb the UV light energy to be able to photolyze protovitamin D3? Thanks for the info :)

    • ThrashInc_

      They need to absorb UVB.

      Sunscreen blocks UVB, but not a lot of UVA (which is more dangerous, in terms of skin damage). UVB activates your melanocytes as well, which give you pigment (tan) and do a decent job of blocking UVA light.

      Oblique angles, like higher latitudes or morning/evening during the winter months, also allow little UVB to reach the surface. Clouds and windows also absorb the light.

      So, you need a genetic predisposition to being efficient at creating Vitamin D, you need to live at the right latitude, at the right time of day, during the right time of year, and have a cloudless sky.
