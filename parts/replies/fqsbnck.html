    My favorite way to bring that lesson home: Go outside on a night with a full moon. Cover one eye and hold a quarter in front of the other so it covers the moon.

    Now move the quarter in or out until it just barely covers the moon. How far will it be from your eye?

    If you find the result surprising, it's very simple trig to work it out: the angular size of the moon is 1/2 degree and the diameter of a quarter is 0.955 inches.
