      • __fuzz__

        You might get better results by looking up "swimmer slouch therapy exercises." I will add a few that I've been given by physical therapists over the years, but please note that I'm not a PT or a medical professional and if you are having shoulder pain I strongly recommend getting evaluated by a doc rather than trusting some random dude on the internet. Gutting it out is how I ended up in this position and now I'm in my mid 30s and still paying for my teenage ego.

        For preventative maintenance it's important to know what you're already working, so you can work the muscles that oppose them. Freestyle, which is most of what many swimmers do, works the chest and upper back pretty heavily. So you need to work your mid and lower back, especially mid/lower trapezius:

        • Rowing and lat pulls are two common exercises for this. They generally require machines but you can also do them with stretchbands.
        • Pullups are great but a lot of people are intimidated by them
        • You can also do "Y, T, I exercises" with only body weight on the floor, or small dumbbells. I recommend getting into a habit of doing these, they're quick and effective.
        • My single personal favorite is a move called "angel wings." Google "Angel wings exercize." I can tell just how bad things are by how terrible I am at these.
        • You also need to stretch your pecs to increase mobility there. Google "pec stretches" for a few.

        Good luck!
