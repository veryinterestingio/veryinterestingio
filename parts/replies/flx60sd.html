    For the more technical answer, modern timekeeping is based on atomic clocks. Atomic clocks measure the number of oscillations of a cesium atoms. Really complicated and mathematically heavy but let's just say scientists pegged the length of a second to the time a cesium atom produces a particular number of oscillatotions/atomic vibrations.

    To clarify, computers themselves don't have atomic clocks to keep track of time (although the internet time servers typically do, or are closely synched to an atomic clock). 99.99999% of computers use a quartz crystal that vibrates at a specific frequency, which means that a certain number of vibrations will equal exactly one second. It's not 100% perfect for say, scientific purposes but for 99.99% of cases, a computer could be left alone for decades and not have enough drift to cause any problems.
