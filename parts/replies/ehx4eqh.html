      There are certainly parasites that exhibit some more complex life histories, requiring two or even more unique hosts in order to complete their life cycles. However things like viruses, including the rabies virus (which is an RNA virus) typically don't have a multi-host strategy. I believe there is some risk associated with eating animal tissue infected with rabies, but the highest risk would be in nervous and salivary tissue where the virus resides so not something most carnivores would snack on.

      Since the main form of transmission is via biting/saliva I think from the rabies point of view it would be best to keep the animal alive and producing the virus for as long as possible, increasing it's chances to infect others. An animal that dies within a few days does not present the same ability to transmit the disease.
