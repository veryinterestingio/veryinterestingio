    • Whatawaist

      High blood pressure is a symptom that can have a ton of different causes. So many that no one could give a specific person good advice without understanding their medical history and examining them personally. So I can't give you better advice than a doctor.

      For the most part cardiovascular exercise and avoiding a sedentary lifestyle will keep your blood vessels in better shape and help avoid arterial hardening that's the one of the biggest long-term threats from high blood pressure.

      Diet wise leafy greens and foods high in potassium can help your body manage fluids more easily (usually through processing sodium better) but a lot of "blood pressure eliminating superfoods" are pretty scant on actual research and filled with sensationalist articles. Keeping a healthy weight very is important though. Extra body mass means more power from your heart and that strain will hurt your arteries.

      I'm really curious what doctors you found that told you not to exercise or eat better. Most doctors I know would still recommend a healthier lifestyle if your chief complaint was a nail in your foot.

      There are plenty of congenital causes for cardiovascular dysfunction though. Exercise and diet can't really help a weakened valve all that much for example. Try asking your doctors to explain the basics of the problem and keep asking questions until you feel like you understand their position.
