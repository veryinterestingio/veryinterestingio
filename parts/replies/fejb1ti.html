      • audigex

        The nerves that can be repaired in this way are relatively large.

        And just to tie this back into your previous comment:

        This is a large part of why major functions can often be restored, but fine motor control and sensation cannot. The major functions tend to be carried by the bigger nerves, so a mostly-good repair to that nerve will restore most of that function: but things like fine control and sensation rely on a network of smaller nerves which are not so easily repaired
