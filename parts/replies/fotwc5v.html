• Whatawaist

    Constant monitoring is important. Transported a suspected stroke. Husband woke his wife up in the middle of the night acting strangely and not making much sense. Still able to walk and speak his answers were just incoherent and his movements were a bit unsteady. Last known normal behavior was hours ago when the couple went to bed.

    I kept asking questions of the pt largely for the sake of his wife in the front seat of the wagon. She gave me the pt history in their house before we loaded him up and the pt was calm but responding with disconnected answers. Because I kept talking to him I noticed when he started wincing. I asked if he was in pain, if he was experiencing a headache, thought he nodded in what might have been an affirmative way. Checked with the wife and she agreed he hadn't shown any signs of pain since he woke her up. It was one of the reasons she was so calm.

    We were transporting to a hospital across the city as it was where the pt was regularly receiving treatment for other issues and he wasn't a priority transport. I called a hospital 15 min closer and confirmed that their CT scanner was unoccupied. I let them know what I was bringing in.

    If that gentleman was asleep I would have learned exactly the same about his medical history because his wife would have filled it in. My treatment of him would also have been identical. He basically just rode in the back of the ambulance while I talked to his wife.

    He would have spent at-least 15 more minutes with a brain bleed though. Though for reasons exactly like that if he had drifted off it would have qualified as a reduced level of consciousness and that new symptom would also have qualified him as a priority transport.
