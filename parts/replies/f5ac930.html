    • tylerthehun

      Pure yellow light has a wavelength of around 580 nm. Our red and green cones overlap a bit at that point, so both get excited when they're exposed to that light, and we see it as yellow. But all our brain knows is that the red and green cones are excited a certain amount. If you shine the right mix of pure red and green light at your eyes, you'll see yellow all the same, even though no yellow light is present at all. That's why an RGB screen can generate nearly every color with only three different wavelengths. It's not because light or color are special in that way, but because our eyes only really distinguish three types of light to begin with.
