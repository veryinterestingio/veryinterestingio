    This is the answer (used to work with those machines.). The trick is that raw sequins come in continuous rolls. The sewing machine can "eject" a sequin on specific stitches and if timed properly, the needle will hit the center of the sequin at about the same time a knife cuts the sequin off the roll. The sequins are typically "pin-fed" like an old tractor-fed printer.
