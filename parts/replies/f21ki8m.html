    I realized I didn't fully answer your question.

    To get a picture of the electrons wave, you just send them one by one until you get enough hits to work out the probability function.

    Similar to the machine gun example, one shot tells you little about the probability of hitting a spot on the target, but with enough shots you can graph the probability of hitting a given spot on the target.

    In the case of an electron, that probably distribution will show you the shape of the electron's wave.
