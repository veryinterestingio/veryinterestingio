    • FogeltheVogel

      The innate immune system is tightly regulated by many different pathways. You can modulate the response by stimulating these messenger pathways in certain ways.

      The innate system is also key in starting/modulating the initial response of the adaptive system. The 2 are deeply intertwined. For example, dendritic cells are part of the innate system, but they are (among the) ones that present the antigens to the adaptive system. And they don't just present the antigen, they also modulate the activation of the adaptive system. That can be primed to be in a certain way.

    • atDevin

      Immune cells need to be stimulated into the proper phenotype (ie provided a danger signal) otherwise they won’t have a strong reaction to a foreign antigen. This is priming and why adjuvants are combined with vaccines. Normal infections are filled with danger signals— for example there are several mechanisms by which cells can detect foreign nucleic acids, and others by which cells can detect bacterial components like flagella.
