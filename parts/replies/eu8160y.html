    • agate_

      Here's an article that briefly discusses the evolution of the bladder. As a soft organ, it's not going to be preserved in fossils very well, but looking at living animals, it appears in all mammals, no birds, some reptiles (turtles and some lizards but not snakes or crocodilians), and in amphibians. Fish have one, but it's different: it appears that the bladder evolved at least twice through convergent evolution.

      According to this article, in contrast to what /u/baloo_the_bear says, at least some animals do have water-permeable bladders, and use them to maintain liquid balance. The article is vague on whether that's true for mammals, though it speculates that since mammals are the only vertebrates that produce super-concentrated pee, it might not be.

      https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC2595796/
