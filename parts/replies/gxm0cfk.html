    I want to emphasize that there is a very low correlation between suffering from schizophrenia and engaging in acts of violence. While there is some evidence that people with schizophrenia are slightly more likely to be violent than the general population, other studies have shown that this is almost always associated with drug abuse and schizophrenia that has gone untreated for a long time. Statistically, people with schizophrenia are far more likely to be victims of violence than perpetrators.

    The reason I want to emphasize this is that the perception that people with schizophrenia are violent, ironically, makes them more likely to be violent. The common perception that people with schizophrenia are “crazy” and “violent” can make sufferers afraid to get treatment. Often, that means they attempt to self-medicate with drugs, and try to hide their condition.

    When you consider the fact that the greatest chance for a person with schizophrenia to become violent happens when that person is using drugs and avoiding treatment, you can see where the problem is.
