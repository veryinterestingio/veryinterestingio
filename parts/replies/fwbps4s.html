  • FoolishBalloon

    To add to this: DNA viruses generally enter the cell nucleus, which isn't able to detect the viruses and tell surrounding immune cells that it has been infected, which is the main method for cells infected by viruses to signal for "help" (assisted suicide).

    And for herpes, it tends to infect nerve cells, which have a very long life compared to epithelial cells. Then they can send out new viruses through the nerve cells that infect epithelial cells, which gives the symptoms. But the virus is still latent in the nerve cells until it is activated again, so it's very difficult to cure herpes.
