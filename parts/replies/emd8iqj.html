      Yes. I was diagnosed with the inflammatory disease, ankylosing spondylitis, at the age of 25. One hour after taking my first dose of Humira (a medication that blocks the cytokine TNF-alpha), it felt like I was waking up for the first time in a year.

      My mind became quicker. I became more aware of my surroundings. I was more motivated and active. I was definitely happier.

      And then I would lose it all periodically, about a week after my dose, because the standard 2-week dosing cycle wasn't frequent enough for me. Getting suppressed by the cytokines again was never as noticeable. I would just start losing track of time, move slower, lose my productivity and my intellectual sharpness. And then my next dose would bring it all back again. This leveled off when I was approved for weekly Humira 3 years later.

      It's interesting how it's so much easier to notice yourself getting smarter than it is to notice yourself getting dumber. It's a really clear demonstration of the Dunning-Kruger effect.
