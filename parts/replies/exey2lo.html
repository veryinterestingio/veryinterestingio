    • phidus

      Four arms is a good way to think of it. There are two paths along these four arms from the emitter to the detector.

      The phase doesn’t determine whether it goes through or reflects. Rather, it’s calibrated so that they can determine the difference in “lengths” of the two paths based on the difference in phase of of the light at the detector, because they know they are precisely in phase at the beginning.

      Yeah that's a fine understanding for the ideal beamsplitter. The phase of the light will determine which path has constructive vs. destructive interference.

      The tricky thing is that you have to keep track of both inputs and both outputs - they can 'go negative' and cancel each other out. So you can't say 'each one splits in half', only considering the intensity, but also the sign/complex phase.
