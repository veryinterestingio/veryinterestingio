    I think that points out a possible method for performing an experiment: rather than comparing a professional a random person, compare therapists with differing levels of experience.

    If professionals are no better than random, then levels of experience should also be irrelevant. If levels of experience have a significant effect on outcomes of their patients (especially if you control for age/sex/etc.) then I think it's reasonable to conclude that professionals are more effective than random people.
