    • leroy020

      I think a big part of it is the non patient care based expectations of American doctors. I think in Europe and in most societies people want to support the doctors by letting them focus all their energy on using their training to help the population. That takes a lot of effort but is not impossible to do in a healthy way and actually is energizing and self sustaining. American doctors are expected to spend that same amount of energy on patient care but overall patient care probably accounts for 35-40% of their total energy. In the US we decided we want to burden doctors with absurd paperwork, billing metrics, corporate metrics, legally defensive practice, fighting insurance companies, prior authorizations, customer service, fragmented records, and a slew of other things that have nothing to do with the practice of medicine. I think a recent survey of US doctors showed about 40% across the board are burnt out and it is no surprise.
