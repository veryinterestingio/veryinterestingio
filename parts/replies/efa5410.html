  • bguy74

    All web-based downloads are "chunks" - torrents may get those chunks for different hosts, whereas a web-request is typically (although not always) from a single source). It's just that a web-server loses track of where it is in the chunks when the http connection is terminated. Not ALL web-servers have this limitation either, some are well capable to continuing a download after it gets interrupted. (e.g. chrome supports a "resume download" concept, which leverages this capability).
