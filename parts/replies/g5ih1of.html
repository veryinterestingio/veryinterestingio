    Additionally, the infected host could spread the virus after it dies, if its remains are scavenged shortly after its death. So even if it died quickly, there could still be opportunity for transmission. For example, the common crow can contract the rabies virus by eating rabies positive tissues and seem to be able to survive for quite a while afterward.
