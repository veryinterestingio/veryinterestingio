    Nice answer ! It is indeed a mix between day length and temperatures. The reason literature seems sparse is because this varies a lot between different species and even different populations/individuals inside a species. It depends a lot of the climate and how the plants are adapted to it. Sometimes frost can happens suddenly and the mean temperature is not a good indicator of it. The plants who are sensitive to it will then use more the days’ length as an indicator that they are in the dangerous period and stop their growth (and shed their leaves). In places where the climate is more temperate and the temperatures drop more progressively, the plants can then rely on the temperature as an indicator. which allow them to adapt their growing period to grow longer the warm years without being at risk the colder years (which they couldn’t do by relying on day length only).
