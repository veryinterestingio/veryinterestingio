    • MirrorPuncher

      Don't confuse streaming with spectating. Streaming in a website like Twitch or Steam is exactly what it is, video streaming. The streamer uses software to capture his screen, and stream it as a live video.

      Most popular multiplayer games like Counter Strike or League of Legends let you spectate someone while they're playing, which is a different thing. In that case, you do establish the same connection to the server and your computer renders the game just as if you were the player.

    It's like the difference between being told to draw a triangle over the phone rather than having to actually send a picture of a triangle through the mail to the other person. Also online games use interpolation and other techniques to hide the effects of delay or lag to the end user.

    More like, it's the difference between having to send someone a triangle, and already having sent them a catalogue of shapes. In one, you need to actually send it but in the other, you only need a reference number. Your copy of the game already has the big stuff like sets and actors, it only needs the script they're dancing to.
