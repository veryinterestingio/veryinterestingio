    • PCivati

      This is a multi-factor problem and is a bit different all around the world. Mainly it's that surgery is hard and surgical patients tend to be more acutely unwell than other patients. Add to this the fact that surgical interventions take a long time (admission, prep for theatre, anaesthetic time, surgical time, recovery, transfer back to the ward) and someone needs to oversee much of this. Surgery as a specialty is also skills based as opposed to internal medicine which is often knowledge based. You can't give someone surgical skills to manage a sick patient over the phone, you have to be there yourself.

      All of these add up and often it means too few surgeons, which leads to longer hours and more calls, which means less time with the family.

      Source: I'm a surgical trainee who's done numerous 30+ hour calls, sometimes up to 9 times a month.
