    I think that you're confusing Peer Review and Replication. Peer review is the process in which a potential publication for a scientific journal is sent to a few people with relevant knowledge to review. These reviewers then make recommendations for the journal to accept, accept with minor or major revisions, or reject the article. Once a paper is published, others will attempt to replicate the findings, but the term peer review always refers to the pre-publication process.
