    Context:

    There are lots of elements in the universe, each with unique properties. If you're really clever and line them all up in a certain way you start to see patterns in those properties.

    The way all of chemistry works is all the atoms of each element play games with their electrons. Sometimes they can be shared, sometimes they are outright stolen from one another. Some elements are more aggressive than others at stealing electrons (electronegative), others are more aggressive at trying to yeet their excess electrons away (electropositive).

    IN GENERAL, in the periodic table, the closer you get to the top right, the greedier the elements get, and the closer you get to the bottom left, the more willing they are to give electrons up. The right most column, Group 18, is an exception; the "noble gasses" that are famous for not reacting.

    Question:

    Why is gold (Au, #79) greedier than thallium (Tl, #81) even though thallium is closer to the greedy side?
