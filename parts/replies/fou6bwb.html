    • KeepThatInMind

      Controlled release of faults has been studied in the past. I can't find the paper right now, but a thought experiment was published back in the 60s to study the feasibility of releasing strain on the San Andreas using a combination of increased pour pressure (i.e., flooding the fault zone) and small yield explosives.

      Immense costs aside, the generally accepted conclusion for such a plan is that it wouldn't work in any predictable fashion. Faults are very complex and dynamic systems... the physical extents and stress fields of even well-instrumented fault systems (e.g., San Andreas) are not fully mapped.

      The most likely outcome for any sort of geo-engineering would be either no impact, or transfer of stress from one portion of the fault to another, which would change the entire system and require a remapping of the stress field.
