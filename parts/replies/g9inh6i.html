    • ItsACaragor

      I work for tax administration in France and I obviously can't speak for the US but in France it is exactly the opposite.

      It does takes more time and effort to audit a rich person but the payout if they find wrongdoings is much much higher. Also since their tax returns are much more complicated to do properly since they tend to make complicated investments there is also a bigger risk of them making mistakes.

      Controls of average workers are easier sure but mostly don't bring in much either and are therefore a waste of time because you have a lot of legal procedure to audit someone which takes some time and effort and which is the same whether it's a high income person or an average Joe. The average Joe's tax revenue is also mostly filled out automatically by the administration with the known informations communicated by employers and so they mostly just need to check that there is nothing wrong, maybe add a couple of things that are missing and sign it, this leaves a much smaller room for errors.

      They do control average worker but it's mainly so there is no feeling of impunity, most of the time the payout is mediocre at best and they generally target files with red flags on them (tons of deductibles relative to your income for example).
