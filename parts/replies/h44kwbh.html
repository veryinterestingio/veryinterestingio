    • JackTR314

      I'll also add to his response as a PT, that RSIs usually involve tendons and ligaments - tissues that take a longer time to recover from exercise and activity than muscles do. Recent studies are showing that after tendons and ligaments experience resistance exercises, they'll actually degrade for about 24-36 hours, then rebuild between 36-72 hours.

      Repetitive injuries tend to occur because these tissues aren't given enough time to recover and strengthen before being exposed to the repetitive stress again - leading to pain, and degeneration.

      Muscles on the other hand recover, generally, within 24-48 hours, and can adapt to recover quicker than that given the right build up and adaptation times.

      "Poor" mechanics more centrally, i.e. weaknesses from the core, spine, and hips can, and often do contribute to the build up of RSIs, because poor mechanics do overload these tissues, creating the above problems.
