    In rare cases cancer can spontaneously regress.

    We began to understand in the mid 1990s with genetic assay that this isn't actually rare, but rather, that this happens multiple times a week, and that it usually happens so early (on the order of invisibly small clusters of cells) that the carrier and thus medicine never knew it happened in the first place

    Kind of like fires in nature, right? There's a hundred thousand a day but the plants have adapted and almost all of them go out nearly immediately, and we only really think about giant forest fires, so we tend to think of them as rare, but they actually aren't
