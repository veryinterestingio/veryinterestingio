    • yuje

      I’d argue that Yue and Min aren’t really neighboring dialects. They’re adjacent to each other on the map, but I’ve been on a bus from Zhongshan (Yue area) to Chaozhou (Min area). It was a six hour drive along an uninhabited coastline that was mostly mountains and rocky cliff sides. It wasn’t until reaching Chaozhou (Teochew) that the mountains opened up into a river valley surrounded by mountains. In the past, likely no one would have tried to walk between the regions and it’d likely only be accessible by sea.

      Going north from the Yue areas is a better example. In the countryside around Shaoguan you see the Hakka and Yue dialects blend together and Hakka continues north into Jiangxi. Going west, Yue continues into parts of Guangxi and merges into Pinghua.

      But yes, in general Min is probably an exception to the dialect continuum. It’s said that the dialects are so distinctive that even neighboring villages are unlikely to understand each other unless using a lingua franca like Hokkien or Mandarin.
