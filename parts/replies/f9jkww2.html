    Slight clarification, our ears do not perform Fourier decomposition. The cochlea directly senses sound in the frequency domain.

    https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK531483/ :

    The spiral shape of the cochlea allows for differing frequencies to stimulate specific areas along the spiral. This results in a tonotopic map that enables people to perceive various frequencies of sound.

    However, there is this binaural beats phenomenon. If you play a sine wave at f1 in one ear and f2 in the other, the signals are integrated by your brain, and you perceive something resembling the beats effect that you could perceive with only one ear.
