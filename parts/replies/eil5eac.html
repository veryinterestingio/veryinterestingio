  • rhn18

    In general, anything that naturally forms will at some point become so heavy that it starts forming an atmosphere of hydrogen and helium. This causes a progress where most of the planet's mass ends up being hydrogen and helium, which turns it into an ice giant (like Uranus and Neptunus) or a gas giant (like Jupiter).

    To elaborate a bit: Technically I don't see anything that entirely excludes the possibility of something like a huge iron "core" only planet from being able to exist. It is however extremely unlikely that such a thing could ever form, given the high percentage of hydrogen and helium in the universe, and the processes in which planets are usually formed.

    So it is a matter the planet forming processes and the available materials for it causing it to be extremely unlikely for something like that to ever form, rather than a mechanic that causes such a theoretical planet from existing in a stable state.
