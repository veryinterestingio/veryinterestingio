      • Digitalapathy

        12 tn is a Gross Market Value estimate to be clear I.e an estimate of ‘absolute’ market value of loss making contracts and profitable ones without cross netting. Since at any given time contracts are marked to market in most jurisdictions it may or may not be an accurate number relative to settlement (most derivatives by value are accrued for settlement) and it will overstate exposure because it’s gross.

        If you take an interest rate swap which is the largest derivatives segment by notional, that starts its life by definition with a value of zero. As the respective legs accrue interest you will either be due to receive or pay that value to the counterparty. However nearly all interest rate swaps are used to hedge interest rate risk on loan/funding requirements. The interest rate payments on those liabilities would obviously not be included in the calculation for the derivative market value.
