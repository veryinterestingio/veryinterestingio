    Not waves in the air. Ultrasonic waves coupled though liquid. Air (gas) is compressible and a poor way to transmit significant energy. Water is for all intents and purposes is incompressible, thus more of the mechanical energy generated by the toothbrush is delivered to the target. The higher the frequency (ultrasonic) the shorter the wavelength thus can cause mechanical fluctuations of the liquid with suspended abrasive grit (tooth paste) into smaller nooks and crevasses, such as the narrowing chasm between your gums and teeth.
