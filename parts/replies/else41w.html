    • ghedipunk

      Iron gets separated from other elements through chemical and magnetic processes.

      Iron is magnetic, so magnetic fields can nudge iron into areas that other elements might not drift into so readily.

      Also, when items are mixed together with different densities, they'll form layers, with the more dense stuff tending to sink to the bottom.

      Some elements like to bond with others more easily, drastically changing their density... Water is much more dense than either hydrogen or oxygen alone. Iron oxide is much less dense than pure iron...

      Heat matters, too... Since carbon and silicon will melt at lower temperatures than iron, a high speed collision between newly forming asteroids, meteoroids, or other small bodies will tend to blow these elements away, leaving large chunks of iron behind.

      And, in planets like ours, this layering, churning, and clumping is more easily facilitated by repeated volcanic processes.
