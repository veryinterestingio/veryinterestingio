    Something that first time home buyers might not know: when you go to the bank for a mortgage, the bank will say "okay, we'll loan you up to this much money as long as we think the house is actually worth it." So when you see a financing clause that's the potential buyer going to the bank and saying "remember when you said you'd loan me the money? It's for this place."

    Even if you're approved for a mortgage you may not be approved for THAT mortgage. The bank may be concerned with the condition of the house, state of repair etc etc etc and deny you financing for that house. Doesn't mean you can't get a mortgage, just means you can't get that house.

    Got cash? No conditions and you're good. Similarly to a commenter above, I put in a bid for $30k less than list in a market that was taking a dip (in two years the market has gone up about 15% from what I paid for similar places) and while the seller's realtor tried to get me to revise my bid up before they would formally accept it, I stuck to my guns and got it despite having another person bidding 20k more than me with a financing clause.

    If they had taken the other offer and the financing fell through, they would have to go back to market knowing that their place wasn't worth what they were asking and would have had to lower the price even more so they bit the bullet.

    CIF is king.
