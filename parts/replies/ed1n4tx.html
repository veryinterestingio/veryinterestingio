    You raise an interesting point. Traditionally, efficiency means the amount of energy converted to mechanical work (while the rest turns to heat waste). So, say an engine or a motor is 40% efficient. However, this has also been applied to light bulbs, for instance, going from 10% for incandescents to 70-80% for the best in class LEDs.

    However, how do you go about measuring the efficiency of a CPU? Surely we can't say they're 100% inefficient and do nothing with their power.

    Although, for the non traditional use cases, I would imagine it's not their actual output that's being measured, but rather how much heat they generate and substract that from the total power usage.
