      What hormone is associated with the feeling when let’s say someone were to shove you, or insult you and you’re hot and bothered by it all afternoon

      Hard to pin down. If you're thinking about it a lot, and feel like a failure in that regard that's probably the ruminations associated with depression. The pathway associated with that is not known, but it is known that it's a very ancient serotonin-related mechanism to get animals out of situations where they continue to hurt themselves.

      If you feel angry about it that's the amygdala and there isn't much of a simple answer there. I don't think cell signalling via hormones is used to mediate anger other than secondary stuff like adrenaline.

      If you feel stressed that's the cortisol / seretonin systems at work, mediated by the amygdala.
