    She said that rhythm -- keeping time, clapping in time -- is among the last mental attributes to decline.

    I can confirm. In her last days, my Alzheimer's-riddled grandmother literally could not put more than two or three words together before losing the plot. She lost her ability to meaningfully communicate well before that. We would sing old songs that she knew from 50 years ago or old lullabies that she used to sing to me together, and she could get through the whole song without any problems. It made her very happy. When it was clear it was time, I sang to her until it was over.
