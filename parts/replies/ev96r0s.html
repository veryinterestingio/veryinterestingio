    Why do we assume meteorites were formed the same time as the sun?

    • Jefzwang

      The solar system formed from a cloud of space dust. A region of the space dust was denser than the rest and therefore had stronger local gravity, which attracted more dust to it. The rest of the space dust started spinning around this point.

      The denser area accumulated more and more dust and got more and more massive until it got big enough to initiate nuclear fusion. It became the Sun.

      The dust spinning around it gradually coalesced into bodies. These became the planets and other small things like asteroids.

      Point being, it all formed from the same space dust, more or less concurrent with the formation of the Sun.
