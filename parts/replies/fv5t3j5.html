      • maveric_gamer

        So in the modern day, you're right to a certain degree, but it's worth stating that in the past, progress bars were expressly meaningless, except as a means to show that the computer hadn't crashed; in the beginning, there were no progress bars, but once computers went mass-market some tasks that were slower than a minute or so would make users think the computer had frozen, and they'd reboot it, thus aborting the process and requiring them to start from scratch.

        In essence, for the barest of UX, it's better to have a meaningless progress bar than to not have a progress bar at all.
