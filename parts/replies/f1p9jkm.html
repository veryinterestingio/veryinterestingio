    • Muroid

      I don’t think I would really consider that broken, since the expectations determine the amount of time and resources that a publisher puts into a book, and their overall financial plans are based on how they expect the books they have coming out to do.

      If they pour enough effort into something to justify a 120k run and it comes up 20k short, that’s maybe an indication that they should have been allocating more resources to something else that could have sold better as a consequence, and means they don’t have money they were expecting to have in their budget.

      If something that they put comparatively little effort into does unexpectedly well, that’s fairly impressive, didn’t cost them anything and adds some unexpected padding to their income which is a nice surprise.

      The expectation of success is generally there for a reason: the book is usually coming with some built in advantages often including an existing fan base, prominent media attention, more advertising, etc. given those advantages, it should sell more, and if it doesn’t that’s not a good sign.

      A first time book by someone no one has ever heard of that doesn’t have a big campaign behind it is going to have a harder time doing big numbers, and if it sells significantly better than most books do under those circumstances, that’s pretty notable.
