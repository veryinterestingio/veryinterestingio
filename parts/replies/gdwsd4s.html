    • Sherool

      They are quite expensive yes. Starting and stopping them safely are incredibly delicate operations and while it can all be automated and done fairly quickly you want to very closely supervise it because of the potential consequences if something does go wrong.

      Once you get it going there are fairly low running costs, the fuel rods last for 5-6 years before you need to swap them out so might as well just keep it running once you have gone to the effort of setting it all up. Starting and stopping it every evening to cover peak load just adds a bunch of wear and tear, meaning more maintenance costs.
