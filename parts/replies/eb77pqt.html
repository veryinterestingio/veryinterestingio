        A year is defined as a single orbit around the sun. Days are measured as a single rotation of the Earth. The number of days in a year are a result of how many rotations we do during a single orbit around the sun, hence 365.24 days in a year.

        If the revolution is changed such that a day now takes 48 of our current hours - 1 hour = 3600 seconds (seconds are defined independently of either orbit or rotation, it’s based on caesium (SI second definition)) - while the orbit does not change then a year is the same number of seconds/minutes/hours, but half as many days.
