    Cognitive ability and neuropsychological performance are not necessarily embedded genetically. One of the main issues is the psychosocial issues that more directly impact ability. As we age, our abilities are formed via parenting, exposure to diverse experiences, quality of education, nutrition, trauma, etc. often, differences in cognition between race are best explained by socioeconomic status. Also, there are many twin studies of siblings that were separated at birth and even though they have the same genetics, end up with different IQs, because environment plays such a key role in formation of cognition.
