    • SJHillman

      Let's say you're doing an 8W (60W equivalent) LED every 10 feet, and only a row near the windows. Using some Google values for city block size, let's say one block is about 400x600 ft. That yields a permiter of 2000 ft, needing 200 bulbs. 200x8W=1600W per floor. Let's say a normal workday is 12 hours (mixed shifts), 5 days a week, so we won't count that as the lights would be on anyway. So over a normal year, that still leaves 5400 hours those lights are on just for effect, coming to about 8,640 kWh. Electricity is fairly cheap where I am at about 12 cents per kWh, so the total would be just over $1000/yr per floor to light up one city block purely for effect. Not a horrible amount, but multiied over many floors in a block, and many blocks in a city, the cost is going to add up pretty fast.

      And that's ignoring the fact you'll be replacing bulbs three times as often (which costs labor, not just bulbs), so the realistic cost is going to be higher still.

      Obviously, this is making a number of assumptions. You can tweak the spacing if the bulbs, or using fewer but larger and more efficient fixtures. But at the end of the day, it's using a lot of power if you want to light up the entire skyline.
