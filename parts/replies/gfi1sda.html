    Although we don't have a definitive answer, sleep being necessary absolutely makes sense and always has.

    Though it feels perfectly natural and effortless, during waking hours our bodies and brains are actually doing a ton of work just to keep us alive and functioning without us even being aware of it.

    To even do simple things like lift your arms to stretch or move your legs to get out of bed and brush your teeth every morning, grasp a pen and put it to paper to jot down a note, etc takes all kinds of unseen mind/body coordination and communication.

    But when we're at rest there's far less pressure. The sole focus then becomes keeping our bodies alive and documenting, sorting and storing our memories/experiences, etc.
