    • Skystrike7

      CPAP machines and any closed-off air-driving machines I can think of are actually fancy compressors. Compressors use mechanical work to increase pressure on the output side. In a CPAP machine, it has your lungs on the output side, and because your lungs expand, pressure does not build up beyond "normal". Pressure only increases if the compressor's output volume does not increase to compensate.

      While it is true that velocity reduces static pressure of fluid, it does NOT reduce it to zero in any case. A CPAP machine driving air in a tube is not hindered by Bernoilli's law.
