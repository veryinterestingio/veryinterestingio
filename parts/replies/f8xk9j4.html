    Coordinated time was a necessity for maritime navigation. You can find your latitude with a sextant but you need a chronograph to using some time standard to figure your longitude. As a result, 18th and 19th century saw standardized time at sea introduced. (Using “London time” as the standard typically)

        There was a huge, highly publicized search for an accurate timekeeping device that could be used on a ship (the motion interferes with hourglasses and pendulums). Prizes were even offered by the British government. Such a clock was finally perfected by John Harrison in 1761, based on successively more accurate versions of a mainspring clock.
