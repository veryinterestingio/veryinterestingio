    • Dovaldo83

      Ultrasound tech here. By 10–12 weeks of gestation, developing fetuses begin taking “practice” breaths. It's one of the metrics by which we check if a fetus is healthy. They're not getting oxygen from it so much as breathing in and out amniotic fluid

      Check out this clip as an example. The dark pulsing thing on the right side is the heart. The dark curved line immediately left of it is the diaphragm, and the smooth grey area to the left is the liver. We watch for the diaphragm to move to assure baby is taking practice breaths. It's usually more subtle than in the clip, but both baby and the probe seem to be moving around a bit.

      Absence of practice breathing in a 20 minute time frame is indication that a preterm delivery is emanate.
