      But at the same time, punk and metal songs are still based off the occidental scale, the building block of western music. They still use typical scales and still use chord combos that are common and liked in western music. Major chords still sound happy/bright and minor chords still sound sad/heavy. However, if you had grown up in Indonesia for example, their music developed off of a different scale. Even with the same internet access and ignoring language barriers etc., it is very unlikely you would have gotten into those genres.

      There was a report a while ago on the Tsimané (an Amazonian tribe) because they hadn't been exposed to Western music. They found that given no prior exposure, people didn't have any preference between dissonant chords (associated with evil/tension in western music and usually thought to sound bad) and consonant chords (associated with good/happy things, usually thought to sound good). They ranked them as equally pleasant.

      While I agree that it's not as straight forward as automatically liking the same specific genre of music as those around you, environment absolutely is a huge factor in what possibly genres you might like.
