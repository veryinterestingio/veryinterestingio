  • Ashwalla

    I’m really glad to see someone touched on bitterness here!

    Many green leafy vegetables (and some vegetables in general) have a bitter aspect to their flavor. Bitterness is strongly associated with poisonous / toxic compounds that can be found in what we may view as food. Our tendency to avoid bitter foods (like vegetables) is associated with our natural instinct to not consume food that may be poisonous / toxic.

    It’s admittedly opinion, but I feel strongly that (at least in small part) what makes it difficult for some adults to stomach eating green vegetables is their not having consumed them consistently as children. Their brains simply weren’t wired from a young age to understand that broccoli = safe food. Other aspects of diet VERY clearly play into this, but I’m confident this is at the root of the issue as well.
