    This is the answer I learned from Corridor Digital, where they talked about "undercranking", where you intentionally film at a lower FPS (by 1-2 FPS) so your action looks a bit quicker and like the actors are more competent fighters than they are able to portray on their own.

    The other possibility now was said to be to film at a much higher FPS, so when you take out some frames to speed it up the time difference isn't as large and it doesn't "skip".
