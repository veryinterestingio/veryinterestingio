    • suicidaleggroll

      Limit the max to 70%, and when you need to use the battery, unplug it, use it, and plug it back in as soon as you are able.

      The results can be huge. Cycling your battery 10 times a day trying to keep it between 30-70% adds up to nearly 4000 cycles per year. That will kill your battery dead within 6 months, probably less. Floating your battery at 100% all the time will reduce its capacity by ~10% per year (going off of memory here, I currently can't find the source where I got that info). Floating your battery at 70% all the time will reduce its capacity by more like 1-2% per year.

      There's a temperature effect in there as well, essentially hotter = more aging. Charging/discharging heats up the battery, reducing its longevity even more compared to the ideal case.
