      • DavidRFZ

        Yes. The other thing to remember is that all insider transactions are publicly reported.

        The value of an insider trade is that a person could quietly accumulate or sell stock ahead of a major press release and then take advantage of the market response to that press release.

        All insider transactions being public means there is no 'quietly'. Each insider transaction is effectively a press release itself! So, if an insider is buying up stock, the media will call up the company and ask them to make a statement before they write up what the insider is doing in the newspaper.
