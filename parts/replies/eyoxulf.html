    Does this mean the best way to get better at running is to frequently go anaerobic in workouts?

    • runasaur

      That's a very common debate among endurance athletes and a particular sore point between long distance runners and other sports (particularly crossfit, mma, cycling, i.e most other sports that result in increased endurance without necessarily being solely an endurance sport)

      So, VO2 max, the volume of oxygen your body circulates is a good indicator of "endurance".

      HIIT- high intensity interval training, which is more or less "frequently going anaerobic" improves VO2 max very quickly and more efficiently than running. So you get more "endurance" in a 20 minute intense workout than an hour jogging. However, if your goal is to run a marathon, then no amount of HIIT is going to prepare your legs no matter how much "better" your heart and lungs are.

      • iankost

        Lactate threshold (or lactate shuttle overload pace) is a much better indicator of endurance/marathon pace. Think of vo2max as your theoretical maximum, but your LT or LSO is the maximum you can actually get to.

        Expanding on that, your training needs periodisation, so sometimes short, intense sessions are what you need, other times longer sessions at your LSS pace are required.

        For a marathon, it's as much getting your muscles ready for the amount of damage they are going to get as training your heart and lungs to be able to get you through it.
