    Duct tape varies very widely as do bandaids. I would say there is overlap at the extremes of each- as in the high end of bandaids is probably about the same as the lower half of the range of duct tape.

    Duct tape has its principal advantage in its “cloth” backing which really stabilizes it against shear and lateral movement. And also it’s wide surface area (remember the area dependence). Traditional duct tape has very poor adhesion at high and low temp extremes (which is - ironically- what makes it a very poor choice for ducts).

    There are several classes of adhesives, and classes of backings. The backings each have performance characteristics and the adhesives have different characteristics.

    For this information- it is best seen on Grangier.com and perhaps McMasterCarr. I had the need to educate myself on the finer points of tape a while back. And those along with 3M were my primary resources. I wasn’t trying to invent new tape formulas, I was just trying to solve a pesky industrial problem that needed more effort than “whatever roll is closest”.
