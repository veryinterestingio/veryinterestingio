  • yumcake

    Probably going too hard. People fall in love with idea of intensity, makes for a great 80s movie training montage...but it's a bad idea for anyone who wants to get started on getting fit.

    Intensity matters for intermediate athletes. But for beginners consistency is king. Oversimplified example, let's rate workouts 1-100. If you give 100, 100, 0, 100, 0, 0...in that first week, you've put in 300 in total and you feel like shit and you don't wanna do the next workout. Instead do 70, 80, 75, 80, 70, 80, 75...you're keeping it in the "fun" zone of effort, and you're not psyching yourself out of doing it more, and your actual training volume completely dwarfs that of someone going 100 and is missing days due to exhaustion or injury.

    Next time you try running for distance or time, try to actively AVOID running hard. Slow way down, slower than you think is needed. Find the pace you feel you can hold for hours and hours and train there instead. You can learn speed and intensity later, you just need to spend more time building a base first by training comfortably.
