    Just to emphasize one of the points here: if a science team already had automated instruments set up before the crisis (a satellite, seismic network, etc) they may be able to take advantage of it during the COVID crisis. And I fully expect to see some great papers that do that, but data analysis and publishing takes a long time. By way of example, there was a great paper on the climate impact of airplane contrails after 9/11: it was published in August 2012.

    But any science that involves setting up something new, or that isn't completely automated, is pretty much on hold right now.
