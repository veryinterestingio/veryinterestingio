    • Herbivory

      "Boiling point" as a technical term means the temperature where a substance's vapor pressure is equal to the atmospheric pressure.

      In the kitchen, it's less straightforward because water at the bottom of the pot is hotter than water at the top of the pot.

      Simmering is when bubbles form at the bottom, and the bulk of the water is around 180-190F (82-88C) at sea level -- so not boiling temperature. Rolling boil is when the bulk of the water is around the boiling point (100C at sea level).

      It might help to understand that a liquid generally can't exceed its boiling point because it would vaporize first -- so the hottest you can get a pot of water is its boiling point, hence simmering being below the boiling point. Microwaved distilled water is a case of a liquid exceeding its boiling point, which causes it to vaporize violently when disturbed.
