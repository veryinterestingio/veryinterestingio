      There's at least half a dozen treatments that are used. Simplest is round, semisoft tubes that hold your nose open. They're cheap, non-invasive and work especially well while sleeping.

      At the other end of the spectrum is rhinoplasty, typically a cosmetic surgery. It's expensive, but bcause it's all hidden and so less worry about visible scars, it's not too pricey. Lots of opportunities for complications, but as cosmetic surgery has become widespread it's gotten more oversight. It's safer now than the 90s for sure.

      There's other options in between those that can help. Everyone should speak to a doctor about your specific issue. Both or neither could help you, depending on exactly what's wrong. Don't decide on a treatment before seeing an expert. Go to an ENT.
