    • Half_Man1

      When you take a thermometer and put it into a pot of water, the thermometer already has a certain temperature. (Temperature is basically a statistical average of kinetic energy)

      If the water is hotter, water molecules are impacted the thermometer and on average contributing more energy to the thermometer than the other way around. The thermometer’s atoms start to vibrate more- and more space is needed in between the molecules of fluid in the thermometer as the molecules repel each other with their electric fields.

      The fluid becomes less dense and expands- we see the line go up the tube. Other temperature measuring devices measure different properties of a material affected by changes in temperature.
