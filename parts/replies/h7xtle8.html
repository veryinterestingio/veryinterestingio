    • Silver_Agocchie

      And that is often why certain animals become ridiculously venomous/poisonous. It becomes an evolutionary arms race between poison potency of the prey and poison tolerance of the predator. First generation of poisonous critter is only mildly poisonous, but still only the most tolerant predators can eat them. This leaves only the most poisonous critters alive to reproduce, resulting on the next generation of critter to be more poisonous. This then puts evolutionary pressure on future predator generations to have greater tolerance.

      The end result is tiny critters with enough poison to kill dozens of humans, but the same dose would only be a mild deterrent to their regular prey species.
