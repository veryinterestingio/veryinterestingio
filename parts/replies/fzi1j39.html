  • Philo_T_Farnsworth

    Isn't HDMI also parallel and not serial, leading to timing issues? I was of the impression that Displayport was serial and HDMI was parallel, hence the former's more simple cable construction, and presumably longer theoretical length.

    • Gnonthgol

      You are partially right. HDMI is a strange hybrid between parallel and serial. The channels are using digitally encoded serial differential signals which enables error correction and clock syncronization for each channel. But then they are using seperate channels for RGB and sync. This does make it simpler to convert between analog video signals and HDMI as each channel is a seperate analog to digital serial converter while still giving the same benefits of modern differential serial signals used everywhere. However because this is used everywhere you now typically use the same serdes chips for everything including USB, DisplayPort, Ethernet, HDMI, SATA and PCIe.
