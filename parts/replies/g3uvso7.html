    The important thing to add about student loans is that they essentially destroy a traditional market. You're giving 18 year olds access to six figure loans. If a college raises its price by 10%, they know that kids will be able to get loans to cover it. Then colleges use that money to add amenities like rec centers and new dorms. Which means they can charge more. In a normal economy, you can't keep raising prices without offering something substantially more attractive, but universities can! It's because of these loans. And a college education has become so essential, that price doesn't phase people anymore because it's just a necessary expense.
