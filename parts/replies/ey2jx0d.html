      For every 2 additional molecules of CO2 that enters the atmosphere in 2 centuries roughly 1 will still be here.

      That's not exactly true in that the exact same molecules will not be present in two centuries. The CO2 absorption timescale by the ocean is only about 7 years, but at the same time the ocean releases CO2 on roughly the timescale; for each CO2 molecule absorbed, you'll almost always get another emitted.

      So while it is true to say that 2 excess CO2 molecules now will result in 1 excess CO2 molecule in 200 years time, it's incorrect to suggest that it's the same molecule.
