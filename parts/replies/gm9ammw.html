    • phiwong

      Both, in a way. This is why this Gamestop thing is so infuriating to the folks who don't understand it.

      The clearing house isn't in the business to underwrite the risk of any transaction. It is a trusted "middle party" and the "record keeper". In normal circumstances, they only require the broker to put up a small percentage of the transaction amount (like a security deposit) to execute the ownership transfer. This makes things work very fast (an absolute requirement given the number of trades handled per second).

      However, if something is very unusual, say the clearing house believes that there are too many trades with a lot of price volatility, they can require that the broker increase the "security deposit" from the broker representing buyers before they will register the transaction.

      In the GameStop craziness. they did so with little notification and essentially said "full payment upfront", this puts the broker in a difficult situation of finding a LOT of cash that they did not expect they needed. Stock trades have T+2 and I believe option trades settle in one day - so this makes a big difference to the broker.
