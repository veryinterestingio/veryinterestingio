    The adrenal gland issues are just for male ferrets, and from what I've heard it's still debatable whether or not surgically castrating really increases the chance for adrenal gland problems.

    Female ferrets ussually stay in heat for the entire breeding season if not bred, which will usually lead to aplastic anaemia. So you should either surgically/chemically spay (or rather castrate, as the entire reproductive organ is removed) a female ferrets, or breed her. Something some breeders do is keep 1 vasectomized male. He will still produce hormones, but is infertile. That way they can choose not to try for a litter with a female, but still breed that male to her, and that will bring her out of heat.
