    • masklinn

      Yes. Not only will it burn ash, glass, sand or asbestos, it's hypergolic with all of them: just putting them in contact with ClF₃ will set them on fire instantly ("no ignition delay has ever been measured"). The bloody thing will corrode metals usually considered non-corrodible like gold or platinum. It will set flesh on fire.

      You can't actually stop a ClF₃ fire, only contain it and wait it out (cooling and / or flooding the area with nitrogen or a noble gas to avoid the spread). Normal fire containment methods are at best useless and at worst dangerous.
