    If a home customer wants super fast internet, the ISP can install whatever equipment it needs between the customer's house and the ISP's central office to make that happen.

    Not really. They only upgrade service in an area when the over subscription ratio goes over by some amount. In almost every case, if a home customer calls up and says, "I want to go from a 15Mbps plan to a 100Mbps", zero new capacity is added to the area. They just adjust the settings to allow that consumer to use more bandwidth and charge them more. Only if you get many customers in an area that are doing that are they going to upgrade (and then only if they feel like they're going to lose those customers to a competitor).

        It would be very tricky to manage all your customers with different speeds and limits, and that administration overhead would massively increase cost, so companies would charge far higher. For most of their customer base, those that want higher speed will use more data, so it makes sense to pair the two when deciding which low number of fixed options to provide (to keep cost down).
