    • helloiamsilver

      I’ve noticed it’s very useful in art and drawing. One form of art practice that’s really common in drawing classes is blind contour drawing. You don’t look at the page you’re drawing on at all and instead just slowly and steadily follow the contours and lines of the object that you’re drawing. The goal is to train your eye to see every little detail of what you’re drawing as well as to get your hand and eye to become synchronized. You want to be able to control your eye movement and focus on the line instead of just darting around to random spots.
