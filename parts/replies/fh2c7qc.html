    • Olmesy

      It's a good question. You're completely right that atomic nitrogen would float above atomic oxygen. However, the reason we see so much more atomic oxygen in the upper atmosphere is simply because it's much more likely to form.

      Atomic oxygen dissociate from molecular oxygen in the presence of the right frequency of UV light, which just happens to be one of the frequencies that the sun emits. Nitrogen would do the same, but its corresponding specific frequency of light isn't as prevalent in the upper atmosphere.

      So you're completely right that nitrogen would be lighter, but because of the relatively similar masses, they stay pretty well mixed and theres a whole lot more atomic oxygen than atomic nitrogen.
