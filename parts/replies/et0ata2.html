    Note: this is for NHS nurses. Doctors work on average slightly more hours per week (often ~46 rather than 36) but with a mix of shift patterns over an ~8 week rota where every week is different. Some weeks you might work 50-60+, others less, depending on the rota structure.

    For most departments this is 8am-5pm, 8am-8pm or 8pm-8am, but in A&E you'll see a lot of 1pm-11pm and 4pm-2am shifts because fuck them having normal lives.

    There isn't overtime per se for doctors, but you can work additional shifts in your own time if there are shortages.
