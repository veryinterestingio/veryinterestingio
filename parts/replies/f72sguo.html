  • rose_cactus

    Without sun you mainly develop lack of vitamin D, which can be easily treated through supplements (while skin cancer can’t be), and is a regular occurence even in people who formally get enough sunlight.

    Skin cancer - the black one, melanoma - seems to have different components leading to it, not all understood, but events of extreme exposure to sun (=sunburn) seems to play a role in it, as does genetics (which is why thorough and frequent screening is recommended for people with a family history). Sunscreen does a good job at preventing sunburns. There are probably other environmental factors at play though, because there has been a raise in melanoma cases for quite some decades now that can’t be explained yet.

    For white skin cancer (spinalioma, basalioma), a family history and the total time of unprotected exposure to sunlight play a major role - meaning that sunscreen can prevent the accumulation of too much unprotected exposure, no matter if you got a sunburn or not, and thus helps reduce cases of that type of cancer. As people are getting older and older, and the probability of skin cells degenerating goes up and up, it’s good to be protected - it may not prevent every case, because other factors still exist, but it helps keeping the number lower. Also, if it’s detected early (which today happens more often with better medical prophylactic screenings, and more awareness in people to go to doctors if something weird’s up with your skin), it often is fairly operable (but will appear in the statistics - which could also play a role in numbers being higher than say, 70 years ago).

    tl;dr: sunscreen is good, use it. You probably suffer from vitamin D deficiency anyway even without sunscreen (at least in latitudes more north than the Mediterranean), and that deficiency is very easily and cheaply treatable with supplements. (Source: I have a Family History of spinalioma, which is also a family history of pale and birthmark-heavy people going without sunscreen, am high risk of developing one myself due to several other factors, and had severe vitamin D deficiency even when I didn’t use sunscreen, which then ceased to exist when I put on sunscreen and took that supplement which cost me 20€ for 6 months).
