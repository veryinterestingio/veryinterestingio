    • The_Dead_See

      Causally disconnected means that no part of one can affect the other in any way. If two regions of space are causally disconnected (e.g. the area inside and outside an event horizon) then nothing - energy, mass, information, charge, angular momentum etc. - can move between them. One region cannot be the cause of any effect in the other region.

      • Midtek

        The regions inside and outside a black hole event horizon are not causally disconnected. However, events outside the event horizon cannot be in the absolute future of any events within the event horizon.

        An example of causally disconnected regions would be, say, our observable universe and some region of space that is well beyond a current distance of 65 Gly. These two regions can never and have never communicated with each other, i.e., no signal in either direction.

        A black hole is different because someone outside the event horizon can certainly cause an event inside the event horizon, but not vice versa.
