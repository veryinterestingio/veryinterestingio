    Even assuming the air itself doesn't instantly turn into a black hole, the sun will gain mass so rapidly that it will become a black hole with infinite mass in about 26 hours.

    Let's assume the gas is stationary relative to the Cosmic Microwave Background. Then, initially the sun is moving through the gas at about 370 km/s.

    .

    Any gas captured by the Sun's gravitational potential would change velocity to the Sun's velocity. Then by conservation of momentum, at any time t we have:

    • m(t) v(t) = m(0) v(0), . . . . . . . . . (1)

    where m(t) is the mass of the solar system and v(t) is the speed of the sun relative to the CMB, at time t seconds after we introduced the gas.

    .

    Using the escape velocity equation, we know that any gas getting within 2G m(t) / v(t)^2 of the centre of the sun will be captured. Call this distance the capturing radius r(t):

    • r(t) = 2G m(t) / v(t)^(2) . . . . . . . . . . . . (2)

    .

    So the sun gains mass at a rate of

    • dm/dt = ρ πr(t)^2 v(t), . . . . . . . . . (3)

    where ρ is the gas density (1.225 kg / m^(3)), and πr(t)^2 is the area from which the gas will be captured.

    .

    Substituting equation (2) into equation (3),

    • dm/dt = ρ π 4G^2 m(t)^2 / v(t)^(3)

    .

    Using equation (1) to express v(t) in terms of m(t), m(0), and v(0):

    • dm/dt = ρ π 4G^2 * m(t)^5 / m(0)^3 v(0)^3

    .

    Collecting all the constants:

    • dm/dt = a * m(t)^5

    where a = 1.723 x 10^(-127) kg^(-4) s^(-1).

    .

    Put this equation into WolframAlpha, with initial condition m(0) = 1.9885 10^(30) kg (mass of the sun):
    [We see that m(t) goes to infinity at 92 800 seconds, which is about 1 day 1 hour 45 minutes.](https://www.wolframalpha.com/input/?i=dM%2Fdt+%3D+%281.723    10%5E-127%29++M%5E5%2C+M%280%29+%3D+1.988510%5E30)
