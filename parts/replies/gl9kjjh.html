  • funhousefrankenstein

    Most chemical reactions occur much slower in cold temperatures. Slower reaction = less electricity generated per second.

    Yup, yup, this is the answer. Car batteries list "Cold Cranking Amps: CCA" on their labels, which is defined by tests carried out at 0^o F (CCA can be approximated by other tests at room temperature, such as the free battery tests at many local auto parts stores).

    (Because modern oils don't change viscosity too much at cold temperatures, the oil isn't the full answer to the hard winter starting problem.)
