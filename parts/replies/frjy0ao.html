    What you see in the mirror is being seen with human eyes, using actual lighting, and at "real life" resolution.

    What you see in a digital photograph is being seen with a microchip, a bunch of computer programming, and an artificial two dimensional projection of what it thinks the light should be, at a very limited resolution.

    Cameras are awesome, but nearly two centuries of development and countless billions of dollars still haven't replicated what people actually see
