    MoonGravForce >> EarthGravForce

    It is interesting that this is not strictly necessary. As an example, the gravitational force between Sun and Moon is always larger than the gravitational force between Earth and Moon. But the Moon still orbits Earth - because the gravitational attraction from the Sun is quite uniform, so Earth and Moon feel nearly the same acceleration and can orbit each other while orbiting the Sun together.

    You need a moon (or a moon of a moon) to be well within the Hill sphere of the parent body.
