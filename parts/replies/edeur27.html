    To add to this a little, many other economic and demographic stats are calculated per capita, where per labor force participant wouldn't make any sense. So, if you want to compare GDP to other indicators, calculating it per capita makes it more portable.

    If you wanted to view the effects of GDP on, for example, health care costs or life expectancy (which we don't usually think of as "per capita" but is) you can easily have them on the same denominator.
