    • Yucatan_Cornelius

      You haven't found a straightforward answer because it's complex and portions of it aren't well understood. We know that onset varies based on nutrition, sex, and some environmental factors (dairy consumption is one if I remember correctly, though I'm skeptical of the common assertion that growth hormones administered to cows are to blame). Genes probably play a role as well. Add to that the complex nature of growth and development, and you've got a serious puzzle to solve.

      I don't know enough about the specifics of developmental biology to get into the nitty gritty of how it happens, but I can speak to general trends. Nutrition is an enormous factor in the timing of menarche (girls getting their first period), which is generally used as a good proxy for puberty in girls. For boys it's more difficult to measure precisely as there isn't as binary of a signal. Age at menarche has been decreasing in a lot of industrialized countries due, partially to better nutrition and increasing rates of obesity (fatty tissue produces a lot of estrogen and is thought to speed the onset of puberty on girls, not sure about how this impacts boys). Stress plays a role, and increases in age at menarche are expected for populations exposed to greater levels of violence (sort of, this gets complex). I'm sure I'm leaving a lot out.

      As for the actual mechanism that triggers puberty, I'm not sure. That's a question better suited for a doctor, but given the complexity of the process and the number of inputs, you're likely to get a shrug emoji even from them.

      There's an enormous amount of variability in terms of individual outcomes and much of this data is based on population level aggregations. It's also based a lot on observations of hunting skill among Hunter gatherer populations (which itself presents a lot of problems). It turns out hunters suck pretty bad until they hit their thirties or forties. The general idea is that the odds of a 12 year old being a good hunter are effectively zero, so it's better to wait until they are 20 or so before they join a hunting party (substitute parenting or a few other complex tasks for "hunting" and you've got the premise for some other papers).

      That's a long winded way to say it's not necessarily about coordination or capabilities in a physical sense. I think we can all agree that we were morons as children or teenagers and probably shouldn't have been entrusted with anything complex. That's the basic idea. It's easy to keep an 11 year old fed, and keep them in line when they're half as big as an adult. It would be a huge pain in the ass if we had a bunch of fully grown 10 or 11 year olds running around.
