    • Coomb

      Certainly it will be the case that plants are less "floppy". Structures that are not rigid enough to serve as adequate support on Earth might very well work on the Moon -- plants that grow as vines on Earth might be able to support themselves. We discovered relatively recently that some aspects of root growth which were thought to depend on gravity don't, which is a good sign, at least for the particular species tested. But apparently phototropism, i.e. growing towards or away from light, is reduced in low gravity. The effect of reduced gravity on various plant behaviors is still an area of active research, including recent ISS experiments.
