    To continue on the training part. If you've ever worked out and tried to do a 1 rep max weight ull understand this a bit better.

    Naturally you only use some % of your muscle to do a lift. This is in part to prevent injury and in part to conserve some energy incase of emergency (like a lion comes out of the bush). We are able to do the same exercise many times in a row because a different set of muscle fibers "fire off" each rep. This gives you recovery time while you continue the same movement.

    However you can train your body to fire higher and higher percentages of your muscle on a single rep. This is why different people of the same size and muscle mass can have different 1 rep maxes.

    The fight or flight mechanism kind of bypasses the training needed and allows for someone to fire almost or all of their muscle at once.
