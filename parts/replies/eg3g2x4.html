    • Lowbacca1977

      You're not accounting for that over those 6 months, Mars has also moved about 2.5 AU (arc, not chord).

      In terms of RA, since sky position is what's measured, Mars right now has an RA of 1h38m, and in 6 months in September, it will have an RA of 9h48m.

      Mars also will have had two very different distances from the earth. Right now, it's actually about 1.6 AU away, and in September it'll be about 2.7 AU away.

      So the 'parallax' measured for Mars ends up being like 120 degrees (if we ignore the obvious problems here). Running that through the equation for parallax, which is d = 1/p with d in parsecs and p in arcseconds (432000 for 120 degrees), we find that the distance from Mars to the Sun is 0.000002 parsecs, or about half an AU, putting Mars well inside Venus' orbit.

      Which is why the parallax method is not accurately done for planets in our solar system when separated out by 6 months apart.
