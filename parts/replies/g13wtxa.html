    • zok72

      We do! The ELI5 version is that we know what color water is and so by carefully measuring the light coming from the planet we can see what colors it is and thus we can determine whether there is water there. The more complex answer has to do with absorption and emission spectra. Basically, every atom, compound, and molecule absorbs and emits energy at very specific wavelengths that we can calculate with quantum mechanics. In labs on earth we can experimentally verify these numbers with lasers even for small quantities of materials. In space we need very large telescopes or arrays of telescopes taking observations over multiple weeks to detect the light coming from planets but once we do we can get a careful enough read to see what wavelengths of light are coming from that planet and from there conclude what is present on the planet.
