    It’s much more actually. The enthalpy of vaporization of water is 2257J/g. That’s the energy required to boil water at boiling point. The specific heat of liquid water is 4.18J/g ° c, meaning it takes 4.18J to raise 1gram of water 1 °c, and from freezing point to boiling point(0 °-100 °) it take 418J. So for the same amount of water (1g), it takes 2257 J for it to evaporate; more than 5x the energy to heat from freezing point to boiling point(418J). If room temp is about 20 °C or a little more, to get it to boiling requires even less heat, about 4/5 as much or less, so less than 340J/g likely.

    For comparison, the heat of fusion of water at freezing point, or energy required to melt, is 334J/g, less than 418J/g, and significantly less the 2257.
