    When we talk about stars "igniting," we're usually talking about nuclear fusion "switching on." But the rate of fusion increases smoothly as the temperature in the star's core increases.

    It is important to note that this is also due to quantum tunnelling.

    Even our own Sun isn't nearly hot enough that we can explain the reactions simply with temperature.
