    I'm more familiar with Japanese, but basically it's guessing there too. Sometimes you can make a pretty good guess - a lot of kanji are built up from parts (radicals), and those parts can denote similar readings (referred to as phonetic components).

    For example, 白 means white, but as a component is read as "haku", and so lots of kanji that incorporate that radical are also read as "haku" in compounds - 泊, 伯, 拍, 舶, etc. But there are a lot of exceptions, so it's not a one-to-one system.
