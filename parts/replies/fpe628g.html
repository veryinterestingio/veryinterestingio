      A material has different properties that deal with heat. You have "specific heat capacity" which means how much heat (energy) can be stored in that specific material without things going funky (melting or deforming). Then you have "specific heat transfer" which is a constant that dictates how fast a specific material transfers heat. With a formula you can figure out how fast energy transfers between the glass and the water and the plastic and the water (as air kinda sucks for transferring heat, it is important but rather insignificant comparatively).

      In short: plastic has a higher specific heat capacity than glass however its specific heat transfer sucks so it needs a longer time for the heat to be stored in the plastic than to store it in glass.
