      Something really interesting to think about is the conservation of angular momentum as mass moves towards or away from the center of the spinning object. If an object is spinning and it moves mass away from its center it spins more slowly, and the opposite is true as well. A real world example of this phenomenon is how figure skaters pull their arms close in when spinning to increase the rate at which they spin. So moving heavy cargo outwards from the center of a station could end up impacting the speed of the spin.

    • Team_Braniel

      You would need something like this I believe. You would have to have a counter mass in order to be most efficient.

      Not having a counter mass would have the ship and the pendulum sharing orbits, with the nodal point somewhere between them. Thus decreasing the effectiveness of the pendulum and needing a longer tether.

      Counter mass would keep the ship centered of the orbits (allowing acceleration) and allow for the most artificial gravity with the shortest possible tether.
