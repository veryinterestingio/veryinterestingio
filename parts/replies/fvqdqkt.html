    • fly-not-fox

      Part of the problem with targeting cancer antigens is specificity. We don't have great characterization of cancer cells and how they differ from healthy cells in a specific enough way to be precise targets. Take FAP+ cells for example: we used to think they were going to be the key to targeting cancer cells for therapies. But further studies have shown this marker doesn't denote a homogeneous population and isn't specific to cancer cells. And sometimes the antigens or markers we can identify are internal, making them a less effective target. We're making a lot of progress! But we're not quite there yet to develop therapies which exclusively target cancer cells. And if we were to sensitize the immune system to itself, well we'd basically be giving someone an autoimmune disease.
