    convection (heat transfer thru fluids like water and air) is usually much faster (better in the case of ice cream making) than conduction (heat transfer thru solids). having salty water/ice allows you to have colder liquid water (let's guess 20 degrees F). unsalted H2O at 20 degrees will be solid ice and is "stuck" using conduction, while salted H2O at 20 degrees will be liquid water and can take advantage of convection.
