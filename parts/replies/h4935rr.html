    • jeffersonairmattress

      You're certainly right to point out the variance of temperatures in use requiring the allowance for movement in different materials used for bridges, railroad tracks, etc.

      It's also important in mold-making for cast metals. Patternmakers would have to account for the coefficient of expansion of different alloys and make their pattern larger to account for "shrinkage." For example there was (for grey cast iron items under about two feet in length) a patternmaker's scaled rule called a 1/8" per foot shrink rule; on it, a "foot" actually measured 12 1/8." For some cast steels and zinc the shrinkage is greater and a 1/4" per foot shrink rule was needed.
