    • PrehistoricAcademic

      Yes and no. While carbon is released as trees die, the trees would not all die at once or decay at the same rate. A new self-sustaining forest, one which grows its own new trees through old fashioned natural processes as the old trees die, would offset this problem and take a certain amount of carbon out of the atmosphere for the life of the forest, not just for the life of individual trees, which could be millennia.

      Edit to add: this is why reducing emissions is more important than carbon capture. That forest will take tons and tons of carbon out of the atmosphere and hold it, but after a while it'll mostly just hold it and not draw more carbon in. We need to store what's in the atmosphere now and also stop putting more in it.
