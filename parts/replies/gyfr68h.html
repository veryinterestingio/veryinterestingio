    • Appaulingly

      Yeah I don't think you're pulling enough of a vacuum. 200 mTorr Torr (if that is the minimum for that pump) is approximately 200 mbar which is far too large of a pressure to boil the water (at room temperature).

      What you could do to demonstrate this to the kids is to heat the water before hand but not to boiling. This increases the lower pressure required to boil the water. I believe you could heat the water to 60 degrees C and you would observe the water continuously boiling until it's all gone at approximately 200 mbar.

      ​

      Edit: Just a post script thought. You could accurately measure the temperature of the water when it boils and use that to accurately determine the true pressure in the vacuum chamber (sometimes the pressure gauges aren't great). Maybe a little experiment for the kids.

      • themocker23

        I am a field service tech for a manufacturer of chamber machines (and other packaging equipment). This is correct; if you aren’t positive you are pulling 200mbar I would heat the water to about 70°c just so the cool factor comes through.

        What I am actually here to say is that doing this in a chamber machine will actually harm your machines vacuum pump. Pulling water vapor into a vacuum pump like this is detrimental to their health, and should not be done frequently without changing the pumps oil much more often than normal.

        That said, it’s a cool trick that we use as a demonstration on why the product being packaged is very important to the process.
