    If you have a mass that is so large that its bottom is at a higher gravity than its top, it can start to have a very wierd tumbling orbit. The object has to be a significant fraction of the size of the thing it is orbiting. For an object in Earth or solar orbit this would lead to stresses that tear it apart. If the object is orbiting an asteroid or something small, it can stay together.
