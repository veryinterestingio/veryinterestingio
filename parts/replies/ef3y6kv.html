    The japanese knife is typically flat ground on the right (or on the left if you buy a lefty knife) and slightly concave on the other so it can slice straight. It is extremely sharp, it's only weak because an extreme 15% angle means it's thin for slicing. You can make a single bevel extremely strong, like a chisel, or bone cleaver. But it won't slice, like a chisel. In the japanese restaurant I worked at, we switched to a small bonesaw for bones. You're right there's no japanese magic, it's the blade geometry.
