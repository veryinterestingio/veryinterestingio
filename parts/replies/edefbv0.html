    Ehh even then it depends on the context still. Median and average together tell a different story than just taking the median and comparing it to other medians.

    For example, average of $100,000 and median of $50,000 tells you that most of the population makes less than $100,000 but there are few extremely high earners. A median of $50,000 wouldn't tell you that.

    Using a per household number is what I most commonly see used, and in some sense it is a metric of income / working population. It also has the advantage of not disregarding the costs of the nonworking population entirely
