    • justamblingon

      The math is right, but you're off by three orders of magnitude.

      Assuming a pool holds about 75,000 litres, a lightning strike (aprox. one billion Joules) would raise the temp by about 3 degrees C — not enough to cook you, but enough that you'd notice!

      Heat (kJ) divided by specific heat (kj/kg) times mass (kg) = temperature difference

      1,000,000 KJ / (4.19 kJ/kg)(75,000L) = 3.182 C
