    Noise is created by the alternating blocks and sipes in tires, and the tread block pattern determines how much noise a tire will make. The road surface determines where the sound waves go, whether or not they will be scattered, etc.

    On a recent road trip, there was a road in Florida that reflected no noise from my all terrain tires. They were as quiet as my snow tires. Was a weird experience as I'm used to, like everyone, hearing more noise on older road surfaces, and that road had the appearance of being well aged. Happened when I crossed into Florida from Georgia. The Georgia road looked equally old, but the second I crossed the border, the tires went silent. The tires have never been silent above 25mph, even on new pavement, and I was doing 75.
