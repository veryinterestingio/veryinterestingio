    • JuicedNewton

      Landsat uses what are known as a push-broom sensors where the image is scanned across a linear array of solid-state light sensors comprising thousands of elements for each spectral band.

      There were US satellites still using film in 1980 but they were military spy satellites. Their film had a very high dynamic range so could handle extremes of contrast but the brightness range in this case could be a bit too extreme to avoid blowing the highlights.
