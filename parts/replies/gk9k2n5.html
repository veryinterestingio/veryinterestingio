    • JakobWulfkind

      As I mentioned in my previous comment, relying on the same computer for all braking power and motion means that a computer failure while the car is in motion will leave you with no control of the vehicle whatsoever while your car continues to move. You need at least a backup system that will allow you to halt your car's motion even if its computer, power system, or stator windings have failed.

      A shaft pin handbrake might solve the problem of a vehicle needing power to remain stationary while parked, but the other function of a handbrake is to be able to stop a vehicle in the event that the primary brakes fail; using a shaft pin while the vehicle is in motion is likely to damage the shaft and cause the vehicle to drift, spin, or even flip.
