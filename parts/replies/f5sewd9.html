  • TiagoTiagoT

    Basically CRTs got 3 beams of electrons, one for each primary color (red, green, and blue), and those beams are arranged so that they always hit their respective color phosphors (there's a grid just before the phosphors that sorta works like a bunch of pinhole cameras, making the beams hit the screen always at specific offsets); the motion of electrons is affected by the presence of magnetic fields, when you bring a magnet you screw the alignment of the beams and they hit the wrong colors; and the system is so sensitive that you even risk magnetizing the grid or some other component and leaving the screen with permanent color distortions (though many later models actually have internal demagnetizers that fire up when the screen is turned on, making that characteristic tweng noise, which often is enough to clear any distortions).
