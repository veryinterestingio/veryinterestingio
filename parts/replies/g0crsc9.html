    • Yeti-Rampage

      Yeah good question! What’s happening here is that you now have kinetic energy. When you move the bottle and there IS an air bubble, the water moves along with the bottle and then when the bottle stops and then reverses direction (I just mean from the movement of your arm as you shake it) then all that moving water will come crashing into the wall of the bottle. This only happens if there’s an air bubble... if there’s no air, then the water isn’t crashing anywhere because there’s already water in the way to cushion the blow.

      This “crashing” process is important. There is a very high pressure in this moment (called the “stagnation pressure”) which is going to create a highly turbulent flow. Turbulent flow is just a fancy term for when the liquid moves according to momentum rather than viscosity... in other words it’s a very chaotic movement. The counterpart is Laminar flow, which is very calm, well-behaved flow.

      This turbulence causes all the water to mix up quickly.

      So you’re absolutely right - the water bottle with some air in it will mix faster.

      This same thing applies to cleaning the peanut butter jar. That big instantaneous pressure will cause the peanut butter to dislodge more easily, and also cause more soap to come into contact with the PB due to the mixing I described before.

      Hope that helps!
