    I think it is important to clarify you can solve full compressible Navier Stokes for a real but chemically inert gas relatively easily as far as computing solutions to nonlinear PDEs go - you get a numerical answer that depends on your turbulence closure model, but it will tell you pretty much what the flow will be minus really small scale effects that don't get resolved. The incompressibility assumption or the ideal gas do not make the PDEs that much nastier to solve - the main problems with nonlinear advection and turbulence occur for any fluid.

    High Reynolds and high Mach might not actually go hand in hand - Reynolds number can drop off with altitude for example, or you have relatively small length scales in play.
