  • PoisonTheOgres

    Woah woah,

    The basic notion is that childhood trauma can lead to long-term health problems like heart disease, diabetes and stroke.

    That's not at all agreed upon in the scientific community. Yes, a correlation is there, but as we all know, repeat after me "correlation is not causation".

    I'm in uni getting my masters on forensic child and family studies, by the way. According to my teachers, all those ACEs questionnaires online are total bull.

    Basically, there are way too many confounding factors involved in both ACEs and health. For example, people who are poor often have worse health outcomes because of stress, no access to (mental) healthcare, low education, more dangerous jobs, bad food, etc.
    Poor people are also more likely to abuse, again relating to stress, low education, (mental) health problems, etc.

    So do people with a lot of ACEs also often have worse health outcomes? Yes. Is this because of the ACEs? Hard to say.
    It's definitely not "you see your dad hit your mom, this gives you a stroke when you're 60"
