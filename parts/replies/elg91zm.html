    CMOS transistors are faster at low temperature and you can clock them higher. It is not that relevant for the normal operating temperature range of CPU in a computer with regular cooling but it will have have a noticeable effect if you cool it down with liquid nitrogen-

    Bipolar transistor are the opposite and is faster the warmer they are.

    It is easy to show that it is the case if you make a RC oscillation and heat up the transistor or dip it in liquid nitrogen, I have done it as a part of a university course and when CMOS speed up Bipolar slow down and vise versa.. The faster transistor is a large part why using liquid nitrogen make it possible to overclock it and not just that you can transport heat away efficiently.

    The temperature depended is more relevant in thing like RC oscillators, quartz crystal or any other stuff that need exact frequency. There is a reson that you need to compensate for temperature in different ways and that some high quality oscillators have a oven-controlled crystal oscillator where you heat it to a fixed temperature to create a stable frequency.
