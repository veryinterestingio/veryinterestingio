      • oxford_b

        Your brain has 12 cranial nerves that control all of the motor and sensory functions of your head and neck (and one that controls smooth muscle in the gut). Of the 12 cranial nerves, 3 are responsible exclusively for movement of the eyes (cranial nerves 3, 4, and 6). The optic nerve is cranial nerve 2 and it controls actual sight, so 1/4 of all the cranial nerves are dedicated to the eyes.
