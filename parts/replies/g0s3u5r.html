  • KiddThunder

    Piggybacking here on an already good explanation. Caffeine binds multiple types of adenosine receptors in the brain, one of which, as mentioned inhibits the build up of adenosine. Adenosine usually builds up over time and causes drowsiness, thus decreasing adenosine is one way caffeine us from feeling tired. Caffeine also binds three additional adenosine receptors, one of which causes release of acetylcholine and monoamine groups. The monoamine neurotransmitter group is composed of dopamine, serotonin, and norepinephrine. This is partially responsible for the stimulant effect of caffeine.

    So in short, caffeine not only prevents drowsiness (through inhibition of a sleep neurotransmitter) it also promotes wakefulness through its stimulant properties.

    OP is correct caffeine does not wake you up, this is a complex interplay between multiple hormones (one of which is cortisol), but it does prevent drowsiness and promote wakefulness.
