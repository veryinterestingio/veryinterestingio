      Not really related. Capital gains are relatively straightforward in principle. Lets say I buy a stock at $50. A couple years later I sell it for $100.

      That's a $50 gain, which I now owe taxes on. It gets a more complex when you start factoring in mixed income streams and start subtracting losses which offset the profit you made on that stock deal, but that's the basic idea.
