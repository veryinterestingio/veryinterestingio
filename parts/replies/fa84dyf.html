      • Khanradski

        Not quite. As someone that looked into setting up a soda tap at my home: CO2 and Syrup are a bit more expensive than many people think. Sure, the ice is cheap, resulting in less syrup/CO2 cost, but if you compare the cost of a 2-liter, to how much is costs you to produce 2 liters from a tap, you're not really making a killing.

        I found that making 2 liters from a soda tap would come out to something like $1.30. Compare that to the stores that will sell 2-liters for around $1-$1.50. These calculations took into account getting the BIBs at no markup from 7-Eleven (Got a friend that's a manager and said he'd sell them to me at the cost the store pays) . If you try to buy a Coke or Pepsi BiB from Costco or something like that, expect it to cost you closer to $2 per 2 liters you produce.

        I also contacted Coke and Pepsi to see if I could order straight through them, and the answer was no. For restaurants, you have to sign an agreement in most cases only to sell their brand, and you have to order a minimum amount of BIBs per month or they won't continue doing business with you.
