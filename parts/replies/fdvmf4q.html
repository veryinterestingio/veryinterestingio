    • Jasrek

      When your laptop is plugged in, it uses power from the outlet to function and doesn't touch the battery - so once fully charged, the battery just sits there, unused, until the laptop is unplugged. So you basically turn it into a desktop temporarily.

      For a cell phone, which will basically charge to full, use some battery, then charge to full again constantly, you might see some reduction in battery life over time. But in almost all cases, you won't notice the difference before you end up replacing the phone with a new one in a few years.
