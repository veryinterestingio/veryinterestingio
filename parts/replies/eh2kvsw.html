      • ImAGeekNotANerd

        Hydrocephalus is not related to drinking too much - alcohol or otherwise. Hydrocephalus is a case where the cerebrospinal fluid which circulates through the brain and spine is either blocked or being generated too rapidly, causing it to flood the brain in excess. This buildup of pressure can damage the brain severely if it's not treated, and when it occurs in unborn children, it can cause the head to visibly swell up as the skull is still somewhat malleable. To stop hydrocephalus, a syringe is used to pull out some of the excess fluid (usually taken out through the base of the spine - it's all connected to the same reservoir of fluid, and that's the safest area of the CNS to poke around in). If there's a blockage, surgery may be attempted to correct it.
