  • hematogone

    Tropism is an important consideration. Hepatitis B, C, and D are all viruses that target the liver so they compete for the same resources. Every virus has a "tropism" or a preference for certain types of cells based on their entry receptor and other factors. Just like a bird prefers a certain tree and fish live in specific types of water. In HIV, which mostly lives in CD4+ T cells, many other viruses with different predilections can reactivate and go wild at the same time, while still maintaining a high HIV viral load (e.g. HHV-8 in vascular cells leading to Kaposi's sarcoma, EBV in B cells, TB reactivation in the lungs or literally anywhere, other bacterial/fungal infections...)
