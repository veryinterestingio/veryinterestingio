      Look at comparisons of skulls. The Neanderthal jaw slopes backwards from the front teeth while the human jaw has a bony projection outward. This projection is what we generally mean by "chin". Obviously all hominids had similar jaw structures including a prominent lower jaw, but they lacked chins per se. Some humans have very small or even more or less absent projections and we tend to notice when they do- saying they have receding chins or are "chinless." Neanderthals could probably pass for humans on a subway, but they'd be noticed for having traits that are a little weird looking.

      Here's just one article you can find with anthropologists saying that the only hominids with chins are humans: https://www.futurity.org/modern-human-chins-897652/
