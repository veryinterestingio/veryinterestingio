        I just know that I can't picture things in my head? I found out that other people could.

        I first heard about aphantasia when it was in the news because of some research article like 5 years ago and I asked a coworker "wait, when people say they're picturing something in their heads they don't mean they're actually seeing a picture, right? It's just a figure of speech for "think about the concept of?"" He assured me that of course people don't actually see an image in their minds. It later turned out we both have aphantasia.

        One example of where it's kind of obvious in hindsight was that I always found mindfulness "visualization" exercises to be silly and I never understood the point. It turns out that other people are actually picturing something though. My therapist tried to get me to go through a "rescripting" exercise for a recurring nightmare that I have, and visualizing the rescripted version is supposed to be an important part of that, but I just can't do that. I was able to imagine some of the sensory experiences instead, but my imagination just doesn't involve a visual component.
