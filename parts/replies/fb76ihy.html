    Ok, so you’re not quite right, but not too far off either. The fluid in the semicircular ducts are what enables our body to track angular momentum (curves and corners). The otolith organ tracks changes in linear acceleration (forward/backward, up/down).

    A very ELI5 is that there is a viscous fluid in the otolith organs that when it moves, stimulates tiny hairs in the organ. The hairs being stimulated makes you “feel” the motion. When you’re on a treadmill that fluid starts to slosh in a rhythmic fashion. When you get off the treadmill you’ve stopped moving, but the fluid is still sloshing. Once the waves subside, so does the perception of motion.
