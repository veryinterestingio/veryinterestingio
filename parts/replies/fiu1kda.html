    • SartoriusBIG

      The best example is spicy food. A chemical (most commonly capsaicin) stimulates thermoreceptors (receptors that sense heat) and they send a signal to the brain that something is “hot.” Capsaicin does not necessarily stimulate other receptors that are responsible for sending other signals.

      Another way to think about it is that you can’t hear with your eyes or see with your nose. There are specific types of receptors that are built to communicate different things to the brain. You can think of chemical sense, pain sense, temperature sense, etc. as different from each other the same way that sight, smell and hearing are different.
