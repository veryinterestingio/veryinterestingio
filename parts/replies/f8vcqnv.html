    Exactly. It's not really teaching animals to communicate beyond a cause/effect type situation. The main takeaway is that animals can communicate, but humans have language.

    There are many aspects of human language that make it unique. One is called displacement, which is the ability to talk about things that are not in the here and now. Humans can talk about what happened yesterday, a year ago, or even what they plan to do in ten years. Animals don't have that ability. Many animals have communication systems like the monkeys that use different calls to signal the type of predator that is coming (i.e., how best to hide/protect themselves), and even honey bees that use a "dance" to tell others in the hive where a nearby food source is. But animals don't talk about what they had for dinner last night.

        I wouldn't say that Koko's communicator was lying when she said that Koko could talk through sign language.
