    • runner382

      Different cells have different molecules on their surface - the viral receptors. The outer surface or capsid of a specific coronavirus strain may contain certain attachment proteins which are able to bind to certain viral receptor molecules on certain host cells.

      This would explain why an upper respiratory system cell is more likely to get infected by virus X over a lower respiratory system cell.

      It's all molecular biology, and how certain molecules on the viral surface fit together better over others.
