    • destroyer551

      That picture likes to make it’s round but it’s a poor representative of the species. Although those are indeed Asian giant hornets, they are preserved (in alcohol or similar substance) wasps which accounts for their dark coloration, and preservation in liquids tends to unnaturally swell the segmented abdomen of an insect. Such can contribute to length not otherwise present in life. Those individuals are also queens, which are the individuals that reach 2”. The workers (the vast majority of the colony) average 1.4”-1.6” in length. Finally, they’re big insects on a small hand.

      They’re still the largest social wasp in the world, but they’re definitely not small-bird size like some pictures or exaggerations are making them out to be.
