      When colours “vibrate” it’s because they are the exact same value, or lightness. If you were to turn the two colours to black and white (a blue and an orange that vibrate for example) they would be the same shade of grey. Our brains perceive images in value first, black and white, a fraction of a second before we perceive the hue or colour, so that vibration is the brain seeing difference and sameness simultaneously.
