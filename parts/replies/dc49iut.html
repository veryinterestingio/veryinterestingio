    Expanding on this outside of ELI5. Since we define a function to be continuous if lim as x -> a of f(x) is equal to f(a) for all a in the domain of f(x). We can look at the function f(x) = 1/x.

    We can then look to see if the lim of f(x) approaching from the right and left like you showed, as lim x -> 0 from the left approaches -infinity and positive infinity from the right we can say that the limit does not exist since the limit from the right and left don't equal each other. Thus f(x) is discontinuous at f(0), hence f(0) has a hole in the graph at x=0 and undefined.
