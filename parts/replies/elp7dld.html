    I have not heard of zeta(3) being anything too interesting.

    Euler proved that for every even integer n, ζ(n) is a rational multiple of π^(n) (most famously, ζ(2) = π²/6). His proof did not work for odd values of n, but surely someone would find a way around the difficulty and prove some kind of formula for all n, right ?

    Wrong.

    We're now more than 250 years after Euler and the best anyone (Apéry) ever managed to prove is that ζ(3) is irrational. Is there any relation between ζ(3) and π^(3) ? No one has any bloody idea (there are obviously conjectures and computational results, but nothing more). And don't even dream about knowing anything about ζ(5), ζ(7), etc., it's just too damn hard.

    TL;DR: the value of ζ(3) has no practical interest (that I know of), it's just infuriating because it's ridiculously hard to tell anything about compared to ζ(2) and ζ(4).
