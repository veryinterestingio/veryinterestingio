      H. pylori is more of a commensal than a pathogen. It can cause gastritis and ulcers, but it only causes illness in a minority of people who have it. For most of human history almost everyone had H. pylori living in their stomachs, you can even use H. pylori strains to track human migration around the globe. Its so well adapted to living in the human stomach that its not naturally found in any other species or environment.
