    A much used way to look at "brain activity" is using fmri, which looks ast the BOLD-signal; the blood oxygen level dependent-signal. It's where you get those pretty visuals from(and how the field earned the nickname blob-ology). In order to use the oxigen in your blood, you need to burn calories, thats what the oxigen is for. I think it's therefore safe to assume that a working brain uses more calories than a hardly-working brain, though I'm unsure if it says anything about the magnitude of the increase in calory use.
