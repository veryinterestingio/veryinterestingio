    • phlogistomancer

      I'm a veterinarian and, while that may be true for humans, there are plenty of species that you'll blow the vein even if you do everything right. Getting venous access in birds is an art and preventing the vessel from blowing requires dedicated attention on pretty much every stick. Ferret veins are so freaking roll-y and their skin is somehow simultaneously thick and stretchy... good luck. Cats are tough because of the claws and teeth, and a lot of sedatives crash the blood pressure. Dogs typically revive my self confidence that I'm able to get a vein if I'm having a bad day.

      I did an IV on a human woman on an airplane once after she collapsed in the galley. It wasn't as easy as bleeding a cow, but it also wasn't particularly difficult either.
