      • mechkg

        CPUs are essentially printed on a very smooth sheet of silicon, the "wafer", called that because it is round and has a wafer-like pattern when it's finished, just google an image. They're not literally printed, but superficially the process is similar. Once you have this large sheet of silicon with multiple CPU circuits printed on it ready, you cut it (or "dice" it, the word "die" means a result of "dicing" something) into small parts that are individual CPU "dies", just like you would print a bunch of business cards and then cut them from a large single sheet of paper. They're not CPUs yet, they still need to be packaged, that is put into a protective casing and have all the external wires from pins or pads connected to the wires on the die.
