    Ooh, that was me. I had total recall as a kid. I distinctly remember being in 2nd or 3rd grade, and a beekeeper came to our school and gave us a 20 minute talk about bees. I found it so fascinating. When I saw my grandfather that weekend, he suggested we walk down to the creek, and then he asked me what I'd learned in school that week.

    I don't remember the bee presentation anymore, but I do remember thinking "Oh, he'll find this fascinating, I'll just repeat what the beekeeper said." I remember the utter confidence that I could repeat everything I'd heard a few days earlier, even the words I didn't know, and I remember it took the entire walk plus a bit to recount it so it must have been a long speech. I also remember how big a fuss he made when we got back from the walk, pulling my mom and grandma aside to tell them that I'd given a really advanced lecture about bees and that I was correctly using words that I didn't actually know.

    Anyway, yep - I lost that ability by 6th grade or so.
