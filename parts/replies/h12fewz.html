      • jaredjeya

        Yes, there is a light time delay. If you were measuring when a lightning strike happened down to the microsecond you’d absolutely need to take account of light travel time, even ignoring special relativity!

        The big deal with relativity is that the lightning strikes don’t just appear to happen at different times from the non-stationary frames - they genuinely do happen at different times. You make your measurements, correct for the light travel time, and write down that one happened before the other. No refinement of your measurements will correct that, because it’s reality.

        Hence this idea that time is relative!
