      • GoWithGonk

        The difference is that Judaism had both a worldly/Eastern mystique and a very long history. Judaism was tolerated and even somewhat admired in the Roman empire despite the monotheistic exclusivity thing (most religions at the time were not seen as mutually exclusive and many people joined multiple religious traditions).

        Christianity on the other hand was new and had a much harsher take on its exclusivity. I think it was seen more like the Scientology of it's day - a new, many would say calculated and fabricated, cult that claimed exclusive spiritual truth which denounced other faiths as false and demonic. So it received little toleration and was seen as dangerous.

        TLDR Judaism was kind of like Buddhism on the West today, Christianity was like Scientology in the West today.

    • GoWithGonk

      I would also point out that at least among the educated class, by the end of Roman polytheism, belief in the gods was less about seeking a supernatural explanation for things (the old myths were mostly seen as allegories for the way the gods worked their will on Earth) and more about identifying a source for both reality itself and human virtue. In fact this goes all the way back to Plato and the forms.

      Christianity adopted these ideas and simply changed the names of the gods, and then certain modern sects started to ignore all the philosophy and start taking their myths literally again, resulting in obvious pushback from educated people and a resurgence in atheism.
