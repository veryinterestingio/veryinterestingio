      The comparison with oxygenated liquid makes sense, although:

      • the body is more sensitive to what's inside versus what's outside, so replacing the blood is a much harder task than applying something externally (even if it's in the lungs, it's considered external for your body, the lung surface is still a protection against aggression)

      • there are many things that don't kill you outright, but increase mortality. Synthetic blood is currently in that category, But I suspect breathing oxygenated liquid would also be. I.e., its possible, but that doesn't mean it's not bad for you in the medium/long term

      • The blood has a hard job, in that it need to attract oxygen a lot to extract it from air when in the lungs, but at the same time not be too attached to it so that it can give back as much as possible to the cells it reaches. An oxygenated liquid has only half this problem. It needs to be easily detach from oxygen to give it to the lungs, but even if it has a hard time linking to it, you can just load it using pressurised pure oxygen to force oxygen in.
