      The mirror is what other people see. The colours are essentially correct, the "resolution" is high, the image has depth, and the focal length is that of a human. The flip is pretty much irrelevant. It feels more natural to you, because you see it more. The non-mirrored is more natural to others. They both look basically the exact same though. You look like what you see in the mirror. Though keep in mind, bathroom lighting is very different than other lighting, like sunlight. People rarely see you in the same lightning as a bathroom mirror has.

      A good high resolution (or high quality film) camera shot with the right lens and correct white balance and all that fun stuff, and then put on a high quality HDR screen or very good printing ink quality can look pretty close. It can even make you look better, given good photography and possibly editing. You using a cellphone camera is not even close to this, the photos usually look worse than you because they are low quality, colour and shape distorted, and generally poorly framed.
