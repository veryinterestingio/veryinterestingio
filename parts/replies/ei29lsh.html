    Isn’t this true for everything you might take a picture of on the surface of the earth without the mirror though?

    In order to see something (even with a camera), what you’re detecting is light bouncing off the object. The sun is the source of light so it must make it through the atmosphere to reach the object. Then in order to see that object, since the picture is being taken from outside the atmosphere, light reflected off the object must make it back through the atmosphere to the camera.

    I don’t think the mirror changes the equation here much. Say you’re taking a picture of someone’s face in the mirror. Light from the sun goes through the atmosphere once, hits the persons face and reflects to the mirror. The mirror reflects the light, which travels through the atmosphere a second time to the camera.

    I can’t think of a practical situation where the light must travel through the atmosphere more than twice without adding more mirrors.
