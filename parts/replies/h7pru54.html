    • wakka54

      What do you mean shrinking past a certain point?

      • Brickleberried

        If you measure the physical volume of every shell with equal thickness, such as spherical shells with a 1 kiloparsec thickness, the volume of each shell moving outward from Earth is bigger than then previous shell's volume until you hit about 10 billion light-years away. At that point, the volume of the shells starts shrinking. This is because you're looking farther back in time to when the universe was much smaller.

        It's not a pure r^3 rule in cosmology.
