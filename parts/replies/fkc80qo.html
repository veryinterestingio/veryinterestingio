    • superguardian

      The Fed announces a schedule of bonds they are going to buy, including the maximum total purchases they will make for each maturity and the actual price is determined via an auction. Essentially institutions that want to participate submit "bids" of how much they want to sell and at what price. The auction mechanism is a bit complicated and allows for discriminatory pricing (i.e. not every participant ultimately sells their bonds at the same price to the Fed), but I recall that empirically the result tends to be a slight premium.
