    Tl;dr: Atmospheres develop independently of a magnetic field's presence, but whether they stay or not is determined by whether the planet has a magnetic field.

    ​

    Almost all planets have the capability to have an atmosphere. Atmospheres are important because they insulate their planet and all that. However, incoming solar radiation from nearby stars easily damages this atmosphere and chips away at it quickly. A planet's magnetic field will act as a shield for the atmosphere, deflecting solar radiation and protecting it from decay.
