    • International_Fee588

      It's not that there isn't money in it, but bringing a new drug to market can take 10 years of development (unless you're a COVID-19 vaccine, of course), with an average upfront cost of $2.6 billion. Promising investigational drugs, including anti-cancer therapies, are abandoned all the time for this reason.

      Obviously there are reasons for the extreme prudence due to incidents like thalidomide, but the pharma industry is very much hamstrung by regulation which has resulted in pharma companies preferring to jack up prices on their existing product lines rather than develop new ones. We've also seen the public almost lose their minds over a negligible number of blood clots from the COVID-19 vaccine, so a lot of companies simply don't want the PR hit if a trial goes wrong. Many modern drugs, including Aspirin, probably would not pass today's regulatory hurdles.
