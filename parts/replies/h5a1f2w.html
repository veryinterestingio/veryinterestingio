    A couple of solutions to this, for anyone who hasn't considered it before:

    • phone apps like Sleep Cycle can monitor your movement during your sleep and wake during a window (mine is set as 30 minutes) prior to your alarm time if it senses you moving around (a sign you are at a light stage of sleep)

    • smart light bulbs like Philips Hue bulbs can be set to come on at minimum brightness and gradually get brighter over a set period, so your body wakes to light.

    I use a combination of these two and believe I sleep a lot better for it.

    • Bacon_Nipples

      Simulated sunrise with HUE bulbs is one of the best things I've ever done. I wake up way easier with the light que. There are pre-wake processes your body performs when it starts to detect light and whenever I accidently turn the lights off at night and wake up to my alarm in a dark room I'm way groggier than with HUE. With the simulated sunrise I have it start 30 mins before my alarm (hitting full intensity at 30 min mark) and I usually wake up before the alarm even goes off, assuming I actually went to sleep at the proper time. My alarm is also just bird noises that get progressively louder over 30 mins, so overall it's a really gentle wake up.
