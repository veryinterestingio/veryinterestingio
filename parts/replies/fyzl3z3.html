    My HS physics teacher, eons ago, had a little setup that he'd present to us as a puzzle. It consisted of two closed boxes. One box had two toggle switches labeled A and B, and a standard AC cord & plug. The other box had two lamps, one red and one green. The two boxes were connected by a single length of two-conductor AC cord.

    Switch A controlled the red bulb and switch B controlled the green bulb, independently. The problem he gave us was "There are four parts inside the boxes. What are they?"

    • Rampage_Rick

      Diodes

      More specifically, switch A is wired to a diode such that only the positive side of the AC waveform can pass and switch B is wired so only the negative side can pass. Each lamp is also connected to a diode so the red lamp only gets powered by the positive waveform and the green lamp by the negative waveform.

      Both switches on = full waveform = both lamps
