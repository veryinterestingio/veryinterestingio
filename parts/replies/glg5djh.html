      So you're saying, we just have to wait?

      Your question made me curious: how long would it take to build up 1 atmosphere's-worth of CO2 at our current emission rate?

      Humanity's currently releases about 30 trillion kg of CO2 per year. The mass of our atmosphere is 5.1 quintillion kg, and the same mass would be required for CO2 at a partial pressure of 1 atmosphere, so...

      (5.1 x 10^18 kg) / (3.0 x 10^13 kg /yr) = 170,000 years.

      That's long...but not as long as I expected. 170,000 years ago, homo sapiens were inventing clothing. (I'll also note this calculation sidesteps the issue that the atmosphere would run out of oxygen first.)
