  • AvenDonn

    You're overcomplicating the problem.

    • White blood cells are irrelevant in the context of a blood transfusion
    • Clotting agents are almost irrelevant in blood transfusions, and we have some solutions for that problem already
    • The other transports are just suspended in the liquid, the stuff we should be able to easily replace

    The red blood cell stuff is still beyond our tech, but be careful not to fall for the nirvana fallacy. Just because something doesn't solve all the problems in a good way doesn't mean it's a bad solution, especially in the absence of anything else.
