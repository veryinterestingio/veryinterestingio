    • immibis

      They can't cancel out because only one of them is valid depending on how you choose to analyze the problem.

      Imagine a ball hits a train, instead of a photon hitting a mirror, because speed of light and stuff.

      If you decide the ball is moving away from the train and the train catches up with it and gives it a bump, then the train is giving the ball extra energy. You could also use a different reference frame where the ball is chasing the train, catches up with it and gives the train a bump, so the ball gives the train extra energy. Somewhere in between is one where no energy is transferred.

      It is surprising, but true, that energy can be different in different reference frames (even different inertial ones). Still, all the equations work out in every reference frame. Also note that the momentum exchange is the same in every reference frame (including non-inertial ones, I think).
