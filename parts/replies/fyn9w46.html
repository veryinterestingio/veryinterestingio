    Having certain markers match between donor and recipient is very helpful and a certain degree of matching is all but required but not sufficient to prevent the recipients immune system from detecting the transplant as foreign.
    Almost all organ recipients need to take immunosuppressant drugs that dampens their immune system to prevent a rejection of the transplant. Those drugs however dampen all immune reactions making transplant recipient prone to infections.
    Directly after the transplantation the dose of immunosuppressants is highest and later reduced to find a dose of immunosuppressants that keeps the transplant safe while allowing the recipient to live a somewhat "normal" life.
