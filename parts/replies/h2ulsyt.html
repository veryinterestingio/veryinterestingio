    This is not true. There is no evidence of the NSA (or FBI, for that matter) watching your screen.

    I know I'm being a wet blanket about this, but it's a pet peeve of mine. I advocate for online privacy and security and I hate it when people pick up the defeatist attitude of "Oh everybody's already spying on everything we do. There's nothing we can do about it" because it's nonsense.

    Yes, the NSA knows everything that you've done using the online services of Facebook, Google, Microsoft or Apple (note: this is not the same as anything you've done using a Google, Microsoft or Apple device or software, only their online services). That is absolutely terrible and needs to be called out. However, there are still tonnes of areas of online life that are private, and Edward Snowden has given recommendations on how to protect them. I think it's important to be precise in talking about what privacy we have lost and what privacy we can still fight for.
