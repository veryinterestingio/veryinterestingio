      • katinla

        Not really... the center of mass of the Earth-Sun system is closer to the Sun (the heaviest object), but the L1 point is closer to Earth. A halo orbit is orbiting L1 close to Earth, not the barycenter close to the Sun.

        What you're describing would make sense if the other planets did not exist, and you had a satellite very, very far away from the Sun, so that the Earth-Sun distance is negligible with respect to the satellite's orbital radius. At this point I would agree, the satellite would be orbiting the Sun-Earth center of mass.
