    • Prttjl

      Look at the other answers to OP, because the way you define temperature for this question is important.

      For the simple definition: "temperature is a measure for the average energy of a mass of molecules" we can't give a temperature for empty space. Not because it doesn't conduct heat, but because there is no mass of molecules whose average energy we could look at.

      Just like with the road analogy. The question of the average speed for a empty road isn't useless because I can't get from A to B, but because there simply isn't anything I can measure.
