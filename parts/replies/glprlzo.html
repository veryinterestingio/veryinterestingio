    • BarbaraBushsBush

      leeches help the venous drainage. Arterial connections are easier to make, but if the blood builds up and there isnt good venous drainage, then the arterial flow will be decreased (this is called venous congestion). Leeches help remove that blood that isnt being drained by veins, allowing for better perfusion and new venous connections to be made.

    • cascio94

      If it's a flap it's not a graft, it's either one of the other (yours is a flap having it's own vascular supply manually attached, grafts don't have it)

      Also leeches aren't used to "pull blood into the graft", they are used when there is venous insufficiency in a flap to avoid congestion (= when there is too much blood that can't escape the flap)
