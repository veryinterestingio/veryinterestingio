    • WorBlux

      For an example of how a complex behavior can arise from simple rules and structure look at John Conway's game of life.

      >at an atomic level one thing is becoming another thing, and/or that somehow one thing can split onto two things.

      No, "thingness" is something we sapiens layer onto the universe from our brains. It refers to patterens or relations of matter, but has not fundamental material referent.

      Cut a straw in half in the right sort of way, you get 2 straws. The division of proto-cells was quite likely mechanical or thermally induced.

      >How does this work with the the conservation of energy?

      Pretty simple, life needs external input. Earth is not exactly closed or stable system. Day/night cycles, thermal convection, and geologic cycles all provide potential sources to drive proto-life cycles.

      Then proto-life simply needs to develop in way that catalyzes these gradients and in doing so becomes entropicly favored and well on it's way to actual life.
