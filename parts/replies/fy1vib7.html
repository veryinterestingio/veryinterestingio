      Anxiety. The single striking mark of autism in my life is anxiety, even as a child. Not knowing what comes next is difficult to deal with. I grew up very poor in a small house in Dublin.

    • linuxgeekmama

      I’m six years younger than /u/Oh_god_not_you, but I’m another high functioning adult who wishes they were diagnosed earlier. There are a couple of reasons.

      One, I knew something was wrong with my social skills, I knew I liked to fidget (stim) with objects, and I knew my sensory calibration was different than most people’s (over sensitive to some stimuli, under sensitive to others). I got bullied in school because my social skills weren’t quite right. It would have been really nice to have a name for what was up with me- why I was quirky and couldn’t fit in even if I tried.

      I figured out some strategies to compensate for some of the things I had trouble with. For example, I learned to make eye contact without staring because I knew that people expected that, although the only information I get from it is the person’s eye color. I learned to be discreet when I stim in public. But it took me a long time to learn those things. They do social skills training with autistic kids- if I had had that, I might have learned those skills earlier and more easily. There are accommodations that teachers and families can make for autistic people, but my parents and teachers had no idea that those existed or that I could have benefited from them.
