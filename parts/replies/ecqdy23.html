  • JustTheP

    Very few being able to eat past the point of profitability is the Law of Diminishing Marginal Utility.

    Let's suppose you are very thirsty. You drink one glass of water and you're partially satisfied, you drink another and then you're satisfied, and you drink another and now the water has lost its utility and is unsatisfying.

    A buffet is no different if you consider food and drink as units of commodity and there is a point where marginal utility becomes negative. There is a point where marginal utility and units of commodity intersect and a successful buffet should measure this point for each unit of commodity because this determines average cost.
