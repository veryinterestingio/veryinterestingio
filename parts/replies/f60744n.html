    In general it is thought that natural solar time is ideal. If you live on the western edge of your time zone, solar noon is slightly later than 12:00, whereas on the eastern edge of your time zone, solar noon is slightly earlier than 12:00. Most time zones are slightly skewed, such that the discrepancy between solar noon and 12:00 is bigger on the western edge. You can see the difference between solar time and standard time across the globe on this map: http://blog.poormansmath.net/images/SolarTimeVsStandardTime.png

    A lot of the research that we at SRBR consider most important is outlined in position paper: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/pdf/10.1177/0748730419854197.
