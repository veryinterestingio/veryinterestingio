      No - The average speed of the molecules in air at room temp is \~500 meters/second (1100 mph). Constantly pinging off each other at that speed even with no net "wind" direction keeps things too well mixed compared to how weakly gravity is trying to pull those tiny masses into layers.

    The follow-up to this is that above ~100 km, it does start to separate out.

    This is a threshold known as the turbopause. Below that height, atmospheric turbulence is stronger than molecular diffusion, and gases are evenly mixed by that turbulence. Above that height, atmospheric turbulence is weaker than molecular diffusion, and gases begin to separate out by mass.
