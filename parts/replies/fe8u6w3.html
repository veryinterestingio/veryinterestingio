    This is correct 100% rarely are train engines equipped with motors that take fuel and turn it directly to physical motion. It’s way more efficient to produce local DC power and put it through a VFD so that it acts like a positrac transmission. In short diesel fuel gets converted to torque Torque gets converted to electrical potential Electric potential is fed through a VFD to extract exactly the amount of power needed to move the load the power is given to electric motors which turn the wheels.

  • blanchae

    On a main line locomotive, there will be 6 axles and a traction motor for each axle. When the train starts up, the traction motors will be in a series configuration to increase the overall resistance and reduce the current requirements. As the train picks up speed, the traction motors will be put through several series-parallel configurations.

    The magnetic field generated by the traction motors in the cab is incredible. I worked designing locomotive control systems in the 80s and had a CRT based oscilloscope in the cab doing some measurements. As we started moving forward, the oscilloscope display turned almost 90 degrees from the magnetic field.

    Locomotives are way bigger than you think when you get up close to them. They also spit oil out all over the fucking place.
