    • Caenen_

      I must point out that a caldera collapse of a shield volcano isn't the same as one from a stratovolcano or even rhyolithic volcano - basaltic volcanoes like in Hawaii do summit caldera collapses frequently, and in this case it was due to magma finding its way through an alternate path, relieving pressure towards new north-east fissures and thus the pressure-less lava lake and caldera floor collapsing.

      A stratovolcano collapsing is worse because its magma doesn't want to come out entirely voluntarily in the first place but when it does, it can't seem to want to stop! Those kind of forces produce a LOT of tephra!
