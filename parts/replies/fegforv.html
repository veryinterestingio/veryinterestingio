    • intrafinesse

      Let's say I'm a company and I want to invest in a venture (a new product for example) and it will cost $x which I don't have.

      I have 3 ways of raising money:

      1 - Borrow the money a bank (or rich person)

      2 - Issue stock (i.e. Sell part of the company)

      3 - Issue bonds

      When a company goes public it typically doesn't sell 100% of all shares, it sells some and retains some. and the initial owners get shares as well. Then at a later point, it can sell more shares. It can award shares to employees as a bonus. And it can buy back shares.

      A company can't just endlessly create new stock and dilute its current shareholders ownership. In some cases if it offers new shares it may first offer them to existing shareholders. And not all stocks have the same voting rights. It gest complex.
