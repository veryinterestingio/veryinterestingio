    You really only want to do a venepuncture on an neonate if you either need a large amount or specifically deoxygenated blood. It's a really hard draw. They have teeny tiny veins that roll and collapse really easily.

    Sometimes with premies the only vein large enough for the smallest needle is in their forehead. There's only a few people in our hospital that are able to do that one. I know pediatric life flight can get it. They are fantastic.

    Source: Phlebotomist who can only do heel sticks and immobilizing for infants.

So I would always send it up the chain and try and get someone who was really really good. They start IVs on babies all the time, so they're usually pretty good, but they're always super busy. After that it's probably a peds ER doctor or the anesthesiologist if he's available.
