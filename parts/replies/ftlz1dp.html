    That's some of it, but there's also that when we're looking for things, we're used to "discarding" most of our environment because it's irrelevant noise, in the same way that you can drive a familiar route on autopilot and not actually remember your drive. So you're "looking", but you're not actually looking, because this is how you always do things in your house--you know what you're doing and just do it without noticing everything else around you. It's not "distraction" per se as much as it's routine disengagement in a familiar place. You can be having all those thoughts while you still pull yourself out of autopilot, and "mindfully" search, ie take notice of where you're looking, what you're seeing, and how you're doing it, instead of scanning. It's like the difference between scanning the daily weather like usual, and stopping to take the time to actually LOOK at each of the numbers.
