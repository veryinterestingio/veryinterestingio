    This statement is misleading at best. The moon is influenced in it's orbit around the sun by the Earth but you cannot say that that the moon "orbits the Earth, not the Sun". As a physics exersize in highschool, we were asked to show what might happen to the Moon if the Earth were to suddenly disappear. The Answer? So little as to be negligible. If you were to plot the moons orbit around the sun with Earths influence and put it side by side with a plot of the Earths orbit around the Sun, the plots would have to be rather large before you could see the difference. In fact, on a regular piece of paper, both orbits would look identical with the thickness of the line being unable to differentiate between the two.
