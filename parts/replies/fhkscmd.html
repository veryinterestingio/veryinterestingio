      Yeah, if the price is $2.599/gal, and you buy ten gallons, you'll pay $2.60 for the first nine gallons and $2.59 for the tenth gallon.

      That's not quite how the math works out. You actually pay 2.60 for the first 5 gallons and 2.59 for the 6th one, due to rounding. 2.599x5 is $12.995, which rounds up to $13.00 or exactly $2.60/gal. 2.599x6 is $15.594, which rounds down to $15.59, or $2.59 more than buying 5 gallons.

      However, in practice you're buying fractions of a gallon that might round up or down at any amount.
