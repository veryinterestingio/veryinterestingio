    • Viraus2

      It's tough because a lot of your comfort and enjoyment of a landscape will be affected by what you're used to and personal taste. There was a cold snap in Thailand a few years ago where the local public reacted with horror at temperatures that most westerners would consider "kinda brisk". And what's locally considered a reasonably comfortable day in Bangkok is probably oppressively hot to those westerners. It's not totally subjective - "comfortable room temperature" is roughly 65-72F the world over - but all people aren't going to agree on which deviations from this are more forgivable. Things like weather are subjective too, nobody likes getting plans cancelled by rain, but some might consider regular rainfall a decent tradeoff to avoid arid environments, wildfire risk, and constant bright sun.
