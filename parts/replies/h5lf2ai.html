      We know the densities of the planets in our solar system.

      Earth, Mercury and Venus are all quite dense, over 5 tonnes per cubic meter. Mars is slightly lower at just under 4 tonnes per cubic meter.

      Pluto and other dwarf planets are around 2 tonnes per cubic meter.

      Jupiter has a density of only 1.3 tonnes per cubic meter. Saturn is 0.7 tonnes (for reference, 1 cubic meter of water is one tonne, Saturn is less dense than water)

      We know the gas giants are made of gas because of this.
