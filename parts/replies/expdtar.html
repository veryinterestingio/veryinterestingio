• Seanzzxx

    No, film does not have that kind of resolution. Sure, you can scan it at that resolution, but at that point no detail is resolved anymore because of the grain. In controlled tests super 35 film, which most movies were shot on, comes in at a lower resolution than 4K (or 8 megapixels). 35mm (so 1.5x larger than super 35) scans at roughly 10-24 megapixels depending on the resolution, and that's slide-film (note that movies are shot on negative film), which has a higher resolution.

    ​
