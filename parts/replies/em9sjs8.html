    • the_muskox

      In the Hadean, the earliest period of earth's history, everything was too hot for plate tectonics to work properly. No large continents could form, and subduction was many times faster than it is today. Since there was almost no continental crust, and since continental crust is 5-10 times thicker than oceanic crust, the crust as a whole would have been thinner then. That probably has less to do with heat flow, however, than the earth's surface being a bajillion degrees.
