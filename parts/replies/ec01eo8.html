    • VieFirionaVie

      It's true that the birth rate declines in those years, but the number of children also rose and the proportion of children peaked in the 60s (I can't find a chart that shows the proportion of teenagers, but it follows that teenagers would peak in the 70s or 80s).

      Remember, the birth rate is not simply the number of children born, it's the number of children divided by number of women or total population. As baby boomer women lived longer through the 70s and 80s, the denominator increases, lowering the the rate despite there being more kids. Hans Rosling called this "the great fill-up" (skip to ~10 minutes).
