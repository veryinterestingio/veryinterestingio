    • JayKayxU

      The study we did had people listen to two screams that were either from the same person or from 2 different people and asked them, is this the same screamer or different screamers? The screams came from people the listeners had not met before, and the responses were accurate more often than not.

      This doesn’t quite reach the level of individual recognition (the ability to hear a scream or other sound and recognize who it is) - instead we call it “individual discrimination.” It’s thought to be kind of a cognitive prerequisite to individual recognition.
