    • LadyCatherinedeBirb

      Hello! Also a pediatrician, but based in the outpatient world (aka clinic.) My practice offers video visits so patients don’t have to come into the office and risk exposing others/themselves, and truly, the number of colds is extremely low comparatively. I’ve had lots of COVID cases (especially with the spikes around the holidays) but every influenza test has been negative. Our county numbers are essentially zero, so I have mostly stopped testing for flu this season.

      Masks and lack of in-person school is what’s really driving decreased influenza activity, in my opinion. We know that flu is spread by respiratory droplets, and that COVID is more transmissible than influenza.

      Personally, I will probably continue to wear masks at the grocery store and on planes during cold/flu season, even if COVID disappears completely and life resumes exactly as it was before. The lack of cold/flu illness among my coworkers during the pandemic has been really striking. I am fully vaccinated against COVID and I get my flu shot every year, but a regular cold still really sucks; and I have gotten a mild bout of influenza (mild thanks to the vaccine) but I don’t wish to repeat it.
