      • Necoras

        40% overall vaccination should absolutely have an effect at the community at large. You'd see it in a reduced R number. R won't trend towards 0 until you approach herd immunity, but you'd still expect to see an effect.

        The kicker is the lag time. 2 weeks after the first shot for moderate resistance, another 2 weeks after the second for maximum efficacy. On top of that, it takes a week or two for new infections to show up in hospitals, and another week or two for people to start dying. The vaccine's impact will take weeks, if not a month or two, to start really showing up in national statistics.
