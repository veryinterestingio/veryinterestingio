    Regarding black stars, how do we define "star"?

    The colloquial definition usually refers to objects that undergo nuclear fusion.

    If a star has burnt out and essentially has no energy generating nuclear activity would it technically still be a star, or an ex-star?

    Stellar remnant is usually the term used, although it depends. If one is talking about low-mass remnants, like white dwarfs and planetary nebulae, one might say post-AGB objects, for example. It depends on the context.
