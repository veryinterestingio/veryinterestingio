  • sunshinesonata13

    I think it mostly happens in highly technical environments, where it can be hard to evaluate where exactly you stand in comparison with everybody else

    Definitely not. It's not that literal. It's a feeling, and a deep anxiety that may be grounded in something society has created, or in erroneous belief patterns, or both. It happens everywhere, and it's largely rooted in feeling you have to prove your worth, that you somehow didn't earn it through hard work, skill and determination, as if you don't deserve a seat at the proverbial table.

    Really bright, motivated young women experience it because people often associate youth and femininity with a lack of competence.

    People of color who work in "historically white" industries (think banking, finance, education) experience it when they feel like they were the "diversity hire."

    People switching industries after being in a field for a long time experience it, because they feel like they might have no transferable skills or degree in that area.

    It's definitely, definitely not just in tech or highly technical/heavy hard skills environments. It's less about a measure of skill and more about a feeling. Some people can have it even if they're the most skilled person (by far) in the room.
