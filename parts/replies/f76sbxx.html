      Hi! Medical student here - I'm so sorry to hear that the clinic isn't providing you with adequate care or counseling on this topic :( - it's a really important topic, and they really should not be judging you or insulting you..

      Just wanted to plug Planned Parenthood and the Mayo Clinic sites as wonderful online resources to get some first-line information; this Planned Parenthood page and this Mayo Clinic page could be helpful places to start.

      Combination oral contraceptives (both estrogen and progestin) are great for managing period symptoms, and many patients will have lighter periods and less pain when using a combination pill. A progestin-only pill (the "minipill") can also stop your period, but some patients choose to use the combined pill instead because the progestin-only pill is less forgiving and *must* be taken at the same time every day to be effective at preventing pregnancy.

      (In case you wanted to learn more - some patients will also choose to get the birth control implant or the hormonal IUD because those often make your period much lighter and less painful. For many patients, their periods will even stop entirely - I personally have had an IUD since 2016 and haven't had a period since!)

    • Cutecatladyy

      I don’t know the specific answer to your question, but I went on an estrogen+progestin combo partially for heavy/painful periods and it’s been a godsend.

      I used to be bedridden for at least a day and up to three every month. Now I barely have to take Advil. I’ve had minimum side effects from the pill, mostly involving sexual function. It didn’t really hurt my libido, like some people though.

      Overall, I don’t feel like it’s really messed up my hormones. I’m not super moody because of it, I haven’t gained weight, and it helped my acne. I’ve thought about switching to an IUD, but my experience with the pull has been so good that I’m not willing to mess with changing.
