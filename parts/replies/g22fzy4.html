      • Allbranflakes18

        Something similar to this is also why when you suddenly look at an analog clock, that the second hand for that first second you’re looking at it seems to tick super slow. This is because your brain blurs your vision when you’re moving your eyes, but our brain doesn’t want us to see that blur every time we move our eyes, so it fills that blur up with a snapshot of whatever it is we are moving our eyes to. So when locking your eyes into the clock, your brain actually fills that journey your eyes took to get to the clock with a frozen image of the clock itself (once you look at the clock) and this gives the perceived effect of time seemingly being slowed down for that initial second. The more you know!
