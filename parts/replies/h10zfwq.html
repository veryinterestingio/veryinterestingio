    • zjm555

      Having your IaaS provider crash and burn catastrophically is just one way that a site might go down, and to be honest it's a much less common one. Most incidents are not due to the infrastructure provider, but rather the site itself exhausting its allocated resources or having a software bug.

      That said, status pages for major sites are geographically distributed with robust failover behavior, i.e. "high availability" distributed services. The exact mechanisms for load balancing and failover vary, but the business of CDNs is to be extremely robust toward exactly these types of scenarios.
