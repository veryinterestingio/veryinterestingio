    • TimoKinderbaht

      The metal in the core is conductive, so electrons are able to move around easily. Organized movement of charge in some direction, i.e. an electric current, produces a magnetic field via Ampere's law (one of Maxwell's equations).

      The reason the movement of the molten metal is organized to begin with has to do with convection. That is: the temperature near the core is very high, so the metal there heats up, becomes less dense, and rises (think of a hot air balloon). Once it rises high enough, it gets close to the mantle (the region above Earth's core but below the crust), which is cooler. Some of the heat is transferred to the mantle, which cools off the molten metal and causes it to sink.

      This process repeats over and over, creating columns of circulating metal. This organized motion of conductive material creates an electric current, and thus a magnetic field.
