    YouTube slows down by buffering, because who cares

    Zoom slows down by dropping frames and quality randomly degrading so that you can keep up with the stream,

    I don't think you know what you're talking about. Both services use adaptive bitrate streaming (as do basically all streaming/video conferencing services). Also, both services will "buffer" if they've shown the last frame of video that they currently have available and new packets haven't arrived yet, there's no other choice. Your program can ask for the lower bitrate streams all you want, but if the network is very congested and the next packets haven't come in yet, there's nothing to do but pause the video until more packets do come in... that's what buffering is.
