    • That_Biology_Guy

      That is certainly an important role of skeletons now, but they are also very important for serving as anchor points for muscles, and the latter function was most likely the initial driving force behind the evolution of vertebrate skeletons. The first hard part to evolve in chordates was the notochord (which is actually made of cartilage, but is basically similar to a spine), and this allowed for relatively strong swimming by side-to-side movements in a way that invertebrates can't really do (this figure demonstrates this idea pretty well).

      Anyway, that's a little off topic, but yeah I think you can sort of argue either way for tortoises. Obviously, their skeleton is derived from an internal one, but they've succeeded in moving pretty much all of their body into it (including moving their shoulder blades within their rib cage).
