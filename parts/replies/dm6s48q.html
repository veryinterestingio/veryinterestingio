Humans have millions of years of Object Recognition development behind them whereas computers have about 50. So while we're still in a state where a human can overrule the machine, it doesn't really make sense to do away with the humans.

      I'd say the error rate of machines right now is probably actually higher than humans, but because we only really use the machine's judgement when our own is uncertain, 1) there's a lower chance of anyone actually paying attention when the error takes place and 2) even if it is wrong, we've decided at that point to trust it so it doesn't really matter.
