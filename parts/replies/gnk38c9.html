    • Scout_Finch_as_a_ham

      I've always found it painfully obvious in episodes of Arrested Development. I think that the scripts were so convoluted that, by the time they got to the editing stage, whole running jokes or plot lines would have to get cut. But it was still necessary to dub in some lines to cover up the gaps and move the story/scene to where it needed to get to. So they'd use a take from a two-shot with someone's back to the camera and have the actor facing away dub in the necessary lines. But it would be obvious when you noticed that the actor's jaw line, just barely visible in the shot, didn't move to match the spoken words.
