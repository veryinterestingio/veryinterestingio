    Fissile isotopes are radioactive variants of some element that decays when it absorbs neutrons, and releases neutrons when they decay. Fissile isotopes can sustain a chain reaction, where one decay can cause an adjacent atom to decay.

    Whoa. Fission and decay are two different processes. Left to itself, a U-235 atom will almost always decay into Th-231 by emitting an alpha particle (He-4 nucleus), with a half-life of 700 million years. However, if it absorbs a neutron it will usually fission, i.e. split into two atomic nuclei, each of roughly half the mass of the original atom. Plus two or three neutrons -- fast neutrons -- which may cause other uranium atoms to fission in a chain reaction.

    [Edit] There is a very small chance that a U235 nucleus will decay by spontaneous fission, which can set off the fission chain reaction.
