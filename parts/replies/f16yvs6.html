    • EZ-PEAS

      Then you can have lots of points on your manifold which have little euclidean discs around them covering the whole manifold - which I guess is what we experience in real life.

      This is exactly the point, your little Euclidean discs are also called "charts," and the collection of all such charts is then an "atlas". A complete atlas implies you've got a well-defined coordinate system at each point on the manifold that lets you do calculus (locally), and now you're in business.
