      Indeed! Eyes are just a way for our biological systems to convert light into electrical impulses for our brains to perceive as colors and shapes. In the same way, your nose's olfactory cells are just a way for biology to convert molecular SHAPE into electrical impulses for our brains. Nerve cells in our fingers convert pressure and heat to electricity, our inner ear bones convert pressure waves to electricity, and so on. Everything needs to be converted to electrical impulses which allow our nerves to conduct those signals to our brain (nerves are specially designed just for this purpose - to transport potential energy changes from one part of our bodies to another). Specifically, with your eyes it's even weirder because your brain does not receive impulses that cover the full view, there's a part of your visual field that's blacked out because the light from that part of the visual field is projected at the spot the optic nerve meets the retina - and therefore does not stimulate rods/cones. Your brain uses a combination of pattern recognition and other tricks to "fill in the blanks" so to speak so that you "perceive" an uninterrupted scene. Perceive is the keyword - note that it's not the same as what real-world objects have stimulated the retina.

      If I implied that you needed eyes to imagine things, that wasn't my intention :)

      The purpose (but not the only one) of your eyes with respect to the above is to provide past visual stimuli to assist in the brain's natural mental image construction processes. In other words, provide your brain with some previous pattern of electrical impulses that have already been defined to be a TV - so that you will stimulate those same nerves in order to generate the image in your mind's eye when asked.
