    • who_is_Dandelo

      Honestly, I quite agree with you. I think those articles and my generalization of their contents give a fair answer to OP's question - as a generalization. There are obviously a lot more factors than can be addressed in an ELI5, and a lot of it has to do with the brain chemistry of emotions, which I know exactly nothing about.

      What I do know is that is that I did music therapy with adolescents for three years and saw music have a tremendous effect on their ability to stabilize their own moods. I had to jump through a lot of hoops to do it, though, because it seemed counterintuitive to my colleagues when they heard the music that my kids chose for their playlists - almost all of it was always extreme in the sense of being heavy, or intensely sad. Nobody was putting pop on their playlists. I had to do a lot of research to prove that it was effective, and my clients had to do paperwork (rating their before-and-after moods on a Likert scale) every time they had music therapy or wanted to use listening to their music as a coping skill (it was an inpatient facility). And the administration still shut down my music therapy program because they just couldn't wrap their heads around the idea the Cradle of Filth or Rammstein or songs about being suicidal really did help those kids deal with anger, depression, etc. So instead they took music away from the kids, then penalized them when they got in fights or cut themselves. Very frustrating.

      Another point that may be more relevant to you - in the course of the research I did for that music program, I found a lot of articles describing a correlation between metal fans and above-average intelligence, and that correlation is independent of mental health. People who like metal are often very intelligent and/or struggle with depression, anger, anxiety, etc. I should have looked up an article about that correlation for my comment above, but I forgot about it until I read your reply. That was a long time ago - 2006, I think.

      In any case, it's a really interesting subject all the way around. And I definitely agree with you.
