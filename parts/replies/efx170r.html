    This is actually super common! I work for a coal mining company (Am not a miner, just an IT guy), but we use something called “Long wall mining”. If I remember correctly this basically means that the miner (Huge ass coal mining machine) drives down the “seam” of coal while actual human miners bolt the ceiling and such behind it. Then once the seam is all finished up the long wall is pulled back and the shaft is collapsed!

    In my area of the country it is quite common to drive around and see houses with wooden braces and brackets on them, due to foundations/walls cracking not long ago part of our major highway collapsed for the same reason. You’ll also find a lot of people lose their water-wells, causing them to have to get water tanks and refill those every week or two.
