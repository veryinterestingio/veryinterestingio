    There isn’t a boundary in the sense you’d expect. When a roams 3-D object expands in normal 3-D space (the kind of scale where Newtonian laws are “good enough” approximations), there’s the object and then there’s the space that it sits in The object expands into the surrounding space.

    When 3D space itself is expanding, there is no “outside” in the 3D sense.

    As other people here have noted, the 2D analogy would be the surface of a balloon. The surface of a balloon doesn’t have an edge to a creature who’s walking around the balloon. They just go around and around. But if you blow the balloon up, the creature has to traverse a longer and longer distance on every walk.
