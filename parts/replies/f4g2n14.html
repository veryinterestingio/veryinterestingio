        XYXCYYX

        AAABBAAAACBBBBAA

      • 6EL6

        Yes— quite a lot of processing power for 4k video. In fact modern GPUs and CPUs (technically the GPU that’s built into most CPUs) have application-specific circuits that do certain kinds of video decoding really efficiently— because they are meant for it— instead of relying on raw “horsepower” to do this through software.

        As a result, whether a computer will play video well can depend more on its age and whether it was designed with a certain format/resolution of video playback in mind, than whether it’s an especially powerful computer. A decent desktop computer more than 5 years old may not play back 4k video smoothly but modern cheap tablets, smart TVs, etc can. This is because they have hardware dedicated for this, rather than putting enough money/energy into their hardware that they generally outperform a 5 year old general-purpose computer.
