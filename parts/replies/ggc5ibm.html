    • rookiestoic

      Yes, dopamine dysfunction is central to Parkinson’s disease. In fact, many of first behavioral / medical studies on dopamine aimed to understand etiology of the motor dysfunction in Parkinson’s as well as other symptoms in the disease such as cognitive impairment e.g. memory, concentration and from there, these studies gave rise to studies that looked at the the role of dopamine in concentration, attention, sleep disturbances, and more.
