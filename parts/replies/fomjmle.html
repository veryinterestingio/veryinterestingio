  • PrincessLapis

    I think most, if not all cats do recognize themselves in the mirror. Although it takes a few tries. Any small cat I've had who was initially introduced to a mirror would hiss at it at first, including a few times one of them spooked himself when he saw himself reflected in the window. But after that, I'm pretty sure they knew it was them. They never greet it the same way they greet a foreign cat. (Read: Poorly, for my current cats) They just don't pass the mirror test because they just plain don't care enough.

    One cat I have is rather exceptional with mirrors, and loves to stare at himself for hours in the mirror. He'd also sometimes stare at Mirror Me. When I stared back at him, I'd lock eyes with Mirror Him for a few moments. And then he'd turn his head to stare straight at Real Me, because he knew I was looking at him. So I'm completely convinced he understood mirrors perfectly.
