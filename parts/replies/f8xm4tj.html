      Edit: I wonder if I am unconsciously adding some kind of mathematical constraint that I am unaware of when I phrase my questions that way?

      Yes, you are. It sounded like the number of numbers was fixed. So the problem was "find the maximum product of N numbers that have a given sum".

      But now it's clear what you want is "find the maximum product of numbers that has a given sum" (implied is that the number of numbers, previously called N, is not necessarily fixed).

      In that case, it's just a few lines more than what I have already written. If the number a is fixed, then the largest product of N numbers that sum to a is

      p(N) = (a/N)^(N)

      and this maximum is achieved when all of the numbers are equal to a/N.

      You are now asking what the largest of those products is, where the number of numbers, N, can vary. That's now just a first-year calculus problem.

      Find the maximum value of p(N) = (a/N)^(N) on the interval N > 0.

      The only critical number of p(N) is N = a/e, and p''(a/e) < 0. So the maximum of p(N) occurs when N = a/e. Then the maximum product is

      p(a/e) = e^(a/e)

      and this maximum product occurs when all of the numbers are equal to e.

      Of course, if a/e is not an integer, then it is not possible to write a as "sum of a/e numbers". So just do the best you can. If a/e is not an integer, then it lies between two integers m and m+1. The maximum product occurs either when a is divided into m equal parts or m+1 equal parts. So just check those two possibilities.

      For instance, the maximum possible product of any number of numbers whose sum is 5e is e^(5), and this maximum product is achieved when all of the numbers equal to e.

      Now consider the maximum possible product of any number of numbers whose sum is 10. Note that 10/e = 3.68, and so the maximum product is achieved either from a product of 3 equal numbers or 4 equal numbers. For 3 equal numbers the product is (10/3)^(3) = 37.037, and for 4 equal numbers the product is (10/4)^(4) = 39.0625. So the maximum product of numbers that sum to 10 is (10/4)^(4), achieved by choosing 4 numbers all equal to 10/4 = 2.5.
