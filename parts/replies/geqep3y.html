      Mostly, we detect exoplanets when they pass between us and their star, causing a dip in that star's brightness from our perspective.

      • VuneAlpha12345

        We also detect planets by looking at the motion of a star. As a planet orbits a star, the planets gravitational pull on the star will cause the star to move. This causes the spectra of the star to red shift and blue shift as it will be moving towards and away from us. Observing the spectra of a star allows us to determine if a planet is orbiting it.
