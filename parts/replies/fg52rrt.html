      Thanks for asking, I'd never looked up the exact details. Sadly, the value was not literally measured in a calorimeter (at least not in the original case), so it seems I was the victim of a game of telephone.

      Strangely, the first detailed result I found is in this research paper (full text): https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4035446/

      Synopsis: Dr Max Wishnofsky came to the (derived, not literally measured) conclusion that a pound of whole human body fat tissue has an energy content of 3750 kcal.

      His conclusion (frequently quoted but almost always over-simplified) was the rule we all know, that the energy content of weight change was about 3500 kcal/lb. But his actual report included the fact that this must be from more than fat loss, and is based on a meta-analysis of multiple reports of weight loss in humans on various kinds of calorie restriction.
