      • Unsolicited_Spiders

        I had a common house spider (P. tepidariorum) that lived in my master bathroom for a little over a year during her sexual maturity (she passed recently; I miss her). I fed her crickets of the same type I bought for my tarantulas. Watching her move them around her web was incredible. These things were as big or bigger than she was. She would hook a line to the cricket (which she'd already envenomated), selectively cut a line or two to which it was already attached, then run up higher into her web and drag the cricket a little way up toward her. Then she'd turn around and head back down, add another strand or two of web, carefully cut lines that were holding the cricket, drag it up a bit, and repeat until it was positioned where she wanted it in her web.

        This type of spider builds "messy" webs (think cobwebs), not the classic orb webs, but they are still marvels of engineering. I will never cease to be awed and amazed by spiders.
