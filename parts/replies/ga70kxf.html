    • ChiefPyroManiac

      Are you able to hold your breath without plugging your nose? When you do it, do you hold your breath by blocking the air from leaving your chest (in the same way you might cough), or does the little flap at the back of your throat close up so air doesn't escape through your nose (like when you talk but don't sound nasally/make an 'n' or 'm' sound)?

      When you are underwater, many people have the natural instinct to do one of those two things, however neither of those will keep water from entering your nose if you go upside down. However, many also do not.

      Try holding your breath on land without plugging your nose, and think about/feel where exactly you are stopping the air from coming out. If your cheeks blow up, you're stopping the air with your nose at the back of your throat. If your cheeks don't inflate or you can still open your mouth without losing air, it's in your chest. Once you figure that out, try holding your breath underwater and see if anything changes.

      However, neither of these will stop water from going up your nose if you flip upside down/roll onto your back. The only option is to either blow out air, or plug your nose (or I guess just let water get in, but most people don't like that). Personally, I use my top lip to plug my nose. I pucker my lips and use the bottom to push the top lip straight up into my nose and cover both nostrils. It's weird to think about until you see it happen.

      Source: I've been around pools since I was a baby, swam competitively from 5-17, and have worked anywhere between lifeguard, swim instructor/coach, and aquatic coordinator between the ages of 15-25 (current). I've spent a lot of my life underwater.
