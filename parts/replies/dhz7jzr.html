    I've been thinking lately about this because I'm a nanny. The kid I care for most often is 3 and will repeat a word or phrase over an over until I repeat it, which shows him that I understood what he said. It also helps him when I repeat after him, because he gets to hear the "proper" pronunciation after his attempt. I think this is definitely connected to what you're saying here about watching the same movie or episode or video over and over again.

  • DoctorB86

    Repetitive learning behaviors are age appropriate for the age range you mentioned. Jean Piaget theorized that learning involves assimilation (taking in info) and accommodation (taking old schemas and adapting them). kids in the pre operational phase of cognitive development involve egocentricism and centration - they struggle to see things from other perspectives and tend to focus on one topic or situation at a time, respectively. Which might explain why a kid is satisfied with watching the same cool stuff rather than explore new stuff. They aren't logical yet so they way they think is pretty concrete. They also can't imagine reversing or undoing a sequence of events (irreversibility). Plus, if a child enjoys something, they want to experience it again. Mastery (and celebrating it) is a fundamental aspect of social learning.
