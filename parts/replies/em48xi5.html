    • Just_a_random_man

      Some clients love to cut corners and do shit cheap. We try our best to tell them not to but if they want to do it cheap then we always take it in writing that we're absolved of liability....

      But you'd be surprised how cheaply even tall skyscrapers can be built. The material is manufactured in places like China, so the costs are drastically cut down. Product quality is monitored, however. Construction isn't as expensive as you'd think. In India we can get good construction for under 50 USD per square foot, and that's the higher side.

      Additionally, the 1.2B is just the construction cost. The finishings, the interiors, the landscape isn't generally divulged.

      Solid metal panels are inadvisable in very high building because they add tremendous weight. Aluminium sandwich panels are normally safe. But freak accidents happen. Which is why quality control is crucial.
