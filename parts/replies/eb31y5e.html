    It might not even be Youtube's servers that perform the caching. Most content providers utilize 3rd party content distribution networks (CDNs). These are provided by companies such as Amazon (through AWS CloudFront) or Akamai, or perhaps in some cases by the content provider itself (possible in the case of Google/Alphabet). It's the CDN that does the caching in nearly every case.
