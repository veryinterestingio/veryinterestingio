    • jacksonj04

      There’s a bit of a blurry area between “fingerprint” in the sense of the absolute physical arrangement of ridges etc at the tip of a finger, and “fingerprint” in the sense of what a forensic specialist considers.

      It’s very, very unlikely (probably never occurred) that any two people share the exact same pattern on their fingertips, but it is far more likely that two people’s fingerprints are close enough that if they were compared forensically (or more accurately that the marks they left behind were compared) a specialist would consider them a match.
