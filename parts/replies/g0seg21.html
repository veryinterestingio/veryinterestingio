    The IC50 (concentration at which there is half maximum blocking of effects) for blocking GABA is 0.36 mM(millimolars). This is about as much as 70 grams of Caffeine suspended in 1 liter of water.

    While I can't find a 1:1 comparison, the binding affinity of Caffeine for the Adenosine (A2A) receptor is at 2.4Î¼M(nanomolars). Since a lower number is better, that makes the Adenosine binding of Caffeine 150 times stronger than that to GABA.

    So GABA-related effects will not be relevant until you overdose, when it can cause convulsions (like other GABA blockers).
