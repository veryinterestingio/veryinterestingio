    At the risk of adding confusion, the master lacquer (not vinyl yet) is cut with a cutting head on a worm drive whose inward rate is varied to make the groove spacing larger during loud passages. When cutting from a tape, that rate is controlled by a second tape head a few inches ahead of the primary one. And yes, one can slow the tape speed and the lathe rpm by half and get a better result, but that requires special bias if filters so it was not common.
