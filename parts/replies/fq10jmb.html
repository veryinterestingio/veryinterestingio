    Depend on what ammunition you use. classic cannonballs produce a lot of shrapnel from whatever they hit (like a wooden ship, a house, or the ground), and punch cannonball sized holes in stuff. (doesn't really matter what part of you is hit, you're out. And it won't stop just because it hit you, it will hit all the guys behind you too)

    And that's just some of the more common types.

  • Hanginon

    There was more variety avalable to artillery in 1776 than just solid round balls, which in themselves, are pretty devastating. A 3 pound solid iron ball traveling at 200meters/second (450mph) is not going to just stop when it hits the first thing in it's path, especially if the first thing is a person. There will be a long trail of it flying through and bashing things up, other soldiers, horses, wagons, anything.

    Explosive shells for field artillery existed, as well as grapeshot and chain, or bar shot which would tear through the ranks while spinning on a common axis. Chain and bar shot was primarily for tearing the rigging out of ships, but was also a rather effective anti-personnel weapon. And if you've got 'em, use 'em.

    Fun fact; The old pirate trope of the phrase "Shiver me timbers!" (Old time sailor talk for (Holy Shit!) refers to a cannonball blasting through the side of a wooden ship. Splintered pieces of wood were then known a "shivers", and the ball would blast dozens if not hundreds of sharp, broken, high speed pieces, "shivers", of the wooden ship hull out of the ship's wall, and likely into anyone near by. Not good, not good at all.
