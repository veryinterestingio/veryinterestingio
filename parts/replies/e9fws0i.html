    There's more to it.

    H-1B law requires the following:

    "An employer which recruits in good faith must offer fair and nondiscriminatory opportunities for employment to U.S. workers. U.S. workers must be given fair consideration for jobs. H-1B workers must not be favored over U.S. workers."

    Companies therefore use phony job postings to demonstrate that they were unable to find qualified U.S. applicants, allowing them to willfully bypass the spirit of the law to hire cheaper, and more exploitable, immigrant H-1B labor. In fact these positions are impossible to qualify for by design. For instance, you may see an "entry level" position requiring three years of experience with a language or tool that's only been around for one year. That's not a typo, that's done on purpose. It's a deliberate and cynical loophole that denies U.S. citizens the right to work in many fields.
