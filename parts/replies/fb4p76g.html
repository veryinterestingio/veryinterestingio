    It can be both. Babies actually have an "accent" when they cry. People of the same larger group can more easily distinguish the types of crying a baby is making than they can a baby from an outside group. Meanwhile, a mother can always identify the cry of their baby from other babies.

    Language is a group's shared code, but language itself is something all humans do. Body language (and similar non verbal communication) is also different across cultures, but still a shared phenomenon.
