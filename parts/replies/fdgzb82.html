      • restricteddata

        Uranium ore does have enough short-ish half-life U-235 to register so prolonged exposure is not recommended, there's a long term cancer risk.

        The exposure risk with ore is not the U-235 content, it's the uranium daughter products (like radon gas). You can handle uranium ore with your bare hands (you know, wash them before you eat something), but you don't want to be around too much of it at once without proper ventilation of the gases.

        Uranium is a heavy metal and has toxicity associated with that which is separate from its radioactivity.
