  • JonesNate

    I call this Mental Chess. I don't know if that's an actual term for it, or for something else, but that's what I call it.

    If I'm in a tough situation, I'm thinking about all the possible things I could say, and all the possible responses, before I open my mouth. As of right now, I'm 32 years old. It's not as bad as it was before, but I still sometimes catch myself doing it.

    The thing is, there are likely thousands of possible things you/I could say, and multitudes of responses the other person could reply.

    No human, aside from some INCREDIBLY brainy person (who's probably smart enough to avoid this situation entirely) is able to figure out all the responses.

    Recognizing that was one of my first steps in dealing with this. I can't expect the other person to say a certain thing in response to me. So, rather than standing there, mouth shut, I'll just take a second and figure out what I mean to say. English is a very precise language, so if I think for a second before opening my mouth, I can deduce exactly what I want to say, and then I can say it.

    If the other person reacts negatively, there's really nothing you can do. That's their choice. You can speak softly and with respect, but some people just won't understand, no matter what you say. So, don't worry so much about how they'll react. Instead, focus on making yourself as honest and precise as possible.
