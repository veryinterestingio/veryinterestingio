    In some countries, maybe. In the UK we use "left" and "right" to refer to the Labour and Conservative parties respectively, but in The House Of Commons, the party that is in government sits to the speaker's right, whilst the opposition sits to their left.

    So right now, with the Conservatives in power, the Right does sit on the right; but should Labour win the next general election, the Right would sit on the left.

    There was a cute story some years ago where a blind MP's guide dog took some persuading to switch to the other side.
