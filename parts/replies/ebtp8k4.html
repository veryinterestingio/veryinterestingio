      If you want to learn something even a bit more interesting, check out "two's complement." Basically it's how you turn addition into subtraction. So 5 - 3 is instead represented as 5 + (the twos complement of 3). Check it:

      3 = 00000011

      two's complement of 3 (invert all the bits and add 1):11111100 + 1 = 11111101

      Okay 5 = 00000101

      ​

      So now we add 5 and two's complement of 3....

      000000101 (5)

      +11111101 (two's complement of 3)

      ---------

      000000010 =2

      ​

      Holy crap... we just *added* two numbers to accomplish subtraction... computers are crazy like a fox.

      ...every math operation is therefore derived from addition... CPUs have a circuit called an "adder" and that's its whole job... of course, we've built all kinds of crap on top of that these days, but conceptually they all work the same way at the lowest level.
