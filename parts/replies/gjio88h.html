    • robbak

      Sign languages are not just signed versions of spoken languages. They are independent languages, with complicated origins and relationships with other signed languages.

      In general, sign languages started when deaf people were gathered together, often in special schools aimed at getting them to communicate using speech and lip reading. Instead, they started to communicate with each other in signs. The system of signs rapidly developed the syntax and complexity of spoken languages, but as the students generally had little knowledge of the spoken or written languages of their countries, there was no reason for them to be related.
