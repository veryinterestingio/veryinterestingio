    It's actually modular and connective at the same time. The different parts of the brain has separate functions, yes, but they also interact with each other. Think different roles in a team that somehow do work well together. In fact, the visual part of the brain is mainly in one region, yes, but also spreads out to other parts of the brain as well. I remember how Oliver Sack's books have a fascinating view of how visual neuroscience works.

    There are conditions, which I unfortunately forget the names of (Just like I keep forgetting most people's names), where you can see objects but you don't actually know what the object is. There are conditions where you, see the world like separate pictures in a comic book, but they don't flow like actual animation. Lastly, there are even conditions where you can't experience language at all, but you can communicate through pictures just fine.
