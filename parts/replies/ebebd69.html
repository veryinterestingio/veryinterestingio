    There was a paper published in Nature a couple of years back that finds evidence that harmony that we like only sounds nice because we were brought up with it culturally.

    In other words, if you are brought up in an isolated tribe your idea of nice sounding harmony is completely different.

    As such, if you want to mathematically model it you are not programming it to write music that is 'intrinsically good', but just music that sounds to relative to how our brains have been trained.

    I will find the paper later when im not on my phone, I imagine people will want the source. I think it was summer 2015 or 2016 in Natures main journal. And it was about isolated tribes and harmonic preference.

      That does sound interesting, but I don’t think the Tsimane have a ‘music culture’ at all do they?

      I’m pretty sure the Tymoczko book goes through different musical cultures, such as some east-Asian tradition, middle-eastern tradition and Indian tradition, and selects the things that they all exhibit.

      So, in all of them, pentatonic scales have been ‘discovered’ and the use of drone has been ‘discovered’, but certain aspects of that have been altered. Like the use of quarter tones. Or the construction of scales. Then there’s 5ths, everyone seems to like perfect 5ths.

      Western harmonic tradition seems to be the only one that came up with triads and complex chords, which move stepwise into other complex chords for the entire duration of a piece. So that does seem entirely cultural.

      Of course now western harmonic tradition has been spread globally, into all those areas and become standard modern practice, especially in the music industry. So it’s hard to separate out the traditional cultural stuff in the sea of modern western influences music.

      So, anyway, it would seem to indicate that there’s a progression from no music culture to fifths, drones and pentatonic scales, to more complex scales, to more complex harmony, to chords, to chords in sequence, to complex chords, to complex chords in sequence. Similarly how there’s a progression in different languages for the amount of colours that have distinct names, which has also been studied and is believed to be cultural.

      So yeah, interesting stuff.
