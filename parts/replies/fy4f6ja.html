      I feel like it's a "little of column A, little of column B" thing.

      Cholesterol and fat are now seen as actually beneficial (though quantity/portion control matters!), and not as bad as previously thought. BUT, too much cholesterol (or fat or...quite a few other things, actually) can get caught in small capillaries and bends and such in the cardiovascular piping system. So when it stacks up, you can get constriction and/or blockage.

      Not sure how exercise fits into that other than I would think it has a tendency to break up those deposits due to more blood flowing and the vessels dilating.
