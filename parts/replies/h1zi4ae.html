        I have a master's and my wife is a PhD. I have worked in higher education for many years, so I have been constantly surrounded by PhDs and I count many as friends. What I can say about them is that they are... people. They are not usually geniuses. On average they tend to be intelligent, they tend to be curious and they tend to be driven. In general they are interesting people, and very passionate about their field and research. But, they are just people. Some are good, some bad, some are nice and others not so much. I have never met someone with a PhD who is a "complete idiot about everything else," but sometimes they lack in social graces and they may not care about mundane things that most people would take for granted, which can make them seem like idiots because they don't know something everybody else does. They could certainly learn it, they are just not very interested in many of those things.
