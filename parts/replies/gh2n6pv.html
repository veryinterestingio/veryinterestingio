  • catwhowalksbyhimself

    While is is true that caffeine is diuretic, coffee is not. People assume that just because one component of a food or drink is diuretic, the whole food or drink is too. That's false if said food or drink contains enough water.

    Think of the caffeine as creating a water "debt." As long there is enough water in the drink to pay the "debt" you still get a net possible of water and the drink hydrates you, just not as much as an equally large glass of water would.

    Normally prepared coffee or tea does not have enough caffeine to incur a net debt. The water contained in these drinks is more than enough to make up for the diuretic effects of the caffiene.
