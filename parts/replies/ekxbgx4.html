    For the gas giants, the convention has been to define zero altitude to be the atmospheric depth at which the pressure equals 1 bar (100 kPa).

    If you were flying an aircraft on Jupiter, could you fly at an altitude of -100,000 meters?

    • slayersleigh

      Yes in fact the Galileo mission survived approximately 140ish km past the 0 altitude point. The temperatures and pressures are rather extreme, but compared to the max extremes further down in jupiter they are quite mild. Youd find a variety of chemical cloud compositions, but besides those factors and rather large wind speeds one could probably design a craft that could stay at that altitude without being destroyed for some time.
