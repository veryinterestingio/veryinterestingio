    • thenesremake

      You don't need a magical camera, you just need to have your camera set to save files in the .raw format or some other lossless format. Lossless formats just describe every pixel of the image and result in no degradation of digital quality, whereas lossy formats use types of compression that degrade digital quality in order to save space. JPEG compression is a good example, depending on the level of compression chosen when encoding the file, it takes similar shades that are too similar in wavelength to be noticeable to the naked eye and simply homogenizes them. It's a lot easier to say "15 blue pixels in a row" as opposed to "1 blue pixel, 1 slightly lighter blue pixel..." and so on.

      • Coomb

        Lossless formats don't guarantee that every image is the same size. It's easy to perform actual lossless compression in general, it's just that you'll get less compression than you would if you were willing to throw away some information. An actual raw image, on the other hand, where every pixel uses the full precision of the color depth and no compression is applied, will be the same size no matter the content.

      • Sir_Spaghetti

        Sure, but lossless describes compression, not a format, and it can save some space.
