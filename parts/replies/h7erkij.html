      In fact, it’s shaping up to look like it really was a gamma ray burst wot dunnit for the Late Ordovician mass extinction, but not from geographical patterns as far as I know. It’s been hypothesised for a long time that a GRB was responsible, because it appears to be rapid, and the astrophysics modellers said that statistically it has probably occurred once during the Phanerozoic when it’s been aimed straight at us.

      What I’ve seen recent research though are theee further aspects which are fairly convincing when taken together (don’t remember too much detail but I’ll mention them anyway):

      (1) physical modelling of what would happen produces a convincing kill mechanism through extreme degradation of the atmosphere (particularly the ozone layer) and subsequent harmful radiation coming through for tens of thousands of years afterwards.

      (2) there is fossil evidence from plant spores in at least one Devonian-Carboniferous boundary section, showing how their spores are deformed in a manner consistent with high levels of UV radiation.

      (3) the presence of certain short lived radioisotopes of elements produced in r-process nucleosynthesis in and end Devonian section.
