      I touched on testosterone. Reduced T leads to reduced muscle mass. Less muscle mass means less energy demand for maintenance. (Sidenote: The popular notion was that each pound of muscle burns 50 calories per day. That's completely false. In reality, it's about 10% that amount - 6-7 calories per day per proud of muscle.)

      But compared to reduced activity, these are honestly secondary, maybe even tertiary, factors at best. The truth is that your lifestyle changes as you get older (move slower, sit more, less exercise, etc.) count for the overwhelming majority of metabolic reduction - to the extent that everything else is negligible in comparison.
