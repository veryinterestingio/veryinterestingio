    • Movpasd

      The key point isn't really about the uncertainty, it's about scaling laws. You can do a little bit of trigonometry to work out the exact angle, actually. If the star has width W, and the telescopes are a distance D apart, and the star is a distance X away from the Earth, then the angle will be a = arcsin((W - D)/R).

      The key point is to think of the scaling law, or the asymptotic behaviour involved, as W/R -> 0. We have a = 0 + O(W/R). The O(1) behaviour is vanishing. That's what a mathematician would say; a physicist would say instead, for a star that is far away, the telescopes are parallel.
