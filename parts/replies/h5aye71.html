    XX/XY - most mammals, some insects (I.e. fruit flies) XX is female, XY is male

    XX/X0 - some insects (I.e. cockroaches or crickets) 0 denotes the absence of a second sex chromosome. XX is female, X0 is male.

    ZW/ZZ - birds, some insects, some reptiles (I.e. Lepidoptera, aka Ancient Greek moths and butterflies, or the Komodo dragon) itâ€™s the opposite of ours, ZW is female, ZZ is male. Some moths and butterflies have been found to be female even with Z0 or ZZW chromosomes.

    UV - algae who are not hermaphrodites. U gives rise to female traits and V gives rise to male traits.

    Haplodiploidy - ants or bees, and other insects. This one is complicated but kinda neat! Long story short, the parent bug can decide what sex the offspring have.

    Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sex-determination_system?wprov=sfti1
