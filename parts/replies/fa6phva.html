    This is wrong

    The Doppler shift is not what causes us to not see the light (since some non-visible ultra-violets get shifted into visible anyway). It's the fact that there is a finite size to the observable universe, because light has only had 14 billion years to reach us. We can't (and never will due to expansion), see stars beyond that distance.

    For example, the Hubble ultra deep field is an image containing stars right on the edge of the observable universe and was taken in visible frequencies.
