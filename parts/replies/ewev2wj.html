      • ray1472

        I dealt with multiple traumas as a child and young adult- could never figure out why therapy/antidepressants/lifestyle changes didn’t work to make me feel alive and shake me out of the numb I felt in general for about 18 years (on antidepressants to treat since I was 10). Why, despite all my effort, could I not shake it?

        After working on mindfulness and emotional tolerance for a couple years my counselor and I used EMDR to help me process the traumas. Changed my brain, changed my life. See if you can find a counselor who practices EMDR and work with them to bring you to a state where you can process your trauma and move on. I will add that EMDR doesn’t erase trauma or memories- it’s like if you have a filing cabinet you an close all the way and a few files are sticking up- emdr helps pat them into place. The files are still there, you’re just not living in them all the time! It feels super weird and seems like a weirdo new age thing but it works for many many people who have ptsd, trauma, etc.

        After hard work and six years of intensive therapy, mindfulness classes, and a year of emdr, I have been depression-free for 2.5 years. I still have normal ups and downs and sad days, but I am not numb anymore and it’s incredible. I’m finally alive at 30.

        I hope whatever you choose you talk to someone and can work through this. Hang in there!

    The cold hollow feeling you get when you receive very bad news is not feeling nothing, nor depersonalization. It is an actual bodily sensation. Many (most? all?) emotions involve what is called a "somatic state" which involves sensations in the body, as the emotions involve biochemical changes throughout the body. Mind and body are not so easily separable as our Cartesian influenced mindset tends to think.
