    Exactly! Water is one of the only (or possibly THE only) substance that is more dense as a liquid and less dense as a solid. So it takes up more space as ice than it does when as liquid water - which is also what allows frozen ice to float (on lakes, ponds) on the surface and not sink to the bottom. All or most other substances behave in the opposite way. As they move from gas to liquid to solid they become more tightly packed bonds, and expand as they go from solid to liquid to gas. I find this really interesting.
