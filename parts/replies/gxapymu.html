    This isn't entirely true. If the species was one where sex determination is environmental and not genetic, e.g. some reptiles, then you could clone both sexes from a single specimen. Even for species with genetic sex determination, if your specimen was the heterogametic sex (males in XY systems, females in WZ systems), then you could theoretically genetically modify a clone to remove the Y/W and duplicate the X/Z to create a clone of the opposite sex. Of course none of this gets around the inbreeding issue, but it isn't 100% guaranteed that inbreeding will cause problems, it just increases the risk.
