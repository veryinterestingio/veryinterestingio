      Partially this.

      It's not just regions, it's the fact content distributors refuse to evolve beyond business models built around the sale of physical media.

      Creating content has a cost, as does distributing that content as part of a physical product.

      The realities of digital distribution are very different: You don't need expensive facilities for pressing disks and making packaging; instead you need server farms. You don't need to maintain inventory at warehouses all over the place, and you don't need to ship physical media around; instead, you have to pay for bandwidth and maintain a website.

      Sure, it is still expensive, but it's a lot easier for someone like Netflix to optimize their back end to minimize costs than it would be for an old school distributor.

      Additionally, as a service you can actually make more in the long run by keeping your month service fees low: Netflix can be a lot like a gym membership.
