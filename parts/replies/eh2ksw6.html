• rhino_ball

    These fuses are usually fitted up the bum of the bomb. On the top middle of the bomb is a little wind turbine that deploys when the bomb is dropped and the fuze won’t arm unless the bomb is travelling a minimum speed. On the nose you usually have a proximity sensor or solid nose that can be fitted.

    The little wind turbine doesn't exist for every bomb - when we drop just FMU-139's with a Mk-122 safety switch, for instance, the FMU-139 has a limited life.

    It's actually quite simple and ingenious how the two work: the jet sends an electrical volt to the fuze for which setting it wants to use and the voltage only reaches the fuze if the Mk-122 safety switch is pulled.

    The catch is that the safety switch is shorter than the wire that sends the signal, meaning the bomb can only be armed if the weapon falls free and clear of the jet (as the bomb falls, the safety switch is pulled first which allows the signal to pass to the bomb)

        The safety switches are wired up to lanyards that are anchored to the jet. When the bomb drops free the lanyards break away and the bomb becomes armed.

        This is the same mechanism that will do things like deploy the steering fins of PGMs. It's very rare, but it has happened that maintainers on the ground accidentally snagged a lanyard with a bomb loading truck and when pulling away, the lanyard broke and the fins deployed and it shattered a guy's arm.
