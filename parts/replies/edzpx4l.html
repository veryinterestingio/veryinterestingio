  • froghazel

    Agree with avoiding dimethicone and other silicones. The silicone covers the strands of your hair and essentially creates an impenetrable fortress. It makes your hair feel silky smooth by not allowing any moisture to escape. But when your scalp starts creating its natural oils, the silicone won't let the oils into the strands of hair, so the oils just sit on your scalp, giving you that greasy feeling. The silicones only wash away with sulfates, which also strip away your natural oils.
