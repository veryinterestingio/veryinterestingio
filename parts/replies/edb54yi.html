    • Scylla6

      No, any blackbody at any temperature will emit radiation of each frequency. It's the amount that varies with temperature. As you bring the temperature up the amount of lower frequency radiation decreases, at very high temperatures it's almost negligible, but it will always give off that lower frequency radiation.

      In fact the opposite is also true, cooling a blackbody down lowers the intensity of the higher frequency radiation but it always emits some amount of higher frequency radiation. Even a blackbody very near absolute zero will do this.

      Now these are only for theoretical blackbodies and, while these results accurately match a lot of objects in the real world, as you start to reach very high or low temperatures or work with more exotic objects these results will differ from experiments. There are no perfect blackbodies in the real world. Not only that but radiation is quantised and this is a classical theory so when you start looking at very small amounts of radiation being emitted then you have to move to a quantum theory which will be stochastic (random) in nature.
