    For those who don’t know:

    • Nucleons (neutrons & protons, not electrons as they are considered elementary particles), are made of quarks

    • In both neutrons and protons there is 3 valence quarks which determine if the particle is a proton or a neutron

    • there’s a lot of other quarks (top/bottom; charm/strange) that cancel each other so they matter a less for the electric charge of nucleons

    Lets see the 3 valence quarks of protons and neutrons:

    • Protons: 2 up (+2/3 charge each) and 1 down (-1/3)

    • Neutron: 2 down (-1/3 each) and 1 up (+2/3 charge)

    Thus their electric charges:

    • Proton: 2/3+2/3-1/3 = +1 (electric charge of a proton)

    • Neutron: 2/3-1/3-1/3 = 0 (The neutron has a neutral charge)
