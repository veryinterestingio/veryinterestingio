Which is why you can "not breathe" for a few minutes and you're fine (when diving for example), but an aortal rupture and you'll go unconscious within seconds as blood pressure drops and the hemoglobin becomes depleted or unusuable to cell tissues.

    • fluffboo

      When your heart stops, the blood stops going through the lungs for gas exchange and stops bringing oxygen to your brain. You lose consciousness faster when your heart stops than if you were to stop getting oxygen (like if you're choking for example), so really you wouldn't be conscious for very long at all. Most of the time you're "alive" from the residual circulating oxygen you are unconscious or very confused if awake at all.
