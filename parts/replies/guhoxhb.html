    An espresso is a shot of coffee that's been extracted in a coffee machine under high pressure. You can grind your freshly roasted beans for this.

        The difference is the pressure in professional coffee machines that allows literal extraction of the oils that produce crema, which is an additional source of flavour complexity. Drip coffee uses gravity and cannot ever 'extract' these oils, especially since they are lightweight and if formed at all would just stay above the coffee grounds in the filter.
