Also, it appears the main disagreement or confusion or doubt about the reaction isn't that it happens or not but rather how it happens or what the mechanism is. If you worked in pharmacology, you would see that the mechanism of action for most medications is not entirely clear. When looking at the mechanism of action and potential reactions, it is not an exact science as there are a million different variables in the real world that you may not even know about. It could be some random protein that is produced in 1 in 1000 patients that hasn't been discovered yet but it interferes with the medication and alcohol and causes this reaction. We just simply do not know but there have been enough cases to still warn people about the potential reaction so they can either avoid it or be aware of what is happening when/if they experience symptoms so they can get the proper care. Trying to 'debunk' cases without sufficient evidence to prove that it never happens may actually cause more harm than good if it causes a patient to dismiss their symptoms and not seek care that may be needed.
