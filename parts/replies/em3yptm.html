    • RogueLeslieKnope

      Yes they are! The have silk glands in their abdomen near their bum that are full of proteins, and they produce silk that gets released through these little structures called spinnerets. Cute, right?

      Webs are built out of silk, but there are a lot of other purposes for silk: they can use them for protective covering if they are in a hole, use them to wrap their prey, use them for a drag line for safety when jumping, make a cocoon like structure for their eggs (females only), or make a web to put their sperm on (males only).

      Also, not all spiders make webs. Some are just opportunistic hunters that wander around trying to catch prey. Some just make a lasso out of their silk and catch prey that way. Another uses a net that they drop on their prey from above.

      Please share these tidbits the next time someone you know tries to drag spiders! :P
