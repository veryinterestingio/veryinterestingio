    • CrateDane

      The liver doesn't really burn much glucose under normal circumstances. Hepatocytes prefer beta-oxidation of fatty acids to cover their own energy needs. They may use glucose for various other pathways, for example the pentose phosphate pathway generating NADPH for fatty acid synthesis.

  • ErichPryde

    I did, but I was late to the party. Brown Adipose tissue (BAT) is the primary source of heat generation (thermogenesis) in mammals. The liver accounts for the lion's share of heat outside of homo sapiens. In humans, the brain is actually responsible for most of it, but the difference is that it's produced as waste heat, and not intentionally for keeping warm.

    ​

    Fun fact: Leatherback sea turtles ALSO have BAT.
