        Density is central to the story and cloud formation can't be explained without invoking temperature, you are correct.

        But the flat cloud bottom is there because that's where the lifted condensation level is there. The LCL is where it is because of the absolute humidity of the air and the adiabatic lapse rate. Clouds do not float on the LCL; density does not at all explain what keeps cloud droplets suspended and to imply that clouds are buoyant is misleading.
