    • Jasonwhut

      What you see thinking of is two different things, a high pressure release that works if the air compressor governor quits working and causes too much pressure for the system to handle, and there are valves at the bottom of the air tanks that you manually operate to drain water, which is mostly about avoiding ice in the lines more than rust in the tanks. The comments above was correct, you release the parking brake by applying air to one system that fills one section of the air brake chamber, the second system connects the brake pedal to the other part of the brake chamber to apply the regular (service) brakes. When you ease pressure applied to the brake pedal this air will release out of the service brake system, it doesn't get put back into the air tanks. When you see a vehicle with air brakes begin to move you will hear this air escaping as they lift their foot off of the brake pedal.
