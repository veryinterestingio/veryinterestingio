    • Pyrex238

      Since bureaus are utilized in nearly every business transaction in the US, a law would probably just cause a lot more problems. The problem with identities is they are the culmination of all data associated with that social. There is no central government database of people's information outside of SSN, name, DOB. The only databases that exist are from private companies such as the bureaus themselves. The biggest is Lexis Nexis, and it contains a frightening amount of data on any one person, but again it's only what has been submitted. For adults the information can be desiminated to reveal fraud, but for children with no history, it's much more difficult as nothing has been reported to show red flags (other than a 5 year old buying a Bentley). Government data requires written authorization to obtain from the government as mentioned before. You can freeze any of your bureaus which prevents someone from using their social. I highly recommend parents do this for their children prior to turning 18. Just visit Equifax, trans union, and Experian and freeze them. If you suspect fraud, you can place an initial fraud alert on your bureau using the same method which alerts creditors to pay closer attention. If you're actively being targeted you can use an extended fraud alert which requires a lender to call you personally and verify your identity prior to extending credit.

        On the note of ID fraud: identitytheft.gov is an excellent website setup by the FTC.     • Pyrex238

      What to you are describing is another type of fraud called synthetic ID's, and they are gaining popularity. A person fabricates an SSN and starts placing information, building an identity of a completely fake number. These are generally defeated by SSA-89's. These are more time consuming and difficult to pull off, but generally are more difficult to track down to the individual responsible. Since there is no direct victim in this type of identity theft, it's also more difficult to get the authorities involved as well. Most law enforcement has better things to do than prosecute complicated white collar crime, so most of these fraudsters get away with it. They'll even do cheeky things like using mugshots of famous criminals for their driver licenses, etc.
