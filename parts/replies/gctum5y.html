    • woaily

      I support Disney prohibiting WB or Universal from making their own "Mickey Mouse" cartoons and theme parks (as I suspect most people would be).

      This is what trademark law is for. Trademarks protect using a word or image as a source identifier of a product or service (this theme park is created and run by Disney, this drink is from the people who make Coca Cola, etc.), and they are indefinitely renewable for good reason.

      Copyright protects copying the work in any form, which is much broader. It includes merchandising of minor characters that aren't used as trademarks, making copies of old movies, putting a picture of Spiderman on your son's gravestone, etc. It also means that when you use stills or clips from an old movie, you don't get hauled into court where you have to prove fair use.
