        The required voltage increases as the distance increases, so lasers usually have small gaps in order to keep the required voltages down.

        High voltages in gas lasers tend to produce arcs, which greatly reduces efficiency of the system (and it's pretty low to begin with), so there are different methods to combat arc formation, including keeping the distance small.

        Obviously lightning is one huge arc, so it's efficiency as a laser (if you managed to contain the lightning in a resonator cavity) would be horrendous, but I believe you could generate some signal among huge amounts of noise.
