      The Colorado River is "Owned" by the 7 states and Mexico which have historical claims to the river's water. This is known as the Law of the River. As the western states have developed and their populations have increased, the use of the water increased as well. These states have some of the highest population growths in the entire country. Many of these areas also value aesthetic over the sustainability of their community and therefore you have multiple thousand acre golf courses in the middle of the desert.

      ​

      The problem is truly that this is the law. These states are legally apportioned this water and they will use it. Another factor is where this water is actually going towards and the vast majority goes to agriculture, a very strong industry.

      ​

      What is even more terrifying really is that this is not a secret. I work in environmental management in Arizona. Everyone knows that climate change and draught are happening but nobody really cares all that much. The idea of a building moratorium or anything of that sort would be unthinkable. Expanding regulations on water quality and quantity are also very taboo. And so everyone scratches their head, wonders why we have such large problems and then goes home on friday and waters their 2 acre lawns in the middle of the desert.
