    While I appreciate the good faith attempt to clarify, this point is more of a "technically correct" statement than representative of the true state of affairs.

    Yes, you are right in saying that Honey does not sell user data. As in, Honey does not literally aggregate information on its users, compile that in a massive database, and then sell access licenses to companies for use of that data for specific advertising purposes. However, a cursory glance over the same privacy policy you linked suggests that even without explicitly selling the data for a direct cash payment, they are still happy to hand off data for a variety of other vague reasons:

    "We know how important your personal data is to you, so we will never sell it. We’ll only share it with your consent or in ways you’d expect (as we explain here). That means we will share your data if needed to complete your purchase, with businesses who help us operate Honey, or if we are legally required to do so...

    ...in an aggregate or anonymized format that does not identify any specific person..."

    In essence, they are committing to a bit of legalese double-speak here. They may not be directly monetizing your data, but to suggest that your data isn't collected and used for monetary gain is incredibly deceptive. It is not just a major component of the app, it is the method by which the company makes money - they would not be able to do most of what they do with the app without collecting your data, and since companies exist to make money, they need to use their access to that data to do it.

    I'm glad that you are happy with the app's privacy policies, and I don't even necessarily think it's a disqualifying factor - I used the app for a couple of years when it first came out, and am a member of other sites including Google and Facebook which do similar things. It's just not fair to suggest they're not collecting data for some monetary purpose and that you get to use the app with no consequences.

    • DiamondIceNS

      That means we will share your data if needed to complete your purchase, with businesses who help us operate Honey

      This is not necessarily legal double-speak in the way you describe. This is a legal CYA for "we use a cloud service to store our data" or similar.

      In 2019, NordVPN experienced a security breach when one of its datacenters in Finland had a flaw in its network security systems. This datacenter was a third party that Nord was contracting to host its VPN services. Nord failed not necessarily because of a failure of its own security, but because it put its trust into a third party that failed for them.

      Honey, like many, MANY other companies, have partnerships like this that they rely on. But by using them in the ways that they do, they are required to state in their privacy policies that, yes, these extra companies are receiving your data. And if for some reason these companies are the failed link in the chain that comes back and burns Honey, no one can point at Honey and cry, "But you said no one else had our data!!"
