      • FrontColonelShirt

        This is an oft-repeated misconception. You will reach the singularity in proper time. While the events you observe outside the event horizon will appear sped up significantly to you, it would be impossible to really make anything out, since all of the photons will be incredibly blue-shifted and, due to the extreme curvature, the singularity will appear to be in front of whatever direction you are traveling, making observation a difficult proposition.

        Finally, it would be impossible to survive transit through the event horizon for all but supermassive black holes due to tidal forces; even within supermassive black holes (the closest of which is "only" some \~40,000 light years away), you would be stretched to death (I hate the term "spaghettified") by tidal forces before reaching the singularity anyway^(1), just after crossing the event horizon.

        ^(1) citation needed; we have no way of ever knowing what happens beyond the event horizon of a black hole.
