    While I agree with much of what you said, I know firsthand for a fact that with training like CBT, DBT, EMDR and strict adherence to lessons taught in therapy many people with add/adhd as well as many other mental impairments can rewire (neuroplasticity) the patterns and symptoms built from many years of reinforcing unhelpful traits. With that rigorous adherence to change, many people can benefit enough to challenge thoughts, redirect patterns of thinking etc, and while our minds may initially jump from subject to subject we can eventually reign that compulsion in and truly decide what to focus on. It just takes lots of work, sometimes many tries (because it is difficult to stay on task in the early years of restructuring patterns, I know that, and as easily as it is for our brains to want to jump around.. it is just as easy for our brains to grow tired of focusing to fight the habits and want to give up). While it may feel without the right support that we are slaves to it, I don't believe that anymore.

    For most people living with a cognitive issue I do have faith in them that if they keep going back to try, they will ultimately gain better control of their thoughts little by little, the ability to choose what we focus on and hopefully enough mindfulness that can lead to a less distressful future.
