        I know this is in jest but this is a good point showing why friction has nothing to do with swivelling in the chair.

        If you have a satellite that spins up a reaction wheel (gyro), it starts spinning. It can keep spinning as long as the gyro spins (indefinitely). It can also stop spinning by slowing down the gyro.

        So, after half a revolution the satellite puts the breaks on its gyros and stops spinning. It has rotated and conserved angular momentum the whole time. This is exactly the same effect that happens in the swivel chair. No contact required.

        The reason you can't spin indefinitely is that you can't spin your arms around in a 360 degree motion continuously.
