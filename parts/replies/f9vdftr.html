      It was originally named something along the lines of "7.62 three lines infantry cartridge" in Russian when it was created 130+ years ago, but was renamed 7.62x54R by CIP, which designates it as "rimmed", meaning the end of the case sticks out in a ring instead of having a groove in it like more common ammo.
