    • turniphat

      Yes. They are cheaper to installer and maintain, but more expensive to operate. So they are typically installed in buildings with lower passenger volumes.

      A cable elevator has a counter weight which balances the elevator cab. This means the motor only needs to lift the difference between counter weight and the loaded cab, which is the weight of the passengers.

      A hydraulic elevator needs to lift the weight of the cab and passengers each time.
