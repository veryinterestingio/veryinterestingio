    • Astromike23

      The moon also protects us from things like meteors hitting the earth

      I'm not sure where this myth started, but it's definitely a myth.

      In terms of preventing impacts due to direct collision with the Moon: the entire area of the sky contains 41,253 square degrees. The Moon itself covers just 0.21 square degrees, or about 1/200,000^th of the sky. That's not really helping much.

      Now the Moon's gravitational effects extend farther out than its surface...but it's just as likely to fling passing asteroids into our path as it is out of our path.
