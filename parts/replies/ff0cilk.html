  • mabolle

    Not exactly related, but even plants have a microbiome of sorts.

    No need for the "of sorts" qualifier! Plants definitely have microbiomes. It's not just mycorrhiza; tons of different bacteria, archaea and fungi live both on and inside a plant. Like with the human microbiome, some of these microbes are parasitic; some are symbiotic; and some have no particular effect on the plant.

    It is suspected even mitochondria started as a prokaryotic/bacterial symbiont

    I'd say this was upgraded from "suspected" to an accepted fact within the last couple decades. Mitochondria can even be placed on the bacterial family tree using DNA analysis (they appear to have evolved from the group known as alphaproteobacteria).
