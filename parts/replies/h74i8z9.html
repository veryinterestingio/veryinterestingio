      You also have to consider political geography too. The central US is great for agriculture, but because so many states have access to the same aquifer, nobody is responsible for its sustainability. Every state looks out for itself, so they over consume and one day the water might deplete. On the flip side, central Canada has lots of oil, but they can't sell it much to international customers because the costal provinces don't want the oil flowing through their jurisdiction. Every province looks out for itself, so they under produce and one day their product might be worthless.
