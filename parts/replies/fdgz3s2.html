      The way nuclear reactor works (in context of this discussion) is that radiactive ore emits particles, these particles collide with other ore and destabilise more particles, which also start emitting and colliding etc, causing chain reaction. This requires a specific amount of material. So its entirely possible that somewhere on earth a required amount of radioactive material has gathered to create a naturally occuring chain reaction.

      • restricteddata

        Natural uranium will not have a chain reaction without very specific and unnatural conditions today (like being embedded in a lattice of extremely pure carbon, or being in heavy water). The natural reactor of the past was almost 2 billion years ago, when the concentration of U-235 in natural uranium was much higher (3%, compared to today's 0.7%).
