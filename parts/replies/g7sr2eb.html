    I agree with all of your points, however, I present a counterargument. I have been the victim of some poor quality updates which have broken apps, removed features or added pay walls. All of which are usually described as "Bug fixes and improvements". As a result, I don't trust automatic updates anymore and disabled them. The result is that my phone works for longer and more consistently.

    I also work in software so am not oblivious to the risks. I run weekly backups and then if I'm sat waiting for something I'll go through and run some updates. If I see devs making the effort to provide release notes, they get updated without much thought as they've shown they care enough to tell users what's going on.

    I know it's not a great approach, but it's the unfortunate state of things (IMO at least). These things are being forced on users in a way that alienates them, rather than improving the experience in a way that makes them indifferent or even excited to update. It's not the case for everything, but it seems to be a growing trend.

    Edit: Just for completeness, I would not recommend anyone take this approach unless they understand the risks of not running automatic updates.
