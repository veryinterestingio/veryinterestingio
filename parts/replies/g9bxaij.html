      but do you know why oxygen is poisonous below 9m? I have a basic understanding of partial pressures, etc

      Yes and I'm really, really glad you have a basic understanding of partial pressures as this is going to make it so much easier.

      Below 9m the partial pressure of pure O2 exceeds the safety margin of (either 1.4 or 1.6) and at this concentration Central Nervous System toxicity happens and this results in loss of control of the body and muscles, usually the diver goes into uncontrollable spasms and has no control over their body at all (or keeping their reg in with facial muscles) resulting in drowning normally.

      Edit.... for shallow diving extra O2 can be added to reduce the on-take of nitrogen and extend the no-decompression dive time, but this comes with reduced depth limits. For deeper diving where the normal amount of O2 become dangerous below 60+ meters, it's then Tri-mix is used so a small amount of Helium is added to the air to reduce the partial pressure of the O2 without increasing the PP of the nitrogen
