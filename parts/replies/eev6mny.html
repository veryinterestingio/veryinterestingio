      My solid state physics isn't the best but until I'm corrected on the nuance this picture will probably work, but lacks a bit of eloquence. You're shearing unit cells apart from each other, which have a little bit of electron sharing as the packing arrangement is favorable. You take something macroscopic that is low in energy and you smash it with a hammer, leaving weird things at the edges of the new crystals. They're not at their minimum in this new situation due to the rapidity of the breaking, as some electron excess and defects occurred in some of the newly fractured crystals. Triboluminescence is seeing the relaxation process of these freshly fractured crystals.
