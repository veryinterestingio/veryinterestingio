    • theartfulbadger

      Viruses are classified according to how they replicate, and Herpesviridae is a large family of DNA viruses including EBV and Varicella zoster virus (chickenpox).

      VZV is closely related to the herpes simplex viruses (HSV), sharing much genome homology..

      Conversely, other viruses like HIV have very little connection to Herpesviridae - although they might use DNA at some points in replication, they're about as different as snails are from giraffes.

        Topically, coronavirus is very different from all those other examples too. They are enveloped viruses with a positive-sense single-stranded RNA genome and a nucleocapsid of helical symmetry. .
