    An important reasoning for thinking this, is that worker ants cannot breed and therefore pass on their traits/genes to their offspring individually. However since all the worker ants are offspring of the queen, they have a certain relatedness to the other female and worker ants, so if their sisters are helped to survive the uncle ant passes on those genes to its nephews and neieces via the female ants.

    TL;DR: since (EDIT: some) ant colonies have a single queen, they are the only part of the colony to pass on theur genes. Therefore in some instances you could treat an ant colony as a single organism, unlike people.
