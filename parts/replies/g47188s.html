        That's a thrilling story but it's far more likely that back when it was smaller groups of people they would be cooking over a fire would wipe out grease and other detritus from cooking vessels using whatever they had to hand:

        Leaves, dirt, water, and—most importantly—ash left over from the fire or coals to use as an abrasive agent.

        It would be a very small step from wiping out vessels with ash to recognizing an unusual property of the ash to then throwing handfuls of ash into a greasy vessel with some hot water (itself an extremely simple and intuitive method of cleaning) before you've got yourself the real beginnings of soap that can be used beyond in situ applications.
