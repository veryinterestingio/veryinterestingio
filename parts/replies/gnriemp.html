      There is a pretty big difference in how a government is supposed to be run vs a private business. A private business can (and should) optimize for the common \~80-90% of the scenarios they encounter, then figure out the exceptions. But a government needs to prepare for those >5% events that can kill people.

      So a coastal town should have building code regulations for the storm that hits every 20-50 years. Not just the "normal" years. This is why places with higher density housing will have stricter electrical codes for houses, for example. But the point should work across all public impact issues.
