    • FragmentOfBrilliance

      That is correct. Even under small magnetic fields, pairs of up and down spin electrons will deviate slightly from their expected energy values.

      See: Zeeman Splitting https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zeeman_effect

      The sorts of magnetic fields that are encountered in astrophysics are crazy, and can distort the atoms even further (past the linear regime) and stretch the atoms out like little needles. This means the energy values of the electrons become totally alien from the perspective of our normal chemistry, when the perturbing magnetic term dominate over the other terms in the schrodinger equation.
