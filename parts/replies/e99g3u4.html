  • jthill

    Yep. Competing effectively means making the right tradeoffs, and the tradeoffs change when the rewards and margins for the different choices change. Spend X amount more efficiently extracting water means you can't spend that soaking up sunlight. In a desert, where sunlight's easy to come by, water not so much, you spend it on getting water. Other places, you spend it getting sunlight.

      • jhwells

        Because there is no "best." Thinking of evolution as a move from worse-to-best is false.

        Eyes, for example, are great. Who doesn't like seeing things? Still, there are dozens, if not hundreds of species that once had eyes and lost them once they became resident in cave systems that had no light. It might be better to have eyes, and they'd certainly come in handy if the descendants of those cave-dwellers ever ventured into the light, BUT in the meanwhile, those eyes are metabolically expensive for every individual and every generation in the caves.

        They take resources to nourish and over time, individuals that accumulate genetic changes that shrink, then eliminate, the eye will have more resources to move, feed, and breed until the eyeless are the only ones left.
