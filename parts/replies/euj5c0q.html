      This more transitions to nutrition/biochemistry, so we're on the edge of my knowledge base.

      Is cognition impaired due to the increasing urge to find food taking priority over other tasks?

      I won't comment on the behavioral aspect of 'an urge to find food', but it may be possible that mental fortitude has something to do with mental capabilities while starving.

      If this is directed at impaired cognition due to *over consumption* of food, the jury isn't quite out (according to the source provided) on that and it would be wrong of me to speculate.

      If considering impaired cognition due to severe *caloric deficit*, it's likely due to a lack readily available energy (energy consumption can be used to approximate the level of activity), how do you keep neuronal circuits firing when you don't have the energy available to use them( imaging a throttle system on your car engine forcing you to use 4 cylinders instead of 6 when your fuel is low). The body does transition from glycogen as a fuel to ketone bodies (and later triglycerides).

      As for brain function decreasing, in the case of starving, it is a minimal change (initially), as the body is well adapted to starvation, and further down the line greater cognitive issues may occur, the decline is likely rapid but linear and would likely happen at distinct times representing the stages of starvation.

      It is also important to note that when I state *starving* I mean, no food at all. Not just a minor/moderate caloric deficit.
