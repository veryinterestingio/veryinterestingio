    • FPL_Account

      The shelf life is almost always accompanied by a storage condition. 99% of drug storage conditions will be one of ‘store below 25 degrees’ ‘store below 30 degrees’ or ‘keep refrigerated’. These are regulated global storage conditions and must be agreed by the market before a drug is sold into that market. If a drug is stored above these conditions ( say at 40 degrees) then that is outside of the approved conditions however drug companies will always have temperature excursion data and accelerated study data (40 degrees/75% humidity) to refer to.
