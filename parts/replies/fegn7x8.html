I've even had triple shelled eggs, where for whatever reason the hen retained the egg for several deposition cycles and just kept layering shell on.       Do you know how similar egg shells are to the zona pellucida of mammalian eggs? In mammals the ZP is involved in fertilization as well as early embryo development before the embryo "hatches"

      • HuthAvian

        I don't have much experience with mammalian reproduction, but from a google search it seems they are not at all similar. The ZP seems to be a protein membrane that binds sperm and aids in fertilization, while in birds by the time the eggshell is being formed the egg has already been fertilized and there would be no way for sperm to penetrate the shell as it is a lattice of crystallized calcium carbonate. When an egg yolk is "dropped" into the oviduct it gets fertilized first, then the albumen is formed around the yolk, then the shell membrane is created, and finally the shell itself is deposited. The vitelline membrane (membrane directly around the yolk, under the albumen) would probably be the closest analogue to the ZP.
