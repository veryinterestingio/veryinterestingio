    They glow and fade because of residual heat, unless it's a flourecent light, in which case the UV light emitted from mercury vapor due to the electrical discharge hits a phosphor (or similar) atom and raises it's electron into a higher state, this higher state has a half life, and when it "decays" back to its normal orbit it emits a photon that we can see.

    After the phosphor stops being hit by UV, it halves in brightness each of its half life, leading to a slow decrease in brightness.
