    No, some states can remain indefinitely with 100% certainty. For example, a particle in an eigenstate of the Hamiltonian (subjectively, the most “normal” states) will remain in that state forever & be observed in it 100% of the time.

    States don’t really “cease existing” either. In fact any state can be linearly decomposed in the Hamiltonian’s spectrum, and its representation there will never change (in a conservative system, which is the only kind that exists microscopically anyway).
