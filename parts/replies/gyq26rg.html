    • DunderBearForceOne

      Ecology is not that simple and trying to reduce it to a number like that is extremely dangerous. Even if it's a relatively small percentage, if they react by replacing that portion of their diet with another species, that targetted species may not be able to keep up reproductively with the spike in death. This could cause food insecurity for another animal that relies on that food source, and ripple outwards in various directions. Nothing exists in a vacuum, so you cannot analyze them in a vacuum to measure their impact.
