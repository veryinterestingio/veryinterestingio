    Chewing and salivary action are the first steps in natural human digestion. Other ways humans have learned to process food before eating it (cooking, cutting/grinding, adding or taking away certain components of food items) have reduced the need for larger, stronger teeth, jaws, and facial musculature. It's probably also affected how our gut functions.

    Humans are omnivores as well as predators, so our gut is neither as large as a gorilla's nor as small as a cheetah's. Its "efficiency" depends upon diet, age, and health of gut flora, a subject which scientists are just beginning to explore in-depth (pun not intended) as it affects not only our body but our brain.
