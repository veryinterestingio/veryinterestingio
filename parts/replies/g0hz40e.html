    • bridgesbuilttoburn

      Yes - "how many drinks" is "the volume of alcohol" which is how we measure the blood alcohol content - the volume of alcohol in the blood. missing a limb will not speed up the process, just lower the threshold. So if it would take you 3 drinks to get drunk, then you lose a leg and it only takes you 2 drinks to get drunk, your body shouldn't process them any faster or slower than it did before. But the rate at which your body processes the alcohol isn't any faster. It takes "less time" because of the volume necessary, not because you process it at a quicker rate.

      So if your new tolerance is only 2 drinks but you still have 3, your body is still going to process the three at the same speed.
