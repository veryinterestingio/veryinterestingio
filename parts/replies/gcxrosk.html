    Yup, and the .357 magnum was originally just a technological update to the .38 Special, since modern metallurgy and powders could handle significantly higher pressures in the same size cartridges, resulting in better ballistics in the same package.

    In order to prevent people from blowing up their older guns, they increased the case length of the .357 but seated the bullet deeper. They have virtually identical case volume but you can't put a .357 magnum into a .38 Special chamber.

    However, the other way around is possible so most .357 revolvers can also shoot .38 Spl.
