  • samtl008

    It's interesting how operators use the terms to their liking and how it varies across countries.

    In the UK, 3G to the subscriber has included UMTS, HSDPA, HSPA+ and DC-HSPA+. When the operators upgraded to DC-HSPA+ tech, there wasn't really any campaign to notify people, it was more a silent rollout. Then when LTE came along, there was a huge campaign for 4G phones etc. So in the UK we either see 3G/H/H+ on our display or 4G/LTE usually.

    When I travelled to the US the first time, I saw 4G on my phone roaming and though awesome, superfast, then realised it was an HSPA connection, not LTE like at home. I guess differing target markets and competition drive the operators to find new ways to make subscribers think they need the shiniest thing going.
