  • ZacQuicksilver

    With a little more detail, at about a 6th grade level:

    Getting just a gram of an element is on order of 10\^21 atoms for heavier elements. The longest half-life on an element is Bismuth-209; with a half life of about 2*10\^19 years - which indicates that, with 1g of Bismuth-209, you're getting about 50 decays/year; about 1 every 6 days.

    The longest half-lives we can accurately measure at this point is not much longer than 10\^22 years for heavy elements, and 10\^24 for lighter elements.
