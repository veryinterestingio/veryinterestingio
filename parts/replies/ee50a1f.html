    This is becoming a hindrance during our vacations (we go on a lot of cruises). Originally, I considered it a feature - work couldn't get ahold of me unless they wanted to shell out absurd amounts of money per minute for Internet or even voice (shore-to-ship calls at that time - early 2000s - were something like $17.50 per minute)!

    But these days, since I have gigabit Internet to my house, I rely on the Internet a bunch more; I basically stream everything I need whenever I'm away from home from my home network.

    Our income has increased considerably as well, so we can now afford the absurd rates on the cruise ship for Internet access. But it's 250-500kbps on average, if you see a megabit you're really fortunate. So these days I bring about 2 terabytes on USB/Flash media with us, and burn a few blu-rays, so I have access to whatever chunks we're currently into from my Plex library (I know, I know - why pay to sit on a boat and watch the same TV you watch at home? Because I'm neurotic).

    I wish the cruise lines were more attentive to their more technical customers. I wouldn't mind paying a surcharge for access to an updated satellite connection that could at least give me multiple megabits. Netflix on a cruise ship would be amazing.
