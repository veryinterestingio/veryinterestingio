      You aren't going to get crystalinity just from flexing the material. Crystalinity is mostly set when polymerization occurs. It can be induced in the mold or if you reheat the polymer into the higher glass transition temperature/ re melt it. If you melt it you can induce crystalinity with the help of an additive, the rapidity of cooling or stretching the material in multiple directions, though this is much harder to get into a crystaline form than during polymerisation.

      You are right, the discolouration comes from the void space between the strands of the polymer chain, rather than the polymer itself.
