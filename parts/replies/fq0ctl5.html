    • RobusEtCeleritas

      Yes. In fact, any configuration of charges such that the net charge is zero has zero electric monopole moment, so the leading-order term in its multipole expansion is the dipole term. At large distances, the higher multipoles all die away faster than the dipole term, so it will behave as a dipole if you're sufficiently far away.
