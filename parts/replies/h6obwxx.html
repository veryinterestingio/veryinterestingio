  • grant10k

    The main issue is that combustion loses efficiency when running in the wrong operation point. A powerplant will always run at it's optimum RPM

    Plus, powerplants, being stationary, can recover power from the exhaust, which is still hot enough to convert to more electricity. Combined cycle plants can be ~50% more efficient than a simple cycle (like a car engine). Cars don't do it because it's inconvenient to lug around a bunch of heat exchangers to eek out every last bit of efficiency while adding weight. Not a problem for a power plant. Some boats use combined cycles too. Probably big ones.
