    I study poultry nutrition. The diet they eat is completely related to their microbiome. They are monogastric and so are humans. However, they have a more significant blind pouch that harbors most of their microbial populations (i.e. more defined appendix)

    There is a similarity in birds and humans when eating grains high in non-starch polysaccharides (e.g. wheat, barley, and oats). Soluble NSP has drastic effects on the birds intestines and can lead to decreased lactobacillus microbes.

    When testing an experimental diet in birds, they are given a 3 to 7 day period to adapt to their new diet before data is collected. A lot of that is related to microbiome changes. Diet is important in feeding microbes. The right diet feeds the right microbes, which can lead to out competing other more harmful bacteria.
