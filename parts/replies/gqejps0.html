    This is true with regards to the higher discharge during the last glacial period and this can be linked to some particular features, but it's also important to remember that much of the large scale fluvial topography was generated over several million years of incision and/or deposition, integrating across multiple glacial and interglacial cycles. So it's not really correct to link the majority of landscape form to the higher discharge period during the LGM.
