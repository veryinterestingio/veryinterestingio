    Umm, I'm not sure about this. I have autonomic dysreflexia, where the sympathetic system is damaged and can be set off by random triggers below the point of injury. I've also had one or two true panic attacks in my life, and seen my sister have many more and she's described them to me.

    I can't speak to the parasympathetic, but I don't think the damaged sympathetic system reaction is similar to a panic attack. A panic attack makes your chest tight and you feel fear; or sometimes other strong mental feelings combined with physical ones.

    But when I trigger my autonomic dysreflexia, it's more of an overwhelming "tickle" in my chest/heart, like a heart arrythmia. I also get really tired. But even when it feels extremely uncomfortable and (initially) shocking I'm definitely mentally in control and it's a purely physical response. Once I identify the trigger especially it's not even remotely panicking no matter how strong the sensation. I might not be able to do anything but clutch at my chest or try to remove the trigger, but I never feel mentally "controlled" by the symptom like with a panic attack.

    Again, just anecdotal personal experience. I think we just don't know enough about the brain and receptors to say what the cause is. But I'd be wary to attribute it to systems that (at least in my experience) seem to control bodily functions more than mental ones.
