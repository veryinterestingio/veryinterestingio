      You're still that person, but with more life experience. If you want to do things you're definitely capable of because you've done them well in the past, take it one day at a time and get a little better every day.

      Something that I've used to great effect is a win list, or whatever you'd like to call it. I have a notebook, and every night, I write down 5 things of any kind I need to and can realistically do tomorrow. Anything from a project at work to laundry to wishing a friend a happy birthday. If I check off everything that day, I write a big W on the page. If not, a big L goes on it. If I can win 5 or more days of a week, that week is a win, and if I win 3 or more weeks in a month, that month is a win. The goal is to win some and lose some, because we're only human. The numbers are arbitrary, so if I legitimately can't think of 5 things to do, I'll write less, but either way, writing that big W still feels nice.
