  • arcticmischief

    Investors don’t have to be outside ones (VC, angel, etc.). A startup can also be bootstrapped—funded by the founders.

    The line between a bootstrapped startup and a small business can be fuzzy. I work for an online travel booking site that started with a unique and novel idea that was fairly disruptive to one segment of the travel industry. It operated at a loss for the first several years but has seen significant growth over the last several years (not anything on the level of Airbnb or Uber, unfortunately). I would say that it started out as a bootstrapped startup that has become a healthy, profitable small business (calling it a small business feels weird, though, because even though there are only 15 employees, we serve several hundred thousand customers a month).
