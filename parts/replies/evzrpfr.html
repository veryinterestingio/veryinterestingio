      Aerobic respiration on the cellular level results in the production of 34 adenosine triphosphate (ATP) molecules from one glucose molecule which is what our body uses for energy. Without oxygen, the same glucose molecule results in 2 ATP molecules and lactic acid. So aerobic respiration is 17 times more efficient.

      If you’re interested in reading more look up the “Warburg Effect”. I’m under qualified to do it justice, but essentially there’s two ways to use glucose: aerobic and anaerobic. Aerobic uses oxygen to fully breaks down the glucose, and it’s used to provide energy. Anaerobic doesn’t use oxygen and produces some energy, but leaves some chunks of glucose. Those chunks are then used by cancer cells as building blocks to fuel their growth.
