    • ebookish1234

      I don’t know much beyond the basic mechanics. I’m a teacher, not a botanist.

      From what I can find quickly find, Darwin and his son showed that the tips around leaf sheaths of grass seedling determine how the plant grows.

      This led a Dutch botanist named Went to use those tips to make an extract that he used to make grass plants grow as if they were being exposed to light when they weren’t, which led him to argue for a hormone he called auxin.

      Because of his setup, he was able to demonstrate that more auxin is associated with one side of a plant than another based on how the plant bends.

      I’m assuming this led other scientists to explore auxin in trees.
