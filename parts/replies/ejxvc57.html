      Other hockey besides NHL gets along fine without fighting. And the style of play is great. The only 'policing' that needs to be done could be done by referees, just like any other sport.

      That's more aspirational than actual. There is plenty of "extracurricular" policing that goes on in other sports, sometimes drawing the ire of the officials and sometimes not. Major League pitchers don't hesitate to "send a message" by way of a 90 mph hardball to the back if they feel the other team is playing dirty.

      Football, obviously, presents plenty of opportunities to lay a punitive hit on someone while staying just inside the envelope of what's legal - or, at worst, drawing a holding or illegal contact flag.

      This is something I'll definitely give soccer (at least, the soccer I watch: EPL, UEFA, and the World Cup), the highly discretionary nature of how yellow and red cards are handed out keeps a lid on this sort of thing remarkably well. There's little ability to hide behind the letter of the law when the ref can just say "that was a cynical foul; you're booked." Before I started watching, I was uncomfortable with leaving so much to the ref's judgement, but having paid attention for a few years, now, I think it's the better system. It means whenever a player pulls one of those "we all know what you're doing, here" stunts, they get penalized for it whether or not there's a specific rule detail that covers exactly that circumstance.
