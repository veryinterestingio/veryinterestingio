  • YRYGAV

    No. The LHC is "calibrated" against reality,

    It's calibrated against the SI definitions

    1 gram is 1 gram. In reality, a gram 50 years ago is not physically the same number of atoms as today, but they are both defined to be equal.

    The concept of standardizing 1 gram to a particular year is impossible. How do you describe what a 1950 gram is? You make a chemically inert thing that weighs what you want it to, lock it in a vacuum and try not to touch it, right? Well that's exactly what we did with the 1 kg reference. It's the best we can possibly do. Any other definition you could come up with to try and describe what a 1950 gram is will be inferior to the reference kg.

    The new definition is something that will allow us to have consistent measurements without having to worry about changes to our definition over time.
