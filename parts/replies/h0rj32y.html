    I'm not convinced clotting due to low flow would contribute to death.

    Someone shot through the heart (for the purposes of this let's exclude "bb-gun" i.e.: small injuries to the heart) would, I agree, experience a more or less immediate cessation of blood flow the timings on the order of a few seconds at a maximum. They would also die (or at least lose consciousness for the last time) after a very short time on the order of a few seconds.

    However, after cardiac arrest it's still beneficial to administer CPR even if the person has had no pulse for 30 seconds to a few minutes. And even during clotting tests with blood outside the body it can still take around 20-30 seconds at a minimum for blood to clot. I'm no expert on clotting, but I would guess from these two things that although the blood may clot later, given that the person is very very dead (or irrevocably heading on a path that shortly ends with death) within a few seconds clotting would be the least of their worries.

    I'd also second /u/kill___jester's point that the blood wouldn't soak the lungs, although i think cardiac tamponade is unlikely given a) I think you need to be alive for it to count, strictly speaking, and b) I'm assuming the bullet needs to puncture the pericardium as a prerequisite to puncturing the heart.

    And another thing that I've just thought of: IANAD but am an emergency first aider. I'm pretty sure syncope is necessarily a temporary and transient (brief) loss of consciousness (as in, they wake up again fairly quickly. More than a few minutes is when I'd start to really worry if I were the first aider). Again, IANAD, but I don't think it's likely that a person who's been literally shot through the heart will regain consciousness any time soon. Pretty sure if they don't wake up it's called a coma, although again, I don't think that applies here given that they're not just unconscious, they're dead.

    You've used a lot of words that make it sound like your answer is informed on the face of it, but actually it doesn't really hit the mark on any of it.
