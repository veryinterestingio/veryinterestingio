    Another cpa here, one thing I didn't understand until I was working for longer than I'd like to admit is the reason for the preferential capital gains treatment. If I buy property in 1980 for 100k and sell it in 2020 for 110k im paying tax on 10k of gain. But really I lost money in real terms due to inflation. Part of the reasoning for the preferential capital gains tax rate is to offset the effect of inflation and the time value of money. Not saying its a rock solid argument or the best way of doing it but thats the logic.
