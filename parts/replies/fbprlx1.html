        Also, to echo u/Odeon42, your understanding of virus replication seems to be a bit flawed. Measles virus is an RNA virus and replicates entirely in the host cells cytoplasm. It doesn't have a reverse transcriptase and doesn't enter the nucleus. Also, your use of 'incorporation' leads me to believe that your understanding of DNA viruses might also be off a bit. Most DNA viruses don't insert themselves in the host genome (as I'm interpreting your use of incorporate). They just exist inside the nucleus and are transcribed like any other DNA material.   • Odeon42

    Measles is not a retrovirus and thus does not incorporate into host DNA. Only a select few viruses can incorporate into host genome (retroviridae). Also, your description of T cell depletion is inaccurate. It is not necessarily about the suite of cell surface proteins. It is more to do with the affinity of the TCR to MHC-self peptide complexes that drives depletion.
