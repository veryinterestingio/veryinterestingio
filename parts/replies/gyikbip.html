      • Bangkok_Dangeresque

        ElI5 version is that Buddhism teaches that we are ignorant to the true nature of reality - we see permanence in things and people and abstract concepts and beliefs that in truth are ephemeral, in a constant state of birth and change and death and rebirth. In being unable to see the truth about the impermanence of things, we form attachments to them that cause us suffering. Including, most challengingly, the impermanence/non-existence of the self as a separable entity, attachment to which causes us to be trapped in a cycle of birth, death, and reincarnation. Buddhist doctrine teaches that a prescribed path of righteous behavior and thought leads to enlightenment, that is, to reach those realizations of ignorance and thus be liberated from the cycle of suffering.

        If we take the gnostic tenets in similarly high level terms, they teach that the reality we experience is an illusion, and thus it causes us suffering through ignorance of and distance from the true nature of god. They suggest a lesser deity is responsible for the delusion; our souls are the manifold emanations from the oneness of a supreme being, which become trapped in a devious fleshy prison and the material world. Salvation can be achieved by obtaining knowledge of the true nature of god, which was brought to earth incarnate in Jesus Christ.
