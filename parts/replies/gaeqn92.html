    Zombie strips. Blondie, B.C....Sometimes the originator wasn't dead. Terry & The Pirates became a big hit, and Milton Caniff asked his publisher to cut him some profit participation. The publisher said no, this is our strip, you're an employee, here's a little raise, now STFU.

    Caniff said OK, best of luck with your strip, put on his hat and was out the door. He changed some character names, published it as Steve Canyon under his own ownership, and Terry was dead in a year. Some papers dropped him during the Vietnam years because of his relentlessly conservative orientation, but Caniff died a wealthy man while various attempts to revive Terry flopped.
