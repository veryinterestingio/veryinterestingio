      • kukienboks

        Direct-shift automatics usually have dry friction clutches. So just like on manual cars they get hot and wear a lot when they are partially engaged. So they are usually programmed to not creep until the throttle is pressed.

        Old-school boxes with hydraulic torque converters, which do creep, are still around in many Euro cars, though.
