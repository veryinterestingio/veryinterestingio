      • appleciders

        Good. It's a terrible way to design things.

        I work in design, and the philosophy of picking an architectural style from across the world for your neighborhood over here has always bothered me. People didn't use terra-cotta roof tiles in the Mediterranean because they were attractive, they did it because it was practical for that environment. Likewise thatched roofs in Britain, and slate roofs around Paris, and whatever other local style you've got. Mimicking styles that made sense in their own environments elsewhere is dumb. Let's develop a sensible, practical California style, and let that be our legacy. Something that's smart, inexpensive, and tuned to local needs. And a hundred years from now, people will talk about the quaint Southern California cottages, with their cinderblock walls and metal roofs, or whatever ends up being the sensible option.
