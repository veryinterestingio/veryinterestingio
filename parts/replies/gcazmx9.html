    • BaconatedHamburger

      Usually no. The more you screw with your skin, the worse it typically gets. I'm not as informed as my partner is, so I'll ask her to confirm, but my understanding is that over-cleansing is what can cause some skin to dry up and flake off.

      For dandruff, lower-temperature showers, and LESS-frequent shampooing is likely the key. Instead of washing your hair daily, wash it with shampoo once every 2-3 days and only RINSE your hair in between. If you have excessively oily hair, finding a dry shampoo to use (if you can't find one, they're usually made with something like kaolin clay, arrowroot powder, and a very small amount of essential oil for fragrance. Very dusty but not impossible to make at home) will help cut down on the oily feel and texture of your hair.

      As an added bonus, the reason hair can get over-oily is because your body is desperately trying to replenish the oil you keep stripping from your scalp with over-cleansing, but it's not very great at judging how much oil to produce so it can go overboard. Which leads to oily hair, which leads to over-cleansing, which leads to excessive oil production which leads to.... you see where this is going.

      As for getting the shampoo to your scalp...uh...don't? If you have long hair and can wash it without shampoo contacting your scalp at all, do that. If you need to get medication to your scalp, part your hair as best you can and apply medication as direct to your scalp as possible.

      Feel free to DM me if you need more. I don't want to end up turning this thread into an advertisement for my partner's business :P
