    I had one of the old belt drive turntables, a Conneisseur, where you just pulled the belt over an extra long pulley to change from 33 to 45. It also included a strobe disk you would place on the table, and shine an electric light on. If the stripes were stationary, your speed was correct; if they were off one way or the other, there was a variable speed control you could adjust until they were.

    I feel like you have some of this backwards. Slower moving disks hold more audio than faster moving ones. So your statement that 33s held "just as much audio" as a 78 is wrong. That was one of the drawbacks of the 78. 78s required albums of discs because they couldn't fit all the audio on one disc.

    Unless by saying "just as much audio" you don't mean the audio length and instead mean audio quality (analogous to bitrate). If that's the case, then maybe find a less ambiguous way to phrase that.
