    For reader clarification, "positive feedback" does not mean "a good thing". It means, the effects of an original change initiates more of the same change. So warming of the permafrost releases global warming gases, causing more warming, releasing more gases, more warming, more gases more.... You get it now.

  • KnowanUKnow

    One thing that I will add to this. Yes there is a lot of methane locked up in the permafrost as clathrates. There's also a lot of organic carbon that will decompose to methane once it gets warm enough for bacterial action to take over.

    But there's also a lot of bacteria that like to eat methane.

    No one is certain if the rate of methane release will be faster than the rate of methane conversion by bacteria. That's another wild card. Once the methane is released/produced, will it make it to the upper atmosphere or will it be consumed/converted by bacteria?

    If it's broken down by bacteria they will convert it to carbon dioxide which, while still a greenhouse gas, isn't nearly as powerful a greenhouse gas as methane.
