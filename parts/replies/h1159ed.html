  • GimmeSomeBreadHomie

    Also want to add that the guy who discovered autism, Leo Kanner, dubbed the syndrome has extremely rare and disagreed with the notion that it might be a spectrum even though many of his fellow colleagues thought so which needless to say, caused a lot of problems in the future and led many children with autistic symptoms to be diagnosed with schizophrenia instead because there was a huge overlap between the two.

    He didn't acknowledge the work of Hans Asperger either who discovered autism around the same time and believed that it was indeed a spectrum.

    Fun fact: Leo Kanner also blamed the condition on bad parenting and coined the term "refrigerator mother" which was a theory that autism was caused by lack of maternal warmth since, majority of his patients' mothers were college graduates (which was heavily stigmatized then) and/or gave the impression of being slightly eccentric.

    A lot of these misconceptions definitely added to the chaos that ensued later in autism research.
