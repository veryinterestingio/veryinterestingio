  • Moar_Cuddles_Please

    Agree. Also, cookie cutter cookie recipes tend to have less rising agents (baking powder, baking soda, etc.) than say a chocolate chip cookie which is meant to be soft and chewy.

    I typically only use 1 tsp of baking powder in my sugar cookie recipe to minimize spread and maintain sharp edges. The recipe calls for 2 tsps but I’ve found that causes it to rise and fluff up a bit too much and I lose the sharp edges. (Yes, I’ve tried chilling and even freezing the dough before baking. Doesn’t help as much as decreasing the baking powder)

    I’ve also made “letter cakes” / cream tarts, gingerbread houses, etc and used a knife to cut out the shape with no issues. It all comes down to the recipe.
