    Juvenile hens and roosters look very similar. For roosters, their comb might be a tiny bit larger, and their legs a bit bigger, but the differences will only become clear when they are 5 or 6 months old (depends on the breed tho). In late July, I incubated half a dozen random eggs from the market, and one of the chicks was a leghorn, and I wasn't 100% sure if it was a rooster or a hen - leghorns are quite large generally, but then it started crowing only a few weeks ago - definitely a rooster then.
