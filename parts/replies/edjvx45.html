      Briefly, if you construct a phylogeny of certain major animal phyla using whole genome sequences, comb jellies are a sister to all other animal phyla. This presents a problem with respect to neuronal and muscle cell types that has two obvious solutions. Either A) The last common ancestor of all animals had neurons and muscles, but cell types were lost in some lineages; or B) comb jellies evolved these cell types independently. This method of placing comb jellies into the phylogenetic tree is disputed.

      No review jumped out at me, but here's a paper that shows how to get two completely different placements of these guys: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1055790313000298
