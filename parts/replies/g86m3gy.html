    • canyonnerd

      “Normal” is 120/80.

      Elevated- 120-129 / 80

      Stage 1 Hypertension- 130-139 / 80-89.

      Stage 2 Hypertension- 140-149 / >90.

      Very high blood pressure can damage the organs. If the person is experiencing symptoms, or if there’s evidence of organ damage- that’s called a hypertensive crisis and it’s a medical emergency.

      Lower is not always better. It can be normal for some people and they don’t have any problems. But a slightly or somewhat low BP can cause dizziness, fainting, etc., for some. Blood pressure is the force that makes sure your blood actually gets to all your organs and delivers oxygen and nutrients to them. If blood pressure is suuuper low, your organs won’t get enough blood- leading to organ failure and death. However, that kind of dangerously low blood pressure is usually related to severe bleeding, brain damage, serious infection, etc.
