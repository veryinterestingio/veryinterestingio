    • MyNameIsRay

      Fuel is a hydrocarbon, a long chain of Hydrogen and Carbon

      To burn it, you combine Oxygen with the hydrocarbon (and an ignition source-a spark/flame/heat/pressure)

      Carbon+Oxygen=CO or CO2 (carbon monoxide/dioxide)

      Hydrogren+Oxygen=H2O (water, in the form of vapor, because it's hot)

      (Oversimplified, of course, but that's the basics)

      The hot water vapor condenses in the cold air. It doesn't just look like a cloud, it literally is a cloud.

      Cars are no different, you'll notice a drip of water out of the tailpipe, or clouds on cold days, for the exact same reason.
