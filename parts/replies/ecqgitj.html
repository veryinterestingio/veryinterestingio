    • sharkmonkeyzero

      The frequency is directly related to the speed of the generator, so if it's spinning slightly slower that's how you get lower or higher frequencies in standalone systems. Airplane engines generate "wild frequency" power that goes up to about 800 Hz, for example, because of the variable speed of the engines. The grid system is just so big that any individual generator spinning slightly off the rest of the system gets pushed back to the system frequency by all the rest of the generators in the system.
