    For the OP, in the US, there's a form called a W-4 that tells your employer how much to withhold. You're allowed to make it whatever value you want. If you're getting a huge refund or owe a huge bill, it's likely that your W-4 is out of whack (unless you did that on purpose).

    The government doesn't particularly discourage refunds because those show up, to them, as a tax free loan from you.

      Adding to this:

      IOW: Beat bet is to fill the W-4 accurately to minimize overpayment while avoiding penalties.

    Taxes are actually due every quarter. If enough of your income comes from non-withheld sources then you have to pay estimated taxes quarterly. Most people don't run into that because withholding normally covers it. It's not recommended to set your withholding too low because if you repeatedly owe too much money when you file your return the IRS will fine you.

    It's also a guess. If you make a certain amount in a two-week period, a formula determines how much to withhold based on what your total yearly earnings would be if you continued to make that much all year. Then it adjusts that number using the filing status you gave them on your W-4 (single, married, etc.)
