    To expand on the question a bit more. Given that teeth are in a socket in the bone, how does orthodontics work? Does the socket move?

    • UtesDad

      Short answer: yes the socket "moves"

      Bone throughout your body is constantly destroying and replacing itself, including the bone in your jaw.

      Orthodontists use this to their advantage by applying pressure to the teeth (and thereby to the ligaments and bone) to amplify bone destruction in the direction we want the tooth to go. The tooth slowly moves into the space created as the bone is destroyed and new bone is deposited on the back side of where the tooth used to be. So, over time, the position of the socket moves as the tooth is moved through the bone.
