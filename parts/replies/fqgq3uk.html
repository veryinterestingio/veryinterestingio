      Oh no, not cooler than ambient temperature. On a second read-through I've noticed I may have misinterpreted your original question.

      Solar panels still only convert about 20% of the solar energy hitting them into electricity. And since they are rather dark, they become quite warm.

      But the reason why malfunctioning panels are warmer than ones actively providing electricity that is being used is even simpler than you think: malfunctioning panels have an efficiency of 0%. So the panels producing electricity take 20% of the solar energy hitting them and converting it to electricity, so there is less heat left to, well, heat up the panel. The panels that are malfunctioning take in all the solar energy that hits them (minus reflected energy obviously) and convert all of it to heat by simply being dark.
