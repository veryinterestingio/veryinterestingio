    There's one bit here though, once something like dopamine hits a receptor the receptor generally not only binds with it for signalling, but can also perform enzymatic function.

    In the case of dopamine, it'll convert it to DOPAL ( 3,4-Dihydroxyphenylacetaldehyde), by the Monoamine-oxidase enzyme or in other cases 3-Methoxytyramine. Either way though in the end they trigger the COMT (catechol-O-methyl transferase) enzyme.

    So dopamine isn't even the same chemical going in and coming out of these reactions. It comes out as Homovanillic Acid after those receptors do their enzyme chemistry.
