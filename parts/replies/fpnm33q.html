      • grimsolem

        Counter Strike came out in Nov 2000, replacing Doom/Duke Nukem to redefine (IMO) the FPS line. Medal of Honor: Allied Assault came out in 2002 and was excellent as well.

        For RTS,the Age of Empires franchise was also huge then, as well as the Command & Conquer series which had started in 1995 (they also released an FPS, Renegade, in '02). The Warcraft series started as an RTS, releasing Warcraft 3 in '02 as well.

        As far as MMOs, Runescape was Jan 2001, Everquest was around for a year already. Sid Meier's Civ series was also big.

        MOBAs, to my knowledge, didn't really except in things like custom Warcraft III games (an RTS which had an amazingly complex custom map maker, allowing the use of a nasty sort of Javascript to change the game's behavior from it's original eco-war standard to anything from an ice-sliding racer to full on 8+ hour multiplayer RPGs.)
