  • demostravius2

    End part is false. There is nothing about being in a caloric deficiency that guarantees using stored energy.

    Energy from the adipose tissue can only be accessed when serum insulin is low enough to allow the fat breaking down enzymes to function. Lipoprotien lipase breaks down triglycerides in the blood and passes it into your cells. If activity is low on muscle cells they will not get energy regardless of calorie deficits because the energy simply isn't passing through. Low insulin also allows Hormone Sensitive Lipase to break fats down from inside the adipose and let it access the blood.

    This is often called cellular starvation. Cells will simply use less energy, or get it from muscle and bone. Weakened immune system, slower cell repair, lower body temperature, etc. There are plenty of ways you can lower cellular output. It's why people often feel tired and cold when dieting via calorie counting. The body is just lowering output to compensate for the dropped calories. If you have a lot of body fat you shouldn't feel hungry or tired if energy is getting to the cells, that makes no sense, but it happens a lot.
