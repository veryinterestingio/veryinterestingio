    This is a good answer, there is indeed a lot of misinformation in other parts of this post.

    Its worth noting that traditional bar soap is typically alkaline with pH in the 9-10 range. The majority of bacteria thrive in neutral or slightly acidic conditions, so the alkalinity of typical bar soap prevents bacterial growth to some degree.

    The same does not apply to most liquid surfactants like shower gel or hand wash, which is one reason that all commercial liquid soap formulations (even those not marketed as antimicrobial) include small amounts of antimicrobial or bacteriostatic preservatives (ie parabens, isothiazolinones, etc). But as mentioned above this does not imply sterility.. preservatives are there primarily to impede microbes from replicating and adversely affecting the formulation.
