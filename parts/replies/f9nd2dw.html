    That's a damn good example for all the people who are having trouble with the concept that silver isn't a colour as such, it's just the name we give to a surface that reflects all colours.

    If you looked at this drawing closely, you could see each of the colours the artist has used, representing each of the things the skull is reflecting. There's no "silver" chalk that's been used, but because the drawing is so convincingly done, the overall effect is that your brain insists this object is silver.
