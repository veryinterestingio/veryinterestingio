    • turniphat

      Insider trading is usually only investigated on big trades on bad news. In the Martha Stewart case, she sold all her stock the day before it was announced ImClone didn't get FDA approval. That's a huge trade on obviously bad news that's going to affect the stock.

      If Tim Cook sells less than 1% of his stock the day before the next iPhone announcement, that's not really a huge issue. Will the stock go up or down, nobody really knows. Everybody has a good idea the next iPhone is coming, will it live up to expectations? That's hard to say either way.

      If he dumped 100% of his stock, and he knows iPhone sales are way down, that's obviously an issue. Everything in the middle, that's up to a court to decide. They'd look at the communications between Tim and his broker. If he's buying a yacht, can he can prove it, he can show the transactions to the yacht broker. He'd have a paper trail showing when the yacht payments were coming due. If it's just a text at 11pm says "dump everything by tomorrow!!!!!" then that looks bad.
