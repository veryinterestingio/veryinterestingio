  • NoRealAccountToday

    This is a good explanation, but for the sake of correctness, a vacuum tube valve used in amplification (in this case a triode) is normally fully "on". That is to say, the "valve wire" (more correctly called the grid) mentioned above when current is added, it actually closes the valve (i.e. reduces volume). The solid-state transistor which replaced the triode actually works as originally described... normally off, but proportionally "on" when the control voltage is applied.
