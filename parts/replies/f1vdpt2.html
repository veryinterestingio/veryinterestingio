    • _corsarius_

      Yes and no.

      Yes, the blades are designed to push the air in a specific direction, making this 'beam' of wind. Were you to run the fan in reverse, the air pushed from the back would likely be dispersed because the blades are not designed to work backward.

      No, you cannot design a fan to suck air in a beam because of how fluids (air is a fluid too) work. The way a fan works is it pushes the air forward, creating a low-pressure zone that the air all around the fan tries to fill.

    • nogooduser

      No it wouldn't. It's because the air in the back isn't being moved as directly as the front.

      The fan moves the close air through the blades which reduces the pressure. This causes air to move into the lower pressure zone causing another lower pressure zone. The air moves into the lower pressure areas from all around which causes the more dispersed movement.

      The air in front of the fan has been pushed in a specific direction so is not as dispersed.
