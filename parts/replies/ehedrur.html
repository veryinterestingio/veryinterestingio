  • Hypothesis_Null

    A good way for this to make sense:

    A bullet hits you with momentum. It gains that momentum from being pushed out of a gun barrel by expanding gas. Which is itself pushing on the back of the gun barrel.

    So if you shoot a pistol, the same momentum imparted into that bullet is imparted into your hand with the recoil. Equal and opposite and all that. The difference is the energy is dissipated over a longer time (accelerating out of barrel vs impacting at full speed) and it is spread out across your hand through the grip rather than focused at a single point.

    Same energy. Greater distance spread over greater area.

    Thus, the point of armor then is to absorb the impact over time and spread it over a large area. To make it feel more like you're shooting the bullet rather than getting hit with it.
