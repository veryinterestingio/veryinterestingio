      Ice cores from the Andes show a super El Nino from 536 to 594, causing 30 years of intense rain followed by 30 years of drought. These events may have contributed heavily to the collapse of the Moche culture in Peru, which had thrived for centuries prior but began a sharp decline in the 6th century and disappeared by the mid 8th century.
