    • RossParka

      I've never heard the resonance argument before, but as far as I know the long-wavelength ("red") cones don't have a second peak. No second peak is visible in this diagram which is a plot of experimental data.

      I have seen peaks in similar diagrams, but that one looks hand drawn and I think it's probably a mistake.

      Many people get confused because they think the cones detect RGB, and violet has red in it, so there must be a second peak. In reality there's a lot of complicated processing between the cone outputs and the RYGB primaries. Primary blue is pretty much exactly the hue of a blue sky. I don't see what evolutionary benefit there would be to seeing the sky as a primary color, but it probably isn't coincidence that we do. If that's what sets the "blue point," then as a side effect the end of the spectrum will continue past that point toward red.
