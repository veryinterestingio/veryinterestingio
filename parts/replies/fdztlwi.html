      It all depends on the size and speed of rifting along with how long it can go on for. Not all flood basalts are equal, but they generally are rather impactful on the environment. I believe the gas content of the rifting eruptions is extremely relevant here in that regard.

      Fwiw, some of the largest explosive eruptions are believed to have occurred during the initial phases of the afar rift event in what is now yemen. These eruptions were much larger than Toba or the Yellowstone eruptions , but there is very limited info on them.

      As for Iceland, it just isn't active enough relative to other flood basalt zones. Basically, unless a flood basalt is adding co2 faster than the atmosphere can remove it for a very long time period, it likely won't cause problems for the global environment. Iceland just doesn't add enough co2 to be an issue right now since it's quieter relative to some of the ancient flood basalts. The Siberian traps on the other hand really overwhelmed the co2 levels, and it's believed to have hit a very large coal seam which made things even worse.

  • cbus20122

    The p/t extinction resulting from the Siberian traps was quick, yet the flood basalt event was a million year long event. Continental breakups can absolutely cause extinction events provided that they're associated with large enough flood basalt eruptions (which frequently occur during Continental breakups). After that, it all depends how much the flood basalt could wreck the ecosystem and environment.

    Climate systems tend to be a series of negative feedback loops. So they can endure a lot of stress, but when something powerful enough can push them past those points of stress, the entire system can break down rather quick. From my knowledge of the Siberian traps event, that's kind of how it occurred.

    So when you think of million year long events, keep th above in mind. Also keep in mind the fact that during these events, there were periods of greatly heightened eruptive volumes and climatic impacts, likely within a short time span.
