    It's also worth pointing out that just making some profit–at any stage–isn't the threshold for success.

    If you throw several hundred million dollars at something then you want to get as much as you can out of it. If you dedicate X% of theater screens to showing that movie then you want to get as much as you can out of that. If you tie up Y% of the actors, directors, writers, editors, etc that are available you want to get the most profit possible out of those resources.

    These opportunity costs are what set the threshold of what success looks like. If your movie makes $50 million when a better written one could have made $100 million with the same resources then it underperformed. Obviously a movie that takes a loss overall is always a financial failure, but even movies that profit can be financial disappointments.
