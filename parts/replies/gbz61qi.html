    Also, nicotine is generally an excitatory molecule in the brain, especially when it comes to reward-type pathways. It is an agonist for acetylcholine. Nicotine in your body acts as if you elevated acetylcholine in your blood. This can cause some short-term, then long-term changes to the receptors found on many types of neurons in your CNS. Along with the decreased level of actylcholine receptors comes a higher tolerance of nicotine. As you shock your system by ceasing to smoke, your neurons increase their number of acetylcholine receptors over time. This will have the effect of you becoming more initially sensitive to the ACh neurotransmitter but over time that will balance out to your original baseline.
