    I’ll point that there’s also a bit of coaching that happens, if you have someone who’s not experienced with TV. When you’re telling a story, you normally will want to speak in the past tense, but that doesn’t work with interviews that they’re planning to intercut with the events as they’re happening. So if a person says “I didn’t think Gary was ready to take on that responsibility”, the director may remind the interviewee to speak in the present tense and go back and say “I don’t think Gary is ready to take on that responsibility.”
