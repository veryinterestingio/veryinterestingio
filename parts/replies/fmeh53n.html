      • DynamiCircuitry

        It’s not really about the displacement, it’s about how you go about making the boom.

        Gasoline engines usually have a ratio between 8:1 and 12:1. Mazda managed to find a way to increase the compression ratio up to 14:1 for their gas engines(and can actually run in some conditions without a spark )

        14:1 is where Diesel engine ratios start. they can have compression ratios up to around 25:1. Diesel engines don’t have a spark plug because compression generates enough heat that fuel is applied at the right time instead of a spark.

        Looking at this number alone I’d argue that diesel engines will always have the efficiency upper hand but, displacement ratio is only part of the game. lately it seems smaller forced air engines have been winning on efficiency.
