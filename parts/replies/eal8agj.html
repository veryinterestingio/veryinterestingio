    To echo constantino1's point, one could ask: What's the smallest example of a system that can exhibit cloud formation whose position relative to "the ground" does change if one is to make the system larger.

    The point here is that if we surrounded the earth with a enormous clear plastic sphere of radius say 10 times the radius of the earth, then the clouds would form at roughly the same height as if we enlarged the sphere to have radius equal to 20 times the radius of the earth. This is not true of your system with hot water inside a plastic box with ice on top. If you enlarge the box a bit, the rain would still form at the edge of the box.
