    • admsteff

      Stratification is the term. Not necessarily frozen, just a specific cold period followed by whatever warmer temps it likes. The cold period(s) counts off a metabolic "clock" during which time the plant can't/doesn't grow. It waits for this clock to run to completion before it will ever germinate, no matter what you do to it (generally speaking).

      The purpose is to avoid germination during autumn, where conditions might be "ideal," but it would be undesirable to grow before it can protect itself during winter. Or perhaps a warm spell during winter, where the plant would then be killed when cold returns. Plants using this system want spring, not just ideal conditions at any given time.
