      • Sir_Jeremiah

        Edit: originally thought the original comment said 10,000kg, redid it with 10kg.

        That much lead would be about 0.00088 cubic meters (assuming no empty internal pockets of air), and that much aluminum would be about 0.00371 cubic meters. We know that buoyant force is equal to the weight of the water displaced, so we can calculate the buoyant force using these volumes. So, 0.00088 cubic meters of water is 880g, making the total weight of the submerged lead 10kg-0.88kg=9.12g. The 0.00371 cubic meters of aluminum displaces 3.71kg of water, making it's apparent weight 10kg-3.71kg=6.29kg, almost a 3kg difference. Since drag resistance depends on the shape and speed of the objects, and I think we can assume they are the same shape which cancels out and let's just assume they aren't being lifted fast enough to cause significant drag force, we can probably assume that these factors won't be significant enough to overcome a difference of 3kg (weight not mass).
