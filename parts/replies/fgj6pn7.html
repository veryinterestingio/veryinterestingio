    Do those nerves and veins reconnect over time? Or are they just forever broken (ahh I can’t think of the right word!)

    • zeiandren

      some, veins are pretty good at growing back, nerves aren't great. Because veins are like, a bunch of microscopic cells. if one dies who cares. grow another one. A nerve is a bunch of very long individual cells. if you cut an inch long nerve cell in the middle you lose that whole inch of nerve cell. Nerve cells can grow back and you do get some function back, but growing a whole new inch of cells and getting it to line up is a lot rougher than growing back a micrometer of new cells to connect up the two parts of a vein.

      In surgery we do our best to mash the ends of nerves close as possible and do things like graft nerves in the holes, and that helps, but nerves are just super long cells, some even a foot or more long, and you can't fix them if they get cut, and just have to hope for long slow regrowth that is always only sort of okay.

      People that lose an arm and get it reattached usually have a very very long period of the arm being dead and then after some years get up to like 60% motion and some pretty bad problems with numbness. It's not great.
