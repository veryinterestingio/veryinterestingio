    • agate_

      What matters is partial pressure of CO2: the concentration times the overall pressure of the atmosphere.

      Earth has a 1 bar atmosphere with 0.04% CO2. Mars has a 0.003 bar atmosphere with almost 100% CO2. If you do the math, you find that the partial pressure of CO2 on Mars is 7.5 times Earth's.

      That, plus the lower temperature, allows CO2 ice to form on Mars.

    • Astromike23

      how did dry ice form on Mars?

      The winter poles of Mars are much colder, as low as -140 C (-220 F). That's enough to freeze out even the low partial pressure of CO2 there.

      EDIT: Phase diagram of Mars (from Longhi, 2006). The surface shows a wide range of temperatures, but around -140 C (133 K) is exactly where CO2 at that partial pressure crosses from gas to solid.
