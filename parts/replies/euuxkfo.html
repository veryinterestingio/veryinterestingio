  • tonybunce

    What that is technically correct for any product in a major store there is actually a master database of barcodes called the "GS1 global registry" or "Global Data Synchronization network"

    If you want a product on the shelf at Kroger, Walmart, Meijer, target, etc. you must subscribe to a service to get your product info into the GDSN. GDSN isn't as simple as "UPC xyz = 16oz spicy BBQ sauce from Historic BBQ" - It has much more data. You have to specify things like product dimensions, case size, pallet configuration, ordering lead time, shelf life, ingredients, nutrition claims, warehouse locations, and more. GDSN data providers charge based on your sales volume and can be a significant expense.
