    In the context of the US, were originally (if briefly) independent, sovereign states. American constitutional law still retains some of this language, for example we still talk about state sovereignty or dual sovereignty. And while sovereign states have the power to regulate all manner of behavior, the federal government lacks what we call general police power. This is the power to prohibit common criminal behaviors like murder, robbery, or burglary. All of these law are passed and enforced by the individual states unless there's some specific relationship to the federal government (like the place burgled was a federal property or the person killed was a federal agent or employee.)
