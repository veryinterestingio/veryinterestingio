    While not all species out-right reject albino specimens, many will have trouble finding a receptive mate.

    Apparently squirrels are more accepting though. I was able to witness this first-hand on a middle school trip to D.C. in which my classmates and I spotted an albino squirrel frolicking along with normal pigmented squirrels at Arlington Cemetery.

    It really does vary a lot by species with prey vs. predator and herd/pack vs. solitary roles playing a big part.
