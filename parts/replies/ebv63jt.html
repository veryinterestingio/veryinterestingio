    • mirshafie

      As far as I'm aware the absence of the normal zeitgebers such as light, temperature and chemical composition of air/water correlate to a loss of organization of biological clock functions. While many cave-dwellers do retain certain clock function seemingly for metabolic purposes, the loss of pigmentation/vision seem to be correlated with a loss in circadian rythm in several examples of convergent evolution, which appear to suggest that the primary rythmic que is light, with some minor support from temperature and chemical fluctuations.

      It might interest you that certain yeasts retain a heavily dampened biological clock in the absence of circadian ques.
