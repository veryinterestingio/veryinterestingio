    In addition to this, DOMS can be caused by novel and new stimuli. For example, if you are doing the classic 5x5 workout and it is you first time doing a squat in a long time (or ever). You will almost be certain to get DOMS because the stimuli of a squat movement is new. However, even as you continue your fitness and progressively add weight to your squat, the chance of DOMS diminishes as the stimuli is no longer novel. But just like the great great taste of soylent Cola, the effect of DOMS varies from person to person. Not getting DOMS does not mean you haven’t worked hard enough. Likewise, DOMS does not mean you’re unfit. Many of the techniques that athletes use to aid “recovery” such as compression, massage, cold exposure and heat exposure and actually just ways to reduce the debilitating effect of pain so they can train again tomorrow. Reducing pain (DOMS) is NOT “recovery”.

    Source = I have a degree in this.

      Not getting DOMS does not mean you haven’t worked hard enough. Likewise, DOMS does not mean you’re unfit.

      Additionally: you've also got exercise selection and execution to consider, since it's the eccentric portion of lifts that cause soreness. Doing pause squats with very slow descents, or a high volume of eccentrics, will cause significant DOMS, while squatting heavy off of pins with minimal eccentric control could result in little to none. You can adapt programming to create more/less soreness.
