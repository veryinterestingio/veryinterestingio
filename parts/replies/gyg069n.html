      True, many artists have no intention to be realistic and I didn’t mean to imply that realism is inherently better than symbolic art. It depends on what the artist intends to communicate. I was simply responding to the OP’s question about why it took so long for art to match what we now perceive as realism. I think of realism as a type of technology that was communally evolved over generations as artists built on each other’s discoveries and techniques. Some artists contributed to that goal and many more chose to explore other avenues of expression.

      Scott McCloud has an amazing book called “Understanding Comics” that delves deeply into the relative strengths and weaknesses of representational and symbolic art. Scott says:

      “When we abstract an image through cartooning, we’re not so much eliminating details as we are focusing on specific details. By stripping down an image to its essential meaning an artist can amplify that meaning in a way that realistic art can’t.”

      I highly recommend that book. Scott is brilliant!
