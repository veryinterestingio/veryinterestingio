    • Tippy_the_just

      In short nothing, I work on a transplant unit and starting to work with Syncardia, a total heart replacement (TAH), and the pulse rate is set to what the body needs. Everyone is different in what they need to sustain their body.

      Total hearts are still in the early stages therefore outside of the hospital they are essentially anchored to a machine with wheels that’s about the size of travel luggage. It’s essentially used as a bridge for people waiting to get a heart but they do have long term cases where it’s more of a destination therapy (meaning your going to live the rest of your life with this).

      Syncardia

      There are a lot of moral and ethical dilemmas surrounding this still and there’s a lot to be learned. Since the heart is a machine it can essentially go on forever even as the rest of the body whithers away around it. So you have questions like at what point do we literally pull the plug which at this point is a huge grey area outside of brain death and even then it’s not an easy decision. Turning off the machine means stopping the heart which isn’t the same as stopping medications and even ventilators (machines to help you breathe) and allow the person to pass (die) on their own.
