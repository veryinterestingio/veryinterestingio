• narmerguy

    The reality is that patients and doctors do not know the precise time for every patient when they should stop. Instead, studies are frequently done on the outcomes of Duration X vs Duration Y. Whichever duration has better outcomes is the one that is recommended.

    Of course every person, and every infection, is unique, so someone could probably get away with Durations A, B, C, but it's hard to know who is who and which is which. Simply feeling better has not been reliably shown across studies to be an effective endpoint. Some people have high pain tolerance and "feel better" even when they are still infected. For these reasons, doctors ask patients to complete the regimen using the time that has been demonstrated to be effective to reduce the necessity of guessing on either the Doctor or the Patient's part. Some people are likely over-treated but seldom is anyone undertreated, which, in most cases, is more immediately harmful.
