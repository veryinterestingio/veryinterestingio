    • Silly_Promise_3342

      Your body stores energy in several ways - in your blood, in your liver, in your muscles and in various fat reserves.

      It doesn’t draw from them evenly. Sugars in your blood and liver are the “instant, on demand” reserve. Visceral fat is one of the next stops down the line and will be drawn on early when calories in are less than calories out.

      Subcutaneous fat is more “long term storage” that your body doesn’t like to draw from - it’ll often try draw from muscles instead, and lower energy use overall.

      So getting rid of it takes a longer and more persistent approach.
