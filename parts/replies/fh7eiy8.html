    Here's some cool work on the higher order details of the tides:

    https://arbic.earth.lsa.umich.edu/research/global-modeling-of-past-and-present-barotropic-tides/

    https://arbic.earth.lsa.umich.edu/research/global-modeling-of-internal-tides-and-the-internal-gravity-wave-continuum/

    tl;dr the images you show are only the two largest tidal modes (something like the L=1 spherical harmonics), whereas there really exists a continuum of tidal "frequencies", the superposition of which gives rise to the spring and neap tides to first order, but also includes all kinds of interesting small-scale effects and mode couplings
