    To add on some simple math to this great explanation, here’s how the breakdown would work for the US based on finding this site when I googled US population by age:

    https://www.statista.com/statistics/241488/population-of-the-us-by-sex-and-age/

    There are 328 million people in America. About 60 million are 14 or younger, so let’s count them as children(I couldn’t find anything official saying what the age range is for the vaccine). That leaves 268 million adults. If 2/3 get the vaccine, then 178 million Americans will get the vaccine, which would be ~54%. So 10 percentage points away from the best case scenario and 25 away from the worst.

    Quick edit: I should probably do the math the other way. In order to hit those herd immunity numbers, then in the worst case scenario 79.3% of Americans need to be vaccinated, so 260 million people, or 97% of adults. For 63.8% best case scenario, 209 million people need to get it, or 78% of adults.
