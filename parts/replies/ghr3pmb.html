    • GinnyO

      There’s a lot of factors that go into it. It’s more related to how many cells were damaged and how many tissue layers were damaged. But essentially if the cell is injured enough to die, whether that’s by a chemical, burn, trauma, etc the end result of a nonreversible cellular injury is cell death, and that process follows specific mechanisms that are essentially the same no matter what type of cell.

      The more cells that die, the more inflammation, the harder it is to heal. There are a lot of complex signals, etc in the healing process and the more damage to repair the less effective it is. So really it’s more about how many cells died and how many tissue layers were affected than the specific mode of injury.
