    • Stargate525

      No one suggests it's atmosphere-like gas all the way to the core.

      We know how gasses behave at temperature and pressure. We know how much pressure the atmosphere we can detect would react under the pressure it must be under for the size of the planet. We can then extrapolate the properties from there.

      It's like those puzzles that give disparate clues; susie wears a green dress, frank is to the left of susie, jeff is not sitting next to someone wearing green. You have a bunch of readings, and only so many theories fit all the information we have.

      A rocky planet like ours with a massive atmosphere doesn't fit.
