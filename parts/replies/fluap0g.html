      • Entropius

        The history of their evolution probably matters less than animal size. Cats are a great example because all cats are similar in hunting habits yet differ in eyes. So there’s a kind of soft control already in place.

        • Tigers, Lions, Leopards, Jaguars, and Cougars have circular pupils.

        • House cats have vertical slit pupils.

        But some interesting things worth noting:

        • Bobcats who’s size is between those 2 groups have a pupil that’s a shape between the two kinds: a kind of shorter rounder slit.

        • Cougars are more closely related to house cats than lions/tigers/leopards/jaguars. Cougars aren’t even in the panthera taxon. Yet their eyes are still identical to the big panthera species. The history of their evolution doesn’t seem to matter as much as size.
