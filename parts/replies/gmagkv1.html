      • TorakMcLaren

        That'd be the Virtual Barber Shop :)

        So, it's to do with what's called the Head-Related Transfer Function (HRTF). You put microphones inside the ears (either of a mannequin like KEMAR or of a specific person), and then record a bunch of clicks from different locations around the head inside an anechoic chamber.

        A recording of a click is called an impulse response. You can use a recording of a short click to simulate being in a room by doing what's called a convolution. Essentially mixing the sound file with the recording of the click.

        To make sounds feel like you're actually there, you use the HRTF. Say you want something to sound like it's coming from 30째 left, 10째 up. You convolve it with the recordings from both ears when the click was at 30째L, 10째U. For spots in between where clicks were, you can sort of mix things together. This works well for a lot of folk using a generic set of ears (like KEMAR's), but it's insanely good if it's done with recordings of your own ears!
