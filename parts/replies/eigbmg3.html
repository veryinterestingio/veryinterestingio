      I don't know a lot about audio equipment, but you may also have signal issues with the slight increase in contact resistance from oxides that forms on the copper. Even when it's not visible, oxide layers will be there and they can cause rises in contact resistance at lower normal forces. Also, many red metals you see in the consumer market are not pure copper - pure copper doesn't have great mechanical properties for contacts. Bad spring forces, stress relaxation, etc. - the conductivity of these alloys drops significantly when alloyed so gold is most likely more conductive than the copper alloy. For example, a phosphor bronze alloy we commonly use is about 9% IACS, which means it is only 9% of the conductivity of copper.
