    • Frednotbob

      They're not trying to milk the studio; a TV screen is only a few inches in size, but a movie screen is about 40 or 50 feet wide, so the corresponding image is much larger. The primary reason for the difference is that the flaws and imperfections are much more visible.

      If you watch the commentary for Serenity (the Firefly movie) the visual artists mention that they had to completely rework the computer-generated ship model because the one they designed for television wasn't detailed enough to look good on a fifty-foot theater screen.
