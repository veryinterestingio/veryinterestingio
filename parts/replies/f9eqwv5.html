  • Haloisi

    You could build on a cosmetic outer shell, but that brings you to the main point:

    The fact is that submarines do use an outer non-pressure shell. Although it is not cosmetic.

    For example the Kursk had an inside pressure hull of 50 millimetre steel, and an outside hull of 8 millimetre, that determines the shape. The space in between was filled with for example rubber to dampen noise.

    Apparently the contemporary design strategy of western subs is less inside-outside hull strategy, but they also build some shell parts that are only there for the shape - and not for the pressure hull.

    Their hull shapes are the end result of immense amounts of design work and testing to produce the quietest ship possible, and a rounded hull that slips smoothly and quietly through the water must presumably be the best way to go.

    A quiet ship is mostly a silent ship. If the shape optimally glides through the water and disturbs it minimally, it is likely to also be a fast shape.
