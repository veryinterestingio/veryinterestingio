      They will only be able to count the number of steps. But the distance will be inaccurate, since it's simply using the number of steps multiplied with some distance around 2-3 feet.

      The GPS signal wouldn't help even if the treadmill was outside, since it can't tell the phone anything about what your feet did.

      It's just used to be more accurate when walking/running outside, since the distance each step takes can vary quite a lot between people and walking speeds.

      So it'll tell you 10,000 steps (as in as many "shocks") and then use the most accurate source to put a distance behind that like 5 miles.

      But the step counter will always work. It does not require anything to happen but the shocks from landing on the next foot.
