      No, a tank feeding down one floor from attic to bathroom will have far lower pressure than the rising main.

      I think the reasoning was partially so that if, for any reason, the water supply from outside was interrupted, water was still available for washing and flushing the toilet.

      Also, standard fitment in UK bathrooms in those years was a bath, not a shower, and typically water tanks were sized to provide roughly the amount of water as needed for the bathtub.
