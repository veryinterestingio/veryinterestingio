    • aClimateScientist

      Pretty accurate, although he underestimated the cooling effect of human aerosol emissions ("man-made dust") a little bit. Yes, temperatures are the warmest they have been in the last 10,000 years and probably much longer. Global temperatures are a bit iffier to date further back than 10,000 years because you have to use more indirect things like ocean temperature records from fossils at the bottom of the ocean and measurements of isotopes from ice cores in Antarctica and Greenland. Turning these measurements into a globally-averaged temperature is hard and pretty inaccurate. For the last few 1000 years we can use tree rings, which are related to temperature, to measure spatial patterns of temperature changes on land pretty accurately.

      Broecker was talking about a brief cooling period from 1940s-1970s. Coincidentally, the cooling trend basically ended in 1975 and we've had a solid warming trend every since.
