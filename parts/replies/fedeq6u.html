  • Breadfish64

    M.2 drives are not necessarily faster, there's three things that are different about newer SSDs which you're lumping together. Form factor: 2.5" to M.2, Interface: SATA to PCI-e, and Protocol: AHCI to NVMe. Many cheaper M.2 drives such as the 860 Evo or MX500 use SATA and will perform the same as a 2.5 inch drive, all SATA drives use AHCI which is a protocol designed with mechanical hard drives in mind. Then there is PCI-e SSDs, these have a straight connection to the CPU rather than going through a SATA controller, PCI-e connections can be made through M.2, actual PCI-e slots, or U.2(a cable that connects to a 2.5" drive) the later two are found mostly on enterprise grade SSDs e.g. from Intel. The vast majority of these use the newer NVMe protocol but it's not a requirement, I believe the Samsung 950 pro still used AHCI.
