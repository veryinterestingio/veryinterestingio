    • mohelgamal

      They aren’t exactly living, they are a protein envelope with DNA or RNA inside, depending on the type, they end up with various mechanisms get in the cell and the genetic material gets released, the cells then start replicating and translating the genetic material, creating many copies of the virus.

      Killing the virus, usually by heat, denature the protein and damage its DNA so that is it doesn’t successfully attach and have its genetic material work.

      When a protein is made, the strand of amino acids is folded in a way that is guided by the sequence of amino acids as well as the way it is made, this means the protein attains a specific 3D shape that is essential to functioning correctly.

      Denaturing protein means that the protein loose its original shape, even though it may still have the same sequence of aminoacids, kinda like what you see when you cook an egg. When that happens the protein can no longer functions, but is still of the same structure.

      So the word “inactivated” is really a more correct description than killed
