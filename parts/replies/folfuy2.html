      That the head loses more heat than any other part of the body is a myth. It was examined by researchers who found that the head, which has about 10% of the total skin surface area, loses about 10% of the body's heat, not factoring in clothing. It can be a major source when the entire rest of the body is covered, but if you covered up your head and left your arms bare instead, they'd be the majority of heat loss instead.
