  • paulexcoff

    So-called "neon" colors don't just reflect more light. They fluoresce. The pigments absorb high energy photons like blues, violets, and ultraviolets, get excited, and re-radiate whatever the emission wavelength of that pigment is. So they're actually reflecting and emitting back more orange, yellow, or green photons than even hit them in the first place.
