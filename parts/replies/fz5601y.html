      • cleverpseudonym1234

        A dozen is 12 because 12 was a common number number for counting. You have a dozen inches in a foot, a dozen hours in a day, a dozen shillings in a penny (once upon a time), etc., all traces of an ancient tendency to divide things by 12, as opposed to 10 as we tend to do now. It helps that 12 is easily divided into halves, quarters, thirds and sixths — you can only cut 10 in half and fifths.

        As for why it’s called “dozen,” that comes from the French word for 12, which of course comes from Latin, where it’s literally “two ten.”
