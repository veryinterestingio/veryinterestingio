    • DoomGoober

      The valsalva technique has gradations based on the number of reps and percent of max exertion based on the weight you are lifting.

      A full valsalva (fill cavity with air and hold) is quite taxing and straining and should only really be used for max effort lifts.

      As an amateur Olympic lifter, you are better off doing a partial valsalva. Close your throat part way and expel air against the closed throat as you deadlift. A little air will escape and sound like a grunt or a "hup" sound.

      The air should be moving towards your throat not further down into your body. Opening your throat a little will force the air in the right direction. Additionally it will let you expel CO2 which is important if you are doing more than 3 reps (so you don't pass out.)

      Btw, of you are doing high reps (say normal pushups or low resistance) you should try to exhale fully during concentric. Or if you are really high reps just breathe normally not timed at all to concentric.
