  • HydrogenButterflies

    Humans are extremely sensitive to the smell of hydrogen sulfide.

    0.00047 ppm or 0.47 ppb is the odor threshold, the point at which 50% of a human panel can detect the presence of an odor without being able to identify it.

    There’s probably pretty good reason to avoid it since it’s often indicative of decaying organic matter and it’s toxic in its own right. I could see the evolutionary pressure being pretty high.
