    • NuftiMcDuffin

      The mythbusters tested this: They only got a lighter to explode from heat when it became hot enough to actually melt the plastic.

      The reason for this is that butane gas is relatively easy to keep as a liquid, even at temperatures above the boiling point of water. That said, a damaged or faulty lighter can of course explode under heat - or one filled with propane gas, which can also be kept as a pressurized liquid, but needs a much higher pressure.
