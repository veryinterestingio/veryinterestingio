      Norco and Vicodin are a blend of hydrocodone and acetaminophen. Percocet is a blend of oxycodone and acetaminophen. So if you were actually given Norco, Vicodin, or the generic equivalent, you were taking acetaminophen, but a lower dosage of 325mg for Norco and 500mg for Vicodin. Those levels are lower than recommended for a single pill as an effective dose for acetaminophen alone, but have a synergistic effect with opioids that make them more effective and allowing less of the opioid to be more effective.

      • ttthrowaway987

        In short, I had massive bone spurs from a lifetime of competitive sports. It reached the point where I couldn't run or jump more than 10 minutes without paying for it with 2 weeks of swelling and ludicrous amounts of pain. I found a good sports surgeon and went for the surgeries with an extremely aggressive rehab plan. I was squatting in the gym again within 2 weeks. Running in 4 weeks. Ran a 5k race in 24:00 something in 3 months. Kickboxing 4x week after 4 months. Ultimate Frisbee after 5 months and competed at national championships after 10 months. Crossfit after six months (I still don't rebound off of box jumps for safety though).

        There was pain, for sure. But only during the activity and nothing after. Probably took 2.5 years to be completely pain free. I'm 100% in both feet now and no lingering issues and full mobility (tons of dorsiflexion). Very happy with the results.

        Fwiw I only had a soft cast for 1 week after each surgery, never used a boot nor crutches.
