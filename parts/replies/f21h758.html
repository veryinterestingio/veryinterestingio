    • AAVale

      Sure, but you seem to have the gist of it there. QM is full of interpretations (or Interpretations) which try to slap an ontology on the math. The math works, and the interpretations exist for those who feel the need to deal with that kind of thing.

      Wikipedia has a pretty good section on the many interpretations that exist:

      https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interpretation_of_quantum_mechanics

      The section on "Comparison" is pretty excellent too, at a glance it gives you a sense of the differences and commonalities: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interpretation_of_quantum_mechanics#Comparison

      Note that there are 17 separate ones before they run into the catchall 18th of "Others" so yeah, it's a pretty packed field. Part of the reason why this is the case is that, as long as your interpretation doesn't contradict the predictions of QM, you can stick pretty much anything that appeals to you onto the math. I think that realistically "Shut up and calculate" is also pretty common, since it either deals with the practical side, or acknowledges the incomplete nature of the theory without stressing about ontology.

      The problem is, as he points out in the blog post, that depending on what interpretation you hew to, things can get pretty weird what with retrocausality or multiple universes.
