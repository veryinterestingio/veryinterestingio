    The amount of air moved for a “loud” sound also depends on frequency. The higher the frequency, the less energy (and air moved) needed to hear a “loud” sound. This is why large speakers are needed for low frequency sounds, and smaller speakers for higher frequencies.

      And for the exception that proves the rule, you have "long throw" woofers, which use a smaller area (say 5" cone instead of 8-10-12") and make up for it with an unusually long travel, to get the same overall air moved. More compact and lower mass, but creates a new set of engineering challenges hence not as common.
