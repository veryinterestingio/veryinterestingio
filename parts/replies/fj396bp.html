  • aliyahk_

    What happens if a person develops a disorder inhibiting their bodies from producing DNAases and RNAses?

    • FogeltheVogel

      There are several known cases where people suffer from genetic DNAse deficiency. They suffer "a wide range of diseases, including lethal anemia, parakeratosis, cataracts and systemic lupus erythematosus". Digestion of DNA/RNA molecules is not really studied there.

      Even disregarding all of that, DNA/RNA molecules can't penetrate cell membranes without assistance (usually in the form of viruses, or specialized channels in cases where such travel is supposed to happen).

      So if I were to speculate, I'd say without digestion, DNA and RNA molecules would simply flush through the digestive tract without ever getting absorbed into the body.
