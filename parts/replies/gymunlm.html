    They only taste test chlorides in that video, but there are actually a good chunk of other potentially palatable anions they could go with, depending on how far you want to stretch the sense of "salt" you're going for here. Perusing wikipedia for edible anions, you've got:

    • Acetate and lactate

    • Various larger carboxylates, including citrate, tartrate, succinate, fumarate, and malate

    • Phosphates

    • Various amino acid / nucleotide-related carboxylates, including guanylate, inosinate, glycinate, and glutamate

    Sodium salts of all of these are food additives (and some pretty tasty), but I don't think it'd be unrealistic to try taste tests of their ammonium, potassium, and magnesium salts if you had infinite time and money.
