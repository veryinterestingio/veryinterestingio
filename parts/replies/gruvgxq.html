      A few things that I think need to be added here;

      1. Kites can actually be flown, in the same way a sailship can tack into a headwind, the rotational speed difference between the fixed anchor point and the kite's location would provide this "wind".

      The difference in rotational velocity between the surface of the Earth and 100 km altitude is only 1.6% for solid body rotation, which is about a 7 m/s difference at the equator. That's trivial compared to the wind at heights above a few km (or even a few hundred meters typically).

      More broadly, even if there were a big velocity gradient, the connection between the kite and the ground would still be mediated by the person holding the string, so the maximum amount of net force they can accommodate is going to be on the order of 1,000 N.
