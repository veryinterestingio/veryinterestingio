    • Aofen

      There are probably many buried or otherwise eroded craters roughly evenly distributed throughout the world. The likely reason more of them are visible as lakes in the far north is the same reason there are so many lakes there in general. The massive ice sheets carved away the former drainage systems, digging out sediment that accumulated in hollows more easily than the surrounding bedrock, and dumping lots of it in ways that blocked the former river channels. Impact structures in other parts of the world just usually aren't as easily visible.
