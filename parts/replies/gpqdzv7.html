    There's an important option missing, the CMB rest frame:

    The cosmological standard model assumes matter is distributed across spacetime in a way that allows slicing into flat homogeneous spatial hyperplanes. Motion relative to this frame can be measured by looking at the anisotropy of the cosmic microwave background. Planck measured that velocity as ~370km/s (when averaging out Earth's orbital motion around the sun).

    So in 6 months, we move about 5.8 billion km - corresponding to 5.4 light-hours - relative to that frame.

      • cygx

        It's not arbitrary insofar that it is adapted to the matter distribution.

        The 'color distribution' of the CMB is rather significant: Until gravitational wave detection matures, it's the furthest we can go as far as direct investigation of the early universe is concerned. The fluctuations are due to things like baryon acoustic oscillations, from which clever people have derived all kinds of things (photon to baryon number density, spatial flatness, ...)

        What's known as the current 'crisis in cosmology' is the disagreement of the values of the Hubble parameter as derived from comological modelling with CMB measurements as one major input, and more direct atronomical observations.
