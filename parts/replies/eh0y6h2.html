    Good question. Vaccines not only vary in terms of their efficacy and effectiveness but also in terms of longevity of their response. While there is substantial variability within different classes of vaccines, generally live attenuated vaccines (i.e. vaccines with live organisms that have been modified to eliminate their disease potential) require fewer doses to produce a robust primary response. Moreover, these vaccines result in longer term immune memory compared to the so-called killed vaccines. Therefore, in general live vaccines require fewer doses and fewer boosters.

    There’re several interesting developments in the world of vaccines. I’ll provide more detail in response to some of the subsequent questions but a brief list of things that particularly excite me include: maternal vaccines to protect moms and their babies, better adjuvants, better “platform” technologies to quickly develop vaccines against emerging threats.
