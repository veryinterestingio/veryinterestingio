      • Average_Cat_Lover

        The current models state that Influenza viruses comes from aquatic birds around the world, but can get human-specific mutations while infecting pigs and poultry in SE Asia.

        Since birds have a different immune system (with less specific antibodies that make the infection asymptomatic) and a different route of infection (oral-fecal), they just have to shit while flying over a pig farm to contaminate their water.

        Also, the genome of these viruses is just like Lego pieces, so they can exchange pieces while co-infecting an animal, normally pigs, while getting random mutations to avoid our immune system. Thus, a strain can get both bird and human specific segments, or just use very old pieces from the 1918 Flu epidemic, and that's when we get highly pathogenic strains (HPAIV) rendering the flu a ducking nightmare to analyse.
