    • S_and_M_of_STEM

      He didn't have the Earth/Sun distance. 1 AU is one "astronomical unit," and it is the length equal the the average distance between the Earth and the Sun. The thing about all of our units is they are completely arbitrary. We decide how long a second is and how far a mile is and how much a pound is (is American units). Kepler basically invented a new unit of length - the distance between the Earth and the Sun. He had no idea how long that is in miles, but for what he was doing that didn't matter. All that mattered was figuring out how much closer to or farther from the Sun each planet is compared to Earth.

      Now, to get the other two lengths we use the trigonometric relations sine, cosine, and tangent. Do you know how those work or do you want an explanation? I'm happy to do that, but I don't want to be too boring.
