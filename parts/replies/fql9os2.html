    This is the actual explanation. I wrote some more analogies for more ELI5.

    Its like if you were popping ten balloons in a line. It's easier to walk over them one by one (force is roughly half body weight) rather than lay on them five at a time (might need more force than whole body weight).

    The part of the angled blade contacting the neck starts at one side moving across the surface as the cut is made. The goal is to minimize the area of "active cutting" because what you try to cut will resist so you minimize that battle.

    Also similar to "pulling" a piece of paper in half. Difficult to do if you evenly pull from each side. If you "tear" the paper you are really just focusing the force to minimize the amount of fibers resisting. You start at one end (like an angled knife or guillotine) of the intended "cut" and also pull across the fibers so you can tear a piece of paper effortlessly. If you pull evenly allowing all of the resistance to work together it will be very difficult to make the same tear.
