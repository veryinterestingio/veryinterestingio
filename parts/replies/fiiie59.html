    Distance from satellite to ground: 35,786,000 metersTotal distance the radio waves must travel (up&back): 71,572,000 metersSpeed radio waves travel at: C = 299 792 458 meters per second

    Thus, the minimum satellite induced latency is 71,572,000/299,792,458 = 0.239 seconds. [EDIT: times two.]

    Once you add routing and computing delays, terrestrial links to your destination, and waiting for your satellite timeslot - this ends up being closer to 300ms in practice.

    But if you have the satellite to yourself and are accessing a remote data-centre - 250ms round trip times are very possible.

    The reason you see 500ms or 750ms ping times is because you have a cheap satellite service and you're suffering from collisions with other users.

    [EDIT: Sorry. As /u/marcan42 correctly points out in reply - I'm very wrong. The latency (RTT) includes two round trips to the satellite, not one - the request goes to the satellite then back to earth (239ms) then the reply goes to the satellite and back to earth (239ms) - giving you an absolute minimum theoretical RTT of 478ms. ... and that's where your 500ms to 750ms ping times come from.]
