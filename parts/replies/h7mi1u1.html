    To add to this, VZV (the virus that causes chickenpox) lays dormant in the peripheral sensory ganglia (little clumps of neurons just outside the spinal cord). When it reactivates, it travels down the axons of these neurons to the skin. Thus, the rash you get from shingles only appears in the areas innervated by the ganglia from which it originated. This is why shingles appears in very characteristic patterns.
