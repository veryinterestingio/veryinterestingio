      Actually it happens more often than you would think. The first stool passed is called meconium, it's black and it's tarry and it kind of protects the intestines during gestation. Late term babies may pass the meconium in the uterus, The concern when this happens is meconium aspiration, or the baby breathing in the meconium stain fluid. The meconium is sterile so we're not worried about infection, but we are worried about that closing up the baby's lungs. This is One of the reasons why we mark the color of the meconium when the baby is born.
