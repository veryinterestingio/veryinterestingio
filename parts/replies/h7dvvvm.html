    • fiendishrabbit

      Melting ice from the pole and greenland drives cold water south along the canadian and US coast and down to mexico. In the gulf of mexico heat and wind drives water to the surface (warm water is less dense than cold water) and powers the water north east along the european coast (making the british islands and norwegian coast warmer than it should be).

      This is helped by prevailing wind conditions (which in turn are generated by the coriolis effect)
