      The reason it’s so dry in the middle of australia is that the different layers in your atmosphere are very very dry. If you managed to saturate the air enough for it to rain enough to make any difference, A) it would flood a lot B) most of the rain would be pushed away from the “lake” by wind so unless the lake was in a valley where all the water would filter into, it would only take a couple of months/years to run dry. HOWEVER, this is ONLY the case if you can get it to rain. The atmosphere normally cools down as you go up, but in desert areas you develop something called an inversion. That’s when the atmosphere heats up as you increase in altitude, if the air below the inversion isn’t hotter than the inversion, air cannot rise and thus wouldn’t precipitate out. So even if you managed to get a massive body of water, you’d have to change the wind to not blow the moisture away, situate it in a valley, and somehow change the atmosphere to remove the persistent inversion. All of which is rather unlikey as you can imagine
