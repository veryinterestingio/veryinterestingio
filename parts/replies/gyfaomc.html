    And also wrist contain much less muscle than arm and leg due to it being a joint.

    Injuries in the wrist itself are typically damaged connective tissue - ligaments, tendons, etc. - not damaged muscles. The muscles that apply power to the wrist are the forearm muscles - still quite a significant size (compared to e.g. the finger adduction muscles in the hand).

    Athletes can damage their major muscle groups through lifting too much weight. They can also get joint injuries from highly repetitive motion with too much strain, e.g. a runner injuring their knee (just like there aren't muscles actually in the wrist, there also aren't muscles in the knee) or damaging the connective tissue in the bottom of their foot (plantar fasciitis).
