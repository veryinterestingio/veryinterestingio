    Nah, its asking about the gravitational slingshot effect. Where a body, say a spacecraft, comes towards some other body, say a planet, at such an angle that the gravitarional pull of the second body accelerates the first, but the angle of attack and velocity of the first body are such that it doesn't end up orbiting or crashing into the second body. NASA has uses in some missions, like the voyager missions.
