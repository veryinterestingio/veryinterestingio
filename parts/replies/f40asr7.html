    • ERRORMONSTER

      That's not true for one big reason that I oversimplified for the sake of explanation.

      Often, the original shape isn't gone, so much as it's unusable. Rust or paint might fill in the head, or corrosion may have etched out the detail necessary for something like a hex or torx head. It's easier to just either dig the crap out or cut through it taking advantage of the pre-existing shape if that shape is just a slot. There's no way you'd be able to carve back out a phillips, square, hex, or torx head.

      Sure you could cut a slot into any sufficiently degraded head, but you'll also have weaker surrounding material from where the head was cut (imagine digging a slot into a hex bolt. Sure it'll work, but now there's a big hole in the center of the head that serves only to weaken the head, making removal more difficult.)
