  • admiralteal

    Also, as you alluded to, you can heuristically identify bad data in the census pretty effectively -- because it's gathering a lot of information. Full address, ethnicity, family connections, et cetera. You can follow up that data with random samples and other kinds of survey data and will get accurate results.

    There are lots of other ways to get to the same data the census delivers other than taking the census. Many nations do not even conduct censuses like this and just rely on these heuristics -- there are even some arguments that following purely heuristic models is more accurate than a door-to-door census.

    It's MUCH harder to heuristically exclude bad election results. For a whole lot of reasons. There's really just no other way to get accurate data on who will win an election other than conducting an election! And so it's very hard to identify bad election data since all you have is the election data to compare it to. Thus it's very important to make sure you collect that election data meticulously.
