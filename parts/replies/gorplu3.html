    • frenchdipsandwiches

      Force fed is something different than exposure though. Your parents did you a HUGE disservice.

      I wish I had the source, but research has shown that kids can learn to love foods they hate or have aversions to simply by them being on the table at many meals. When my oldest was small, she went through this phase where she hated so many things. Someone recommended this program to me based out of the UK, it was sort of a sticker-type thing, where my kid would list the foods she didn’t like, and then we put a sticker each time she just TRIED it (but she didn’t have to). The research showed that in a low pressure environment, kids would gradually get used to smell, texture and taste after 10 times of just tasting it. The experiment worked - if she hated cheese, by the tenth time, she would say while she didn’t prefer it, it wasn’t worth rejecting a whole meal just because cheese was in it.

      Kids can model just what is in their surroundings in a low pressure way. I can now say, my kid is 16, and there’s nothing from FOOD anymore that she won’t EAT; simply because we refused to stop making meals or severely modifying meals just because she didn’t like this or that. Either she ate it, picked around it (ie if she didn’t like mushrooms) or she had toast instead.

      More valuable to me from the entire process is that she really trusts me. When we are travelling and go to a restaurant, she will ask me to pick something out and say “what would I like?” (say if it’s a ethnic restaurant where she doesn’t know the names of things) because she trusts I will pick the item that has the most things that she likes. It’s really been a give and take process, she tries things and I don’t force.
