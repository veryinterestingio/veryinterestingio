      It's more a time/benefit analysis. It's mostly quicker to restart than to diagnose and fix. It's not always errors necessarily either, the user might have put the system in a state that they don't understand and don't know how to get out of, in which case a restart is easier than doing the leg work trying to figure out how to return to the desired state.
