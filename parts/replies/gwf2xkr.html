    • mcs175

      Yes, the cylinder wall is typically more than heavy enough, for pressure reasons alone, to take a fall, but as others have said, it's the valve getting sheared off during the fall that poses a risk. The larger industrial sized cylinders have a heavy, threaded on valve cap that is to be left on whenever the cylinder is not in use. And if the valve comes off, it can become a projectile easily, but won't necessarily explode. Just having all that extra oxygen in the room with flammable materials just makes it way easier for a fire to start, and get out of control very quickly.
