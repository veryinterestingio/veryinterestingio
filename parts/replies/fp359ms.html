    Some rural areas of Oregon where I live are asking the state to let them end curbside recycling because China (the major buyer of our recyclables) has slowed their buying because of contamination, and that's after the most contaminated recycling goes to the landfill. So even much of what was being bought by China was contaminated too! But Chinese environmental regulations were lax enough to make it profitable. Now that China has (slightly) raised their standards, it seems like most household recycling is contaminated (and mostly by food which can't be solved by sorting the goods). We're a state with a long history of recycling where people take pride in doing it and we still can't do it right!
