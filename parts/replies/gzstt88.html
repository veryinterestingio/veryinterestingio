    • Sahaquiel_9

      One thing to note is to salt your food beforehand. If you’re using any liquids with salt in your dish like soy sauce, fish sauce, broth, or cooking wine, or any powder seasonings with salt, use less salt than you would normally. It’s important not to forgo salt while cooking, because when you add it to the meal beforehand, it gives the salt time to dissolve into the food evenly, which is best for taste. If you do it afterwards, you risk putting too much because the salt crystals aren’t fully dissolved, so you don’t taste all the salt you’re eating and it still tastes bland. But yeah, you have to learn your own tastes for putting salt into things, but that’s why you should taste as you go along. If you like things saltier than other people, be sure to put a little less than what you think tastes right. And remember not to put salt in multiple parts of your dish (for example, don’t have a sauce with cooking sherry AND a salted dry rub, do one or the other.). Seriously. That’s the main thing people do that makes things salty.
