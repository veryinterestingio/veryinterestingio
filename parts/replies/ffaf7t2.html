    • ooa3603

      For injuries occurring during physical exertion, pain is suppressed because of how much adrenaline is flowing through your blood. It's a hormone secreted during a flight or fight response induced by fear or surprise.

      Adrenaline or epinephrine, suppresses pain. This is an adaptation that is supposed to allow you to run away from what ever is causing the fear induced response. Then once you've gotten away adrenaline decreases, swelling increases and consequently the pain increases.

      It just so happens that it's also secreted during physical exercise. Presumably because evolutionarily speaking physical exertion may mean something bad is causing that need, so adrenaline is also secreted then too.

      In addition, the knees have relatively less synaptic connections, due to how many different types of non-circulatory tissue is crammed into such a small space. Fewer connections = less pain response.

      The body is not a perfect system, there are compromises everywhere. In fact most of evolutionary biology is an exhibition in tradeoffs.
