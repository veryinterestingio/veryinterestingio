    • EvanDaniel

      Micrograin propellants are just elemental Zn and S powders, with no other chemicals. The reaction produces the sulfide, not the sulphate. It's a messy and incomplete reaction, but that's the primary product. I'm sure there's plenty of afterburning in the air, and associated sulphate production, but that's not terribly relevant to the rocket motor operation.
