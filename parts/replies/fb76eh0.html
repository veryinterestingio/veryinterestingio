    • aaron4400

      Former flight attendant here. We're never going to ask everyone this. Also it's aircraft dependent. The overwing exit windows usually don't have window shades at all, for the reasons being mentioned in this post. Your other exits are the main entrance and usually another door on the opposite of the main entrance. Larger aircraft will sometimes have another set of doors in the aft.

      Some aircraft have a small window in those front doors. So it's not necessary for crew to run to the 1st row window to check for fire or debris. The CRJ700, which I worked on, has aircraft stairs as the door, so no window. We're trainer to go directly to the row 1 window in the event of an evacuation to check that the exit is safe. You don't want to walk straight in to burning fuel. We're trained to ask row 1 passengers to keep their shades open for take off and landing. It's inconsistently practiced. Some flight attendants I know just remove those shades all together, to avoid people acting weire at them.

      Some general advice to you as a passenger. Take and landing are by far the most dangerous times. Keep your shoes on and your shades up, and a coat ready if the weather is cold. It's rare but accidents happen.

      Tldr: it's aircraft specific. Some have better line of sight for flight attendants.
