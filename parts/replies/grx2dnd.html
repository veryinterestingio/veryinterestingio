    • tigermelon

      We're talking about two signals here. The "baseband" signal is what the radio operator hears. They absolutely control the volume (amplitude) and the the channel (frequency). The modulating signal is mixed with the baseband signal. This is "controlled" by the radio to reconstruct the baseband signal.

      AM: The radio is tuned by the operator to say 600kHz. It receives waves at that frequency. If the waves are low in amplitude, that corresponds to bass sounds. If they are higher in amplitude, higher pitched tones.

      FM: The radio is tuned to 98.1MHz. It receives waves at around that frequency. 98.10006 MHz corresponds to bass tones. 98.101 MHz would be a higher pitched tone. (It would actually straddle the modulating frequency, but you get the idea).

      A transmitting radio will do this "mixing" (modulating) and the receiving radio will do the demodulating automatically based on the operator-tuned frequency (channel).
