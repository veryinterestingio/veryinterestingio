    Best answer in the thread. Example: If you're running a machine shop you can buy a set of precision-machined "gage blocks", each of which is guaranteed to be, say, "x centimeters plus or minus y centimeters", and you can check your own measuring devices by measuring them.

    Those blocks came from a company that checked its own measuring devices against a much more expensive set, bought from someone who had yet a better set, and there's an unbroken chain of calibration up to NIST in the US, and then to other agencies in France and Germany which keep the master standards.

    So, basically, precision costs money: how much would you like?
