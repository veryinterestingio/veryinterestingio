    • Tricker126

      Microphones, like speakers are transducers. This means they turn sound into electric currents, or they other way around. Since they have to be made out of materials that allow them to move based on what sounds they hear or need to replicate, a lot of models end up sounding different. The material, housing, stand, environment and even the way it's recorded can affect the sound. Not only that but, speakers can completely sound different because of these factors. I'm sure you've heard good vs. bad speakers. Imagine that but the other way around. This is called the response curve, how much of a certain frequency the microphone picks up. This can affect the tone of anyone's voice, especially when it's a microphone that can't record below 150-200 Hz. This is the fundamentals of the human voice, or where our voice starts in the frequency range. That means all of the bass would be missing and you would just sound all nasally and annoying. This also occurs because the vibrations your voice makes in your throat can be heard through your bones and your brain perceives it as sound, so this basically doubles that effect. Hope that explains it.
