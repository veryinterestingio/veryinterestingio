    If you were to fall into the black hole (assuming you weren't ripped to shreds first), time would pass normally for you, and you would watch the universe shrink away behind you until you were surrounded by darkness and/or ceased to exist.

    Veering away from ELI5 here, but although this is true of general relativity it is seemingly incompatible with quantum mechanics. There is no generally accepted resolution to this discrepancy. One (controversial) proposal is that there is a seething mass of high-energy particles just at the event horizon. See https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Firewall_(physics). Matt Strassler has a more in-depth explanation of the paradox (still appropriate for the layman - ELI20 maybe) on his blog here.
