    Don't trout require oxygen-rich water? If the lake has no inlet/outlet, how would a population of trout survive over time?

    • gyrinophilus

      Montane lakes are fed mainly by snowmelt. So, they do have inlets that can flow heavily, there just aren't any fish above them naturally. Cold water also holds more oxygen and there is less decomposition occurring in clear (oligotrophic) waters, which consumes lots of oxygen in more nutrient-dense systems.

    • hupupmyhearties

      Gyrinophilus is correct. Other sources of oxygenation include eckman transport (wind-driven upwelling) and seasonal overturning.
