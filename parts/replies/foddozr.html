    • yukjo

      I don't think it's a good idea no, a healthy turtle in the wild keeps all that in check, it's a naturally pretty symbiotic relationship so I really wouldn't see a need for someone to be scrubbing a wild one. And yeah if you didn't know what you were doing, I'd worry you could cause some damage. Their shells do have sensation, so it could potentially hurt if you were scrubbing hard. When we rescue a turtle, we have to be really careful about whether or not we pick off barnacles; some are so well adhered that they could pull a scute off with them if you didn't play your cards right.

      And yes certainly, that's one of the benefits of a covering of algae and other things, it can be great camouflage to protect from predators, so generally, all things considered, best to leave it on. Unless like I said before, they're in rehabilitation programmes or otherwise out of their natural environment.
