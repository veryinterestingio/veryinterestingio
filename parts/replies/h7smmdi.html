    This is a big overstatement. Unlike smaller RNA viruses, coronaviruses like SARS-CoV-2 have a proofreading mechanism that slows down their mutation rate. You can find various differing estimates, but just to give an idea of the ballpark, this paper from November 2020 "informally estimated the mutation rate over our alignment as 9.8 × 10−4 substitutions per site per year". Since the virus genome is nearly 30,000 nucleotides long, that corresponds to only 30 changes over the course of a year.
