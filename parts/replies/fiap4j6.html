    This is a pretty good eli5, but I feel it could be simplified.

    1. Electronics depend on electricity going where we want it to go. Unless it's VERY pure water will act as a conductor causing it to go places it shouldn't. This can cause it to behave wrong, which can lead to the components getting overloaded and permanently breaking.
    2. Water will likely corrode the electronics. If it's not too bad it may still work, especially if an expert cleans it. If it's bad enough in order to fix it you usually need to replace it (again, you need an expert for this).
