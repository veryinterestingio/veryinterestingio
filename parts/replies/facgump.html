      • pike184

        Its not how water molecules are interacting with the non-polar substance to dissolve it in the supercritical phase, it is how they are not interacting with each other. Since they cannot interact with the nonpolar substance, they interact more efficiently with each other becoming more orderly ( -ds ) which nature doesn't like. The number of molecules made more orderly is reduced by forming micelles, or a new surface dictated by density.

    • tungstenEEboron

      The molecules the water is made up of are definitely still polar. Technically things that are often called non-polar such as hydrocarbons are slightly polar. The reason they are called non-polar is because they are only very slightly polar. Water doesn't normally dissolve non-polar things well because the water molecules are attracted less to the non-polar molecules than the other water molecules. In the case of supercritical water there is no longer as strong of a bond between water molecules. This allows for non-polar molecules to dissolve in much more easily.
