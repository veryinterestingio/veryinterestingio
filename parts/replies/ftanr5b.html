    is it just the actual protons and neutrons and they are stuck together and thats what it is, with no "container"

    Yes, the nucleons (collective term for protons and neutrons) are bonded together with the Strong Nuclear Force, the strongest of the 4 Fundamental Forces in our Universe. The more neutrons there are, the more unstable the nucleus is - which is why you might have heard of the phrase "every element wants to be iron", which is applicable during nucleosynthesis. The instability means that the nucleus is in a collapsed state (so in that case the shape might be a bit wonky) as it gives off alpha radiation which is helium nuclei, lowering the "mass" of the nucleus, changing its element of course.
