    • Vanniv_iv

      Your sound recording is effectively all of the different waves added together. They each overlap, they interfere with each other, they complement each other.

      Think about you sitting in a concert hall listening to a performance. Sound waves come from every direction, but you only have two ears. Your hearing is effectively what the combined waves look like at each of your two eardrums.

      Your speakers are doing the same thing, in reverse, playing back two different sets of "all the sounds combined at a certain spot"

      And there's this concept in recording called "binaural sound", where they actually do recording by placing two microphones in a position designed to precisely mimic the human ears. If you play back such a sound recording on some good in-ear headphones, the sound should be just as directional, dynamic, and "surround" as if you'd been in the original recording studio hearing it live.
