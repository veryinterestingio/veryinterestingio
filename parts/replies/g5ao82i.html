    • SeniorResearcher3

      There are many types of symbiosis. This is arguably an example of mutualism (both host and virus benefit from the symbiotic relationship).

      You could also make an argument that this is an example of parasitism (virus benefits, host is harmed) if the yeast is made less competitive by being used as a factory, or commensalism (virus benefits, host is neither harmed nor enriched) if the stress from being used as a factory "cancels out" the advantage of having the competition removed and leaves the yeast neither better nor worse off for being infected.
