    • FireMoose

      No, a star is created by hydrostatic equilibrium. That is when the forces pushing outwards balance those pushing inward. In stars like the Sun, gravity pulls inward while the energy from fusion in the core is what pushes outward as radiation pressure. In white dwarfs, the force pushing outward is electron degeneracy pressure and in neutron stars it is neutron degeneracy pressure. A black star would be a neutron star or white dwarf that has cooled enough to no longer emit light. A black hole forms when gravity over powers even neutron degeneracy pressure and there is no force to prevent the star from collapsing further.

      Edit: The universe is not old enough for any white dwarfs or neutron stars to have cooled enough to be 'black stars'. So right now, they are purely theoretical.
