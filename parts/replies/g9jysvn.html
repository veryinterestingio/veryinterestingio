    The answer that I learned in biology 101 to this is that the surface are a to volume ratio makes it problematic for cells to get too large.

    https://www.reference.com/science/limits-cell-size-ba9023807e9f60b8

    For a sphere, the volume increases proportionally to r^(3) but the surface area only increases proportionally to r^(2). That means that as it grows larger, it as much more stuff inside to maintain relative to the area touching the outside through which it can gather nutrients. This is not an insurmountable problem, but it'd require significant adaptation to address.
