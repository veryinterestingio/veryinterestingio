    Ascites. Fluid accumulates in the peritoneal cavity due to portal hypertension caused by liver failure. That’s also why « beer gut » feels tight like a drum and not flabby like fat.

    Visceral fat accumulation is also a real thing and quite possibly an issue for many of these patients too.

    But the beer gut specifically is ascites. Fun fact, we confirm this in the field with a “fluid shift test” that is basically starting a wave on one side of the abdomen and watching it ripple to the opposite side

    Source: am a paramedic. This is a daily occurrence.
