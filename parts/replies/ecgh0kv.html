    Suing is how you get their attention. Nobody said it has to be successful. Most successful lawsuits don't even make it to trial; they settle out of court.

    In some cases, the lawsuit, in and of itself, is only meant to grab headlines. Once the initial papers have been filed at the courthouse, a headline stating "Google has been Sued for YouTube Drama" is no longer false. This is why most news articles with headlines similar to this can't get any comment from the company being sued -- it was filed earlier that day and the company probably hasn't even received the official notice and summons yet.

    It would cost me around $500-$1000 to get a headline in a major newspaper that Reddit is being sued for slandering and libeling Russians as a whole. The lawsuit would be immediately dismissed and my attorney could be sanctioned for wasting the court's time, but the Los Angeles Times would have a fun headline to print.
