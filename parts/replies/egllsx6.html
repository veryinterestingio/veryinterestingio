    The ester bond in a triglyceride is with the fatty acid and the glycerol backbone. The trans fat refers to the orientation of the double bond in the actual fatty acid. To make a fat into an oil, you need to reduce the melting temperature by having unsaturated fatty acids make up a higher % of triglycerides. This can be done without the inclusion of trans fatty acids. Simply put, it doesn’t mean that there are trans fatty acids added during the interesterification process.
