    To emphasize why we know why the math works: mathematical rules hold up in real life tests that can be predicted and measured. Whether this is working out problems with different variables and getting consistent answers, performing physical experiments, or producing algorithms that produce the solutions that are needed consistently.

    As you said, the numbers and symbols and equations are just abstract, often arbitrary, methods of communicating these concepts.

    A good example of the arbitrariness of some of the depictions of scientific concepts is the negative charge of the electron. Many people in STEM agree that it would have been much easier to navigate the equations if electrons were considered positive instead. It was just arbitrarily assigned a value.
