    • limahaurix

      The Starlink satellites are not in geostationary orbit, they are at a much, much lower orbit. This means in most circumstances the round-trip time via Starlink will be far lower. 350km orbit vs. 35,000km geostationary.

      Instead of having a large diameter cone from high orbit to project coverage over a wide area, the Starlink satellites are set up in a loosely spread "net" over the coverage zone so there will always be multiple in connection range.

    • xQx1

      Starlink uses low Earth orbit satellites, instead of geostationary orbit satellites.

      This means they're much closer to earth, and thus solve the problems with latency with satellite internet.

      The advantage of geostationary satellites is that they stay in the same position in the sky, so you can point a (fixed) satellite dish at them. Low Earth Orbit satellites go whizzing past in the sky - so you either need a lot of them in a long line (which is what Elon Musk is doing), or you need really expensive satellite dishes which move with the satellite.
