    In the cases you mentioned it's used to open up the tube that connects the inner ear to your nose/mouth/sinuses. Because that channel is normally sealed the pressure inside the inner ear cannot rise if the outside pressure rises (e.g. under water). That's why you need to open it up once under water and you're good until you dive deeper. If you go back up the pressure inside your inner ear will be higher then the outside pressure but that's not such s big deal because the tube inside your ear can open by itself if the pressure inside is high.

    Now if we have snot and other junk in our sinuses we can get relief by increasing the pressure. Sometimes it makes it even worse though. Instead of 'pushing' while you close off your nose you can also "suck" air in and decrease the pressure inside your sinuses. This can sometimes help too.
