    • whyisthesky

      Quantum mechanics is used to describe single subatomic particles, but it applies at all scales. For many larger objects the quantum mechanical description is pretty much the same as the classical description but this is not always the case.

      CPU transistors work by manipulating the properties of energy levels of electrons at junctions between different semiconductors, this is 100% a quantum mechanical effect and it cannot be explained purely by classical physics. The effect of phosphorus/boron dopants on silicon is part of a field called condensed matter, which at its core is quantum mechanics.

      Quantum Mechanics tells you how electrons will behave in molecules. Before Quantum Mechanics, we didn't really have any theory to help predict that. This allowed chemists to predict the properties of compounds before making them, and so start to create compounds with the desired properties on purpose, rather than by blind trial and error.
