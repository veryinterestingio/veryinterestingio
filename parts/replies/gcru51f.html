    • kscholten

      I regularly perform peer review for a few journals. I will read the article that is being published, perhaps some of their older articles, and I might re-perform some of their analyses, but I won't recreate any of their experiments or scrutinize the nitty gritty data. Here is what I look for:

      • Are the results new and worth publishing?
      • Is the experimental methedology sound?
      • Do their results support their claims?
      • Are their results plausible?
      • Do their results contradict previous work, and if so is there a plausible justification?
      • Is the presentation clear to the reader?

      And that's about it. It's a much shorter, much more basic review than the type of intense, high-level review involved in FDA approval.
