    • tashkiira

      simple is friggin' HARD, man. there's miles of gap between easy and simple.

      Easy means it takes little time to understand.

      Simple means that when you learn it, it covers everything.

      easy: Pythagorean theorem. "If you have a right triangle, and attach squares to each side so that the side of the triangle is a side of the square exactly, the long side's square has the same area as the other two squares together." Requires a right triangle, a Euclidean plane (you don't even learn other surfaces are different until much later), and, possibly, a lot of paper or other flat stock to measure out the squares on.

      simple: the law of cosines. "For any triangle of sides a, b, and c, on a Euclidean plane, c^2 = a^2 +b^2 -2ab * cos Θ , where Θ is the angle between sides a and b." This covers any triangle in a euclidean space of dimension 2 or greater (the concept of triangle in a euclidean 1-space is undefined, since a 1-space is just a line; also, I'm not certain if fractional dimensional spaces can be euclidean, so I'm not including those just in case).

      The Pythagorean theorem is literally just an easy special case of the law of cosines where Θ is π/2 radians, or 90^o because then the cosine term is 0 and it collapses away. Good luck explaining that to a 3rd grader though, unless the kid's a lot brighter and more highly taught than I was.
