    It has long been observed that in the human visual system, at least a part of the visual processing, such as identifying lines and shapes in the visual field, are performed not in the brain, but instead in the optic nerve paths on its way to the visual center in the brain. This "pre-processing" seems like it is where camouflage affects vision. It's a fair question to ask whether that feature of visual processing is highly conserved across species? ie. do deer, elk, etc also process their visual fields the same way? What about other non-herbivore game species like Bear, Wolves, Cougar. Also, what about arboreal animals like Squirrels vs ground dwellers like wood chucks?
