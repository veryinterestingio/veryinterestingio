    Designing and situating a shelter to allow a thermometer to accurately measure the air temperature (rather than measuring local effects caused by the shelter) is significantly more complicated than just calibrating the thermometer.

    Weather Underground has an extensive article examining how much credence we can give the 134F record temperature from 1913. (The short of it: not much credence. Largely because the weather observer at Greenland Ranch in 1913 may have been incompetent.)
