    Pure exponential growth is only valid when the disease first begins spreading. For most diseases people who have recovered from the disease are no longer susceptible (at least for a while). The SIR model takes this into account.

    Importantly, the SIR model predicts that even for diseases with a very high R0 and no external barriers to disease spreads, there will always be a population of people who never get the disease purely through chance.

    Here is a webpage that lets you calculate a simple SIR model. On this website, R0 = (beta / gamma). If you use the parameters beta=3, gamma = 1 you will find the model predicts \~5% of people are never infected.

    Of course, external factors such as quarantines can also affect the effective R0.
