      Babies generally can't develop antibodies to diseases they encounter when they are first born, which is why maternal antibodies play such a big role in protecting them from disease at that stage. After a few months, babies can develop short-term antibody responses, but long-term memory cells still don't form. This is why many childhood vaccines are given multiple boosters during infancy - to keep antibody production going until long-term immunity kicks in.
