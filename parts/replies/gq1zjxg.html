    • NowImAllSet

      Black pepper also interacts with TRPV1 receptors, but due to a different chemical called piperine, instead of capsaicin. Sichuan peppers have the same fundamental science behind them, but a completely different chemical, receptor and sensation. The molecule is called hydroxy-alpha sanshool and it interacts with somatosensory neurons. Where TRPV1 receptors are associated with pain, somatosensory neurons are associated with "gentle touch" feelings, and blocks them from activating which causes an effect similar to local anesthetics, i.e. numbing sensation.
