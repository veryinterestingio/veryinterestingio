      • YoYoChamps

        Most of Earth's water, maybe 90%+, is actually in Earth's mantle. There could be a lot of water inside Mars that is just locked up because its core has now solidified, trapping it there. The water on the surface either froze, got stripped off by solar activity, or got blasted out by asteroid/comet impacts.

        Gravity is everything when it comes to keeping an atmosphere, followed by a magnetic field (which Mars lost because it's too small), followed by getting continuous new outgassing from the planet below.
