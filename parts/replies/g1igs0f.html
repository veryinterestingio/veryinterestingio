    Is it realistic to actually only Target "pain" nerves and isolate them completely? Do "aches and pains" nerves differ in any measurable way from, for example, "paper cut" nerves? If you were to Target " pain" nerves would you likely include both?

    • _floop_the_pig

      They absolutely do!

      We have lots of different types of nerves. Some are insulated with a fatty sheath along their whole length, called myelin. These conduct signals quicker than those nerve types that lack the myelin sheath.

      With pain nerves, the fast myelinated nerves are called Adelta nerves. These are responsible for the fast, sharp feeling of pain, like a paper cut Useful to tell the body to quicky move your hand out of the flame, for example. Then we have C type pain nerves. These are unmyelinated, and are responsible for the dull aching type of pain that comes on later, and is useful to remind you to look after that damaged body part.
