  • Whenthetimeisnigh

    Fun fact. In the navy there is a saying to "warm the bell" meaning basically do something, or show up too early.

    In the olden days sailors would ring a bell every half-hour (or every flip of the hourglass) to mark the time.

    Sailors, usually at night, would often keep the hourglass in their coats or hands to warm it up, with the thinking that it would expand the glass throat and make the sand flow faster, bringing their watch to an early end.
