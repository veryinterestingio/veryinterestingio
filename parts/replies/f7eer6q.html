    • marr75

      I don't believe this is possible with any known examples of rock and gas and at the same time, it's not really very interesting when the frame for the question was Saturn's Rings (orbital systems). "Couldn't you have X floating (held up by normal forces) on top of Y?" is more of a geology question and the only answer is "the layers will tend to organize densest to least dense". This is an extremely different kind of arrangement than a ring system and they wouldn't really even co-exist in any interesting way (the rock, fluids, and gases that were "floating around on top of" one another could never be anywhere near any kind of ring system, it would cease to exist rapidly and just become part of the body).
