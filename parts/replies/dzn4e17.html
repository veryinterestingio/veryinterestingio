    • CheckMyReality

      I am an emergency dispatcher. When I answer a 911 call, it only takes a couple seconds at most for the Phase 1, which is the tower location, to come up. I then refresh the ALI information until I can get a Phase 2, which is an approximate location of the caller. Sometimes Phase 2 will come up immediately and sometimes not at all. On average it takes less than a minute, normally 15 seconds or so. There are varying factors that play into Phase 2 accuracy, such as weather and connection strength. It’s usually harder to get a Phase 2 location on a cell phone that’s not connected to a carrier service, or when the caller disconnects before a Phase 2 can register. Generally speaking tho, Phase 2 locations are accurate within about half a mile. It’s not great but it is usually closer than that.
