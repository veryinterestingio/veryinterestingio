    • CraftySwinePhD

      You are sort of right. Capsaicin binds TRPV1, which detects dangerous heat. But TRPV1 also binds many endogenous molecules. Some of these are produced when tissue is damaged or burned and signal inflammation. Plus, TRPV1 is regulated by many inflammation pathways and will release inflammatory signals as well. So basically, when these inflammatory molecules are produced, they sensitize and/or activate TRPV1 to produce pain, allowing you to know something is wrong, as well and amplifying the inflammation signal. Fun fact: many endocannabinoids also bind TRPV1 and regulate its activity.
