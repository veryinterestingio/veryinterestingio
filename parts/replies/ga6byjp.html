    • half3clipse

      The point there is that over arbitrary time and space scales, the likelihood of interaction is the same.

      ie if you have two photons one meter apart, it'll take some amount of time for them to likely interact. If you take those two photons a light years apart, then after some (far larger) time, they'll have the exact same likelihood of having interacted. The behavior is exactly the same, and the only way to distinguish the two cases is by the timescale.

      The idea is that if there is no clock there is no timescale and, the behavior of the universe is scale invariant: There's no way to distinguish an infinitely large and uniform universe from an infinitely small and uniform one. Presupposing that an infinitely and uniform small universe is the condition for a big bang to occur, the conditions at the end of an aeon are the exactly same as at the beginning of one. That process can then repeat and scale appears again the same way it did in our aeon.
