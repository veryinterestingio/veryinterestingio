    • TooBusyToLive

      Basically like others have said a blackhead is a pore filled with material clogging it. That solid stuff is the clog. I had talked about a pimple being a clog with some infection behind it. What we call a blackhead isn’t quite there yet and doesn’t have the infection/pus behind it yet. What some people call a “whitehead” is similar. Little blocked pore that may have some of the oil from the blocked gland behind it but doesn’t have the infection aspect, so it’s not red and swollen and painful yet. Sometimes these will develop into a big inflamed pimple with pus and sometimes not. It just depends on your skin biology etc.

      Some people do colloquially refer to blackhead/whitehead as pimples. It’s an area where medical terms and lay terms don’t always line up, and even among what we would call pimples there are different types medically, but again this is ELI5. In general when I’m using lay terms I tend to call blackheads and whiteheads as “clogged pores” which could predispose to pimples depending on the person, and define pimples as “there is enough stuff accumulated behind the blockage that you could pop it”. Again, that’s not a medical definition but it fits with how most people look at those terms
