        Using the two most common painkillers, Ibuprofen and Paracetamol. Ibuprofen works as the site of pain as a Non Steroid Anti-Inflammatory Drug or NSAID. It’s good for muscle pain or toothache as it works at the site of the pain and is certainly my preferred option.

        Paracetamol works at the pain receptors in your brain so you still hurt, but your brain doesn’t know it. Kind of.

        Because they work differently you can alternate them for really bad pain as long as you leave four hours between each dose of the same painkiller. And always read the label!
