    • Farnsworthson

      Not so much the ball as your body. The slide kills your momentum in a controlled way.

      The "normal" way of bowling, you walk forward as you swing the ball, ending on one foot; the ball mostly just swings on your arm. When you do it right it makes for a very smooth delivery. But that walking gives you forward momentum - you can't just stop dead. The slide kills your momentum under control.

      If the shoe suddenly grips too much instead (which sometimes happens the first couple of times you approach the line, if they haven't been worn for a while, or even you just aren't warmed up and your technique is off) - you'll be lucky if (a) you stay on your feet, and (b) the ball stays out of the gutter. Sneakers would have the same effect.

      (Only one foot actually slides; if you buy your own shoes, you'll only have a slide sole on that one.)
