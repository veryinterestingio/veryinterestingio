      • FreeEdgar_2013

        I can't speak for Europe, but in Canada we don't vote for everything at once. There are typically 3 election cycles:

        Federal: 1 ballot, 1 vote. You only vote your local representative (the prime minister is the current leader of the party that won the most representatives). Must be at least once every 4 years, but can be called for sooner (e.g. if a budget bill fails that auto triggers an election)

        Provincial: Same system as federal, just at the provincial level. (Instead of Prime Minister the leader is called the Premier). Typically every 4 years, but vary which year/ when from province to province.

        Municipal: Can vary but most commonly 2-3 votes: Mayor, ward representative, and often school board trustee. Mostly every 4 years, with some exceptions in the Territories. In most provinces they're either on opposite rotation of the provincial election or the same year, but at a different time than the provincial election

        Additionally it's rare to have any extra measures voted on, I can only remember 1 time and it was over 10 year ago

        tl;dr In Canada there are different election cycles for the different jurisdictions, at different times, and they're much simpler.
