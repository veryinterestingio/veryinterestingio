    We don't exactly know but the biggest leads come from studies around memory and dementia. REM sleep & dreaming have been correlated with memory processing. These activities seem to assist the brain with transferring the day's experiences from short term to longer term memory storage. There is also mounting evidence that being chronically under-slept may be a major driver of memory degradation and dementia later in life.

    I started learning about this because I've had trouble with my sleep for most of my adult life and my experiences point to similar areas. In my case I still slept a normal amount but with none of the restorative aspects. Unsurprisingly I lost the energy to do much during the day, along with my quick wit. My memory for things that had happened to me became hugely worse as that bridge between short and long term memory eroded. I began to stumble over my words because I couldn't think at the speed of speech and would often have periods where I would be unable to think of anything at all hard, as I tried. I almost completely stopped dreaming for years.

    Also surprising was that I saw little results for years as doctors tried treating my lack of sleep directly. Nothing worked until we started treating my cognitive issues. Then I saw my sleep also start to return to normal. Dreams returned, energy levels rose, memory started to improve. Hope that helps illuminate a bit.

    I’m reading a book by Matthew Walker called Why We Sleep. You should if you are interested in answering this question. My take on his take: we need sleep because there’s a finite amount of storage in the brain. During NREM, the brain prune’s unnecessary memories. A consolidation period. During REM, we create connections and make sense of the old and new memories to reinforce them.
