    • kdawg0707

      Yes, that is the exact concept behind plasma donation as treatment. It is called active immunization, has been shown to be very effective against other viruses, and early results for COVID are promising.

      It does last, the reason being that the antibody complex that forms has a dual purpose of keeping the virus in check, as well as jump starting the production of your own antibodies.

      The process of coding for the new antibodies is completely separate from developing them, so in theory you are cutting out the main source of lag time that typically occurs with a new exposure.
