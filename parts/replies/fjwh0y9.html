      I'll over simplify. Instead of doing a big printing press, let's print on a printer at home (or use your office's laser printer) Let's just make a 4 page "signature"... Say you were printing a book by printing on a normal letter size piece of paper, That's a bit big for a book, so what you do is you set up the text and the printer to print 2 pages on each side of the paper. This makes the printing go quicker and doesn't waste paper. So you lay out the pages so that when you fold each sheet in half and sew (or since we're going DIY, maybe we staple them into the book). But that means we're always going to have a number of pages divisible by 4. If you need to make a book that is 61 pages, you need to have 64 total because the last signature only has one printed page and 3 blank pages.

      Higher production printing will actually be even more complex and have 8 or 16 page signatures where they cut and fold so you end up with 2 or 4 folded sheets within eachother, and each folded sheet has 4 pages.
