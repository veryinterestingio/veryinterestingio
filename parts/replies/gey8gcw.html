    • rndrn

      Yes, but not necessarily the same way as a human. For example, double glazing will isolate almost entirely from conduction, but much less from radiation. Whereas an aluminium sheet will insulate a lot from radiation, but much less from conduction.

      Usually, thermostats are shaded from IR by a perforated casing, that will stop radiation and will let air pass, so they will mostly pick air temperature and disregard the IR exchange.

      • uwauwa

        look at the equation for radiant heat transfer and get back to me.., Actually I'll provide it. In simplified form Q=hA(T1 - T2), where h is the "coefficient of radiant heat transfer" (which is made up of a whole bunch of other factors related to materials and geometry), A is the surface area involved, and T1 - T2 is the temperatures of the 2 objects. T1 - T2 will either be a positive or a negative number if the temps are different and thus the heat transfer will either be positive (rejection of heat from the T1 body) or negative (absorption of heat to the T1 body). Thermal radiation only occurs in one direction, from hotter to colder.
