    Glass is denser than plastic

    Since this is such a common misconception, I'll put this here, even though you are probably oversimplying for the sake of ELI5.

    Density has very little to do with heat capacity. Iron, for instance, has about a quarter the (volumetric) heat capacity of water but is far denser. I chose iron specifically because it has a very high heat capacity among all metals. (I think, if I remember correctly, that it's the highest of all metals, but that might be mercury.) Among similar materials, the denser material will have higher heat capacity, but plastic and glass are very different materials with respect to heat storage.
