      In microgravity plants germinate in random directions and need to be trained towards a light. They have no sense of up or down, fluids don't drain so good. It's hydroponics and sealed bags, and that can be sickly.

      One thing that'll be interesting to see is if these low gravities are enough to provide that sense of direction. It might be that there's a straight-line in the intensity of effects; it might be that one or both are as bad as microgravity; it'd be really nice if everything worked at even lunar G.

      And they may have a neat swirl from growing through a small simulated gravity field - coriolis veggies, curling antispinward as they get taller.

      Edit to add: Great big spherical bobble-matoes would be fun. :)
