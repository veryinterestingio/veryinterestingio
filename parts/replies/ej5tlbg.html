    So what causes our voices to change so much as we age. I know people who when they were in their 20s sounded way different than in their 30-40s and obviously once we get 60+ we sound like dick.

    • Zuribrix

      As you age, your vocal cords change in size and thickness, all through your life. Around puberty is the biggest change, when an influx of hormones causes the entire laryngeal space (the voice box basically) to change shape quite drastically. Everything gets bigger and this has an effect similar to the difference between a string on a violin vs. a string on an upright bass - when they have the same tension on them, the upright bass produces a lower sound than the violin because more of the string is vibrating.

      Around 30 is when your voice finally settles from puberty. Yes, it takes this long; the majority of the settling happens in the first few years but your voice never officially finishes growing and settling until 30, which is why you even sound different at 22 vs. 35, for example. The body basically has to re-learn how to use the autonomous muscles which control the larynx after their growth spurt, and that takes a lot of time.

      Around 50 is when another voice change occurs, but this is primarily due to degradation instead. The body‚Äôs mucus production doesn‚Äôt work as well, and can over or under-produce lubricants that help the vocal folds slide smoothly past one another. This and a few other random things that can occur (differences in shape of the pharyngeal cavity, or the back of your mouth, primarily) change the overall sound of your voice.
