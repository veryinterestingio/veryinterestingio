      • parlez-vous

        Using an air conditioner, the air coming out of it will be less humid (have less water vapour per unit volume) than the air already in the room. This decreases the rooms relative humidity, decreasing how "hot" it feels. Since water is a better conductor of heat than air, and since humid air has more water in it per unit of volume than non-humid air, the humid air feels warmer than the drier air.

      • Wisdomfighter

        Water carries heat better than air. You can put your hand without danger in a hot oven (200°C), but I wouldn't dare you to plunge your hand in boiling water (100°C). Thus moist air feels hotter when hot and colder when cold. Furthermore, sweating doesn't work as well with high air humidity, which makes warm air feel even warmer.
