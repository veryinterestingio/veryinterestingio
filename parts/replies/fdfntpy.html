    • DavidRFZ

      Yes, the old name of Réunion is "Île Bourbon".

      "Bourbon vanilla" is basically the same as Madagascar vanilla. (Réunion is just off the coast of Madagascar).

      Mexican vanilla is the same plant but the beans are deactivated by leaving them out in the sun. This sun-drying gives the beans a slightly woodier flavor.

      Mexico is where vanilla originated (it is a New World spice), but the Madagascar area now greatly outpaces it in production.

      Tahitian vanilla uses beans from a different subspecies. They create a sweeter and more floral vanilla. It's most commonly used in uncooked desserts.
