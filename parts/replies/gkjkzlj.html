      All three have resource usage problems. Lots of energy to making all the steel etc for a nuclear plant, but longevity plays that out.

      Wind and solar require a LOT more maintenance, and consume large amounts of plastics and oil in fabrication, and continued operations. They're not nearly as carbon free as you'd think.

      It boils down to the same basics as always. What it takes as initial investment and continued operation, vs longevity to balance the costs out. Generally put, even old tech nuclear still ends up better for the environment. The initial costs are just the highest due to the size. Europe however has a great many newer plants that are more advanced and working towards smaller and smaller, mitigating these issues, especially compared to power density
