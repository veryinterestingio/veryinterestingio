    • eljefino

      Digital satellite video uses something called Forward Error Correction which is essentially resending redundant parity bits. If they encode more FEC you get a more reliable signal and can get a picture with a worse S/N ratio, but it cuts into the available bandwidth that they profit from.

      • Dalebssr

        You can sacrifice Eb/No for better reliability if you increase the gain of the down converter. Most carriers drop the gain to nothing so they can enjoy a >16 Eb/No that isn't needed. 7 or higher will pass packets no problem AND protect your downlink from any potential hits by leveraging the gain.
