    • nathanielKay

      It's a combination of how good the lines are coming to your house, and how much you're willing to pay. For speed, telco<cable<fibre, but the difference between telco (DSL) and cable is small enough that the line condition is the determining factor.

      Good DSL can outperform crappy coax cable, but good cable will outperform good DSL. Fiber leaves both of those far, far behind.

      Assuming your lines are good, the speed of your connection is determined by the software on the ISP side, which caps your connection at a maximum rate. As the client, there are very few (usually none) workarounds, but if you work for the ISP you might find something.

      Had a buddy once that created a 'testing profile' on the backend that set the speed limit to max attainable, and then set his home connection to that profile 24/7. He didnt even get in trouble when folks found out- it was an extremely useful profile for client testing, and became a standard in the test kit. Got a memo telling everyone to never ever do that, but that was it. Worked for the better part of a year, iirc.
