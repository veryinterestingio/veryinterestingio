  • hackometer

    It's not about the engine running slow; actually the engine's point of highest efficiency is at quite low RPM.

    The main point is running at low output torque. Just to run, the engine must overcome a fixed amount of torque (to a first approximation) coming from internal friction. If you demand an output torque that is similar to that, you're wasting a lot of energy.

    This is why automatic transmission chooses high gears when you don't accelerate hard: the lower the gear, the higher the torque output to produce the needed power output.

    One way to improve the efficiency of an ICE (not used in cars, but used in diesel-electric), is to optimize its engineering towards a very narrow band of RPMs and power outputs and let it drive the electric generator within that regime all the time, producing fixed energy output that gets stored in the battery and delivered as needed.
