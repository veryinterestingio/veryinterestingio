    Not “typically.” US bonds are the gold standard (figuratively, I mean) in debt.

    Every financial formula banks around the globe use that involved calculating a “risk free rate” is whatever long-term US Treasury bonds are on the date of calculation.

    The only hiccup to this “risk free” status was when Congressional brinksmanship over raising the debt ceiling a few years ago caused Moody’s, one of the big three debt rating agencies, to downgrade US debt to essentially “almost risk free.”
