    I mean no ill will, but no. Your explanation is incorrect.

    The eardrum is a thin layer of tissue that is moved by the variation in air pressure that is “sound”. In order for the eardrum to function under variable conditions (at sea level, the top of a mountain, during good and bad weather) there needs to be a way to equalize the pressure of the space inside your eardrum with the ambient environmental pressure. The structure that does that is the Eustachian tube. The “pop” is the pressure being equalized by the Eustachian tube and your eardrum moving back to a neutral position where it is not being push in or out by a pressure differential. This can be related to congestion because the opening of the Eustachian tube can become occluded when a person has copious secretions, throwing off the normal pressure equalization that occurs when you chew, yawn, etc.
