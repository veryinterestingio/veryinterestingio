    people have done studies where they have subjects sleep in a room without a clock or natural lighting, and then say how long they think were asleep for.

    It appears that your brain keeps tracking using sleep cycles - you wake up and your brain says alright you completed 5 cycles, so that should have been about 8 hours.

    People that had interrupted sleep - say they did 3/4 of a cycle, woke up and then went back to sleep for another 2 - thought they had woken up like 5 minutes into their 3 hour sleep, when it was actually 1 hour into a 4 hour sleep.

    surgical amnesia doesn't put you in proper sleep cycles, so your brain says 0 cycles, 0 time. Similar things can happen with people taking sleeping pills where their sleep clock will be short and hour or two, or if you have bad sleep apnea or similar where you never actually get all the way to REM sleep it's also difficult to keep track of time passing.

    Nap disorientation comes from a similar effect - bright light, especially natural light, can the body from falling all the way asleep, which is why people are so often time disoriented from daytime naps.
