    • CasualPlebGamer

      Shaving doesn't make your hair thicker, but it can appear to due to:

      • When people first start shaving, it's usually during puberty when your body is rapidly changing, you might shave, then see the next wave of hair is much thicker than before. This is simply because you can get spurts of new hair growth during puberty, and is only a correlation with the fact you started shaving.

      • Hair tips are tapered to some degree, and you cut off the thinner tips when shaving, so it can make it look a bit thicker than before when they first grow back, since now the tips are all thicker hairs with a clean shaving cut, rather than a tapered end. Eventually that effect will go away if you stop shaving.
