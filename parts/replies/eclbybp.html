    Am also a geneticist and vouch for this poster's answer. The specific test for determining relatedness between individuals is called Identity By Descent, which looks at the fraction of shared polymorphisms between people. We use this test in our own lab to verify that the families we study are related the way they state they are, very important when you're looking at inheritance patterns for a genetic feature. And I will tell you...the data doesn't lie. Some days figuring out the real pedigree is like an episode of Maury.

    23&Me, while they are direct to consumer and there is a lot of mistrust for how some larger companies have handled private data, have also been making actual research contributions from their dataset. And not just funsie genetics, they've done studies on real medical topics like autism and infertility too. That's nothing to sneer at!
