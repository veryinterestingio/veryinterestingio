      You are correct. I glossed over that aspect, but I did say “so [your body will] stop digestion”, which is at least partially due to redirecting the blood supply. The “fight or flight” response also causes many other bodily processes to be temporarily suspended, such as hair growth, gamete (sperm / egg) production, and just about everything else related to body maintenance. Essentially your body says, “the kitchen is on fire! Let’s focus on putting that out. For now we can skip vacuuming the living room and repairing the leaky faucet in the bathroom”. Note that this response is intended as a crude one-size-fits-most emergency measure, and as such, the details often won’t make sense in a specific situation. This is part of why people who will work in stressful environments undergo extensive training: to give those people more tools to respond, rather than a single hammer with witch to nail down all problems.

      The “fight or flight” response is intended to be used for infrequent, short-term emergencies, and leaving it active for an extended period (longer than 15 minutes or so), or using it frequently, means that your body’s maintenance continues to remain suspended / drastically reduced. When the body experiences stress for an extended period of time, that neglected maintenance causes bad things to happen (suspending hair growth can cause hair loss, suspending skin maintenance can cause premature wrinkling, suspending gamete production can cause low libido, suspending maintenance on your immune system reduces your immune capacity and can result in illness, etc.).
