  • Fruity_Pineapple

    When people talk about automated trains, it's not about reducing costs by employing less drivers.

    It's about increasing fluidity. By being able to automate driving you can launch more trains with less safety distance. Instead of having 1 train with 16 wagons departing every hours, we could have 8 trains each with 2 wagons departing every 7 minutes. We could have better service in rural areas where we have currently 1 or 2 trains a day.

    And the answer is it's a good idea but when energy will be more expensive, not now. The only advantage of trains over buses, planes, is they use less energy. But energy is cheap now. Trains use a lot of R&D, and maintenance and that's expensive; we'd need to develop new cheap engines, produce a lot of them, maintain them. That's more expensive than the cost of the energy we'd save.
