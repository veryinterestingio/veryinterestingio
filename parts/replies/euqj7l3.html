    Sound in air dissipates more quickly than light. Additionally, the further sound travels, the more it's reflected, so the less of a "quick clap" and more of a "long rumble" that you get.

    You can see the light from a strike from around 100-120 miles away, but the sound of thunder only carries 10-15 miles. Sound travels about 1 mile every 5 seconds, so at those distances, the sound comes around a minute after the light (if you can even hear it).

    Basically, a big strike 20 miles away and a small strike 1 mile away can look identical, but, will sound drastically different.
