      Think about it this way.

      If you have a shoebox with stacks of quarters, nickels, dimes, and pennies, and then you shake it up, you would expect it to become a mix. You could keep shaking it, but it won't ever not be a mix.

      This shows how particles prefer to be mixed when there's nothing stopping them from being mixed. It's a result of entropy.

      You could introduce some kind of magnetic field and some coins would separate out to one side while the other coins wouldn't move. That's kind of like oil and water; there's some kind of interaction that prevents everything from staying mixed.

      So unless there's a reason it shouldn't mix, you'd expect things to stay mixed.

  • Forkrul

    To get the clumps of fat near to each other and start combining back into bigger clumps is a higher energy state, and in general, things want to stay at the lowest energy. It basically takes more energy to get the water (like little sticky magnets) out of the way that's keeping the fat separate.

    And even then you have to open up the micelles (little balls of fatty acids) so that the hydrophilic (lit. loves water) heads get out of the way so that the hydrophobic (lit. fears water) tails can mix and form larger micelles. Which also requires more energy.
