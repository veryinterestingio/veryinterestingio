    So, if I had 2 H2O molecules, then I remove one. Then I'd have exactly one molecule of H2O. Then let's say I add electricity to split it apart. Those 2 H atoms are going to bond and be a happy H2 molecule and go float away.

      so the answer is the reaction won't happen with only one H2O.

      The mechanism of the reaction is that on one battery terminal, two water molecules split up to become O2 and 4H+ ions, (which will only exist if there's other water around to stabilise them) and they send electrons through the battery to the other side where those electrons combine with 4H+ ions over on THAT side of the battery to make 2 lots of H2
