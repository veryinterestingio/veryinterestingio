• Derigiberble

    Shockingly close to 100% since they use forced draft and a counter-flow heat exchanger. Simply put a fan forces the exhaust from the burner to go through a bendy path opposite to the direction of the flow of water, so as the exhaust cools due to transferring heat to the water it encounters still cooler water. Since heat energy can only flow from a high temperature to a lower one this arrangement ensures that there's always heat exchange.

    Storage water heaters on the other hand send the gas up a central chimney type exchanger where it encounters warmer water as it loses heat. Less efficient but there is no need for a fan or indeed any power at all.

    Just checked and the exhaust for my on-demand heater was just 120°F/50°C on a 60F/15°C day. You can hold your hand over it no problem. That might sound not great, but the flame temperature of natural gas is roughly 3500°F/1930°C so damn near all the heat has been removed.

    The electric ones even have some nifty engineering in them. Switching that much power generates a lot of heat, so they just heat sink the transistors to the incoming water flow to pre-heat the water before it hits the heating elements.
