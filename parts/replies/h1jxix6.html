    • beingsubmitted

      This is true, but caloric counts on food actually do account for the your ability to access the energy - not by way of absorbtion, but on whether you can metabolize it at all.

      Today, caloric counts are generally done by the Atwater system, adding up the expected calories of various macros. Dietary fiber is a big one - it has the same energy as other carbs, but you can't metabolize it, so it doesn't add to the caloric count. Same with some artificial sweeteners.
