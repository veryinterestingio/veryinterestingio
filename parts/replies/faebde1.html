      gravitron ride

      If you're referring to the carnival rides where you're stuck up against a wall as the entire thing spins, they actually have to generate quite a bit more gravitational force than just 1g, in order to counteract the forces already pulling you down toward the ground. It's a vector sum of the two forces so in order to have it at least more toward the wall than toward the ground it would need to be at least 1.41 g (square root of 2). So yes you feel quite a bit heavier on those spinning rides than you do just standing on the ground and yes the force falls off very rapidly as you move away from the wall. (Try suddenly turning your head or lifting your head up and turning it to get a sudden jolt of nausea from those Coriolis effects mentioned in the video.)
