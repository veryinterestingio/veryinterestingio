    • Mil_lenny_L

      Keep in mind that flutes are wind instruments, so the primary source of sound is standing waves in a tube of air. The tones produced primarily depend on the geometry of the flute rather than the natural frequencies of the flute itself.

      Of course the flute itself is a structure and does vibrate. The vibration of the flute does interact with the standing air waves to boost the tone. Selecting a different material, say silver alloy vs. silver plated brass, is going to make a subtle difference. I already mentioned material damping (tendency for free vibration to decay more quickly) will make some difference. Silver alloy isn't as stiff as brass, and it's heavier, so all else the same, the silver flute will have lower natural frequencies than brass. And finally, build quality is going to make a difference in lots of respects, geometric accuracy, losses at valves, etc. Chances are that $2000 flute is going to be built much better than the $399 student special.
