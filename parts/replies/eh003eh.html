  • ultraswank

    The reason it was called the Spanish Flu is that it broke out during World War One. The warring parties, not wanting to acknowledge any issues that might benefit the enemy, suppressed reporting on the flu. Since Spain wasn't in the war, they didn't censor the news, and so the first reports people heard were from there even though it was raging all across Europe. So the governments did worse then stick their head in the ground, they actively suppressed information that people/medical institutions could have used to realize how dangerous the situation was.
