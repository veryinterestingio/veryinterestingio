    If the question is why do we get a sore throat in particular, some pathogens* do actually target the throat for infection. By inducing inflammation there we feel an irritation and so we cough to relive the itch. This is convenient for them because then they can tag along on airborne droplets into the throat of the next person.

    *eg some 'group A streptococci' / strep throat (https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC88944/)

      • SNova42

        It’s worth noting that sore throat can often develop without much (or any) cough. Infection-induced inflammation alone is enough to damage the throat tissue and cause pain. Coughing can cause further damage, but isn’t required. The mechanism and characteristics of damage from inflammation and from coughing (mechanical) are different and largely independent from each other.
