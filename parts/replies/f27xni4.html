        The reason why it's not a good idea to self diagnose is because we're terrible self reporters. An equally plausible explanation for your story would be that you do not believe your emotions matter or you attempt to suppress them if you cannot logically justify them, while at the same time possibly having low confidence in your own perceptions which allows you to too readily take another person's perception as fact. Essentially, your emotions are screaming at you that something is unjust and you're sitting their thinking 'shut up, I don't have evidence' instead of being curious as to why you may think that way.

        Honestly could be either, but my guts say you're too logical rather than too emotional as I'm that way and I felt I could relate somehow to the way you speak about yourself. People who fit the description I just gave often think they're being too emotional when they're actually doing the opposite. Best to speak to a professional about this to get a less biased view or you could end up trying to solve a problem that doesn't exist, thereby making the problem that does exist worse.
