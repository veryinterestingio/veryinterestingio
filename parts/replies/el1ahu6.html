    The cloudy part of the ice you make at home is the dissolved gasses in the water. The purer water freezes first on the outside of the cube. The gasses then get concentrated in the center. Commercial ice makers are designed to freeze the water partially, then drain off the part with the concentrated gasses. This is how they make the clear flavorless ice.

      I own a water business and make ice, the cloudy ice is from impurities in the water. Melted ice tastes different as it has a lot more bacteria then freshly purified water, try leaving a glass of water at room temp for a day and it tastes nearly the same.
