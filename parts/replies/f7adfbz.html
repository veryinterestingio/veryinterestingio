      • Monetdog

        Not quite... Most lithium batteries are charged using constant current up to around 60-70%, with the voltage raised to whatever is needed within some limit. Constant current is what lead-acid and NiCad's generally always use. To charge the last part, they switch to constant voltage, with the current dropping as the battery continues to charge; this is slower at getting charge into the battery. During this second phase, spiky "dendrites" tend to form inside the battery; these are liable to damage things if the battery gets banged around or exposed to heat.
