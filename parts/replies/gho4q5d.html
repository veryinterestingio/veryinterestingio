    I'll just add on a bit of common sense for the "why"

    Ganglia and neurons are immunoprivlidged sites. Any infection (including varicella) that has the ability to settle into nervous tissues also has the capacity to evade the immune system, a feature that enables the primary -> latent -> reactivation phenomenon caused by the same virus. The immune system can keep the local infection from spreading (until the immune system takes a weekend or is weakened which is usually when shingles arises), but it can't be completely eradicated due to the protection of the nerve cells. Mycobacterium tuberculosis can also have this pattern of active -> latent -> reactivated, but for a different "why." It hide inside macrophages and suppress the local immune response (presumably via cord factor).
