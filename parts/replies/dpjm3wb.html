  • savinbean

    Does shaking your leg while sitting help to alleviate the potential for developing a DVT?

    • slappinbass

      Perhaps a little, but walking is much more effective because it recruits more muscle fibers. This means more cross-sectional area, taking up more space, increasing compression on the vessels, giving a greater and more prolonged squeeze than just fidgeting.

  • Golden_Rush

    I just want to say clots are extremely rare. Unless you have a pre-existing condition, it's very unlikely or impossible to develop clots by sitting for a few hours.

    • slappinbass

      Unlikely. Yes. The odds are less than 50% on a long haul. When you say “or impossible”, I disagree. They certainly are possible. I’ve had that patient before.

      It’s still best to minimize risk even in otherwise healthy individuals. For long flights or road trips, it’s still recommended to get up from time to time. And further to that point, you don’t always know if you have an underlying condition (which is probably what you mean by “pre-existing”).
