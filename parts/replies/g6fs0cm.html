    • tinker_toys

      You don't have a gas cap sensor.

      You have a computer that can determine that an evap (fuel vapor emission management system) leak exists, and that it didn't happen until right after the fuel level reading increased significantly. The logical conclusion is that the gas cap was left loose. So, the programming throws out a gas cap warning. Problem is, these systems rely on the readings of sensors, layered with computerized human logic. And there are always gaps and nuances in the programming that can have unintended effects.

      If your gas cap is always tight every time, you have a different problem.
