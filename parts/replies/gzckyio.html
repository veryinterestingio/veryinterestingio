    Yep, this is correct. Water exists in 3 compartments in the body: in the blood vessels as part of plasma, in the cells as part of cytoplasm, and in the interstitial (“in-between”) spaces of connective tissue and other cells. Compression over time pushes this fluid out causing the indentation. Take the compression away and it comes back.

    To the other poster’s point about heart failure - one of the key characteristics of heart failure is volume overload: too much water in the body. Complicated and tangential to this question. But suffice to say, their limbs get big and swollen with this “third spacing”, or interstitial fluid collection, and you can very easily indent their skin. If you go to the doctor and they press on your shins, that’s what we’re looking for.
