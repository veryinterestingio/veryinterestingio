    Vocabulary in general works like this, I think.

    If you see a word you've learned before, you'll probably remember what it means. Especially for somewhat ordinary words.

    But it's much more common to forget a particular word when you have a meaning in mind. Even simple words sometimes.

    So when you're trying to speak a second language that you're not fluent in, you're always in that mode of trying to find the right word for something. It's harder.

    But when you hear the words in that language, you're more likely to remember their meanings, and you have context to guide you.
