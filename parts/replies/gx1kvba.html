    • JuanElMinero

      The bonds between parts of dye molecules have a specific energy level that allows them to absorb parts of the visible light spectrum. So, a blue dye will absorb everything except the blue part, which is reflected back to you.

      Breaking up these bonds will make the dye lose or alter its absorption qualities. It happens to different molecules at different times, which gives you the slow fading. How the color changes is very dependant on the dye and the bond that was broken.
