    Moving away from binary coding. Having only on or off in each piece of information is limiting if simple to engineer and code. Moving to a system that had more states (trinary with off, on and kind of on) can help with processing speed if done properly. But also not easily integrated into our current system.

    Binary isn't simply "easier to engineer" it has worked out to be more efficient in practice. Making two states is pretty easy. Making three states is a lot harder, you need a more complex and thus bulkier part. As it stands we've made transistors so small that even having two distinct states is hard. Something of comparable size with more than two states, which is also highly reliable, doesn't currently exist and is actually pretty hard to imagine.
