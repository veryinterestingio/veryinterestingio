      "In a scenario where a concoction of different gaseous molecules aremixed in a fish fish tank with no forces other than gravity, wouldn't itseparate eventually, albeit very slowly?" No. Even if the starting point were oxygen at the bottom and nitrogen on top and there were no convection (no wind), the gases would mix completely. In theory there would be a tiny excess of oxygen on the bottom due to tiny differences in gravitational force, but I doubt it would be a measurable difference.

      The molecules in liquids have substantial intramolecular forces, and this can cause liquids to be immiscible (meaning they separate into layers). Molecules in gases have no intramolecular bonding, so they don't behave like liquids.
