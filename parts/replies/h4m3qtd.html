    The critical point of nitrogen is at 126 K, 3.4 MPa. Oxygen and argon have ~150 K, ~5 MPa, which is not too different.

    In the above sea level atmosphere the scale height is very roughly 8 km, if it's not too different below sea level we expect to reach 3.4 MPa after ~8km*ln(34) = 28 km. That's a very rough estimate, but it's clearly a very small fraction of Earth's diameter. Oxygen and argon shouldn't change that much.

    Going beyond that... this phase diagram suggests a pure nitrogen borehole would stay a fluid, but with such a large depth we might need to consider the separation of heavier gases towards the bottom, and then things get complicated.
