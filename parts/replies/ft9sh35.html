    It is worth pointing out that this evolutionary process happened incredibly fast. Up until ~3 million years ago baleen whales were at most the size of minke's (who are the smallest species of baleen whales) and adapted to living in vast continental shelf regions. Their size was also likely constrained by macro-predators such as Megalodon and Lyvjatan. Then the current ice age begins, the continental shelfs are vastly reduced in size and the macro-predators go extinct. As do most baleen whales in fact, genus diversity among rorquals has fallen from a peak of ~12 before the Cenozoic to 2 extant genera. But the survivords remarkably quickly figure out that there are massive algae blooms happening during the polar summer.
