    • Flocculencio

      It's architectural and socioeconomic. Not at all theological. I mean the priory is economically critical to the town and constructing the cathedral so you do get some religion and politics but no one sits down for theological discourses. It's more along the lines of the Earl of Badguyton undercutting wages to deprive the cathedral project of workers and getting the Bishop to transfer it to him so the Prior and the master Mason cook up a plan to whip up volunteers to work part time on the cathedral in penance for their sins. It's religion as part of medieval life not philosophical theology.
