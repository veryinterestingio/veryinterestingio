    • SpreadItLikeTheHerp

      I wouldn’t worry about it for live videoconferencing. Anything recorded, and especially things that will get a lot of traffic should be recorded well, including a pop filter and possibly some post processing. Also, keep in mind how end users are going to be listening. Earbuds, cheap corporate Bluetooth headsets, laptop speakers are all shit and will hide imperfections. Any decent pair of headphones will quickly highlight crap recordings.
