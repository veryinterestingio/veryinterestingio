      This is a good question but sadly there are so many pieces of data that it is near impossible to offer you a percentage chance based on distance.

      Consider the following:

      • Ant scent path density is not absolute. Scent maps might look like this, with decayed areas and long "arms" going out to food sources. If the ant crosses an orange path that's an 98% chance to get home, but who knows whether that will happen?
      • You don't know if the ant you flicked was picked up very close to her nest, or far from it. Flicking her further may carry her closer to home.
      • Ant nest behavior is dictated by need - common black garden ants might be 10m from their nest if food is scarce, but in abundance they'll stay closer to home.

      Thus, there are simply too many unknowns to provide an answer. Even if we did know the exact behavior and scent trail layout for this particular nest, the math would be incredibly complex to even know whether a short flick was better or worse on average.

      I will say that a short flick which places the ant close to where you picked her up means that she's very likely to be close to her own scent trail and pick it up again. A long flick is a crapshoot, who knows!
