    • Zoten

      There are 2 components to your immune system: the innate and adaptive. The innate just travels around and freaks out at everything it doesn't recognize. It doesn't matter if it's the common cold or anthrax. (slight exaggeration, since it does matter if it's bacterial or viral). It's going to react the same way. These are responsible for fever, phlegm, swelling, etc. These happen within hours of being infected. The end goal here isn't to kill the infection. It's to fight until your adaptive cells can bring reinforcements.

      You also have an adaptive immune system. These are (primarily) your T and B cells, which take time. They have to 'study' the infection, find a good way to kill it, and then mass produce the antibodies. This can take anywhere from hours (if you've already made antibodies to it before and just need to mass produce them) or days-weeks (if it's something brand new).

      The "information" is stored in your B cells that can mass produce specific antibodies when they encounter the antigen that they were made to fight. If they can't (either they don't recognize it or maybe the B cells died off and you lost immunity to that disease), then your body has to make new cells to create antibodies.
