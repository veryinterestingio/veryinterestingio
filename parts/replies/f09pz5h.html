    I don't think it's a "differential" problem. It's still a problem of the amount of pressure.

    If you dive to 140ft, that's roughly 77psi. Yes it's all over your body, but it's relatively low amount regarding crushing force.

    A 5gal bucket is roughly 10" diameter on the bottom. That's roughly 79 square inches. 5 gallons of water weighs roughly 41.7lbs. if you drop that bucket from 10 feet, the force applied to say a person, is roughly 20,016lbs. But this is exerted over 79sq inches of bucket, which is around 253psi, or like diving to 550 feet.

    Also absolute pressure doesnt mean there's pressure all around you. At 100ft, there's 4 bar absolute pressure. 3 bar water pressure (1 bar per 33feet), plus the 1 bar of atmospheric pressure applied to the water at sea level.

    The only reason a "differential" of pressure would kill you vs all-around pressure is because the smaller surface area creates MORE pressure.
