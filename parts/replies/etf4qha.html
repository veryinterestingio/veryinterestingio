    • NPPraxis

      Generally, I feel like the effects of climate change can be overexaggerated on Reddit- most scientists do not believe we are facing an extinction event. If you are alive today, you won't be dead from climate change by old age. A lot of Redditors, however, believe this.

      On the flip side, I always wonder if it's worth correcting them. Most scientists do believe we are facing an environmental catastrophe- climate change will displace communities, likely causing wars, causing dramatic increases in cost to maintain existing cities, likely making a lot of land uninhabitable, etc. It will be a massive economic drag and cheaper if we deal with it in advance, and will likely cause tremendous loss of life and political instability and refugee crisises.

      So...it's I guess better that people are too worried than not worried enough?
