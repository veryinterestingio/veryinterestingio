    That is mostly only around Frankfort, Bardstown, and other whiskey producing areas, as stated above the barns in most of the state are black because they are tobacco curing barns.

    The paint color of whiskey aging barns isn’t really because of the fungus, most of them are painted how they are in order to influence how the whiskey ages. Thermal cycling causes whiskey to age faster, so some barns are black so they heat up more during the day to age the whiskey more quickly.
