• Optrode

    A few errors here. The kidneys are not responsible for detecting hypertonicity (e.g. as might occur after a salty snack). The kidneys are responsible for regulating blood volume, and will only induce thirst (via the renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system) in the case of low blood volume.

    Osmometric thirst (e.g. due to salty snacks) is controlled by sensory organs in the brain itself, which detect blood osmolarity by measuring osmotic pressure (this is different from baroreceptors!) across the osmoreceptor cells' membranes.
