    • Manofchalk

      To add to this, nobody is really making hard disks smaller these days.

      And if anything they want to start getting bigger. The 3.5" format physically limits how large the drive can be, which wasnt a problem all that long ago because innovations in making the spinning platters denser was relatively simple and cheaper than manufacturing a drive with multiple platters.

      Now were finding it really difficult to innovate more in making the platters denser and its coming with tradeoffs, so were making HDD's larger capacity by having more and more platters in them, which takes up physical space. So to fit ever more platters in they have to get thinner and closer together, while fitting a read head under every platter, while they spin at 7200 rpm... Drive manufacturers would probably enjoy a bit more room.
