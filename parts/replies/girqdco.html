    Both of these paragraphs don't address the kind of randomness the OP is talking about. Randomness in quantum theory doesn't mean equal probabilities and it doesn't mean "undirected" either. It means you have a set of possible outcomes, can calculate their probabilities and if you perform the same experiment many times you reproduce the calculated probability distribution but you cannot predict which outcome will occur a particular time.
