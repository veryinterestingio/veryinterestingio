    Liquid hand soap also uses potassium hydroxide, actually. Bar soap uses sodium hydroxide. Shaving soap typically uses a 60/40 blend of the 2. You're right that potassium hydroxide gives more lather though.

    • earthwormjim91

      The biggest thing with potassium hydroxide is that it keeps the whole thing liquid. Sodium hydroxide hardens up.

      You can make a pure castile body wash with just olive oil and potassium hydroxide, or you could make a bar castile soap with olive oil and sodium hydroxide.

      "Gentler" usually means a higher superfat percentage. The fats that are leftover after the saponification process. If you go for a perfect saponification with no remaining fats, it will be much harsher. By putting in just more oils than can be fully saponified, you're left with a smoother feeling.
