      • DamienGranz

        This is also why a lot of the fatalities in the 1918 flu pandemic were younger, healthier people. Your body will make conditions hostile for the invaders, like giving you a fever, runny nose, etc. but those effects can be dangerous.

        The 1918 flu caused what is called a "cytokine storm", where in a nutshell, your body produces too much of a substance meant to make things difficult for the flu but also weakens the patient, and in many cases contributed to deaths where older & younger patients couldn't field as strong of a response.

        Generally a stronger response is better, but not always if it gets out of control.
