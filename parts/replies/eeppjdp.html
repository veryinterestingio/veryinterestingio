      I will assume the article is correct in its claim that "The basic design shown here will scrub its own consumption and approximately 24 pounds of carbon dioxide from the atmosphere every year."

      24 pounds per year = 30 grams per day. An average human exhales somewhere around 720 grams CO2 per day (this number depends on diet and metabolic rate, for better calculations see: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/ina.12383)

      You will need to build around 24 of those algae scrubbers, assuming they work as well as claimed, in order to take up the CO2 from one human.

      Of course, now that you provide more context, the amine scrubber sounds like an even worse idea (since it would be toxic if a leak occurred).

        Most homes lack good ventilation or an HVAC system. The only way for them to vent CO2 is to open windows, but any home in the more extreme winters or summers cannot do that since they would lose heat or air conditioning.

        It's just a reality that keeping windows open every day of the year is infeasible.

        There needs to be an affordable and simple solution for individuals to reduce CO2 concentrations in their homes, and most people think a few plants can do that.

        Potted plants don't intake enough CO2 to reduce that concentration significantly unless you have extreme quantities of plants.
