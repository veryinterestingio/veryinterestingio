    Okay, so a strain gauge is the business end of a load cell. The way they work is, like previously mentioned, they pass electricity through a set of resistors and measure the change in voltage. Those "resistors" are actually just really tiny copper wires glued to the surface of the part in a grid-like pattern. As the part flexes microscopically, those copper wires are either stretched or compressed by a very tiny amount. When you stretch a wire, the cross-sectional area is reduced and when the cross-section of a wire is reduced, it's electrical resistance goes up which changes the voltage across the strain gauge. The electrical side is a bit more complicated but that's essentially how a strain gauge works.
