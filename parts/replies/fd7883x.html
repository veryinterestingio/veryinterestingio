      I actually kinda laughed at this. I work with a site that does operate its own microgrid. We’ve got 30 buildings on a small hydro-electric generator to produce the power. We’re in the early stages of expanding to add a second generating station, and selling the power to a major industrial site next door who’s currently running their operation on diesel power.

      The sale portion of it is pretty simple; we sign a Power Purchasing Agreement with the customer, and they agree to pay us $x/mo for power within the specified limits. We then have calibrated meters on the feed lines, ensuring that they’re complying with their contractual limits.

      The whole blockchain thing is silly. You just have recording meters, and ensure that at the end of the month, things settle up.
