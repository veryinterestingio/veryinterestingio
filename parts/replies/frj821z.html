  • Teknicsrx7

    Also cars are built to fight/dampen specific resonant frequencies, which is why different cars sound different on the same surfaces. It’s more noticeable in higher end cars where more R&D goes into eliminating a larger range of resonant frequencies. This is done from the materials used, the method of mounting items together, installation of noise/vibration dampers or absorbers, types of noise insulation/fill, bushing types etc etc.

    Nowadays some cars even use the stereo system to monitor noises in your cabin and then produce a noise cancelling sound to eliminate or reduce your ability to hear that specific noise.
