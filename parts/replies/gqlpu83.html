    When pasting long links, you can often look for a question mark and delete everything after it, just posting the much shorter link. It makes it look better, and it leaves off all the irritating tracking info (that can also cause problems, since the website might choke if the format of the tracking part of the URL changes in the future). So, your link could just be:
    https://www.lowes.com/pd/Utilitech-0-5ft-2-Out-Outlet-Extender-White/1002815162

    Good to test the link before you post it, but that is much easier to look at that your original link.
