    Two main ways.

    1) Lubrication. Water is the key, and you drink it quite liberally to help food compact/coagulate more readily, and to get it down faster.

    2) Physiology. The peristaltic mechanism of the throat can—with practice that’s usually not worth the invested time unless you’re kobayashi or better—be “beefed up,” as it were, by bobbing one’s head in sync with one’s swallowing. By doing this, you also give the food the largest possible passageway at all points from teeth to trachea.
