    • Goooooogler

      Basically barcodes are just numbers/letters that can be read with a scanner, there are many formats but nowadays a scanner can read them all.

      Behind the barcode there is often no encryption, for example if you take a few movie tickets and read the bars, you will probably find a pattern in there (maybe the date, seat number, etc). If you figure out the pattern completely, you can print your own movie-ticket barcodes (thats why barcodes arent really a protection mechanism).

      The talk gives funny examples of how sometimes the systems dont even check that the data is valid, just that the barcode format is recognised (using a pack of cigarettes as a parking pass, etc).
