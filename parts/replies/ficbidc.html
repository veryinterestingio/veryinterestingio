    A big distinction between apoptosis and necrosis is pretty much whether it's a graceful death or not. Apoptosis is like shutting down a computer, where the computer takes a bit of time to clean everything up after the command is given. In contrast, necrosis is like yanking out the power cord. It leaves a mess behind. Necrosis can happen in infections where the fact that the cell just explodes and leaves all the mess behind can mean bad news. An example of this is tuberculosis.

    There actually are molecular pathways that induce necrosis similar to apoptosis.

  • sayacunai

    There are also a bunch of other forms of cell death that have been studied over the last few years including necroptosis, pyroptosis, and ferroptosis! They have different biochemical features, but broadly share the fate of cells halting their metabolic processes and being cleared. There are more types of cell death being worked on, but these are the most accepted ones.

    I'm a cell death researcher. I work on apoptosis and that's my area of expertise, so I'm only passingly familiar with the other forms, but I've been to some cell death conferences and it's really interesting to hear about all the other death pathways people are studying!
