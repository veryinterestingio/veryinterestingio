    • ttystikk

      There's a bit more to it; notice there's no surface that's perpendicular to the ground to reflect radar. No tail, no inside corners. Engine intakes and exhaust hidden from view. Add radar absorbent materials and paint and the radar signature is equivalent to that of a bird. If your radar is really good, you might spot that bird going 600mph at 25,000ft, so the pilots still plan missions to stay as far away from radar installations as possible. Finally, engine exhaust is mixed with air as it leaves the aircraft, reducing heat signature and noise.
