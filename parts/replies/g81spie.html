    • TypicalPakeha

      The idea that serious chess players actually compete against themselves is largely a fiction. You see it a lot in movies and stuff but I've never met anyone in my 10+ years of over the board chess play who plays directly against themselves with the aim of winning.

      That being said, serious chess players do spend a lot of time at a board by themselves studying positions. Sometimes with books or a computer to assist but often just replaying a game you played recently and exploring all the variations to see what short term tactics and tricks you missed, or how you could have played better long term/strategically. From the outside that probably looks like you're playing yourself to someone who isn't that interested in chess, but it is a bit different.
