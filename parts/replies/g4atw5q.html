        Yes, both Uranus and Neptune have magnetic fields, and they're both weird.

        On both planets, the magnetic fields are tilted at a large angle relative to the rotation axis, as well as being off-center. This unusual shape is a by-product of the field being generated in a shallow shell rather than right at the core.

        We believe that instead of liquid iron being responsible for the field, Uranus and Neptune use superionic water for their magnetic field dynamos. If you squeeze regular water under incredible pressure, the oxygens lock into a crystal matrix while the hydrogen ions are free to flow within the matrix. If you had a lump of it sitting on your kitchen table, it would look a lot like slush, but the free flowing hydrogen ions means it could also conduct electricity.
