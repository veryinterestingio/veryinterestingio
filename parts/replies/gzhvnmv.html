    I've been doing a lot of research and experimentation with diets, and this summarizes my findings. As blood sugar drops, you feel hungry. If you ride it out, you'll stop feeling hungry and be able to work off your fat reserves. Conversely, eating high sugar foods will spike your sugar, and then drop it very quickly, making you feel constantly hungry. You can mitigate this by avoiding high sugar foods and eat slow releasing carbohydrates instead so there's less of a spike and plummet of blood sugar, or you can tough it out until the hunger goes away by itself and wait until you're really hungry to eat.
