      You have the innate and adaptive immune systems. Within these systems are several different cell types that work in concert to protect you. And even within that each different cell type usually has several different ways that it deals with threats.

      This drug targets 1 specific mechanism that 1 specific cell type uses. The other cells and their mechanisms are unaffected. This generalization really doesn't have anything to do with type 1 diabetes. However in type 1 diabetes, this particular cell and its defense mechanism seem to play a role with the destruction of beta cells, so targeting it in this way is intended to help prevent the destruction of the cells with limited trade-off in suppression of the immune system
