    • EmilyU1F984

      Chemical properties of the element are virtually completely based on how many electrons are in the outermost cloud/shell, (followed by the number of "shells" the element has).

      That's why elements that are in a column of the periodic table are usually pretty similar. They have the same number of electrons that are able to interact with other atoms.

      So Sodium, Potassium and Lithium are all in the first column. That means they have one line electron on their outermost shell, that makes them interact with other atoms.

      Why is a very useless question to ask for fundamental rules of the universe. They simply are. A better question is to ask how.

      And chemical reactions dependent on how those outermost electrons can react.

      Those with few electrons on the outermost shell usually prefer to simply give them away. Those on the other end will prefer to steal them, those in the middle may even share them.

      This is all because a "full" shell is most favourable.

      Why is that the number eight in the more common elements? That's because the universe is that way.

      It's like the speed of light in vacuum is 300,000 km/s and not twice that.

      You could go a bit further to explain this with quarks and spin etc, but it doesn't really matter for anything, it's always that way.

      So number of electrons would be how many "sticky" sides the Lego piece has, and number of shells would be how big the Lego piece is. (Very simplified).

      And most of the interaction between those atoms is due to electromagnetism.
