    • RhynoD

      Your circulatory system is less like a water line coming into your house, and more like city roads and highways. If there's a blockage in your veins, the blood will back up right there a little, but the pressure will back up and send blood through other veins - not unlike when there's an accident, cars take alternate routes.

      A chronic backup of your veins is bad because it makes your heart have to work harder to move blood, which is bad for your heart, or if it's a major "highway" like a big main artery, it's cutting off the blood to your whole body and there aren't many "side streets" for your blood to get there.
