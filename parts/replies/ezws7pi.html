    The pre-revolutionary colonies had a handful of experiments with fiat currency, all of which ended up with sharp inflation. This is the reason why the founding fathers (who were of the monied, pre-revolutionary colonies) were so strongly opposed to it.

  • Galileo182

    A good bet is that when paper is substituted in for a precious metal, the currency spends more easily, which benefits the purchasing class more than the merchant class. A merchant wants to know he is getting a one ounce gold coin vs a note representing the value of a one ounce gold coin, because it is much harder to forge and has a steadier overall presence. Stable prices and values of currency favor those that possess the bulk of it.
