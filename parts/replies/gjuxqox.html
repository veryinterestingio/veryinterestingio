    • fishywiki

      Absolutely. You could spray someone you don't like with isopentyl acetate and they'd be targets for bees any time they went close to a hive. Beekeepers use artificial chemicals all the time, a great example being to attract a swarm to a swarm trap: you can buy a pack of commercial swarm lures that'll last you a season, or simply use lemongrass oil, a little bottle of which will last years for the same price. To get that person you don't like who I mentioned above, put a few drops in awkward spots like a vent into the roof of their house - there's a good chance a swarm will move in.

      Less common is the use of artificial queen substance which is used to control the hive when they lose the queen.

      So, yes, chemical warfare is common with bees.
