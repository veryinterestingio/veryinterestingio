      It might not be sufficiently meaningful but the thermal expansion coefficient is related to the difference between heat capacity at constant volume and heat capacity at constant pressure by

      Cp - CV = VT a^2 /K

      where a is the volume thermal expansion coefficient and K is the isothermal compressibility.

      There are Maxwell relations/other relations that say some partial derivative = expression that includes heat capacity and thermal expansion coefficient. I believe this includes derivative of temperature w.r.t to volume with entropy fixed and internal energy w.r.t to volume with pressure fixed.
