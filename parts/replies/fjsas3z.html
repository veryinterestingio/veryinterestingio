    Stock for aluminum cans and ends use a significant amount of recycled metal. First is the scrap from manufacturing (web and process scrap) which is 100% recyclable into new can or end stock.

    With UBC (used beverage cans) the recycling will use some virgin metal to get the alloy right (partly because the end stock (5182 alloy) has a higher magnesium content than can stock (3104 alloy).

    Beverage cans are the largest single use of aluminum in the country. If it couldn't consume recycled material (both pre and post consumer), there would be mountains of unused aluminum piling up.

    Source: Years of engineering at the corporate level of the then number two can manufacturer.
