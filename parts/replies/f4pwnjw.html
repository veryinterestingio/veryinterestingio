    • latenerd

      It's a good question, but don't worry, it's very unlikely. Anesthesia is a bit more complex than just "knocking someone out." Anesthesiologists have looked at brain waves during anesthesia (in fact, there are now machines that monitor EEG during surgery just like they monitor vital signs like heart rate and oxygen levels). What they find is that the waves look similar to brains in a coma, not brains that are awake. They also usually give a cocktail of drugs including opiates which we know block pain even while people are awake. Also, if the patient were just paralyzed but still feeling pain, their heart rate and blood pressure would probably start to go haywire, and the dose of drugs would be adjusted until they came back down.
