      Well, that's true of any brain fundamentally unless you're accounting for hormonal regulation etc., and neutral networks are simulated and have only an abstract geometry made of links but not locations.     And then there's cephalopods.

    Not the nautilus, which is probably about as aware as a sponge with googely eyes glued to it. It's well in the range of "clusters of ganglia".

    Maybe some squid, the esophageal ganglia ring can get pretty complex in larger species, notably humbolt.

    Octopuses, especially the larger species, have definitely evolved proper "brains" from those esophageal ganglia, and is probably our best axample of what OP was originally getting at: convergent evolution of a self-aware and proper "brain" in a non-tetrapod.

    Beyond what's been mentioned with nervous systems showing up pretty early in Animalia, cephalopod brains are about as close as we've encountered to "intelligence" evolving along a completely different lineage.
