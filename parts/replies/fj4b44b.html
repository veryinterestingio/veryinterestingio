    In addition to what's already mentioned, some frogs have anti-freeze proteins, which bind to ice crystals as they form to prevent the damage ice crystals pose to living cells. There can also be manipulation of the sugars in the blood, which can lower the freezing point of the solution. So the frog must reach several degrees cooler to actually freeze.

    Edit: to clarify, the temperatures where these frogs hibernate is typically stable toward the lower end. So it's not like the frog necessarily reaches -20 degrees like winter air temperatures can.
