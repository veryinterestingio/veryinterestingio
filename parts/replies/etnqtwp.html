    • ipsum_stercus_sum

      The wax melts and soaks into the wick, where it is slowly vaporized by the heat of the flame. The vapor is a hydrocarbon, so it burns immediately. The wick isn't what is burning; the vaporizing wax keeps it cooler than the ignition point of the material. The end of the wick only burns when the wax can't make it there before being completely vaporized.
