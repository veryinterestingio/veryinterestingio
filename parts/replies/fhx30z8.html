    • metagravedom

      I would think that the bigger problem would be the salt water in the top soil itself. evaporation doesn't really have much of an impact on the damage done by the salt. Over time minerals bind with each other and create layers of sediment. this is why you can break open some rocks and find salt formations inside of them. for instance some stone counter top companies specifically use stone near sea areas because the stone will have salt formations that shine and make it appear more aesthetically appealing.
