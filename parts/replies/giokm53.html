    • manVsPhD

      I think you may be giving the terminology too much import. We basically call any system where the spins are aligned in opposite direction antiferromagnetic. The reason this is a distinct scenario is that you end up with a unique topological phase that is different than, say, the spin Hall phase. The spin Hall phase can have the atoms in the exact same positions as the antiferromagnetic system but the spins align differently due to energetic considerations (i.e which atom will have what type of spin). I specifically design photonic topological insulators so I have the freedom to deform the atoms to invert those energy levels as I see fit. For example to get a spin Hall insulator I’d break inversion symmetry and I can show that the Hamiltonian in that case has a Spin Hall term. If I break C_6 symmetry but maintain C3 and C’_2 out of plane symmetry then the Hamiltonian will have an antiferromagnetic term.
