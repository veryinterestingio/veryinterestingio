      No, salt in the amounts of a "typical" western diet are horrible for us. They are very bad for the heart especially. Salt in small amounts is absolutely fine, and in reasonable quantities we need salts to use as electrolytes which helps our body use electrical signals via the nervous system.

      But I digress. There is so much added salt in highly processed foods and most restaurants that most people who aren't paying attention are eating too much salt. Usually it's shown as "sodium" on an ingredients list or nutrition label.

      One way to discover if you're eating too many salts: make homemade vegetable soup with a low-sodium stock from the store, and use all fresh or frozen vegetables (most frozen veggies keep their nutrients very well) and do not add any salt to the soup. Just chop the veggies and throw them in and add some pepper and maybe some herbs if you like, BUT NO SALT. If you find this soup to have basically no flavor, you're probably eating foods that are way too salty. By adding more salt to the soup, you'll start to recognize the threshold where your tongue picks up the salt ir is used to. If you practice eating less salt, you will greatly enhance other flavors.
