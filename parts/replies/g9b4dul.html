      • ZylonBane

        1/60th of a second. Early-gen consoles didn't output a proper signal composed of interlaced odd and even fields, instead they just pumped out repeated odd (or even) fields, for essentially a 240p image at 60 or 50 Hz. That's why scanlines were so noticeable on those old games, because half of each frame's fields were missing.
