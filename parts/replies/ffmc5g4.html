    • Calm_and_Caffeinated

      Interesting, how did you train for this? I can go crosseyed and move either eye back and forth. But at the very end, you have one eye looking straight ahead and doing it. What, if any exercises did you do?

      • b_ootay_ful

        I can go crosseyed

        That's the hard part. You've completed Step 1.

        I've taught a few people to do this. I picked right as a dominant side to learn, but either one works

        Step 2) Try look right with both eyes normally. Then slowly cross your eyes. Then look left. Don't rush it, just do it slowly. Now go back to the right. Congrats, you can move one eye at a time!

        Step 3) Repeat step 2, slowly. Instead of starting by looking right, start looking straight forward. Now as you slowly squint, slowly start looking look right while concentrating on focusing through your right eye.

        Step 4) Once you are used to it, and can focus through an eye, you can slowly try tilting your head while keeping focus. Have you ever looked through a rifle scope or microscope with both eyes open? You need to try focus on what one eyes sees.

        Doing this infront of a mirror, friend or phone camera helps.

        I taught myself how to do this when I was 11 and bored.

        Disclaimer: Since you are using muscles that aren't used to being stretched in this order or sequence, it might hurt a bit. Don't push yourself. I haven't hurt my eyes before, but that doesn't mean that there is no risk.
