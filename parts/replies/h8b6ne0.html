    HVAC Engineer here. I would add to this that it has long been best practice in healthcare facilities to NOT recirculate air from certain spaces such as sterile processing, soiled holding rooms, airborne infectious isolation rooms, emergency department waiting rooms, etc, to other spaces. Infection control in hospitals always calls for air movement from cleaner areas to dirtier areas. Rooms are also positively pressurized or negatively pressurized for this purpose.
