    A surrounding atmosphere or liquid does dampen vibrations by absorbing them and turning them into sound.

    Or, imagine two pieces of chalk shot at each other at high speed turning into dust upon impact. In an atmosphere, the dust cloud spreads rapidly at first but quickly slows down as the motion is absorbed by the atmosphere and turned into a sound wave (and some heat). In space, the dust cloud just keeps spreading.
