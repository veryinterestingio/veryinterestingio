    The RS-68 used on the Delta IV rocket takes this approach.

    That is also why the Delta IV, despite using the same hydrolox propellant mix as the RS-25 on the Space Shuttle, has a much more luminous, yellow exhaust plume - what you see there is not the "actual" engine exhaust, but mainly the ablated nozzle material (which has a lot of carbon in it, thus the yellow glow) being carried away by it.

    There is an alternative much simpler approach: ablative cooling. Just make the nozzle from something that's OK to slowly burn away, usually something based on graphite.

    The nozzles are made of the composite material: the structure is woven from the tungsten fibres enveloped into copper. Copper boils and ablates away. As you understand, copper has a high heat of evaporation. I do not know, though, whether this design was used in the engine in question.
