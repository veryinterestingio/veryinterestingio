    • samanime

      Basically, the sounds are all waves. The waves don't really add up to one total wave, like colors do for sight, but are still somewhat separate.

      As an example, try drawing 3 random waves on a piece of paper. Just visually, you can still probably distinguish the three from one another. It is kind of like that, but 3D (meaning they're even less likely to fully stack up), and your brain is really good at telling the difference, usually.

      Sometimes, if they're too similar (or too many), your brain can't always split them up and you can't quite tell. Things like the chirp of a fire alarm with a low battery can be weirdly difficult to locate partially like this.

      Your brain is also good at picking certain sounds out of the background, like the voice of a parent or spouse in a crowd.
