    • Spoonshape

      Seas and oceans moderate weather - soil and rock are good insulators - if you measure temprature a few metres down it doesnt change much from middle of summer to middle of winter. Water on the other hand mixes so 20 or 30 metres of it changes temprature, but this in turn means it acts like a sponge soaking up and releasing heat both day/night and summer/winter.

      Basically water can buffer much more heat than soil. Phase change of water also buffers a lot of heat (freezing/thawing) which can be an influence.
