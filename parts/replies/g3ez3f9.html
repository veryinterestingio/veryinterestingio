    It’s been a bumper year for a lot of things. I blame humans staying inside all spring. My neighborhood didn’t do a lot of its regular yearly bug spraying this spring, either because the company that does it was shut down for COVID or they couldn’t justify the cost if no one was outside to appreciate it.

    I have a lot more insects in my garden this year. There are bees visiting every day, more beetles, more mosquitoes, more flies, and I got butterflies for the first time this year. (Urban area. We don’t see a lot of butterflies, sadly.)

    I went for walks almost every day this spring. There were more birds than I usually hear in this area. This summer I noticed an uptick in the number of spider webs I found. It feels like I saw a new one in my rose bushes every day. Today, I saw a lizard on my porch, just a little blue tailed skink, but it’s the first one I’ve seen since i moved here.
