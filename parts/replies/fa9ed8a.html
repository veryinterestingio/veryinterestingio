    • JohnStuartMillennium

      The closest would be the exact same orbit. The laws of gravity permit stable trojan orbits, which are at the same altitude but 60 degrees ahead or behind another body.

      I'm not sure if this works when both bodies are earth-sized, but you could definitely have an habitable mars-sized planet trailing behind a gas giant with habitable moons, or something like that.

      As for separate orbits, that's still entirely possible. The TRAPPIST-1 system has seven exoplanets, three of which are in the habitable zone. (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TRAPPIST-1)
