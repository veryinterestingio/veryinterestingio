    • lucky_ducker

      No, there have been many periods of deflation, especially when currency was tied to precious metals like gold and silver. The global Great Depression of the 1930s was deeply deflationary. One of the reasons nations gradually abandoned the gold standard in the 20th century was the realization that tying a currency to gold made it impossible to control inflation and deflation, and the result was endless cycles of high inflation followed by destructive deflation. Since the late 1900s national currencies have been fiat currencies, which can be controlled in a way that gold-backed currencies cannot.
