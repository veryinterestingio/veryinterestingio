    Unfortunately most airliners, while they have ADS-B as required by regulation, don't have the fancy bits that provide weather data to general aviation aircraft.

    Partially it is because of an issue with regulations, and partially cost. For the regulation piece, US-certified carriers can only use approved weather sources for flight planning and enroute. Most major airlines have a meteorology staff, and that combined with NOAA aviation forecasts makes up the bulk of "approved" weather sources.

    As for the cost, since most aircraft have Wi-Fi installed, pilots can just use that to provide connectivity for their iPad to grab the approved weather data, rather than rely on ADS-B in. Also, every airliner has weather radar onboard, which is harder to use correctly but gives you real-time data rather than composite radar that is filtered and therefore delayed by up to 15 minutes.
