    It's not the advertisement itself that's the problem in this case. The problem is that YouTube collects user data and tracks user behavior to display ads targeted to specific demographics. YouTube was knowingly collecting data of children without parental consent and using that data for commercial gain (i.e. sell targetted ads to advertisers), which is what COPPA prohibits. After YouTube's recent changes, content creators are required to mark if their videos are "not for kids", which tells YouTube it's okay to use data of users watching that video for targeted ads, or "for kids", which tells YouTube not to use targeted ads.
