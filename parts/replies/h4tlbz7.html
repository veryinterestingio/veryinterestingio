      • Life-Suit1895

        Yeah, this type of thermometer has been around since the 18th century.

        Another way to determine a maximal temperature are the appropriately named maximum thermometers were the mercury can expand unhindered, but cannot simply flow back due to a choke point at the reservoir. The old medical thermometers used to measure your body temperature work like this. I don't think this type of thermometers has seen much use in meteorological studies, though, as you have to manually "reset" them (by shaking), which isn't very convenient.
