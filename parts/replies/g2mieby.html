      • jferry

        Solar is even a good choice for so-called "brown sites."

        Sites that are too toxic for other uses (say: building a middle school) can work just fine for solar/wind farms. And you can often pick the land up cheap. Former coal plants are a good choice since they often already have transmission lines.

        Given how fast the cost of panels have dropped (from ~$68.61 in 1976 to $0.40 in 2019), cutting some of the other costs has become proportionally more important.
