  • elwahrio

    To add to this, the most complete answer is that sucrose and dextrose are both 100% fermentable to brewers yeast. So unless the alcohol content gets so high that the yeast dies, a decent brewery should be able to convert near 100% of that sugar into alcohol and carbon dioxide. So therefore, it no longer actually contains sugar.

    It's a pretty easy calculation to figure out the original sugar content if you know the alcohol by volume and the residual sugar amount. Plenty of online homebrew calculators can work it out for you, like brewersfriend.com for example.

    If a serving size were 330 ml, and using 13g of sugar would yield 1.9% alcohol in the final drink. So more likely around 30g per serve was originally used.
