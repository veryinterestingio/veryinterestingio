      Yes, to completely eliminate electron capture you need isolated ions, you don't get that condition in chemical reactions.

      Chemical reactions generally affect the outermost shell only while electron capture prefers the innermost electrons, so normally the effect is very small. Beryllium-7 is the lightest nuclide that can only decay via electron capture, even there you have two inner electrons and two electrons in the second shell. Increasing its half life by 1% was an extraordinary large effect.
