    • karantza

      It's not specific to the Sun, it's just a property of fusion. Essentially, to fuse two nuclei, you need to overcome a particular input energy barrier. Without that threshold of energy, the nuclei simply cannot get close enough, and fusion cannot happen. Unless, due to quantum tunneling, the fuzziness in their position allows them to jump over that energy barrier. It's very unlikely for any given interaction, but when you've got an enormous number of interactions happening, it becomes statistically relevant. You essentially get slightly more fusion output than you'd expect; some small fraction of it is easier to achieve.
