      • PhysicsBus

        The anti-protons and anti-neurons would probably be produced in a collider (although it's possible you could get them naturally occurring in cosmic rays or something). The key idea, though, is that you would not just collide things together and wait for a bunch of anti-protons and anti-neutrons to spontaneously be created together in a bound state. The very nature of such a process is that it becomes exponentially rare for more complicated objects.

        Rather, you would generate your anti-protons and anti-neutrons, cool them down (since they would be moving ultra-relativistically), then combine them together in an (anti-)nuclear reactor, or whatever future technology you want to imagine.

        Likewise, when you want to make an iPhone, you collect the raw materials and them assemble them in a controlled manner. You don't just throw them at the wall :)
