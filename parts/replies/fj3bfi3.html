    One of the concerns about GMO foods is centred around this: if DNA or RNA is robust enough to be reliably inserted into new organisms through vectors like phages (viruses), then will it hang around long enough in organisms that eat that food (i.e us) to be incorporated into their genomes as well? Still under study, but a valid worry.

    One that has pretty much disproven with complete certainty already. In close to 40 years of commercial use of GMOs as stock feed and in human food, not a single trace of such an extremely hypothetical gene transfer through food has been found, despite the immense scrutinity under which genetically modified organisms had been put.

    In addition, even if there were such a mechanism, it would work for all kinds of food containing nucleic acids, not just GMOs.
