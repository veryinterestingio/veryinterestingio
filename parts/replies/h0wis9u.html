    I just posted a reply to the top comment about this, and I agree with you whole-heartedly.

    I have a hunch back due to misshapen vertebrae in my upper back. When I'm hunching, my back and neck muscles become fatigued much faster. I think I realized this more after I got Lyme disease and the muscle fatigue that comes with that. I just simply could not keep my head upright anymore. It was scary.

    I've started to pay attention to my posture much more after that. When I'm in a more upright position, I can balance my head over my shoulders and there is much less strain on those muscles.

    I've been treated for the Lyme, but I still get some of the symptoms like muscle fatigue and general fatigue. I don't know if it's some kind of permanent damage, if I've been reinfected, or if I didn't get rid of the disease with the meds the doctor gave me.
