    Your number for speed is correct (I did not know this, I googled to confirm). The speed varies by impulse type, the signal to move a muscle is the fastest, at "up 119m/s" which is roughly 425kph.

    ​

    I was curious about applying that speed to human scale. Let's call it 100m/s because it makes the math trivial, so the signal takes 0.01s to travel one meter. Head to foot on a 2m man would be 0.02s, or 20ms. A short person at 150cm would be 15ms. A difference of 5ms.

    ​

    The signals for pain travel slower, but they're like 70% as fast as the muscle impulse, so still in the tens of msrange for the impulse, and single digit ms for the difference between tall and short.

    ​

    I think that the difference is measurable but negligible given the range of normal reaction times. From my work, I know that most people can't press a button faster than about every 250ms. The impulse might travel in 20ms, but performing the motion takes time.
