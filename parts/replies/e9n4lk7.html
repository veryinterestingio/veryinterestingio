    I live in California. I grew up in San Diego and lived there in 2003 and 2007 when the firestorms hit and a third of the county burned. I'm currently about 90 minutes away from where the Camp fire is currently burning, and where the fires burned in Napa over the summer. I've got a friend who lives in Alberta.

    He was checking the other day to make sure I wasn't anywhere near the fires, and we got to talking about other things, and he mentioned a fire that they'd had in Fort McMurry a while back. "Yeah, it started burning in May of 2016, they finally put it out in August of 2017." It burned 1.5 million acres, and it was on fire for over a year.

    I was astounded. He said once it stopped threatening homes and winter set in, they just let it smolder. Hard to fight fire in the insanely cold weather, and it was just burning vegetation for the most part. You can't let a fire burn for over a year in California. There's not one and a half million acres in California where there's not people. The Camp fire, for all it's destruction, is only at 125k acres as of this morning, which is massive, but a tenth of the size of the Fort McMurry fire. San Diego's Cedar fire was just under 300k acres, still massive, but still a fifth of the size of the Fort McMurry fire..
