    Small correction.

    NaCl, KCl, and CaCl2 are ionic compounds, which are formed from ions but are not ions in and of themselves. In solution, such as it would be inside and around a cell, the compounds dissociate and exist as free ions: Na^+ , K^+ , Ca^2+ , and Cl^- . It is these individual ions that pass through ion channels.
