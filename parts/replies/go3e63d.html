    • blablahblah

      It means you're looking for lots of loans, and people who are looking for lots of new credit are riskier than people who aren't.

      Yes, it's a bit of a contradiction- you can only get loans if you don't need them- but that's the way the risk calculations work. But to expand on what /u/Some_Kind_of_Fan said about home loans: they don't penalize you for shopping around for a mortgage or car loan. If you request auto loan quotes from 6 different banks in a two week period, it gets treated the same as getting one quote when they calculate your credit score because the models assume you're only buying one car. Your score isn't going to be significantly lower at the sixth bank. The banks only really get worried if it looks like you're trying to take out a bunch of loans at once, like if you apply for an auto loan, a personal loan, and three new credit cards in quick succession.

      • Some_Kind_of_Fan

        To add to this, if you have four checks in a couple of days for a car loan, that's no big deal. If you have four checks now and then four more in a few months and then four more a few months later, that indicates a risk or someone who is prone to risk. These are all based on patterns of behavior. They set up an algorithm (really, many algorithms) that is meant to detect the type of behavior associated with someone who makes risky financial decisions. Seeking multiple types of credit or credit of the same type several times in a year is an indicator that this person might make risky decisions and might not be a great choice for a loan.
