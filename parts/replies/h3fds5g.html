      The amount of product that producers decide to create is not supply. It is the quantity supplied.

      Supply is a measurement of how difficult or possible it is to create a product. If a producer decides to make 400 donuts instead of 200, but they are just as difficult to create, the supply has not changed at all. Only the quantity supplied.

      Producers generally have very little control over the supply of their products.
