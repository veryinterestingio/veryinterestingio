    • forte2718

      Actually, the answer is no -- on the contrary, the existence of magnetic monopoles would make Maxwell's laws more symmetric, and it would also immediately imply quantization of electric charge which is something we observe in nature but which remains unexplained. Many grand unified theories also predict the existence of magnetic monopoles. In general, a number of things would work out better mathematically if magnetic monopoles existed, so it is a bit of an oddity as to why magnetic monopoles don't actually seem to exist.
