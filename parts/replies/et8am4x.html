    If the participant actually buys a product from the parent company, with the intention of re-selling it, they are acting like any other legal company. They are part of the supply chain of selling and re-selling. The problem is usually the product they sell is overpriced, so it's difficult for the re-sellers to move the stock they have. The parent company always makes money, while the re-seller may lose money. In some cases, depending on the market, the re-seller may actually make a profit; however, as soon as the market becomes saturated with more sellers, then they will all lose money because there is not enough buyers.

    This is why Herbalife and and similar stay afloat, because they actually sell a product; not everything is joining them as a seller; depending on the product, many people keep buying without even joining as sellers, so the market doesn't saturate.
