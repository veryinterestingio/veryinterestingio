    While I agree with the majority of your comment, I don’t think the cancer effect was completely negligible either. The idea that longevity beyond reproductive age doesn’t matter to evolution is wrong. For a crocodile that births, takes care of its eggs, and then ditches the kids, yes longevity beyond reproductive age isn’t important. But for animals where the individuals who have stopped being fertile are able to care for the newborns of the species, and reduce the risk of death for those individuals in their pre-reproductive years, their is an evolutionary advantage to longevity. So, while the folate effect should not be ignored as it’s probably the most important, I believe it was likely a combination of the folate and cancer effects that pushed for the change.
