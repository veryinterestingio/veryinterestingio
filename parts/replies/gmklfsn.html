        This comment may be trashed, but as a zooarchaeologist, I identify and reconstruct Native American economies based on animal remains from archaeological sites. Dog remains are difficult to identify if coyotes are a possibility. However, in the Early and Middle Woodland period in eastern North America (500 BC to 700 AD, it is not unusual to find dog remains (complete skeletons) interred with or adjacent to human burials. By 1000 AD, as social complexity and warfare became endemic to the Southeastern U.S., dog remains show up in War Feast deposits. Ethnographic reports indicate that they were included to demonstrate loyalty to the War Chief, prior to supping on other dishes. Has anyone tested DNA on North American prehistoric remains?
