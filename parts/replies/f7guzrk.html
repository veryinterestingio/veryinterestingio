  • IamNotTheProclone

    In my undergrad years in university I did research in a Microbiology lab regarding silver and it's antimicrobial properties. In conjunction with the engineering department we developed a silver nanofoam that can be used to coat surfaces and essentially keep the surface sterile. When I first started I had to do tons of reading into silver and its past uses. The ancient Egyptians, Romans, Greeks and Phoenicians all have record of using silver as an antimicrobial. The most common practice was storing water in silver containers. In fact, Greek historian Herdotus chronicled that no Persian king would drink water that was not stored in a silver container. This practice was crucial in military operations where fresh water was not available up until WWII. It was such a fun and interesting project. The engineering department ended up patenting the silver nanofoam and used all my physical experimentation to demonstrate it works!
