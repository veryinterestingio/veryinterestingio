      FM or AM?

      If FM, probably because your antenna is at the back of your vehicle, particularly if it's in the rear window.     Apart from this, there's also the position of the broadcasting antenna to take into account. If the antenna is near the east end of the tunnel, then the radio waves will propagate further into the east end of the tunnel than they will into the west end.

  • osgjps

    That's assuming your receiver actually has a PLL circuit. PLLs aren't used for "boosting" or "making it clearer", they're used for the actual demodulation of the signal. But an FM radio may not have a PLL-based demodulator, it may have a simple discriminator or a diode-based ratio detector.

    • Shannock9

      Not disagreeing, but remember it's all in a single chip (unless a vintage vehicle) so the PLL is no real extra cost, and easier to tune digitally.
