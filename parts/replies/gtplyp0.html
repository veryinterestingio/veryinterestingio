• TheSkiGeek

    Atoms are made out of protons/neutrons in the core, and electrons orbiting that core.

    Protons/neutrons are made out of quarks, electrons are made out of leptons. There are other subatomic particles, see: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subatomic_particle https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Standard_Model

    AFAIK it's currently believed those are the most fundamental units... but then there are things like https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/String_theory that try to tie together how those fundamental particles work at an even lower level. However, I don't think there is any experimental proof of anything like that. Experiments with particle accelerators have (so far) agreed with the "Standard Model".
