    • agate_

      Just to correct some expectations, /u/rhomboidus said that the rock would behave "like a liquid", which is right, but they didn't mean that it was like molten flowing lava.

      The pressure forces on the rock are so great that even though it's nowhere near molten, the rock slowly creeps in from the sides to fill the hole. Imagine drilling a hole in clay, or bread dough. Often this creep can take days or weeks to occur, but even so the rock flows in faster than the drill can cut it away, and eventually traps the drill in the hole.
