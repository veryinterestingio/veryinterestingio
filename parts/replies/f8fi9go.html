    • Marvin-Magical_Sword

      In a two energy level system there are two ways a photon can be generated from the high energy state. Spontaneously, or as a result of another photon of the same energy interacting with the atom (stimulated emission).

      Within a material, spontaneous emission occurs at a rate dependant only on the number of atoms in the high energy state, while stimulated emission depends on the number of high energy atoms and the density of photons. As it turns out, the coefficient for photon absorption from the lower energy state and the coefficient for stimulated emission are one in the same, so the only way for stimulated emission to dominate over absorbtion is for more atoms to be in the higher energy state than the lower, hence the name, population inversion.

      Look up Einstein A and B coefficients for a more in depth explanation.

    • colouredmirrorball

      As I understood it, it's some kind of resonance effect. Spontaneous emission is the electron falling back to the ground energy level by itself. Stimulated emission is the electron falling back faster than it originally would, because another photon comes along with the same energy as the transition to the lower energy state. You now have two photons of equal energy/wavelength, which can both stimulate other excited particles. It's a cascading amplification effect.

      I've seen the equations that explain this stimulated emission effect a long time ago in quantum mechanics, but my prof was horrible at explaining it properly so I can't make it more intuitive as to why it happens. These equations were derived by Einstein.

      Typically there are more energy levels than just ground level and first excited level. The electrons could be excited to, say, the fourth level, then fall back immediately to the third level, which is metastable. This means the decay from third to second excited state takes longer than usual, so the electron stays in the third state for a while. You need materials with such metastable states so you can have a lot of your electrons excited at the same time. This is the population inversion. And then, while in that third state, when a photon of the exact right wavelength comes along, you get the decay much sooner than it would normally. Then the electron would continue on decaying to first and ground level.

      There could be more metastable energy levels as well in the same path, or there can be multiple possible paths depending on ionisation or molecular states.
