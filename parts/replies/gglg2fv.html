    • Spanktank35

      When photons are "re-emitted" they don't preserve their wavelength. They have a range of wavelengths including the original. As the above commenter said, they're "re-emitted" as a black body spectrum. So you're going to have a significant decrease in that wavelength's intensity.

      I think your confusion stems from you thinking re-emitting means that the photons' wavelengths are the same when re-emitted. But when the photons are absorbed they're turned into another form of energy.
