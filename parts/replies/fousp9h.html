    • LeoUltra7

      There’s been a lot of discussion about your question already, and I like it a lot, but I’d like to add one more thing; if you did successfully dig a large tunnel, an artificial lava tube underground, (which would necessitate an accurate model of the internal structure and a whole lot of luck that there’s nothing else in your way like a reservoir or an otherwise-harmless fault line) it would presumably be leading somewhere. If that’s to an arbitrary place in the middle of nowhere, okay, that’s nice, eventually you will end up with a little lava flow out in the desert, which could potentially become its own little volcano. If that’s in the ocean though, then you probably have some issues in the vein of disrupting animal habitats. Lava flow through a coral reef is no-good. Even if nothing too endangered lives there, you are still creating a massive hazard; undersea volcanoes are incredibly dangerous to surface ships for a plethora of reasons, like poisonous and superheated gases and such. Bottom line is that unless wherever lava goes, it’s probably going to displace something that’s living there.
