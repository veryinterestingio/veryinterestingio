    I don't know the exact causes but a few that I can contribute

    1: Farmer's insurance to keep prices stables even during a downturn. This is because once a farmer goes down under, that land tends to stop being used for farming = more crops being out during a good economy = less crops. This makes it so most Farmers can survive during a downturn and better during an upturn.

    If there is really little demand, and the farmers all flood potatoes into a supermarket (when much of those crops were suppose to head towards hotels and restaurants, or processed foods) it will cause the price of potatoes to drop down dramatically...... this is a bad scenario because all the farmers that depended on potatoes as part of their income will soon find out that once the economy gets back up, the price of their crop will still be very low and not worth growing.

    We see part of this scenario play out during the Great Depression when it came to crops.

    2: The supply chain has been messed up, places that farmers depend on for income for everything from: Restaurants, Hotels, Supermarkets, and large social gatherings may have been hit. So less trucks of those crops are being needed or used because it is cheaper to burn than to rent a large loading truck to send it somewhere.

    3: There are some programs, that takes some of those resources and switch it to a free meal program during the summer (a lot of k-12 students are eligible to just to to a school or local gathering place to receive free food.)

    I don't know what other government programs are out there.

    Basically, keep Agriculture from feeling the effects of price fluctuation due to outside forces like the economy, and prevent current farmland from turning into something else (as once it turns to something else.... there is very little chance it will revert back to a farmland.)
