  • Milskidasith

    This makes sense as an explanation between 2006 and 2017. However, in 2017 "Tier 3" gasoline emission requirements were put in place, lowering gasoline sulfur emission requirements to 10 ppm. This makes treating diesel and gasoline similarly difficult, with both types of fuel generally requiring expensive and high quality catalytic hydrotreating units.

    Seals cracking in older vehicles appears to be because the hydrotreating process lowers aromatics, which has some sort of affect on the seal quality. I say "appears to be", because almost all of the talk I can find about it is on car forums filled with people who seem very into fuel processing and have absolutely zero knowledge about it, including claiming that hydroprocessing increases aromatics (no) or that aromatics are an additive (not really).
