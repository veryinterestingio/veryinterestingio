    Their art teacher was wrong, but only because Magenta, Yellow and Cyan are the true subtractive primaries, what you call primary pigments, not Red, Yellow and Blue.

    RGB and CMY are inversions of each other, they are the same just with different frames of reference. It’s not incorrect to call any of them primary colors, the trick is just to clarify if you’re talking about the additive or subtractive primaries.

    • woodbetween

      Other way. Blue Red and green make yellow.

      Whenever you think you see yellow, what you are really seeing is light that stimulates your blue and green cones almost equally.

      One way to do this is with light in the yellow part of the visible spectrum, which will stimulate both the blue red and green cones almost the same.

      Another way to do this is mixing light in the blue red part of the spectrum with light in the green part of the spectrum with near equal intensity.

      Your brain can't tell the difference.
