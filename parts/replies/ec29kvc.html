    Beyond the insulin thing (that's the pancreas) this is pretty spot on. It's worth explaining that glycogen is stored glucose - that is the primary energy source for all of your cells. Some of our glycogen is spread around the body inside of our muscles, but our secondary storage, the "Fort Knox" of glycogen I'd you will, is in the liver. This means a lot of stored energy lives in the liver waiting to be broken down when needed.
