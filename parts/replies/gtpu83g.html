      It isn’t just fat that can ruin a meringue. Your egg whites should be at room temperature with absolutely no yolk in them, and the bowl and whisk need to be clean and completely dried. Don’t put your sugar in until you have soft peaks. You can put the cream of tartar in at the beginning. Start the whipping on half power for a minute or two and then bring it up to full power. Even the glass you separate the whites into should be clean and free of water.
