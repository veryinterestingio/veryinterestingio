  • arcosapphire

    when you study the mathematics that physics is built upon, it treats time exactly like a 4th dimension of space

    That isn't true. They are closely related in relativity (in that everything has a constant velocity that is divided in some proportion between time and space), but in general time is treated differently than space in physics. That's why our familiar spacetime model is called "3+1 dimensions" and not simply "4 dimensions". Time isn't just another spatial dimension.
