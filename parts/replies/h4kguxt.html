    EASA has similar rules, but the FAA mandates a maximum duty period of 14 hours with a maximum flight time of 8 hours for commercial pilots per day. Sure people are upset when their flight is delayed, but half the time it happens is because the crew is (or could become) “over clocked” and a relief crew has to come in. If a pilot exceeds the 8 hours flight time by one second, they have to be off duty to rest for an extended amount of time and likely can’t even fly the next day. There is zero tolerance for exceeding a 14 hour “duty day” which is simply the time a pilot has to be present and available to do a flight, even if all they end up doing is sleeping in the nap room at the airport.

    Just to further your point, fatigue kills an enormous amount of people. Sleep deprivation can inhibit alertness and decision making worse than drunkenness at times. The only reason we have these rules is because one mistake is considered a national tragedy while doctors presumably only kill one person at a time. I wish there was protection and support for physicians.
