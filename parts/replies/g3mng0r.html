      • dougalcampbell

        The device decides at what rate it will charge, not the charger. Though the charger does indicate what rate it can support, by tying different values of resistors to the data lines. So when you connect a charger to your phone (or tablet, or whatever), the phone looks at the data lines to see if it supports charging at 1A, 2.4A, or of it should fall back to the old 0.5A USB standard (in the worst case). Then the phone configures its internal battery charge controller to draw the matching current.

        If you’re interested in some basics of how these chargers are put together, check out Ken Shirriff’s teardowns.

  • synthead

    Most phone chargers don't have a ground, so the ground is not actually connected to the earth. It's just relative to the +5V. This means that the ground connection does nothing until the circuit is completed. On devices connected to the earth as ground, you could neutralize static electricity or electromagnetic interference, but not without it.
