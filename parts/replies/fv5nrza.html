      A common practice in programming is to gather all the data and then perform calculations on it.

      The gathering tasks can be relatively fast. That would account for your first 90% of the bar.

      That last 10% could be where the program finally USES the data it gathered. Number crunching is resource intensive so it takes longer.

    • jon_the_mako

      Different calls to different services take different times.

      Imagine trying to bake a cake with only ingredients from your neighbors. The sugar is right next door so it's quick. While the eggs might be from down the road and take much longer to return with.

      Depending on the code you might even do these call concurrently meaning you could have an aid that goes out and gets ingredients with you. This can also speed up the process.

      One last thing is when the developer considers the call "done". Is it right after getting the eggs or after preparing the eggs for use. When calling services you don't always get exactly what you need. You will combine pieces of data from one call or another to determine what to show the user and depending on the data size that may take time. I know I prefer to finish loading when everything is done and user gets to see my product.
