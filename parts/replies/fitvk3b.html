    • Tayuven

      Was it a particularly strong, or painful massage? Like a deep tissue, or something where they use their elbows? One of the big myths of massage is that it flushes "toxins" out of your system. Scientifically, that has no proof at all. Massage hasn't been proven to flush "toxins", or lactic acid (which has been mostly dismissed as the the reason for sore muscles).

      However, deep tissue massage can, potentially, give you a form of mild-rhabdomyolysis (it's called massage-induced rhabdomyolysis). Symptoms like muscle fatigue, fever, nausea, urine changing color, dehydration, etc... So, in effect, harsh painful massages aren't removing toxins, they are actually dumping them in your bloodstream by crushing your muscles and damaging them.

      Don't get me wrong, I love massages! However, just keep in mind that, from a scientific standpoint, most of the benefits of massage are seen in the form of stress and anxiety reduction (and temporary, minor muscle relief). Your massage should be relaxing, not painful.
