    Everyone learns about “antigenic shift and antigenic drift in Virology 101, so they think antigenic shift is as common as drift. In humans, antigenic shift has happened about 4 times in the past 100 years, while antigenic drift happens many times each year.

    • TheSandwichMan2

      And antigenic drift is driven by influenza's lack of proofreading capacity, a quality not shared by coronaviruses.

      • iayork

        Not true. Measles and polio, among many other examples, have no proofreading and mutate just as fast as influenza, yet they don’t show antigenic drift. The difference is not in the proofreading, it’s in the unique ability of influenza proteins to tolerate mutations.
