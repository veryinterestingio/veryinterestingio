    • InfiniteImagination

      Yes, and for anyone else curious, here's (as documented in this StackExchange discussion) the IMDb version at the time:

      weighted rating (WR)=(v/(v+m))R+(m/(v+m))C

      where: R = average for the movie (mean) = (Rating) v = number of votes for the movie = (votes) m = minimum votes required to be listed in the Top 50 (currently 1000) C = the mean vote across the whole report (currently 6.8)

      I'm not sure whether this is what they still do. There might be something in place now to try to account for the known biases like a movie being dishonestly bombed with negative reviews because of stirring up controversy, or the voting demographics being different from the audience demographics or something.
