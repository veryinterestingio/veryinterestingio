    Still incomplete. It's not about "different fluids"; it's about reversal of the temperature gradient.

    Ordinarily, as you go up in altitude, the air gets less dense (an effect of gravity). But on a sunny day in the desert, the heated air at the surface expands and becomes less dense than the air above.

    Now refraction comes into the picture. As light travels through a medium of varying density, it gets refracted (i.e. bent) toward the more dense region. With that low-density region at the surface, light coming down from the sky gets bent up and hits your eyes instead of the sand. In other words, the surface layer acts like a mirror.
