      It's closer to the opposite, since swelling tends to make you far less mobile. Inflammation helps other stages of your immune response, reducing the chances of serious infection, and helps immobilize you so you don't reinjure yourself as the injury heals. Homicide and predation definitely put a non-zero amount of selective pressure on our ancestors, but accidents, child birth and malnutrition/starvation were bigger players.

      In modern medicine infection can almost always be prevented or treated with enough medical attention, and a large portion of hard to treat infections are a product of antibiotic-resistant bacteria. The levels of inflammation produced by a body that evolved under circumstances where a stubbed toe could become life threatening are completely inappropriate in a world with antibiotics, x rays and physical therapy, so reducing inflammation generally helps with comfort and quick recoveries.

      Source: absolutely no expertise, but I've got a lot of family in medical jobs that love talking about this stuff.
