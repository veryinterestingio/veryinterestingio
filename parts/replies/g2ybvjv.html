    The launching site needed to be as close as possible to the equator, because the earth is rotating faster there, giving a boost to rockets leaving the atmosphere. So Florida was, scientifically speaking, the best location for the launch site.

    Having an ocean downrange of the launch site was also an advantage in an era when a lot of rockets blew up.
