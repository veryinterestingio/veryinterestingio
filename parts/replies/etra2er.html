    A knob is anything that looks like a knob. It doesn't matter what it's connected to under the panel. Could be a pot, an encoder disc, a rotary switch, or whatever. You stick a knob on it, it's a knob.

    Also, no, devices that use rotary encoders do not track the position of the "wheel" (or disc, knob, marble, trackball, etc.). They can't, and they don't care. All the software cares about is which direction it's turning and how fast.
