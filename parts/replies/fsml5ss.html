    A stroke is actually going to result in hyperreflexia. This is due to the fact we are dealing with a upper motor neuron injury (brain and spinal column tracts). Whereas hyporeflexia is seen in lower motor neuron injuries (spinal cord nuclei and peripheral nerves).

      To amend this slightly, in the acute setting, strokes can present with hyporeflexia similar to a “spinal shock” phenomenon. Just as an acute stroke will not present with spasticity but rather a flaccid paralysis. Over time, this develops into hyperreflexia and spasticity and classic UMN clinical pattern.
