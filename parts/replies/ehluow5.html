    • mewithanie

      Your glottis is a hole. The sides of the hole are your vocal cords, they make noise by vibrating against each other, like a really fancy version of that grass blade whistle we all did at summer camp as kids. There are little muscles in those vocal cords that make them do that. They have to close up when you swallow so you don’t get food and liquid in your lungs.

      When you’re crying you get stressed out, and stress makes you want more oxygen (more air), so glottis wants to be open to let more air through. But you’re also making crying noises, which means your vocal cords want to rub together, and you’re also making snot which your body wants you to swallow, which means they want to close up so you can swallow all that snot. So the vocal cord muscles get confused and try to do everything at once, and that causes them to basically cramp like any other muscle.
