    • angermouse

      Y-chromosomal Adam is much more recent than mitochondrial Eve. And MRCA (most recent common ancestor) is even more recent and is likely only a few thousand years old.

      The important thing to remember is that these three are not fixed individuals. They are constantly changing as family lines die out. Several thousand years in the future, the MCRA could be someone who's alive today.

      If you go back just 10 generations or around 300 years you have a maximum possible of 1024 ancestors and for 20 generations it's more than a million (of course the actual number is much smaller). So there are lots of possible individuals from whom the common ancestor can arise.
