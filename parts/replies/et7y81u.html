    • ViskerRatio

      It infers it.

      Normal languages are constantly innovating from all points. The neat new word someone comes with over here doesn't necessarily make sense to people over there.

      However, math advances much more slowly - and in a more rigid fashion. Your work is heavily based on other people's work, so you might as well use their symbols - especially when you're trying to explain it to people who only know those concepts when associated with those symbols.

      It's the same reason that Chinese programmers understand 'while' loops. 'While' is an English word, not a Chinese word. But from the standpoint of computer programming, it's really just a symbol - and that symbol means the same thing whether or not you understand the language where it originated.
