        That's an excellent point that I'd genuinely overlooked: there could be ranges of radius in which the gravity actually increases with decreasing radius by reason of the increase in density with decreasing radius.

        But certainly the trend is decrease of gravitational field, beginning with g at the surface and ending as 0 at the centre. And for there to be a reversal of the trend the rate of change of density with R would have to be pretty steep: it would take ρ(R) < M(R)/2πR^3 (or density less than ⅔ of the mean density interior to that point) ... it's a matter of geological data whether that actually occurs anywhere inside or on the Earth or not.
