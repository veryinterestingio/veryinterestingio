    You could still make that work. You'd just need a full bridge rectifier (preferably with a low forward voltage to minimize the drop) for every single battery. You can then just hook up the output of all of those rectifiers in series to get the desired voltage.

    It's a huge hassle and needlessly expensive though. Hence why nobody does it.

      • Ralath0n

        You wouldn't want zener diodes for that. Zener diodes are the ones that break down when the reverse voltage is some specific value. So you can use them to clamp a voltage to some maximum value.

        Schottky diodes are the ones you are probably thinking off. Those are designed to switch really fast and have a very low forward voltage drop (I've seen as low as 0.2V). So they would be a lot more efficient than the zeners, though it'd still be pretty bad for the overall efficiency, especially when you use low voltage battery cells.
