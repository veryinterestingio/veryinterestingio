    • TotallyHumanPerson

      You can accelerate the separation with salt.

      Salt doesn't dissolve in alcohol, so a classic method for cleaning bongs is pouring in some iso and salt and shaking. Because the salt doesn't dissolve, it remains crystalline and acts as an abrasive. Salt does, however, love the shit out of water, so if you use rubbing alcohol of a lower concentration (70% vs 99%), the salt will soak up the water in the solution and settle on the bottom in a thick slush of semi dissolved (and no longer abrasive) salt enveloped in a bubble of cloudy water.

      At the concentration level of beer, I'd imagine using a whole lot of salt.
