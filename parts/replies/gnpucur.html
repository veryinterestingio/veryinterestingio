      Perhaps we do to a degree, but it’s not quite the same structure. If you look at the legs of creatures that will travel in the snow, they often have a thinner pinch point. On our bodies, it’s our ankle. It’s pretty close to the ground and fairly wide as compared to say, a deers. At these points the vessels are forced closer together for longer runs allowing more transfer of heat. But, because of the same transfer length, the body doesn’t get shocked by the difference in temperature between the extremities and the core body.
