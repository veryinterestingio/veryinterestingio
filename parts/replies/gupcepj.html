  • OStigger

    Another thing to note is: space isn’t cooling down per se, it’s all becoming the same temperature due to entropy. Space is full of hot things in specific locations. Some of these things are hot because they produce their own energy (stars fusing elements like hydrogen and helium) and some are hot because they are receiving energy via radiation from other hot things (earth, for example). Space itself can’t really have a temperature because there aren’t many atoms to move, so it’s sort of just a void through which energy can radiate via subatomic particles and photons.

    Eventually, things making their own energy will run out of gas, and all the energy they had at the end will radiate off through space into all the other bits of matter, over and over until eventually all mater will be the same temperature, aka the heat death of the universe.
