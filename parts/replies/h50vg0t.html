    Yup, fatal familial insomnia - it's generally inherited. It's also incredibly rare - only 50 or so families in the entire world carry the mutated copy of the prion protein gene (PRNP) that causes fatal familial insomnia, but there's also a variant called sporadic fatal insomnia caused by a very specific mutation in a very specific region of the prion protein gene that causes one amino acid to be replaced with another amino acid. It's even rarer than the inherited version - as of 2016, only 24 cases in total have been diagnosed.

    Basically, prions are proteins that've somehow become misfolded. They can cause other correctly-folded proteins of the same type to refold themselves into the incorrect configuration, which is how prion diseases progress.
