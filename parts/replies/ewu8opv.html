    • Hjemmelsen

      Using intermediaries is different. The money you receive on the other end aren't "the same" as the ones you sent. The intermediary just have an account in each country, sometimes in each bank in the country as well, and when you transfer money across borders, you are actually just transferring money internally in the two banks - from your account to theirs, and from theirs to yours.

      This is possible because the intermediary takes the risks associated with the transfer, and therefore you also pay a small fee.

      On a totally separate note, it also makes it hella easy to launder money in small amounts.

      • ajd341

        True, but my point stands... there’s twice as many accounts involved yet it’s done in half the time.
