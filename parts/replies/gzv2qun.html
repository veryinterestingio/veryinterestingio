• Nienordir

    It isn't just size or drivers wasting fuel through idling, etc.

    In a small combustion engine you're only interested in the mechanical energy, created by expanding gases, that rotate the engine. But the majority of the chemical fuel gets wasted as heat energy and in a car you can't do anything useful with it, aside from using a little to heat the cabin in winter.

    That's why fuel is always better used in a power plant, because you can build infrastructure around it, that requires heat to work and 'double dip' to maximize the efficiency of the power plant by utilizing the waste heat of the power generation for other industrial processes (that otherwise would have to waste more electricity/fuel to power their heaters).

    For example a lot of cold climate countries have cities with centralized heating, so the electric power plants waste heat is used to heat buildings in the entire city, maximizing the efficiency of the fuel used.
