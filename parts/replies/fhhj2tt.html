    According to my (very non-expert) understanding, heat regulation definitely played a role in why relatives of elephants and rhinos in ice age Eurasia and North America (mammoths, wooly rhinos, etc) evolved to be bigger than their modern African relatives. When you're big, you have a smaller surface area compared to your volume, meaning it's easier to retain heat in a cold climate.

    It does seem that expert opinion on why they went extinct leans more towards being overhunted by humans. But the world is complicated, so it's likely a combination of various contributing factors, including hunting and changing climate.
