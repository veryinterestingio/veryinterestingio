• BoredCop

    And it's electronically very similar to headphones, too.

    Headphones or speakers work by sending the audio signal through small electromagnets, that make the speaker membranes vibrate.

    Those casette adapters have a pair of small electromagnets (one for left and one for right in the stereo signal). From the point of view of your phone or whatever device you're playing audio from, the casette adapter is indistinguishable from a set of headphones because an electromagnet is an electromagnet (assuming equal impedance) no matter if it drives a membrane or induces a signal in the casette deck's read head.

    So, the device sending the audio signal doesn't know or care if there's a casette adapter.

    The casette player also doesn't know or care. Its read head is designed to convert changes in magnetic field into an electric signal. Wether those changes come from a magnetic tape being passed in front of the head or from the electromagnets in the adapter doesn't matter.
