    The phenomenon’s called the coffee ring effect lol source: I’m a phd student in an adjacent field Edit: it happens when you have a drying drop containing some dissolved solids. If the edge of the droplet is immobilized, then there has to be a net flow of liquid out towards the drop’s edge. Otherwise, the edge of the drop would move in. This carries and deposits particles near the edge of the drop, leading to the formation of a ring. There’s other ways it can happen, but this is one basic case

      The way I've seen it explained is that as the edge dries(liquid evaporating leaving solids behind), the inner liquid flows out, bringing more solids to the edge.
