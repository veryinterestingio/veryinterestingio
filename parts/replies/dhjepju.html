    I came here to mention this.

    A person with a normal digestive system could probably create a diet of 'supplements' rather than food that would meet their nutritional needs. I don't think anyone has put together a system like that simply because it's such a poor replacement for proper food.

    TPN, total parenteral nutrition, was invented to keep alive people who had no working digestive system. TPN uses two solutions, one water based, one fat based, to provide all of a person's nutritional needs (or at least it originally did, no idea about current practice). I encountered it in the late 70's where it was used with people who had had their bowel removed, I think it was due to really bad Crohn's disease. In the years since it's been used for critically ill people to good effect.
