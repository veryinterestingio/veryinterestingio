      • neildmaster

        Some terminology. Hedge funds are private investment vehicles, not "very risky investment opportunities". Hedge fund managers can invest however they want to, assuming their operating documents and investors agree to it.

        If someone has a proven track record of betting on horses they could setup a hedge fund to do just that. They are private, unlisted, generally unregulated investment vehicles.

        Also, not all hedge funds are risky. Some are setup to be risk averse.

      • pole_fan

        The general idea of a hedge fund is that it should generate return not correlated to the market situation. Hence the name hedge. If you hedge your investment you buy "insurance" against crashes (also called shorts). Eg the 2008 crash. Normal investment funds lost a lot of value during the time, because they have strict rules on how to invest their money. Hedgefund don't and can do whatever they want. So they could hedge against crashes. Some big hedgefunds generated a lot of return during the 2008 crash bc of that. The public thinks it's more risky because hedgefunds are not regulated as much as other investment funds.
