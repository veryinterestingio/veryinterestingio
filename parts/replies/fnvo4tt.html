      People definitely experience time differently. There is something called time blindness, which is basically the inability to gauge the passage of time. It could be that time is going faster or slower than it really is. Many people with adhd have this, which is why they can hyper focus on something and have no idea how much time has past and then miss something they were supposed to do. It also goes in the other extreme and they can get bored instantly and 10 seconds can feel like an hour. Time is a weird concept I don’t really understand, probably because I have adhd. Time is passing but I never know how long and sometimes a month has past and I haven’t even realized it, like it seems something happened yesterday but it was actually a month ago.

      Edit: in response to your comment about professional athletes or esports professionals - their awareness of the situation is higher. They are able to take in more variables than an average person because they have practiced so much. So playing a game, it might seem like things are going fast because everything is new to you, but for them, it becomes very repetitive and habitual. This is why you need to practice to be good at things. So in some sense, they are experiencing time more slowly than you but it’s something they practiced and prepared for.
