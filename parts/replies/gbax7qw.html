      Substances like soap actually are able to make nonpolar things water soluble by forming structures called micelles. They're basically microscopic bubbles that orient all of the soap's nonpolar tails inwards, which creates a little nonpolar space that can isolate oils from the surrounding water molecules. The whole micelle is water soluble because the polar heads are pointed outwards where they can be solvated by water, and by encapsulating nonpolar molecules they are able to dissolve them in water
