      • theRogueTrombonist

        I think it's important to clarify that fixing the window is of value, because you're upgrading a broken window to fix it. What is not of value is breaking a window in order to create a demand for labor to fix it.

        It might sound like I'm splitting hairs, but it's important to remember that a huge percentage of the valuable economic work being done today is maintenance work (I think David Graeber has some good comments on this). Heart surgeons, mechanics, and even window fixers are adding value. The broken window fallacy just shows that it's wasteful to artificially and unnecessarily create a need for more maintenance workers.
