    Fun fact! Although he's not specifically talking about the Trans-Atlantic accent, the two are related.

    Actors back in the day were trained to speak like that for 2 reasons.

    1 - everyone was super racist back then, and the fake accent made it impossible to tell if the actor/actress was Irish or Jewish or too Waspy or whatever.

    2 - Speakers back then had TERRIBLE bass (which is kinda what OP is talking about), so they adopted the trans-atlantic accent because it's basically impossible to talk like this without raising the pitch of your voice, see. Say, now just you wait a minute, see. Try it now. Talk like Al Capone for a minute and see what happens, see. So, the raised pitches had the added benefit of making the actors easier to hear and understand on the primitive sound systems they were using.
