  • moonie223

    That is incorrect.

    Fluorescent tubes are all ultraviolet, we can't see any of it without assistance. The coating on the inside of the bulb is what makes light. On a white light, it's stuff that glows under UV. On a blacklight bulb, the coating blocks harmful short wave UV and lets longer safer UV light past.

    If you've ever seen a sterilization UV bulb, you'll notice the tube is perfectly clear. Will also give you arc flash, welder's burns on your eyeballs, so no good.
