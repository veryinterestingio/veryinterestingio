        OK. So DNA is a doublestranded helix (you've probably seen cartoons of it) and each base of one strand interacts with another. Both strands are complement to each other - one has an A the other has a T. Interactions are only possible between A and T and G and C (there are a few chemicals that can mimick some bases but that is not important for this). The machinery in the cell recognizes certain sequences that mark the start of a gene. For example the start codon is always ATG. To make sure that no random ATGs are by mistake recognized as start codon there have to be some other motifs in the right distance as well. You can imagine it like sitting down in your favourite chair in front of the TV with the remote, a beer and chips at the right distance. If there are no snacks you get up and grab some. ðŸ˜Š And as others have pointed out DNA is stored by wrapping it around proteins called histones. Which genes to express at what time is a tightly regulated process. For proteins to "read" the sequence of DNA it has to be unwound from the histone proteins. Some genes also exist in several copies. When the whole concept of DNA as a storage molecule for genetic information was new we thought that we can just start at one end of the chromosome and turn each gene "on" and "off" until we come to the end. Unfortunately it's much more complicated than that I hope that answers your questions a little bit. If you have more just ask!
