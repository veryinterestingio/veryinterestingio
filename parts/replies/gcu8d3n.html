    In socialism, the MoP are run by the workers. For example, the workers at the local steel mill would democratically decide on the price of their product, who to hire and fire, et cetera.

    This is a very specific form of socialism--libertarian socialism or syndicalism, or the ultimate form of communism. It's interesting that most of the comments here are giving this utopian form of socialism as the definition of socialism.

    The most common form of socialism in practice has been State socialism. The State controls industry and the means of production, and in theory is meant to guard and represent the interests of the workers. This is done through a centralized, planned economy.
