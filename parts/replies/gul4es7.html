  • KinoranaUPSB

    surface brightness of a resolved object doesn't depend on distance

    That's crazy, I never really thought about that! So if you walk closer to a lightbulb, it doesn't really get brighter per unit angular diameter, but just takes up more space in your field of view, giving the illusion of getting brighter? Does that mean that as the Andromeda galaxy approaches, it won't get brighter in the sky, just larger?

    • Astrokiwi

      The total power you get does increase as you get closer, but the power per square degree of sky doesn't change. So yeah, Andromeda won't really get brighter in terms of surface brightness (maybe a little bit because there'll be less dust in the way) but we will be getting more total light from it, because it'll take up more of the sky.
