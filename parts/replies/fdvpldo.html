        Dunno. Capacitors are different from transformers, but same kinda principle. If the capacitors are the biggest thing in the amp, then unless they come up with a clever trick to eliminate them, you're stuck with them.

        A transformer needs to hold all the energy that it transfers per "cycle", so a switching converter works around that by doing a lot faster cycles (this is over-simplified). Capacitors also store energy, but typically, the way they're used means they stay charged - they don't get charged and discharged over and over, so that particular solution won't work.
