      You'd need to ask a protein biochemist/structural biologist. I think it is possible, but in a different perspective. A slow change in conformation suggests numerous (ten?) closely related conformational states. I'd think that's quite improbable, as having ten discrete states might be too complex a model. What might actually be happening, as proposed by that same paper, is that the enzyme binds the ssDNA in the closed conformation state of a hinge-like architecture. p58 would recognize the template and p49 initiates synthesis. As the former binds the 5' end, p49 would get further and further from it while synthesizing. Then due to some deeper mechanism, at a given distance, p49 is no longer able to synthesize further.
