      Mean arterial pressure /MAP= average of pressure in one cycle (from pumping the blood from heart to full relaxation of heart+filling back the blood for next pump). Since Diastolic BP/DBP(relaxation time) is twice longer than Systolic BP/SBP (pumping time), so MAP = (2xDBP+SBP)/3

    • jdbnsn

      The pulse pressure is the difference between the systolic and diastolic values, in other words the range in pressure from it's highest to lowest readings. For blood to adequately circulate it needs a sufficient pressure gradient to drive it through the blood vessels. The narrow pulse pressure of 100/90 only provides 10mmHg which is not strong enough to properly perfuse the end organs. A wide pulse pressure of 180/60 produces too much impact on the vessels with each beat and causes damage (or rupture).
