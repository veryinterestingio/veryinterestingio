    This. Some people can be perfectly happy and healthy with 6 hours of sleep a night, others would do better with 9 or 10. And the ideal amount changes over the course of your life.

    Also, the cycles of "levels" are, ON AVERAGE, about 90 minutes, not exactly 90 minutes for everyone all the time. Minutes are made up arbitrary units of measurement, after all, not biologically determined.

    Which is why if you can get into a rhythm of waking up before your alarm goes off you'll probably have much better mornings. If you sleep without an alarm your body will wake up after a cycle, whenever that is.

    • YoungAnachronism

      Another thing to consider, is that age plays a part in things. My experience so far has been that what your body is comfortable with, sleep wise, goes through phases as you age. You have the baby to toddler phase, where patterns are widely varied and strange, theres a relative normalisation during childhood, right up until puberty kicks in and throws the entire thing in a spin for a few years. Then you reach young adulthood, and you can go 72 hours with no sleep and still have a bounce in your step right up until the age of about 32, when you NEED sleep like you need your next breath, and given the choice between the sleep and the breath, you'll take the sleep. I am 35 now, and still something of a nightowl, but I do need my Z's. Never used to care much up until relatively recently.

      I imagine things will continue to be in flux until I die, with regard to sleep cycles. I could end up being one of those folks who falls asleep at the drop of a hat, or I could wind up being one of those crotchety old bastards who gets sleep in hour long doses, and always looks like they are one step away from starting to say "Braiiiiiiins" and trying to nom peoples skulls. No idea till it starts to happen.
