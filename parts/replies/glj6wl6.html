    Gravity and Shape

    I just want to expand on this point a bit, as it's the primary way we figure this stuff out for gas giants - and is also the primary science goal of the Juno spacecraft currently orbiting Jupiter.

    While an orbit around a perfect sphere will not change, an orbit around a slightly squashed sphere will gradually change over time. In particular, two things will happen:

    • The perihelion will precess

    • The nodes will regress

    By carefully measuring the spacecraft's position and velocity, we can watch how the orbit subtly changes in these ways. We match these changes to mathematical models of the interior; as we alter each layer's density in the model, this affects how spherical that layer is, and in turn, how that layer contributes to the changes in the orbit.

    Early models were generally just two layers, a small central dense core that's more spherical, and a surrounding mantle that's less dense and less spherical. The actual math is pretty gnarly with integrals that don't have analytical solutions (pro-tip: Maclaurin spheroids, not even once), so we really needed computation to get better before our models got better. These days they're really sophisticated, with potentially numerous layers and density gradients, and enough to demonstrate pretty clearly that Jupiter's core has been slowly dissolving into the surrounding mantle of liquid metallic hydrogen.

    • Astromike23

      I think you might be confusing ferromagnetism (that really only works for Iron, Nickel, and Cobalt) with induced magnetism. There are many different ways to generate a magnetic field.

      It's not the fact that it's iron here that matters; it's the fact that it's a material that can conduct electricity. After all, consider that a wire loop made of pure copper will generate a magnetic field if you run a current through it.

      Similarly, for a dynamo effect inside a planet, what matters is the motion of a fluid that can conduct electrical charges. In the case of Jupiter and Saturn, the source of their magnetic fields is hydrogen squeezed so tight that it becomes a liquid metal. In the case of Uranus and Neptune, their magnetic fields come from water under extreme pressure, which becomes an electrically-conducting slush.

      • Astromike23

        The most likely explanation for Venus’s lack of field is that it’s rotating too slowly

        That was definitely the common explanation a few decades ago (Russell, et al, 1979), but recently it seems most hypotheses have focused on the lack of internal convection (e.g. O'Rourke, et al, 2018) as the missing component of the Venusian dynamo. Even in the first linked paper, while Russell suggests that slow rotation is a cause, he also notes that Mercury is a slow rotator yet maintains a magnetic field, and scaling that value to Venus parameters does not properly predict the field's weakness.

        It remains unclear whether convection is being quenched by a shallow temperature gradient throughout the planet's interior, or perhaps just in the Venusian core where planetary accretion would have left stably stratified layers of metal that were never homogenized enough to encourage convection.

    To expand on seismic waves a bit. There are two types of seismic waves that get to the core. One can pass through liquids and the other cannot. The earth is dotted with recording stations that check for these waves constantly. What happens when an earthquake occurs is some of 'solid only' waves will hit stations on the other side of the planet but they will not hit ones DIRECTLY on the other side of the planet. That's because the core creates a kind of 'shadow' effect with this type. With the other type the wave will actually bend in a similar way to light at the boundary between the solid mantel and liquid core. It will do this whenever it hits a liquid/solid boundary and the amount it bends tells us a lot about the makeup of what that liquid is. That bent wave will hit one of the recording stations and seismologists use a bunch of math in combination with the readings they got of the other kinds of waves to pinpoint the earthquake epicenter and then can calculate where all of the boundaries are using certain known variables like the speed of each type of wave etc.
