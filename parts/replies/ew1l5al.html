      But some other bacteria thrive WITHOUT oxygen

      True but they're pretty rare in the normal aerobic environment so they'd have a hard time reaching the leaf during the window of opportunity, and anaerobic bacteria aren't super duper active and don't seem to be huge degrader e.g. pretty much all of our fossils (as well as our fossil fuels) come from organic matter being trapped in anaerobic environments and decaying slowly enough to imprint on the environment or even get converted.

      The anaerobic environment can contribute further by being acidic/desiccant (bog bodies) or very cold, but AFAIK amber or mud burials and the like are pretty straight anaerobic with little further.
