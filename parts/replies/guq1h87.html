    • tonsofsoul

      It's very counterintuitive, but that's not actually how it works. The speed of light is constant for everyone. So if I'm moving towards you at half the speed of light, and I shine a flashlight at you, from my point of view the light leaves my flashlight at the speed of light and from your point of view it moves towards you also at the speed of light. And if there is a third person moving towards you at half the speed of light from the other side we both see the light from his flashlight moving at exactly the speed of light. This is due to time dilation and length contraction from Einstein's Relativity.

      So you can see anything that emits light no matter how fast it's moving.
