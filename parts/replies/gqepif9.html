    The Colorado's water is used to irrigate an area of farmland roughly the size of Missouri throughout the basin and outside of it through diversion projects. Some of this farmland is among the most productive in the world and is a large part of why we have access to things like strawberries and lettuce in January in the US. The system remains relatively inefficient with old wasteful irrigation methods still common while water intensive low value crops are still grown in several areas. Water law does little to incentivize efficiency except by sheer market forces and we are seeing the beginning of the impacts of the strain on the basin which will only grow due to climate change.
