    Language and culture have a reciprocal relationship, so in general the fact that the Hawaiian language makes these distinctions means that the age difference is important culturally (or was at some point in time). Essentially what I mean by this is that there is no real limit to the complexity or specificity that familial terms can take, other than the specificity still being culturally important.

    Think about the role of Godparents for example, culturally they are/were important (if something should happen to the parents Godparents step in) so they get a specific appellation, despite not necessarily being related by blood.
