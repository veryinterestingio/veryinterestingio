    • MonsterMathh

      Muscle “spasms” are otherwise known as cramps. They can occur for a multitude of reasons, however the most common is injury or overuse.

      When a muscle is being contracted repeatedly, more and more calcium is “left over” within the muscle cell. If you do not give it enough time to pump all of the existing calcium out, it will contract quicker than before, and may end up in a state of prolonged contraction. This is sometimes localized to only part of a muscle, or the entire muscle.

      To improve the pumping of calcium out of muscle cells, you can only do resistance training. If there is more calcium within the muscle cell more often, then these calcium pumps will tend to be inserted in greater numbers, along with the size of the muscle cell increasing, meaning there will be a higher threshold for calcium ions.
