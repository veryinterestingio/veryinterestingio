    Maillard, with an A, but otherwise yeah definitely.

    It's also easier to cook the "raw flour" taste out of it by mixing it with fat and exposing directly to a hot surface (making a roux), rather than mixing it into liquid and hoping it doesn't clump and that the simmering cooks it.

    So coating your meat with it and searing essentially gets you maillard browning and a roux at the same time. Then you add that to your stew liquid and get both flavor and thickening.
