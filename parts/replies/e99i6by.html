  • robbak

    The tale of the prickly pear in Australia deserves telling. Imported to create self-repairing fences and to grow cochineal insects for dyes, it rapidly took over, becoming a major plant pest. But once they imported an insect to feed on them - appropriately named the 'cactoblastus' moth - the plants were quickly killed off and out-competed by local flora.
